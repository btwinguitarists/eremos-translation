# ἐκκλησία → คริสตจักร

**Scope:** The Greek noun ἐκκλησία wherever it refers to the Christian community (gathered or institutional). Two Matthean occurrences (16:18, 18:17) set the precedent for ~110 NT occurrences in Acts, the Pauline corpus, Hebrews, James, 1 Peter, 3 John, and Revelation.

**Decided:** 2026-04 during Matthew end-of-book review (see `docs/MAT_END_OF_BOOK_REVIEW_2026-04-19.md` §1).
**Evidence base:** Matthew 16:18 (Πέτρος / πέτρα / οἰκοδομήσω μου τὴν ἐκκλησίαν), Matthew 18:17 (the church-discipline triad: τῇ ἐκκλησίᾳ → ὁ ἐθνικὸς καὶ ὁ τελώνης).

---

## The rule

**ἐκκλησία (referring to the Christian community) → คริสตจักร**, consistent across the NT corpus.

Apply when:
- The referent is the Christian community in any sense — local congregation, regional grouping, or trans-local body of Christ.
- Includes proleptic uses (Jesus speaking about a future church before Pentecost, as at Matt 16:18 / 18:17).

**Do NOT apply when:**
- ἐκκλησία refers to a secular Greek civic assembly (e.g., Acts 19:32, 19:39, 19:41 — the Ephesian riot uses ἐκκλησία in its non-Christian original sense). In those cases use **ที่ประชุม** or **การชุมนุม** with a verse-level note explaining the disambiguation.
- ἐκκλησία refers to the OT/LXX assembly of Israel (Acts 7:38 — Stephen's reference to Moses ἐν τῇ ἐκκλησίᾳ ἐν τῇ ἐρήμῳ). Use **ที่ประชุม** + note.

## Why คริสตจักร and not ที่ประชุม / ชุมนุม

**Argument for คริสตจักร (chosen):**
- Standard Thai Christian terminology since the 19th-century missions; immediately recognizable to the target audience as "the church."
- Captures the Christological dimension that ที่ประชุม does not (คริสต = Christ; the term is etymologically tied to the Messianic identity of the community's founder).
- Matches Thai church usage across denominations — Catholic, Mainline Protestant, Pentecostal, Independent. No tribe-marking effect.
- For 16:18 specifically: Jesus speaks of *his* community (μου τὴν ἐκκλησίαν). Christological-possessive force is intrinsic; คริสตจักร makes that visible.

**Counter-argument (Grok-flagged 2026-04, see `docs/MAT_END_OF_BOOK_REVIEW_2026-04-19.md`):**
- คริสตจักร carries post-Pentecost institutional connotations.
- At Matt 16:18 and 18:17, Jesus is speaking proleptically — the institution does not yet exist. ที่ประชุม / ชุมนุม would better preserve the pre-institutional semantic range.
- For 18:17 ("tell it to the ἐκκλησία") an early-1st-century hearer might think more of a synagogue-shaped accountability gathering than a fully-formed church.

**Why we lock to คริสตจักร anyway:**
- The proleptic objection cuts both ways: NT itself uses ἐκκλησία freely from Acts 5 onward without anachronism worry. The original audience of Matthew (post-AD-70) read 16:18 / 18:17 as foundational sayings *for the existing church*, not as time-locked predictions.
- Splitting the rendering — ที่ประชุม at 16:18/18:17 but คริสตจักร from Acts onward — would obscure the verbal continuity Matthew is establishing. The whole point of 16:18 is that Jesus *names* the future entity; the Thai should let the reader hear that name.
- Translation note at 16:18 + 18:17 can flag the proleptic dimension without forcing a different rendering.

## Why not transliterate (เอกเลซีอา)

Considered. Rejected:
- Foreign-sounding; defeats the readability goal.
- Thai church history has settled on คริสตจักร; introducing a foreign term would be revisionist without payoff.
- Reader confusion: คริสตจักร IS the established term for what ἐκκλησία refers to in the NT.

## Why not coined neologism (e.g., ชุมชนคริสเตียน "Christian community")

Considered. Rejected:
- Wordy; breaks reading flow at every occurrence (~110×).
- Loses the singular-noun quality of ἐκκλησία.
- คริสตจักร already serves this function in standard Thai.

## Forward implications

This lock determines:
- **Acts** (~23×): ἐκκλησία ἐν Ἱεροσολύμοις (5:11, 8:1, 11:22), αἱ ἐκκλησίαι (15:41, 16:5), τὴν ἐκκλησίαν τοῦ θεοῦ (20:28). All → คริสตจักร except secular-civic uses (19:32, 19:39, 19:41).
- **Pauline corpus** (~60×): including ἐκκλησίᾳ θεοῦ (1 Cor 1:2, 2 Cor 1:1), αἱ ἐκκλησίαι τῶν ἐθνῶν (Rom 16:4), αἱ ἐκκλησίαι πᾶσαι τοῦ Χριστοῦ (Rom 16:16). All → คริสตจักร.
- **Pastorals + Hebrews + James + 1 Peter** — same.
- **Revelation 2–3 letters**: ταῖς ἑπτὰ ἐκκλησίαις (1:4) and the seven address-formulas (2:1, 2:8, 2:12, 2:18, 3:1, 3:7, 3:14). All → คริสตจักร.

## Alternatives considered

- **คริสตจักร throughout (chosen).**
- **Split: ที่ประชุม at 16:18/18:17, คริสตจักร from Acts.** Rejected — splits the verbal thread Matthew establishes.
- **Transliteration เอกเลซีอา.** Rejected — defeats readability.
- **Phrase-level: ชุมชนคริสเตียน / ชุมชนของพระเยซู.** Rejected — wordy, breaks flow.

## When to revisit

- If a future Thai Christian theological consensus shifts (e.g., a major denominational rendering change), revisit.
- If a specific verse forces the issue — e.g., a strong contrast between gathered-assembly and institutional-body within a single Pauline letter — verse-level note can carry the nuance without changing the term.
- If readers report comprehension difficulty (does คริสตจักร feel anachronistic in the Gospels?), spot-check with native-Thai readers and revise if needed.
