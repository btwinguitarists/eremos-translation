## 1. The Hebrew idiom

`גָּלָה עֶרְוָה` ("uncover the nakedness of [X]") is a Hebrew euphemism for sexual intercourse with X. The construction appears in three forms in the Holiness Code:

| Hebrew | Direct sense | Euphemistic sense |
|---|---|---|
| גָּלָה עֶרְוָה (piel `gillāh`) | "to uncover [someone's] nakedness" | sexual relations with that person |
| רָאָה עֶרְוָה ("see the nakedness") | "to see [someone's] nakedness" | same (sometimes intentional voyeurism) |
| הֶעֱרָה (hiphil `heʿĕrāh`) | "to expose / lay bare" | same |

In LEV 18:7 the formula introduces a list of prohibited relations; in LEV 20:11-21 it serves as the legal-paragraph clincher for each penalty.

## 2. The hybrid policy

Two competing translation pressures:

- **Modern Thai readability** — most readers will not pick up the euphemism. A literal `เปิดเผยความเปลือยเปล่า` for every occurrence risks obscuring *what is being prohibited*, especially in the rapid-fire catalog of LEV 18:6-18.
- **Hebrew shame-register preservation** — the euphemism is *not* a coy avoidance; it is a juridical-legal device. LEV 20:11 `עֶרְוַת אָבִיו גִּלָּה` ("he has uncovered his father's nakedness") makes the *offense against the father* explicit by preserving the literal phrase. A purely sanitized translation loses this.

**The locked hybrid policy:**

| Context | Thai rendering |
|---|---|
| LEV 18:6-18 prohibition catalog (programmatic openers, relationship-pronouns clarify the prohibition) | **มีความสัมพันธ์ทางเพศกับ [relative]** (explicit) — preserves readability; relationship term carries the legal weight |
| LEV 18:7 opening formula (Hebrew explicitly contrasts father's and mother's nakedness) | **เปิดเผยความเปลือยเปล่าของบิดา ... มีความสัมพันธ์ทางเพศกับมารดา** (hybrid in single verse) — preserves the father-shame logic |
| LEV 20:11-21 penalty clincher clauses (`עֶרְוַת אָבִיו גִּלָּה` etc.) | **เปิดเผยความเปลือยเปล่าของ [father/sister/etc.]** (literal) — the legal-finding requires the juridical formula |
| LEV 20:17 incest with sister + mutual seeing | **มีความสัมพันธ์ทางเพศ ... [และ] เห็นความเปลือยเปล่า** — preserves the doubled formula (seeing + uncovering) |
| LEV 20:18 menstrual-period contact + "source of her blood" exposed | **เปิดเผยแหล่งที่มาของเลือดของนาง** — anatomical-legal register requires the literal |

## 3. Why hybrid, not uniform

A **uniform literal** policy (`เปิดเผยความเปลือยเปล่า` everywhere) would obscure the LEV 18 catalog for ordinary Thai readers and risk being heard as describing *seeing nakedness* rather than *sexual intercourse*.

A **uniform explicit** policy (`มีความสัมพันธ์ทางเพศ` everywhere) would erase the LEV 20 penalty-formula's deliberate shame-register and the LEV 20:11 "against your father" extension that the euphemism enables.

The hybrid is what the shipped LEV already does. The doc locks the pattern.

## 4. Decision rule (for reviewers)

When facing a `גָּלָה עֶרְוָה` occurrence in a future book:

1. **Is the surrounding clause a prohibition catalog where the relationship term itself carries the legal weight?** → use `มีความสัมพันธ์ทางเพศกับ [relative]`.
2. **Is the surrounding clause a penalty-formula where Hebrew explicitly says "against X's nakedness"?** → preserve the literal `เปิดเผยความเปลือยเปล่าของ [X]`.
3. **Is the Hebrew using a paired construction (e.g., seeing + uncovering, or two relatives being referenced)?** → use the hybrid — explicit for the first relationship, literal for the second clause.
4. **Is the anatomical/medical register doing real work (e.g., LEV 20:18 source-of-blood)?** → preserve the literal anatomical register.

## 5. Forward protection — Deuteronomy

- **Deut 22:30** (`לֹא־יְגַלֶּה כְּנַף אָבִיו`) — the "wing of his father's [garment]" idiom is a variant of the uncover-nakedness formula. Locked Thai: **ไม่เปิดผ้าคลุมของบิดา** with explanation in the note.
- **Deut 27:20** (curse for sleeping with father's wife) — penalty-clincher register, use literal `เปิดเผยความเปลือยเปล่าของบิดา`.

## 6. Forward protection — Ezekiel

- **Ezek 16:8** — YHWH's covenant marriage metaphor with Israel: "I spread my garment over you and covered your nakedness." This is a *positive* use of the formula (covering, not uncovering). The Thai must preserve the metaphor with `กางผ้าคลุมของเรา ... และเราคลุมความเปลือยเปล่าของเจ้า`.
- **Ezek 16:36-37 / 22:10 / 23:18, 29** — Ezekiel uses the formula extensively for Israel's "spiritual adultery" with idols, mostly in the literal exposed-nakedness register (shame-imagery, not just sexual). Use literal `ความเปลือยเปล่า` consistently throughout these chapters.

## 7. Forward protection — 1 Corinthians 5:1

Paul refers to a Corinthian "man who has his father's wife" using language that consciously echoes LEV 18:8 + 20:11. The Thai for 1 Cor 5:1 (already shipped) renders `ภรรยาของบิดา`. Lock the cross-reference so any future polish on LEV 18:8 / 20:11 stays in sync with the shipped 1 Cor 5:1 Thai.

## 8. When to revisit
