## 1. The two formulae

### Kareth (`כָּרֵת`, "to cut off")

The kareth penalty is **divine-passive** in the Hebrew: `וְנִכְרְתָה הַנֶּפֶשׁ הַהִוא מֵעַמֶּיהָ` ("that soul shall be cut off from its people"). The grammar leaves the agent unstated; God is understood as the implicit cutter. This is not the same as a court-imposed death penalty (which uses mot-yumat).

| Hebrew form | Locked Thai |
|---|---|
| וְנִכְרְתָה (passive, "shall be cut off") | **จะถูกตัดออก** |
| נִכְרְתָה ... מִקֶּרֶב עַמּוֹ (full formula, "cut off from among his people") | **จะถูกตัดออกจากท่ามกลางชนชาติของเขา** |
| נִכְרְתָה ... מֵעַמֶּיהָ (alternate "from her people") | **จะถูกตัดออกจากชนชาติของนาง** |
| First-person divine subject (`וְהִכְרַתִּי`, "I will cut off") | **เราจะตัดออก** |

### Mot-yumat (`מוֹת יוּמָת`, "shall surely be put to death")

The mot-yumat formula is a **judicial death penalty** imposed by the human community, expressed as a Hebrew infinitive-absolute construction for emphasis (`מוֹת יוּמָת` = "dying he shall be put to death"). This requires Thai that signals court-judicial register, not divine action.

| Hebrew form | Locked Thai |
|---|---|
| מוֹת יוּמָת (singular) | **ต้องถูกประหารชีวิต** |
| מוֹת יוּמָתוּ (plural) | **ต้องถูกประหารชีวิต** (Thai number unmarked) |
| מוֹת יוּמָת ... דָּמָיו בּוֹ ("his blood is upon him") | **ต้องถูกประหารชีวิต ... เลือดของเขาจะตกอยู่บนตัวเขา** |

## 2. Why the two formulae must stay distinct

The legal distinction is theologically and judicially meaningful:

- **Kareth = divine action** — the offender will be removed by God, often by death + denial of progeny / corporate-Israel inheritance / afterlife.
- **Mot-yumat = community action** — the offender must be put to death by Israel's courts.

Some Hebrew penalty clauses use **both** (LEV 20:2-3: court-stoning required *and* divine cutting-off declared). The Thai must signal which mechanism is operative without merging them.

The locked split:
- Court-judicial → **ต้องถูกประหารชีวิต** (judicial passive, signals court action)
- Divine-removal → **จะถูกตัดออก** (divine passive, no agent named)

## 3. Special cases

### "His blood is upon him" (דָּמָיו בּוֹ)

The post-mot-yumat clause `דָּמָיו בּוֹ` is a legal-formula declaring the executed person's blood-guilt does not pass to the executioner. Locked Thai: **เลือดของเขาจะตกอยู่บนตัวเขา**. This phrase is preserved alongside the mot-yumat formula whenever the Hebrew has it (LEV 20:9, 11, 12, 13, 16, 27 + parallels).

### Stoning specification

When the Hebrew specifies execution method (`בָּאֶבֶן יִרְגְּמוּ אֹתָם` "they shall stone them with stones"), the Thai uses **จะขว้างด้วยหิน** alongside the mot-yumat clause. Both elements are preserved.

### "Devoted to destruction" (חֵרֶם)

This is a *different* category — ḥērem is a sacrificial-style devotion, not a kareth or judicial penalty. See `sacrificial_vocabulary_2026-05.md` §6 (or future ḥērem-specific doc) for the locked Thai (`เฮเรม` transliteration).

## 4. Forward protection

- **Deuteronomy** uses both formulae extensively in its legal sections (Deut 17, 21, 22). The locks above apply directly.
- **Joshua** uses ḥērem (not kareth/mot-yumat) for the conquest narratives — different category, see above.
- **Prophets** use kareth metaphorically (Isa 53:8 `נִגְזַר מֵאֶרֶץ חַיִּים`, similar root). The metaphorical extension may require a register-aware variant; review case-by-case.
