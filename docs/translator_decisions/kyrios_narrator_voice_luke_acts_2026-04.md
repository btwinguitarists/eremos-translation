# ὁ κύριος (narrator voice, referring to Jesus) → องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า

**Scope:** Narrator-voice uses of ὁ κύριος where the referent is Jesus (not the Father, not a human master). A Lukan-Acts signature — Luke is the only evangelist who calls Jesus "the Lord" in his own narration, and Acts amplifies this. Mark and Matthew do not use this narrative convention.

**Decided:** 2026-04 during Luke end-of-book review (see `docs/LUK_END_OF_BOOK_REVIEW_2026-04-22.md`).
**Evidence base (Luke, 13 target verses):** 7:13; 10:1; 10:41; 11:39; 12:42; 13:15; 17:5; 17:6; 18:6; 19:8; 22:61; 24:34. (Luke 24:3 is text-critically affected — τοῦ κυρίου Ἰησοῦ omitted per SBLGNT Western non-interpolation; not a target.)

---

## The rule

**Narrator-voice ὁ κύριος referring to Jesus → องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า**, consistent across Luke and forward into Acts.

Apply when:
- The evangelist/narrator (not a character inside the narrative) refers to Jesus as "the Lord" in third person.
- Example trigger: καὶ ἰδὼν αὐτὴν ὁ κύριος ἐσπλαγχνίσθη … (Luke 7:13 — narrator's voice).

**Do NOT apply when:**
- A character inside the narrative addresses or speaks about Jesus as κύριος. That's covered by character-voice rendering (see §Vocative κύριε below and `narrator_vs_character_voice_2026-04.md`).
- κύριος refers to God/YHWH (OT-echo contexts like the Magnificat, Benedictus, the angel at 1:11, quotations from the LXX). Use the OT-context rendering (พระเจ้า or องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า depending on quotation style).
- κύριος refers to a human master/owner in a parable (e.g., the master of the vineyard, Luke 20:13–15). Use นายของ- or เจ้าของ- as the context demands.

## Documented exception: Luke 10:41

One narrator-κύριος verse in Luke departs from the rule:

> ἀποκριθεὶς δὲ εἶπεν αὐτῇ ὁ κύριος·Μάρθα Μάρθα …
> **Thai:** พระเยซูตรัสตอบนางว่า «มารธา มารธาเอ๋ย …»

Rendering: **พระเยซู**, not องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า.

**Rationale (preserved):** The Mary-Martha pericope is an intimate-rebuke-comfort scene. องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า would sound bureaucratic-distant where the Greek emphasis is pastoral proximity. The verse-level key_decisions entry already carries this rationale. **This is the only narrator-κύριος intentional departure in Luke**; the doc preserves it rather than flattening.

If Acts or subsequent books have comparable pastoral-intimacy narrator-κύριος verses, apply the same judgment; document per-verse.

## Why องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า and not alternatives

**Argument for องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า (chosen):**
- Standard Thai Christian rendering of "the Lord" with full divine-sovereign weight. องค์ = honorific classifier for a venerable person; พระผู้เป็นเจ้า = "the one who is Lord."
- Distinguishes from พระเยซู (personal name + พระ-honorific): when Luke says "the Lord" instead of "Jesus," he is making a theological claim — Jesus is the κύριος of the LXX Psalms — and Thai needs a rendering that carries that weight.
- Matches Thai church reading conventions for narrator-κύριος in NT passages.

**Counter-argument considered: always use พระเยซู for readability.**
Rejected — flattens Luke's theological intention. The Greek conspicuously alternates Ἰησοῦς and ὁ κύριος; the Thai should too.

**Counter-argument considered: transliterate κύριος as เคียริออส.**
Rejected — foreign-sounding, non-standard, and loses the sovereign-divine register.

**Counter-argument considered: พระคริสตเจ้า (Christ-Lord).**
Rejected — adds a Christological load the Greek doesn't carry in these narrator-voice verses; κύριος alone is Septuagintal-divine, not Messianic-titular.

## Forward implications

**Acts (~25 narrator-κύριος verses referring to Jesus):**
- 1:24 (κύριε καρδιογνῶστα — vocative in prayer; different rule)
- 2:21, 2:36 — Pentecost κύριος claim (theologically loaded)
- 4:26 (LXX quotation — OT-echo rule, not this one)
- 9:10 ("the Lord said in a vision to Ananias")
- 9:11, 9:15, 9:17, 9:27
- 11:21, 11:24
- 18:9 (vision of Paul)
- 22:10 (Paul's retelling)

All narrator-voice Jesus-κύριος → **องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า** unless the verse is an OT-quotation or a pastoral-intimacy scene on the Martha-10:41 model.

**Pauline corpus:** κύριος Ἰησοῦς and κύριος Ἰησοῦς Χριστός formulas are different patterns (titulary constructions, not narrator voice). Those get their own doc if/when a consistent pattern emerges.

## Alternatives considered

- **องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า throughout (chosen).**
- **Split: พระเยซู in intimate-scene narrator-κύριος, องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า elsewhere.** Partially adopted — Luke 10:41 is the documented exception, handled per-verse rather than as a blanket policy.
- **พระเยซูเจ้า ("Lord Jesus," compressed).** Rejected — injects the name where Luke deliberately withholds it.
- **Transliteration เคียริออส.** Rejected — foreign, loses divine register.

## When to revisit

- If Acts narrator-κύριος readings feel stilted to native Thai readers (spot-check recommended at Acts 2, 9, 22).
- If a Pauline passage uses ὁ κύριος in a narrator-like voice (rare; but if, say, 2 Thess or Hebrews runs a narrator-Jesus κύριος construction, revisit).
- If a future native-Thai review flags องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า as over-formal in Gospel contexts.
