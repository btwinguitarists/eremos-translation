# ἱλαστήριον — HEB 9:5 mercy-seat vs ROM 3:25 propitiation distinction

**Scope:** The Greek noun ἱλαστήριον when referring to (a) the *physical cover of the ark* (the LXX-rendering of Hebrew kapporet, Lev 16) vs (b) the *Pauline-soteriological means of atonement* in Rom 3:25.

**Decided:** 2026-05-04 during Hebrews end-of-book review. Flagged in §H/I-cluster as a forward-Romans-sensitive coordination question. Both ChatGPT and Gemini external AI reviewers concurred (2026-05-04 audit response, Item F): the two NT uses of ἱλαστήριον require **different Thai renderings** because they refer to different things.

**Status:** Both verses are *already shipped* with the recommended renderings; this doc records the rationale + locks the cross-corpus distinction so future work (OT translation, audit scripts) does not attempt to harmonize them.

---

## The two NT uses

### Heb 9:5 — physical mercy-seat (kapporet)

**Verse:** "ὑπεράνω δὲ αὐτῆς Χερουβὶν δόξης κατασκιάζοντα τὸ ἱλαστήριον"
**Thai (shipped 2026-05-04):** "และเหนือหีบนั้นมีเครูบแห่งพระสิริ ที่ปกคลุมพระที่นั่งกรุณาด้วยปีก"
**Rendering:** ἱλαστήριον → **พระที่นั่งกรุณา**

The verse describes the *physical gold cover of the ark of the covenant* in the tabernacle (per Exo 25:17-22 LXX, where ἱλαστήριον also renders kapporet). Thai biblical convention for this object is **พระที่นั่งกรุณา** ("seat of mercy") — a translation, not a transliteration, that captures the OT-tabernacle-furniture function (the place above which God's presence appeared, where annual atonement-blood was sprinkled on Yom Kippur).

### Rom 3:25 — propitiatory atonement (Christ-as-means)

**Verse:** "ὃν προέθετο ὁ θεὸς ἱλαστήριον διὰ πίστεως ἐν τῷ αὐτοῦ αἵματι"
**Thai (shipped earlier):** "พระเจ้าทรงตั้งพระองค์ไว้เป็นเครื่องบูชาไถ่บาป โดยทางความเชื่อ ในพระโลหิตของพระองค์"
**Rendering:** ἱλαστήριον → **เครื่องบูชาไถ่บาป**

Paul's use is *not* a tabernacle-inventory reference. Paul publicly presents Christ as the decisive *atoning means*. The Thai evangelical-Protestant standard for this soteriological-Pauline use is **เครื่องบูชาไถ่บาป** ("sin-atoning sacrifice"), which captures both the *wrath-aversion* (propitiation) and the *sin-removal* (expiation) dimensions that evangelical readers expect.

## The rule

| Greek | Thai default | Context |
|---|---|---|
| ἱλαστήριον (tabernacle / Levitical-cultic furniture) | **พระที่นั่งกรุณา** | Heb 9:5 — the physical gold ark-cover. Echoes Exo 25:17-22 LXX kapporet. |
| ἱλαστήριον (Pauline soteriological-atonement Christology) | **เครื่องบูชาไถ่บาป** | Rom 3:25 — Christ as atoning sacrifice. Captures both propitiation + expiation. |
| ἱλασμός (cognate noun) | **เครื่องบูชาลบบาป** / **การไถ่บาป** | 1 Jn 2:2, 4:10 — atoning-sacrifice cognate. Distinct from ἱλαστήριον. |
| ἱλάσκομαι (cognate verb) | **ทรงลบมลทิน** / **ทรงไถ่บาป** | Heb 2:17; Luk 18:13. Verb form. |

### The non-negotiable axis

The two NT uses of ἱλαστήριον refer to **different things** — a tabernacle-furniture object (Heb 9:5) and a Pauline-soteriological reality (Rom 3:25). Forcing a single Thai equivalent across both would either:
- **(if พระที่นั่งกรุณา wins)** create a foreign-feeling Pauline verse that loses the wrath-aversion dimension and reads as mercy-seat-typology Paul did not foreground; OR
- **(if เครื่องบูชาไถ่บาป wins)** flatten the Heb 9:5 description of the physical-gold-furniture into a generic sacrifice-noun, breaking the OT-furniture-narrative the verse is in the middle of.

Both verses already shipped with their respective contextually-correct renderings. This doc locks that distinction.

---

## Considered alternatives

### Option A — `พระที่นั่งกรุณา` for both
Rejected. Rom 3:25 is not in tabernacle-furniture context. Pauline mercy-seat-typology is a *theological-interpretive frame* some commentators pursue, but the verse's surface-Greek does not require it, and most Thai evangelical-Protestant readers will not parse `พระที่นั่งกรุณา` as a soteriological-instrument designation. The lexical-echo-with-Lev-16 is preserved in scholarly footnotes / translator decisions, not in the running Thai text.

### Option B — `เครื่องบูชาไถ่บาป` for both
Rejected. Heb 9:5 is in mid-sentence describing the *physical contents of the second tabernacle room* (incense altar, ark-with-objects, ark-cover, cherubim). `เครื่องบูชาไถ่บาป` would read as a *generic sin-atoning sacrifice* rather than the *specific gold cover above the ark* — that is a referential error.

### Option C — `เครื่องลบล้างบาป` (expiation-only)
Rejected for Rom 3:25. Tilts toward expiation-only and underrepresents the wrath/propitiation dimension that evangelical-Protestant readers (and the project's RULES §0 confessional position) expect. ChatGPT 2026-05-04 confirmed: "Avoid Option C as a default because it tilts toward expiation-only."

## Forward implications

### OT translation (when Genesis through Pentateuch reach the queue)
- **Exo 25:17-22, 26:34, 30:6, 31:7, 35:12, 37:6-9, 39:35, 40:20**: kapporet → **พระที่นั่งกรุณา** (locks the Levitical-tabernacle term).
- **Lev 16 (Day of Atonement)**: kapporet → **พระที่นั่งกรุณา**. The Day-of-Atonement-blood-sprinkling on the kapporet is the OT ritual the NT-author of Hebrews argues from typologically.
- **Num 7:89**: kapporet → **พระที่นั่งกรุณา**.

### NT cross-corpus consistency
- 1 Jn 2:2, 4:10: ἱλασμός (cognate, distinct lemma) → **เครื่องบูชาลบบาป** (Johannine-soteriological; locked at 1 Jn ship).
- Heb 2:17: ἱλάσκεσθαι (cognate verb) → **ทรงลบมลทิน** (locked at Heb 2 ship).
- Luk 18:13: ἱλάσθητί μοι → **ขอทรงเมตตาข้าพระองค์** (publican's prayer; verbal-prayer register).

The lemma-family is internally varied; the **noun ἱλαστήριον specifically** is the case where the same Greek lemma takes two distinct Thai renderings based on context, and this doc locks why.
