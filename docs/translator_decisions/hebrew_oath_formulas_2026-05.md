## 1. The four major Hebrew oath-formulae

### 1.1 Raised-hand oath (נָשָׂא יָד)

**Hebrew form:** הֲרִמֹתִי יָדִי / נָשָׂאתִי יָד אֶל־יהוה ("I raise/have raised my hand to YHWH").

**Locking precedent:** Gen 14:22 (אַבְרָם said to the king of Sodom: "I have raised my hand to YHWH, God Most High..."). The gesture signifies a solemn oath-bond — the hand-raising itself is the oath-act.

**Thai lock:** **ข้าพเจ้ายกมือสาบานต่อ[องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า]** ("I lift up my hand and swear to [YHWH]"). Preserve the body-part anthropomorphism + the to-YHWH directionality.

**Other OT occurrences:** Ex 6:8, Deut 32:40, Ezek 20:5–6 + 15 + 23 + 28 + 42, Ps 106:26, Neh 9:15.

### 1.2 Place-hand-under-thigh oath (יָדוֹ תַּחַת יָרֵךְ)

**Hebrew form:** שִׂים נָא יָדְךָ תַּחַת יְרֵכִי ("Place your hand under my thigh") — a gesture-oath specific to the patriarchal-narrative era. The "thigh" (יָרֵךְ) is a euphemism for the reproductive area; the oath invokes the oath-taker's descendants as witnesses.

**Locking precedent:** Gen 24:2–9 (Abraham's servant; the oath to find a wife for Isaac from Abraham's kin). Recurs at Gen 47:29–31 (Jacob's deathbed-oath to Joseph: bury me in the cave of Machpelah, not in Egypt).

**Thai lock:** **จงวางมือของเจ้าใต้ขาของเรา** ("Place your hand under my thigh") — preserve the literal gesture + euphemistic "thigh." Verse-level KD names the cultural-anthropological backdrop (descendant-witness function) and notes that the gesture is patriarchal-narrative-specific (no occurrences post-Genesis).

**Other OT occurrences:** none. The gesture is uniquely Genesis (24:2, 24:9, 47:29).

### 1.3 "By myself I have sworn" (בִּי נִשְׁבַּעְתִּי)

**Hebrew form:** בִּי נִשְׁבַּעְתִּי נְאֻם־יהוה ("By myself I have sworn, declares YHWH"). When YHWH is the oath-taker and there is no higher being to swear by, YHWH swears by his own being.

**Locking precedent:** Gen 22:16 (the Akedah, post-Isaac-binding: "By myself I have sworn, declares YHWH, because you have done this thing and have not withheld your son, your only son..."). The most theologically-weighted self-oath in the OT, anchoring the Abrahamic covenant promise.

**Thai lock:** **เราสาบานในตัวของเราเอง** ("I have sworn by myself") — first-person divine speech in elevated register. Preserve the rhetoric of the highest-stake oath (YHWH binding himself to his own promise).

**Other OT occurrences:** Isa 45:23 (the messianic-oath: "to me every knee shall bow"; cited Phil 2:10, Rom 14:11), Jer 22:5, Jer 49:13, Heb 6:13 (NT citation of Gen 22:16). The Isa 45:23 / Phil 2:10 trajectory is one of the OT's clearest forward-anchors into Christology.

### 1.4 Cairn-witness / mizpah oath (גַל־עֵד / מִצְפָּה)

**Hebrew form:** מִצְפָּה / גַל־עֵד — pile-of-stones with a name commemorating the oath. The cairn (גַל) and the oath-eponym (עֵד "witness") and the mizpah (מִצְפָּה "watchtower" + "may YHWH watch between us") together constitute the oath-form.

**Locking precedent:** Gen 31:44–53 (Jacob-Laban boundary covenant at Galeed / Mizpah). The classic OT example of a heap-of-stones oath with a YHWH-watches-between-us formula.

**Thai lock:** **กองหินพยาน / มิสปาห์** (transliteration of place-name + Thai gloss). The "may YHWH watch between us" core formula: **ขอองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้าทรงเฝ้าดูระหว่างเรา**.

**Other OT occurrences:** Josh 22:9–34 (the Reubenite-altar-as-witness episode, structurally similar but on the West-vs-East-of-Jordan tribal scale), Judges 11:11 + 20:1 + 21:1 + 21:5 + 21:8 (Mizpah as oath-place in the early period).

## 2. The Genesis oath-formulae catalogue

Genesis exercises oath-formulae more densely than any other OT book. The complete catalogue of Genesis oath-occurrences:

| Verse | Oath-formula | Speaker | Hearer |
|---|---|---|---|
| 14:22–23 | Raised-hand (§1.1) | Abram | King of Sodom |
| 21:23–32 | Beersheba (§1.4 cairn-witness adjacent) | Abimelech / Abraham | mutual treaty |
| 22:16 | "By myself I have sworn" (§1.3) | YHWH | Abraham (Akedah) |
| 24:2–9 | Place-hand-under-thigh (§1.2) | Abraham | his servant |
| 26:28–31 | Treaty-oath (Abimelech-Isaac, parallel to 21:23–32) | Abimelech / Isaac | mutual |
| 31:44–53 | Cairn-witness Galeed/Mizpah (§1.4) | Jacob / Laban | mutual |
| 47:29–31 | Place-hand-under-thigh (§1.2) | Israel/Jacob | Joseph (deathbed) |
| 50:5–6 | Oath-fulfillment | Joseph + Pharaoh | (delayed action) |
| 50:25 | Oath-extracted | Joseph | "sons of Israel" |

The Joseph 50:25 oath (regarding the bones) is the bookend of the Genesis oath-cycle — set up for fulfillment in Exodus 13:19 (Moses carries Joseph's bones).

## 3. Cross-corpus thread (forward-look)

The Hebrew oath-formulae compound forward into:

- **Exodus 13:19** — Joseph-bones-oath fulfilled
- **Numbers 30** — vows-and-oaths legislation (terminology overlap with §1.1 raised-hand)
- **Deuteronomy 6:13, 10:20** — "swear by his name" (using שָׁבַע + שֵׁם)
- **1 Samuel 14** — Saul's rash oath at Michmash (multiple oath-formulae)
- **2 Samuel 21:1–14** — Gibeonite-Saul-oath unfulfilled and famine consequence
- **1 Kings 1–2** — David's deathbed-oaths + Solomon's executions
- **Psalter** — many psalms reference YHWH's oath (Ps 89:35 + 132:11 cite Gen 22:16 + 2 Sam 7)
- **Hebrews 6:13–18** — explicit NT citation of Gen 22:16, naming the oath's theological function
