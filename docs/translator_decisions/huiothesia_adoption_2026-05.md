# υἱοθεσία → สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร (Pauline adoption-as-sons; Greco-Roman legal-rights force)

**Scope:** All 5 NT occurrences of υἱοθεσία (Pauline-only lemma): Rom 8:15, 8:23, 9:4; Gal 4:5; Eph 1:5.

**Decided:** 2026-05-02 during the Romans end-of-book audit, after external AI review (Gemini MAJOR CONCERN + ChatGPT CONCERN, 2026-05-02) flagged the silent corpus-drift between GAL 4:5 (rendered **การรับเป็นบุตร**) and Rom 8:15, 8:23, 9:4 (rendered **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร**). Both AIs independently recommended adopting the Romans rendering as the corpus default + retroactively revising GAL 4:5.

**Greco-Roman legal-technical force the rendering must preserve:**

In Greco-Roman law, **υἱοθεσία** (literally "son-making" or "son-placing") was a formal legal act granting FULL legal status as a son — including inheritance rights — even for adoptees not born into the family. Paul exploits this legal practice: through Christ's redemption, believers (Jews and Gentiles alike) receive ADOPTION as God's full sons-and-heirs. The rendering must preserve this legal-rights weight, not merely the act-of-receiving-as-a-child.

**External AI review affirmation (Gemini + ChatGPT, 2026-05-02):**
- Gemini (MAJOR CONCERN): "There is a distinct semantic gap between **การรับเป็นบุตร** (the event/status of adoption) and **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** (the legal rights/privileges). Romans introduces a Greco-Roman legal precision Galatians lacked. **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** better captures the legal weight; spot-revise GAL 4:5."
- ChatGPT (CONCERN): "Real corpus drift. **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** better captures the legal-status / inheritance-rights force. Romans should set the corpus default; revise GAL 4:5 accordingly; write the doc before Eph 1:5 ships."

## The rule

### Default rendering

| Greek form | Thai rendering |
|---|---|
| υἱοθεσία (substantive, abstract noun "adoption") | **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** |
| πνεῦμα υἱοθεσίας (Spirit of adoption — Rom 8:15) | **พระวิญญาณแห่งสิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** |
| εἰς υἱοθεσίαν (purpose: "for adoption" — Eph 1:5) | **เพื่อสิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** or **สู่สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** |
| υἱοθεσίαν ἀπεκδεχόμενοι (awaiting adoption — Rom 8:23) | **รอคอยสิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** |

### Why สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร (and not การรับเป็นบุตร)

- **สิทธิ์** ("right / privilege / legal claim") explicitly carries the legal-rights component the Greco-Roman υἱοθεσία legal-practice requires.
- **การเป็นบุตร** ("the status of being a son") preserves the sonship-as-status sense.
- The composite **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** = "the right/privilege of being-a-son" — captures legal-status + inheritance-claim simultaneously.
- The Romans 8:23 context (the apocalyptic "groaning while awaiting our adoption" alongside τὴν ἀπολύτρωσιν τοῦ σώματος = "the redemption of our bodies") makes the legal-rights-not-yet-fully-actualized force clear; **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** + waiting-for-bodily-redemption captures the not-yet eschatological tension.

### Why NOT การรับเป็นบุตร (rejected — was GAL pre-revision rendering)

- **Smoother prose.** การรับเป็นบุตร reads slightly more naturally as a noun phrase.
- **Why rejected.** The simpler "receiving-as-a-son" sense loses the legal-rights / inheritance-claim weight that the Pauline argument requires (esp. at Rom 8:17 immediately following 8:15: "if children, also heirs"). The legal-rights force is theologically load-bearing for the Pauline doctrine of believers' inheritance with Christ.

### Why NOT บุตรบุญธรรม (a possible alternative)

- บุตรบุญธรรม is the standard Thai word for "adopted child" in modern legal usage but reads as a single-term-noun rather than a status-with-rights. Less precise for the Pauline force.

## Forward implications

### Romans (already locked)

All three Rom occurrences (8:15, 8:23, 9:4) already use **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** as of book-romans-v1 audit shipping.

### Galatians retroactive revision

GAL 4:5 revised 2026-05-02 from การรับเป็นบุตร → **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร**.

### Ephesians (forward)

Eph 1:5 (προορίσας ἡμᾶς εἰς υἱοθεσίαν διὰ Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ — "predestining us for adoption through Jesus Christ") will apply this lock when Eph ships.

### Companion Pauline-inheritance vocabulary

- κληρονόμος (heir) → **ผู้รับมรดก** (Rom 8:17, GAL 3:29, etc.) — already locked corpus-wide
- συγκληρονόμοι (co-heirs) → **ผู้รับมรดกร่วมกัน** (Rom 8:17) — already locked
- κληρονομία (inheritance) → **มรดก** — already locked
- The υἱοθεσία lock here completes the Pauline-adoption-and-inheritance vocabulary cluster.
