# Exodus 34:6–7 attribute formula — Cross-corpus identical-rendering lock

**Scope:** The OT's foundational divine-self-revelation formula, declared by YHWH at Sinai (Exod 34:6–7) and recited verbatim or near-verbatim across ~10 OT occurrences. This doc locks the Thai rendering as a single identical phrase across all occurrences, so Thai readers can recognize the formula at every recurrence as the same canonical text.

**Decided:** 2026-05-09 (Ben + cross-AI review). Triggered by Jonah end-of-book audit, where Jonah 4:2 quotes the formula in a context of prophetic protest.

**Companion docs:** `chesed_covenant_love_2026-05.md` (locks חֶסֶד → ความรักมั่นคง — used in this formula), `nicham_divine_relenting_2026-05.md` (relevant to Joel 2:13 + Jonah 4:2 addendum), `divine_names_table_2026-05.md`, `leitwort_handling_policy_2026-05.md` Rule 1.

## 1. The formula

### 1.1 The Sinai canonical form (Exod 34:6–7)

```
וַיַּעֲבֹר יהוה עַל־פָּנָיו וַיִּקְרָא
יהוה יהוה אֵל רַחוּם וְחַנּוּן אֶרֶךְ אַפַּיִם וְרַב־חֶסֶד וֶאֱמֶת
נֹצֵר חֶסֶד לָאֲלָפִים נֹשֵׂא עָוֹן וָפֶשַׁע וְחַטָּאָה וְנַקֵּה לֹא יְנַקֶּה
פֹּקֵד עֲוֹן אָבוֹת עַל־בָּנִים וְעַל־בְּנֵי בָנִים עַל־שִׁלֵּשִׁים וְעַל־רִבֵּעִים׃
```

(YHWH passes before Moses and proclaims:) "YHWH, YHWH, a God merciful and gracious, slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love and faithfulness, keeping steadfast love for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin, but who will by no means clear the guilty, visiting the iniquity of the fathers on the children and the children's children, to the third and the fourth generation."

### 1.2 The Thai canonical form

```
องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า พระเจ้าผู้ทรงพระเมตตาและทรงพระคุณ ทรงกริ้วช้า
ทรงบริบูรณ์ด้วยความรักมั่นคงและความซื่อสัตย์
ทรงรักษาความรักมั่นคงไว้ถึงพันชั่วอายุ ทรงยกโทษความชั่ว การละเมิด และบาป
แต่จะไม่ทรงพิจารณาผู้กระทำผิดให้พ้นโทษ — ทรงลงโทษความชั่วของบรรพบุรุษต่อบุตรหลานและหลานเหลน ถึงสามชั่วและสี่ชั่วอายุคน
```

**This is the canonical full-form lock.** Every full-formula occurrence (Exod 34:6–7 itself + Num 14:18 if quoted in full) uses identical Thai phrasing.

### 1.4 The paqad-clause and chesed-clause (Decalogue recitations + Exod 34:7)

These are the most-recited Pentateuchal occurrences of the paqad-clause (liturgical/catechetical use of the Decalogue). **Locked-recitations list (must use the canonical Thai surface above):**

- Exod 20:5–6 (Decalogue paqad-clause + chesed-clause)
- Exod 34:6–7 (the canonical full-form)
- Num 14:18 (Spies-crisis recitation)
- Deut 5:9–10 (Second-Decalogue paqad-clause + chesed-clause)

The Decalogue occurrences are the **liturgical anchors** of the formula — they recur in Jewish + Christian catechesis far more often than the Sinai-narrative form. Locking them here protects the formula-thread for every downstream Psalms/Prophets recitation and every NT Decalogue-citation cluster.

## 2. Why a cross-corpus formula lock

### 2.1 Liturgical recurrence is the formula's purpose

The Exod 34 formula is the OT's most-recited divine-self-revelation. The repeated recitation by prophets, psalmists, and post-exilic prayers is itself the formula's theological function — the canonical-readers learn to recognize "X is gracious and compassionate, slow to anger, abounding in chesed" as YHWH's self-defined character.

If the Thai rendering varies across recitations, the Thai reader cannot recognize the formula. The character-cluster becomes invisible. Cross-corpus traceability is lost.

### 2.2 Two corpus-locks already converge here

This formula is the convergence point for two prior corpus-locks:

- **חֶסֶד → ความรักมั่นคง** (per `chesed_covenant_love_2026-05.md`).
- **נָחַם (divine-relenting) → ทรงเปลี่ยนพระทัย** (per `nicham_divine_relenting_2026-05.md`, applies to the Joel/Jonah addendum).

Adding the formula-level lock ensures that the surrounding character-cluster vocabulary (חַנּוּן, רַחוּם, אֶרֶךְ אַפַּיִם, אֱמֶת) is also stable across the corpus.

### 2.3 The Jonah audit's specific finding

Jonah 4:2 was the first OT chapter where the formula appeared in our project. The earlier draft used **พระเมตตาเชิงพันธสัญญา** ("covenantal mercy") for רַב־חֶסֶד — a Jonah-specific qualifier that broke the corpus-thread. The Gemini + ChatGPT audit (2026-05-09) flagged this as MAJOR CONCERN. The correction (now ทรงบริบูรณ์ด้วยความรักมั่นคง) restores the formula to its locked form.

When the formula appears, the reader-edition footnote (in `output/textual_variants/<book>_<chapter>.json`) should:

1. Identify this as the Exod 34 character-formula.
2. List the OT recitations.
3. Note any verse-specific variations (addendums like Joel/Jonah's נִחָם clause, omissions like Ps 86:15's mishna of the children-clause).

A standard footnote template (used at Jonah 4:2's `output/textual_variants/jonah_04.json`) is in place and should be reused for future occurrences.
