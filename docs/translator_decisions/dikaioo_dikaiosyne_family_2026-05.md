# δικαιόω / δικαίωσις / δικαίωμα → ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม (forensic-declarative)

**Scope:** The Greek δικαιόω-verb (justify, declare-righteous) and its closely-allied abstract nouns δικαίωσις (justification-act) and δικαίωμα-as-verdict (Rom 5:16) when carrying the Pauline forensic-declarative sense. The cognate noun δικαιοσύνη ("righteousness" — abstract quality) is rendered separately as ความชอบธรรม and is NOT in this lock's scope. The cognate adjective δίκαιος ("righteous person") rendered as ผู้ชอบธรรม is also not in this lock's scope.

**Decided:** 2026-05-01 during the Romans 1-5 corpus-consistency audit, after Galatians end-of-book external-AI review (ChatGPT + Gemini, 2026-05-01) affirmed `ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม` as the Pauline-corpus default.

---

## Evidence base

**Galatians (locked 2026-05-01 via ai_review_response_GAL_2026-05-01.md):**
- GAL 2:16 (3×): οὐ δικαιοῦται ἄνθρωπος ἐξ ἔργων νόμου / δικαιωθῶμεν ἐκ πίστεως / οὐ δικαιωθήσεται πᾶσα σάρξ → all `ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม`
- GAL 2:17, 3:8, 3:11, 3:24, 5:4 — same lock applied throughout
- Both AIs (ChatGPT + Gemini) explicitly affirmed: ChatGPT "theologically and contextually precise"; Gemini "flawlessly safeguards the Protestant declarative doctrine"

**Gemini's explicit forward-warning (Galatians review):**
> "the THSV-style **นับว่าเป็นผู้ชอบธรรม** is slightly smoother for reading flow, **but it overlaps too heavily with the λογίζομαι (reckon/count) word group coming up in Romans 4**; maintaining strict lexical distinction is worth the extra syllable here."

This warning predicted exactly the drift that materialized in Romans 1-5 (translated before this lock was formalized). The Galatians-pre-Romans formalization-step was missed; Romans 1-5 used `ทรงนับว่าชอบธρρμ` which collapsed δικαιόω with λογίζομαι.

**Romans 1-5 audit (2026-05-01):** 13 verses revised from imputation-rendering (`ทรงนับว่าชอบธρρμ`) to forensic-declarative (`ทรงประกาศว่าชอβθρρμ`):
- Rom 2:13, 3:20, 3:24, 3:26, 3:28, 3:30 (×2), 4:2, 4:5, 4:25, 5:1, 5:9, 5:16, 5:18

## The rule

### Default (forensic-declarative): use ประกาศว่าชอบธรรม

| Greek form | Thai rendering |
|---|---|
| Passive δικαιόω + θεόν-implicit subject (e.g., δικαιοῦται, ἐδικαιώθη, δικαιωθέντες, δικαιωθήσεται) | **ทรงถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** (royal divine-passive) |
| Active substantival ptc (ὁ δικαιῶν, τὸν δικαιοῦντα) | **ผู้ทรงประกาศว่า...ชอบธρρμ** |
| Active future (e.g., δικαιώσει — God will justify) | **ทρงประกαศว่α...ชอบธρρμ** |
| Noun δικαίωσις (abstract justification — Rom 4:25, 5:18) | **การที่ทρงประกαศว่αเρα...ชอβธρρμ** (or contextually paraphrased) |
| Noun δικαίωμα-as-verdict (Rom 5:16, NOT 1:32 / 2:26) | **การที่ทρงประกαศว่αชอบธρρμ** |

### Critical: do NOT use นับ (count/reckon) for δικαιόω

`นับ` is reserved for **λογίζομαι** (Pauline-bookkeeping-vocabulary). The Romans 4 chapter is built on λογίζομαι quoting Gen 15:6 LXX (καὶ ἐλογίσθη αὐτῷ εἰς δικαιοσύνην) — the verb appears 11× in Rom 4 alone. Collapsing δικαιόω into the same Thai verb destroys Paul's deliberate two-step argument structure:

1. **δικαιόω (ประกาศว่าชอβธρρμ):** the courtroom-act — God's verdict declaring the believer righteous
2. **λογίζομαι (νับ):** the accounting-act — God's reckoning of the verdict to the believer's account

The two are theologically related but lexically distinct. Romans 4:5 contains BOTH in a single verse:
- δικαιοῦντα → `ผู้ทρงประกαศให้คนอธρρμเป็นผู้ชอβθρρμ`
- λογίζεται → `ความเชื่อของเขαก็ได้ρับการทρงนับให้เป็นความชอβธρρμ`

This is the load-bearing test verse. A translator who confuses the two will collapse Paul's argument.

### Exception 1: divine-subject LXX cite (Rom 3:4 / Ps 51:4 LXX)

Rom 3:4 quotes Ps 51:4 LXX with δικαιωθῇς (2sg pass subj) addressed to GOD:
> Ὅπως ἂν δικαιωθῇς ἐν τοῖς λόγοις σου

Here the verb means "be vindicated / shown-to-be-righteous" — God being justified in his speech. Rendering as `ทρงถูกประกαศว่αชอβธρρμ` would mis-frame God as receiving a courtroom-verdict from humans. Current rendering: `ทρงสำแดงความชอβธρρμ` ("manifest righteousness") — preserves the divine-vindication sense without imposing a courtroom-frame on God-as-defendant. **LOCK as principled-exception.**

### Exception 2: δικαιόω + ἀπό + genitive idiom (Rom 6:7)

Rom 6:7: ὁ ἀποθανὼν δεδικαίωται ἀπὸ τῆς ἁμαρτίας ("the one who has died has been justified-FROM sin"). The ἀπό-clause syntactically requires a discharge/release sense, not a courtroom-declaration sense. Current rendering: `เพราะคนที่ตายแล้วก็พ้นจากบาป` ("the one who has died is freed from sin"). Most evangelical Bibles render similarly (ESV/NIV/CSB: "freed from sin" or "set free from sin"). **LOCK as principled-exception.** Cf. parallel ἐλευθερόω at Rom 6:18 — same rendering family in Thai.

## Why ถูกประกาศว่า (and not นับว่า / ชำระให้เป็น)

### Argument for ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม (chosen)

- **Forensic-declarative precision.** δικαιόω is the courtroom verb par excellence in Pauline soteriology — God as judge issues the verdict-of-righteousness. ประกาศ ("proclaim / declare") preserves the public-judicial-pronouncement sense.
- **Lexical distinction from λογίζομαι preserved.** Both AIs (Galatians review) explicitly identified this as load-bearing for Romans 4. นับ stays bound to λογίζομαι; ประกาศ stays bound to δικαιόω.
- **Reformation-evangelical-Protestant consensus alignment.** ESV/NIV/NASB/CSB all render δικαιόω as "justify" (declarative); none collapse it with λογίζομαι. This Thai lock matches the Protestant doctrine of forensic-imputation as a TWO-step structure.
- **Galatians AI affirmation.** Both ChatGPT and Gemini independently identified ถูกประกาศว่าชอβธρρμ as theologically precise.
- **Soteriological-not-transformative framing.** Catholic and Eastern-Orthodox traditions tend to read δικαιόω as transformative-justification ("make-righteous"). The Thai `ประกาศว่α` resists this drift; `ชำρะให้เป็น` would not.

### Argument for ทρงนับว่าชอβธρρμ (rejected — was Romans 1-5 default)

- **Smoother Thai prosody.** Fewer syllables; flows better in narrative-Thai.
- **THSV-style precedent.** Thai Standard Version uses similar imputation-language.
- **Why rejected:** collapses δικαιόω with λογίζομαι. Especially destructive in Romans 4 where Paul deliberately distinguishes them across 11 occurrences of λογίζομαι. The Galatians AI review predicted this exact collision and recommended the lexical split.

### Argument for ทρงชำρะให้เป็นผู้ชอβθρρμ (rejected — was Luke 18:14 default)

- **Strong Thai religious register.** Standard formulation in Thai Christian discourse.
- **Why rejected:** "ชำρะ" carries cleansing/purifying overtones (cognate with "ชำρะล้าง" = wash). Imports a transformative-justification frame that the forensic-Pauline reading specifically rejects. Acceptable in Luke 18:14's context (parable-of-Pharisee-and-tax-collector); not acceptable as Pauline-corpus default. Luke 18:14 retained its current rendering as a principled-context-specific choice (Luke is narrative-Thai-religious; Paul is forensic-doctrinal). Cross-ref: Luke ai_review_response_LUK_2026-05-01.md Item 4.

## Forward implications

### Rest of Romans (6-16)

- Rom 6:7 (δεδικαίωται-from-sin) is a principled exception (see above). All other Rom 6 δικαι- terms are δικαιοσύνη (noun, ความชอβธρρμ) — no action.
- Rom 8:30 (ἐδικαίωσεν aorist active 3sg in golden-chain) — apply lock when this chapter is translated.
- Rom 8:33 (δικαιῶν present-active substantival "the One who justifies") — apply lock.
- Rom 10:4, 10:10 — δικαιοσύνη nouns, not in scope.
- Rom 14:23, James 2:21 (Abraham-justified-by-works counter-claim) — apply same lock; the Pauline-James "tension" is a separate doctrinal question, not a Thai-rendering question.

### Other Pauline letters not yet translated

Apply this lock as the Pauline default for δικαιόω-verb across all remaining Pauline corpus:
- Romans 6-16, 1-2 Corinthians, Ephesians, Philippians, Colossians, 1-2 Thessalonians (verb forms, if any), 1-2 Timothy, Titus

### Hebrews

Hebrews uses δικαιόω rarely (Heb 11:7 of Noah; cognate-form). When the Hebrews chapter ships, evaluate whether the same forensic-declarative lock applies or if Hebrews's distinct argumentative-frame warrants a separate decision. Initial assumption: same lock applies; revisit at Hebrews end-of-book audit.

### James 2:21-25

James's Abraham-justified-by-works passage is famously in dialectical tension with Pauline justification-by-faith. The Thai rendering should use the SAME `ถูกประกาศว่าชอβธρρμ` to allow Thai readers to see the same Greek verb in both Paul and James — preserving the textual tension rather than smoothing it through different Thai vocabulary. Document at James end-of-book audit if this warrants a separate decision-doc.

## When to revisit

- If a native-Thai-reader Pauline-review finds `ถูกประกαศว่αชอβธρρμ` reads as legalistic or distancing in pastoral-Romans-12-15 contexts, revisit. Current hypothesis: forensic precision is what the doctrinal chapters (Rom 1-11) need; pastoral chapters (Rom 12-15) rarely use the verb.
- If James 2:21-25 native-Thai-review prefers a softer rendering for the dialectical-with-Paul context, document as principled-James-exception.
- If a Greek-text scholar identifies that the THSV `นับว่αเป็นผู้ชอβธρρμ` actually preserves the Pauline-LXX echo better than the Reformation-Protestant declarative reading, revisit with original-language scholar input.
