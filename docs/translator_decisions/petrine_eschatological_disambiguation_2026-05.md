# Eschatological-passages disambiguation principle

**Decision date:** 2026-05-03 (formalized after 1 Peter end-of-book external AI review)
**Anchor passages:** 1 Pet 3:19 (preserve ambiguity); 1 Pet 4:6 (disambiguate)
**Status:** LOCK. Names the principle behind the project's mixed-strategy treatment of contested eschatological passages.

## The principle

> **Preserve ambiguity when the multiple readings are exegetically irreducible.**
> **Disambiguate when one reading is theologically incompatible with the rest of Scripture.**

Greek eschatological passages frequently carry multiple defensible readings. The Thai translator must choose, for each passage, between two strategies:

- **Lexical-ambiguity-preservation:** render Greek-grammar-faithfully with no interpretive narrowing; let Thai readers receive the same options Greek readers receive.
- **Explicit-disambiguation:** insert a clarifying word/phrase to commit to the mainstream reading; foreclose readings that contradict broader-canonical theology.

The choice between strategies is governed by a single principle: **does any of the candidate readings contradict the rest of Scripture?**

- If **no** — all readings are exegetically defensible against the canon → **preserve ambiguity**.
- If **yes** — one or more readings is theologically incompatible with broader Scripture → **disambiguate** to foreclose the incompatible reading(s).

## The 1 Peter test cases

### 3:19 — preserve ambiguity

> 3:18-19 GK: `... θανατωθεὶς μὲν σαρκὶ ζῳοποιηθεὶς δὲ πνεύματι· **ἐν ᾧ καὶ τοῖς ἐν φυλακῇ πνεύμασιν πορευθεὶς ἐκήρυξεν,**`
>
> 3:18-19 TH: `... ทรงถูกประหารในเนื้อหนัง แต่ทรงได้ชีวิตในพระวิญญาณ **ในพระวิญญาณนั้น พระองค์ได้เสด็จไปประกาศแก่บรรดาวิญญาณที่อยู่ในที่กักขัง**`

Three mainstream readings (per BDAG, Davids, Achtemeier, Schreiner, Jobes):

1. **Fallen-angels** preached-to between death-and-resurrection (the "Augustinian" reading-of-Calvin / Kelly / Achtemeier; cf. 2 Pet 2:4 + Jude 6 + 1 Enoch 6-21) — Christ proclaims-judgment over the Genesis-6 demonic-spirits.
2. **Dead-humans-from-flood** preached-to between death-and-resurrection (the "patristic" reading; postmortem-evangelism).
3. **Pre-incarnate-Christ-preached-through-Noah** to Noah's contemporaries while the ark was being built (the Augustinian-classical-Reformed reading — Wayne Grudem's preferred reading).

**All three readings are exegetically defensible.** None contradicts broader Scripture in a way that requires foreclosure:

- Reading (1) coheres with 2 Pet 2:4 + Jude 6.
- Reading (2) is in tension with HEB 9:27 ("appointed once to die, then judgment") if pressed into a postmortem-evangelism doctrine — but the Petrine text does not necessarily entail postmortem-evangelism (the proclamation could be of *judgment*, not *gospel*), so the tension is interpretive rather than canonical-incompatibility.
- Reading (3) coheres with 1 Pet 1:11 ("the Spirit of Christ in [the prophets]") and the broader OT-prophets-as-Christological-witnesses tradition.

→ **Preserve ambiguity.** The Thai renders Greek-grammar-faithfully:
- **ἐν ᾧ** ("in which" / "in [the Spirit] in whom") → **ในพระวิญญาณนั้น** (preserves antecedent ambiguity)
- **πορευθεὶς ἐκήρυξεν** → **ได้เสด็จไปประกาศ** (preserves the *journey-and-proclaim* sequence without specifying the *where*)
- **τοῖς ἐν φυλακῇ πνεύμασιν** → **บรรดาวิญญาณที่อยู่ในที่กักขัง** (preserves the spirits-as-fallen-angels vs spirits-as-dead-human-souls ambiguity)

The verse's `thai_summary` field documents all three interpretations for Thai pastors who want to pursue further study; the main text remains exegetically uncommitted.

### 4:6 — disambiguate

> 4:6 GK: `εἰς τοῦτο γὰρ καὶ νεκροῖς εὐηγγελίσθη ἵνα κριθῶσι μὲν κατὰ ἀνθρώπους σαρκὶ ζῶσι δὲ κατὰ θεὸν πνεύματι.`
>
> 4:6 TH: `เพราะเหตุนี้เอง ข่าวประเสริฐจึงได้ถูกประกาศแก่**ผู้ที่บัดนี้ตายไปแล้ว**ด้วย เพื่อพวกเขาจะถูกพิพากษาตามมนุษย์ในเนื้อหนัง แต่จะมีชีวิตตามพระเจ้าในวิญญาณ`

Two readings:

(a) **Postmortem-evangelism**: the gospel was preached *to people who were already dead at the time of preaching*.
(b) **Preached-while-alive-now-dead**: the gospel was preached *to people who heard it while alive but have since died*.

**Reading (a) is theologically incompatible with HEB 9:27** (`καθ' ὅσον ἀπόκειται τοῖς ἀνθρώποις ἅπαξ ἀποθανεῖν, μετὰ δὲ τοῦτο κρίσις` — "it is appointed for men to die once, then comes judgment"). HEB 9:27 forecloses postmortem-evangelism as a doctrine: judgment follows death immediately, with no gospel-receiving-window in between.

→ **Disambiguate.** The Thai inserts **บัดนี้** ("now") to convert the ambiguous Greek νεκροῖς ("to the dead" — *when?*) into the explicit "those-who-are-now-dead" reading. Mainstream English translations (NIV/ESV/CSB/NASB) all do this same disambiguation.

The verse's KD names the disambiguation:

> Per uW figs-explicit: 'dead-ones' refers to those-who-heard-the-gospel-while-alive but have-since-died. Render with explicit 'ผู้ที่บัดนี้ตายไปแล้ว' — disambiguates from a postmortem-evangelism reading (which would contradict HEB 9:27).

## Why this is consistent, not arbitrary

The two passages look like opposite editorial strategies, but they implement the same principle. The decision criterion is not *the translator's exegetical confidence* but *the canon's tolerance*:

- 3:19 — the canon tolerates all three readings; the Thai mirrors the Greek's openness.
- 4:6 — the canon does not tolerate one of the readings; the Thai forecloses it.

This is *consistency at the principle level*, even though the surface-level outputs differ. Future eschatological passages will be tested against the same criterion before commitment.

## Anticipated cross-corpus applications

- **Hebrews 9:27** — the canon-anchor for the disambiguation criterion. Already locked: `καθ' ὅσον ἀπόκειται τοῖς ἀνθρώποις ἅπαξ ἀποθανεῖν, μετὰ δὲ τοῦτο κρίσις` reads unambiguously in Thai (death-once-then-judgment); no disambiguation needed because the Greek itself is unambiguous.
- **2 Peter 2:4** — `ἀγγέλους ἁμαρτήσαντας ... σειραῖς ζόφου ταρταρώσας παρέδωκεν εἰς κρίσιν τηρουμένους` (angels-who-sinned reserved-for-judgment) — coheres with 1 Pet 3:19 reading (1) but does not require selecting that reading at 1 Pet 3:19. Render 2 Pet 2:4 on its own terms; cross-reference at 1 Pet 3:19's `thai_summary` only.
- **Jude 6** — same canon-position as 2 Pet 2:4. Same approach.
- **Revelation 20:11-15** — final-judgment scene; explicit rendering with no disambiguation needed (the Greek is unambiguous).
- **2 Peter 3:9** — `μὴ βουλόμενός τινας ἀπολέσθαι ἀλλὰ πάντας εἰς μετάνοιαν χωρῆσαι` (not-wishing-any-to-perish-but-all-to-come-to-repentance). Two readings: universalist vs Calvinist-restrictive-of-the-elect. Both readings are exegetically defensible against the canon. → **Preserve ambiguity** when this verse ships.
- **2 Thessalonians 1:9** — `αἰώνιον ὄλεθρον ἀπὸ προσώπου τοῦ κυρίου` ("eternal-destruction from-the-presence-of-the-Lord"). Two readings: annihilationist vs separationist. Both have evangelical defenders. → **Preserve ambiguity**.
