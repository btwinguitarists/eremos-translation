# ἔθνος — three-way contextual split

**Scope:** All occurrences of ἔθνος / ἔθνη / ἐθνῶν / ἐθνικός in the NT corpus, especially the dense Acts cluster (28 verses) and the Pauline forward-pass (Romans 9–11, Galatians 2–3, Ephesians 2–3).

**Decided:** 2026-04 during Acts end-of-book review (see `docs/ACT_END_OF_BOOK_REVIEW_2026-04-26.md` §1). Confirmed by Gemini 2.5 Pro + ChatGPT external review.

**Evidence base (Acts, 28 verses):** 4:25, 4:27, 7:7, 8:9, 10:35, 10:45, 11:1, 11:18, 13:19, 13:46, 13:48, 14:16, 14:27, 15:7, 15:12, 15:17, 17:26, 18:6, 21:19, 21:21, 22:21, 24:2, 24:10, 24:17, 26:4, 26:20, 26:23, 28:28.

---

## The rule — three Thai renderings, one Greek lemma

| Sense | Thai | Trigger context |
|---|---|---|
| Geopolitical "nations" — cosmic / Psalmic / universal-scope | **ประชาชาติ** | Psalm 2 quotations (Acts 4:25); creation-of-all-nations (Acts 17:26) |
| "Nation" as specific people-group (incl. Israel itself) | **ชนชาติ** | Stephen citing OT (7:7); seven nations of Canaan (13:19); Paul before Felix/Festus on "this nation" (24:2, 24:10, 24:17, 26:4); λαός↔ἔθνος contrast applied to Israel (26:23a) |
| "Gentiles" — non-Jewish mission target | **คนต่างชาติ** | Pauline mission contexts (10:45, 11:1, 11:18, 13:46, 13:48, 14:27, 15:7, 15:12, 15:17, 18:6, 21:19, 21:21, 22:21, 26:20, 26:23b, 28:28; etc.) |
| "Samaritans" — collective people-name | **ชาวสะมาเรีย** | Acts 8:9 (τὸ ἔθνος τῆς Σαμαρείας) |

The cleanest exegetical proof is **Acts 26:23**: τῷ τε λαῷ καὶ τοῖς ἔθνεσιν → "**ชนชาติของเรา**และแก่บรรดา**คนต่างชาติ**" — λαός→ชนชาติ (Israel-as-nation) maps against ἔθνεσιν→คนต่างชาติ (Gentiles) within a single doublet.

## Why three renderings, not one

**Argument for the split (chosen):**
- Greek ἔθνος legitimately spans three semantic fields the Thai must distinguish. ประชาชาติ carries a cosmic-Psalmic register; ชนชาติ a specific-people-group sense; คนต่างชาติ the salvation-historical "Gentiles" identity.
- Forcing one rendering would either flatten Romans 9–11's "Israel + Gentiles" architecture, OR import "Gentile" connotations into Acts 17:26's universal-creation context.
- Thai readers natively distinguish all three; flattening reduces clarity, not increases it.

**Argument against (considered and rejected): single rendering คนต่างชาติ throughout.**
- Would mistranslate Acts 4:25 (Psalm 2 quotation) — the Psalmic ἔθνη isn't "Gentiles" yet; it's the cosmic "nations rage."
- Would mistranslate Acts 17:26 ("from one [man] he made every ἔθνος of mankind to dwell on the earth") — universal creation, not the Gentile-Jewish division.
- Would erase Acts 26:23's deliberate λαός / ἔθνος contrast.

## ชนต่างชาติ vs. คนต่างชาติ — register normalization

**Decided 2026-04-26:** Both ชนต่างชาติ and คนต่างชาติ are grammatical Thai for "Gentiles." ชน-prefix is more literary, คน-prefix is everyday register. The Acts corpus had drifted to ชนต่างชาติ at 4:27 + 14:27 (vs. 16 verses using คนต่างชาติ).

**Normalized 2026-04-26 to คนต่างชาติ throughout** (corpus-wide uniformity).

The ChatGPT external review proposed preserving ชนต่างชาติ as a marked literary variant in elevated discourse (formal corporate prayer, formal church report). Rejected on simplicity grounds: a 16:2 ratio reads more as accidental drift than principled register-marking, and corpus-wide uniformity removes a special case for future translators to remember.

## ἔθνεσιν / λαοῖς doublet handling

When ἔθνεσιν appears in apposition or contrast with λαός / λαοί (Acts 4:27, 26:23, etc.), the Thai distinguishes:
- ἔθνη as cosmic-Psalmic plural (rare; Acts 4:25 LXX Ps 2:1) → **ประชาชาติ**
- ἔθνεσιν as Gentiles-vs-Israel (Acts 4:27 prayer applying Psalm 2; Acts 26:23 Paul's gospel summary) → **คนต่างชาติ**
- λαός / λαοί for Israel → **ชนชาติ / ประชาชน** (depending on whether nation-as-collective or people-as-population)

**Acts 4:25 + 4:27 example:** Ps 2:1's ἔθνη quoted as ประชาชาติ (cosmic); the prayer's application of the Psalm to the Pilate-Herod gathering at 4:27 then identifies the players: ἔθνεσιν (Pilate's Roman side) → คนต่างชาติ; λαοῖς Ἰσραήλ (Israel) → ประชาชนอิสราเอล. The semantic-field shift between v.25 and v.27 is preserved.

## Forward implications

**Pauline corpus:**
- **Romans 9–11** ("Has God rejected his people?"): the most load-bearing ἔθνος passage in the NT. Apply the rule strictly. ἔθνη (Gentiles) → คนต่างชาติ; λαός (Israel) → ชนชาติ / ประชาชน.
- **Romans 16:26** (διὰ προφητικῶν γραφῶν...εἰς πάντα τὰ ἔθνη γνωρισθέντος): πάντα τὰ ἔθνη here = "all the nations" universal — likely ทุกชนชาติ or ประชาชาติทั้งปวง depending on cosmic-vs-mission emphasis; per-verse decision.
- **Galatians 2:7–9** (Paul's apostleship εἰς τὰ ἔθνη + Peter's εἰς τὴν περιτομήν): clearly Gentile-mission → คนต่างชาติ.
- **Ephesians 2:11–22** (the ἔθνη "in flesh"...wall of partition): salvation-historical identity-marking → คนต่างชาติ.

**General epistles + Revelation:**
- **Revelation 7:9 + 21:24** (πᾶν ἔθνος / τὰ ἔθνη in the final-vision): cosmic-eschatological scope → ประชาชาติ.
- **1 Peter 2:9** (Christians as a γένος ἐκλεκτόν...ἔθνος ἅγιον): Christians-as-a-nation analogy → ชนชาติบริสุทธิ์.

## Alternatives considered

- **Single rendering (any of the three) throughout.** Rejected — flattens semantic distinctions Greek deliberately preserves.
- **Two renderings (cosmic vs. mission, collapsing the people-group sense into one).** Rejected — Acts 26:23's λαός↔ἔθνος doublet would lose its parallelism.
- **Preserve ชนต่างชาติ as elevated-formal-discourse variant.** Considered (ChatGPT external review). Rejected for simplicity.

## When to revisit

- If a Pauline-forward verse forces a fourth distinction (e.g., ἐθνικῶς "in a Gentile manner" at Galatians 2:14), document at the verse level.
- If native-Thai review flags any of the three terms as unnatural, revisit individually.
- If Romans 11:13 (ἀπόστολος ἐθνῶν) or 11:25 (πλήρωμα τῶν ἐθνῶν) appears to demand a marked-register variant, document.
