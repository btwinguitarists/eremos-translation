# ἁγιασμός / ἁγιότης — sanctification / holiness vocabulary

**Scope:** The cluster of Greek nouns for *sanctification / holiness* — ἁγιασμός, ἁγιότης, ἁγιωσύνη — when referring to the *process* of being made holy, the *resulting state* of holiness, or the *required moral condition* of believers.

**Decided:** 2026-05-04 during Hebrews end-of-book review. Flagged in §F of the HEB audit as render-or-amend; both ChatGPT and Gemini external AI reviewers concurred (2026-05-04 audit response): document context-dependent renderings, do **not** force a single Thai equivalent for the entire family.

**Locked here before** the corpus-wide phrase-consistency audit catches any verse "non-compliant" with a more rigid rule.

---

## The rule

The Greek family of holiness-nouns has a real internal aspectual distinction (process vs state), but the line between *process* ἁγιασμός and *state* ἁγιότης / ἁγιωσύνη is **not always observed by Thai readers** — and forcing a strict morphological mapping (process → `การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์`, state → `ความบริสุทธิ์`) produces unnatural Thai phrasing where context demands the noun-state form (or vice versa).

| Greek | Default Thai | When |
|---|---|---|
| ἁγιασμός | **การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์** | Process of being made holy is foregrounded — the *act* or *ongoing work* of sanctification (e.g., 1 Thess 4:3 "this is the will of God, your sanctification"; 2 Thess 2:13 "sanctification of the Spirit"). |
| ἁγιασμός | **ความบริสุทธิ์** | Resulting state / required holy-condition is foregrounded — the *what-must-be-pursued* sense (Heb 12:14 "pursue ... the holiness without which no one will see the Lord"). |
| ἁγιότης | **ความบริสุทธิ์** | State-of-holiness — Heb 12:10 ("partake in his holiness"); 2 Cor 1:12 (cognate). |
| ἁγιωσύνη | **ความบริสุทธิ์** | State-of-holiness with moral-character emphasis — Rom 1:4; 2 Cor 7:1; 1 Thess 3:13. |
| ἅγιος (adj.) | **บริสุทธิ์** | Standard adjective; covers most NT use. |
| ἅγιος (subst., "the saints") | **ธรรมิกชน** | Corpus standard from ROM 1:7, 1 COR 1:2, etc. |
| τὸ ἅγιον / τὰ ἅγια | **วิสุทธิสถาน / อภิสุทธิสถาน** | Tabernacle/sanctuary use; HEB 9 cluster. |

### The non-negotiable axis

The choice between **การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์** (process) and **ความบริสุทธิ์** (state) for ἁγιασμός is **context-dependent, not morphologically rigid.** Both are defensible defaults; the verse's argument-context selects the right one.

Forcing ἁγιασμός → `การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์` universally would make Heb 12:14 read as "pursue the *act of being made holy*" — losing the verse's explicit contrast with εἰρήνην (peace) as a *condition-to-be-pursued*. Forcing ἁγιασμός → `ความบริสุทธิ์` universally would flatten 1 Thess 4:3, 2 Thess 2:13, 1 Tim 2:15 where the *process* sense is theologically active.

---

## Heb 12:14 lock — `ความบริสุทธิ์` justified

**Verse:** "Εἰρήνην διώκετε μετὰ πάντων, καὶ τὸν ἁγιασμόν, οὗ χωρὶς οὐδεὶς ὄψεται τὸν κύριον"
**Thai (shipped 2026-05-04):** "จงแสวงหาสันติสุขกับทุกคน และความบริสุทธิ์ ซึ่งหากไม่มีสิ่งนี้ ไม่มีผู้ใดจะได้เห็นองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า"

Why **ความบริสุทธิ์** (state) over `การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์` (process):

1. **Verse 12:10 already used `ความบริสุทธิ์`** for the cognate ἁγιότης ("partake in his holiness"). Using `การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์` four verses later for the cognate ἁγιασμός in adjacent context would create a confusing register-shift without a corresponding shift in the Greek argument. The author's argument across 12:10-14 is about the *holy-condition that discipline produces and that we must pursue* — a single sustained register.
2. **διώκετε εἰρήνην … καὶ τὸν ἁγιασμόν** is a coordinate-pursue construction. εἰρήνη is a state (peace, not "the act of being made peaceful"); ἁγιασμός in coordinate-grammar with εἰρήνη naturally reads as the parallel state.
3. **οὗ χωρὶς οὐδεὶς ὄψεται τὸν κύριον** ("without which no one will see the Lord") frames ἁγιασμός as the *required precondition* for vision-of-God — that's a state-of-being, not a process.
4. **Mainstream Thai reception:** ความบริสุทธิ์ is the natural-Thai for the holy-condition-of-life that this verse demands. การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์ would read as more clinical-doctrinal (the *ordo salutis* doctrine), shifting the pastoral force of the verse.

**Reviewer concurrence:** ChatGPT 2026-05-04: "ความบริสุทธิ์ is understandable and idiomatic … 12:10 already uses ความบริสุทธิ์ for ἁγιότης in close context." Gemini 2026-05-04: "imposing artificial morphological constraints will lead to unnatural Thai phrasing where context demands the noun-state form."

## Forward implications

### Pauline corpus
- **Rom 6:19, 6:22**: ἁγιασμός in process sense (sanctification *as result of being-set-free-from-sin*) → expect `การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์`.
- **1 Thess 4:3, 4:4, 4:7**: ἁγιασμός in process sense (sanctification *as God's will for behavior*) → expect `การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์`.
- **2 Thess 2:13**: ἐν ἁγιασμῷ πνεύματος → "in sanctification of the Spirit" → process sense → `การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์`.
- **1 Tim 2:15**: ἐν ἁγιασμῷ → process / ongoing-condition; either Thai works depending on emphasis.
- **Heb 12:10**: ἁγιότης → `ความบริสุทธิ์` (state). LOCKED at ship.
- **Rom 1:4**: ἁγιωσύνη (Christ's holy-divinity) → `ความบริสุทธิ์`.

### Petrine corpus
- **1 Pet 1:2**: ἐν ἁγιασμῷ πνεύματος → mirror of 2 Thess 2:13 → `การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์`.

### Verb cognate
- **ἁγιάζω** consistently → `ทรงชำระ ... ให้บริสุทธิ์` (active divine action). Locked across the corpus already (HEB 2:11, 9:13, 10:10, 10:14, 10:29, 13:12).

## Why two renderings (and not three)

Some translation projects further split *holiness* by source — *moral-conduct holiness* (ἁγιωσύνη) vs *cultic-set-apart holiness* (ἁγιότης) vs *sanctifying-action holiness* (ἁγιασμός) — and assign three Thai terms. We do **not** do this because:

1. **The Greek itself does not consistently observe this split.** ἁγιασμός in 1 Pet 1:2 has clear cultic-set-apart force despite the -μός noun-form; ἁγιωσύνη in Rom 1:4 is divine-attribute, not believer-conduct.
2. **Thai readers do not need a three-way split.** The contrast between *process* (`การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์`) and *state* (`ความบริสุทธิ์`) is the only distinction that pays interpretive interest. A third term would be artificial.
3. **Corpus simplicity.** Two-term flexibility (with documented context selection) covers all NT use without forcing the per-verse decision into a semantic-overdetermination loop.

---

## Alternatives considered

- **Hard-lock ἁγιασμός → `การชำระให้บริสุทธิ์` universally.** Rejected — would produce non-compliant Heb 12:14 (per ChatGPT/Gemini concurrence).
- **Hard-lock ἁγιασμός → `ความบริสุทธิ์` universally.** Rejected — would flatten 1 Thess 4:3 + 2 Thess 2:13 + Rom 6:19,22 process-of-sanctification register.
- **Coin a new Thai term for ἁγιασμός distinct from `ความบริสุทธิ์`.** Rejected — `ความบริสุทธิ์` is the established Thai Christian term; coining a competitor breaks corpus-hygiene.
