# πρωτότοκος — three-sense corpus split

**Decision date:** 2026-05-02 (formalized after COL end-of-book external AI review)
**Anchor passages:** Colossians 1:15 (rank-comparative); Colossians 1:18 (partitive-of-the-dead)
**Forward applicability:** Romans 8:29 (relational-of-believers); Hebrews 1:6 + 12:23; Revelation 1:5
**Status:** LOCK. Three sense-clusters with three distinct Thai genitive-particle renderings.

## The split

| Sense | Greek pattern | Thai genitive | Anchor |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rank-comparative-of-creation** | πρωτότοκος **πάσης κτίσεως** | บุตรหัวปี**เหนือ**สิ่งที่ทรงสร้างทั้งสิ้น | COL 1:15 |
| **Partitive-of-the-dead** | πρωτότοκος **ἐκ τῶν νεκρῶν** | บุตรหัวปี**จากในหมู่**ผู้ตาย | COL 1:18; Rev 1:5 |
| **Relational-of-believers** | πρωτότοκος **ἐν πολλοῖς ἀδελφοῖς** | บุตรหัวปี**ในหมู่**พี่น้องจำนวนมาก | Rom 8:29; Heb 12:23 |

## Why the three Thai renderings differ

The Greek genitive admits multiple senses that the same Thai particle (ของ) would flatten:

- **เหนือ ("over") at COL 1:15** — Christ ranks ABOVE all creation, not first-among-creation. The exegetical evidence is internal: COL 1:16 says πάντα ἐν αὐτῷ ἐκτίσθη ("all things were created IN him"), which makes a partitive "first-created-thing" reading grammatically impossible. The Thai เหนือ encodes what the immediate context already requires.
- **จากในหมู่ ("from among") at COL 1:18 and Rev 1:5** — Christ is the first to rise FROM AMONG the dead; this IS partitive (he was once dead like them, then was the first to come back). Same Greek lemma, opposite genitive function.
- **ในหมู่ ("among") at Rom 8:29 and Heb 12:23** — Christ stands relationally AMONG his brothers/the firstborn-assembly. Honorific-relational, not rank-comparative or partitive.

## The "go more-explicit than mainstream English" criterion

COL 1:15's เหนือ goes a step further than the major English translations (NIV / ESV / CSB / BSB / NASB), which all use "firstborn of all creation" and rely on context to block Arianism. Eremos's choice to make the rank-sense lexical is principled, but the project should articulate when this kind of step is permitted.

**Two-prong test (per Claude's recommendation):**

A more-explicit-than-English rendering is permitted when **both** are true:

1. **The alternative reading is exegetically excluded** by the immediate Greek context (e.g., COL 1:16 makes the partitive reading grammatically impossible).
2. **The alternative reading is associated with a heresy that has live Thai-language reach** (e.g., JW NWT translates COL 1:15 as "firstborn of all creation" with the explicit Arian gloss — and JW evangelism is active in Thai-language ministry).

COL 1:15 πρωτότοκος passes both prongs cleanly:
- Prong 1: ἐν αὐτῷ ἐκτίσθη τὰ πάντα (1:16) excludes the partitive reading.
- Prong 2: JW Thai-language tract distribution + door-to-door evangelism uses the partitive reading explicitly.

Future verses where this test might apply (likely candidates pending audit when shipped):

- John 1:1 (θεὸς ἦν ὁ λόγος) — Watchtower mistranslates as "a god"; English mainstream is "the Word was God"; Thai pattern would be context-locked already.
- Hebrews 1:8 (ὁ θρόνος σου ὁ θεός) — Watchtower reads "God is your throne"; mainstream addresses the Son as "God."

These pass the test. Cases that **fail the test** (and where Eremos should NOT go more-explicit than English):

- Rom 9:5 χριστὸς τὸ κατὰ σάρκα ὁ ὢν ἐπὶ πάντων θεός — christological doxology is debated by orthodox-Trinitarian scholars too, not just heretics. Test-prong 2 fails (no live Thai-language Christological-distortion using this verse). Eremos uses a Tier-2 footer note acknowledging the doxology-to-Father alternative; doesn't go more-explicit.
- 1 Tim 3:16 ὃς ἐφανερώθη ἐν σαρκί — textual variant; not relevant to the test.

## Gemini's CONCERN

Gemini argued for revising COL 1:15 from เหนือ → ของ, citing optimal-equivalence purity + parallelism with COL 1:18. The point about parallelism is real (both verses use πρωτότοκος), but the parallelism is WITHIN the hymn's structural bracket (1:15 / 1:18 frame the cosmological-Christology + resurrection-Christology halves). The bracket holds at the Greek lemma level (πρωτότοκος in both); the Thai differentiates because the genitive function differs. ChatGPT and Claude both accept this; project lock follows the 2-of-3 majority.

Surfaced for awareness: if Ben prefers parallelism-purity, COL 1:15 could be re-revised to ของ in a follow-up PR. Default is to keep เหนือ.
