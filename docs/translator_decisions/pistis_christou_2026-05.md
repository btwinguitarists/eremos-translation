# πίστις Χριστοῦ — objective genitive default with verse-level review

**Decision date:** 2026-05-02 (formalized after PHP end-of-book external AI review)
**Anchor passage:** Philippians 3:9
**Cross-checks:** Galatians 2:16, 3:22 (already shipped — confirmed objective-genitive)
**Status:** **Default = objective genitive** ("faith IN Christ"). Verse-level review required at each occurrence; the default is not a mechanical rule.

## The crux

The Greek genitive πίστις Χριστοῦ admits two readings:

- **(a) Objective genitive** — "faith **IN** Christ" (Christ as object of believing). Traditional / Reformation / NIV / ESV / CSB / BSB. Supports forensic justification: the believer's faith-in-Christ is the means of receiving the alien-righteousness.
- **(b) Subjective genitive** — "the faith / faithfulness **OF** Christ" (Christ as subject of believing). Wright / Hays / Campbell / NIV2011-marginal-note / CEB-text. Supports participation Christology: Christ's own faithfulness-to-the-Father is the means of believer-righteousness.

## Project default

**(a) Objective genitive — ความเชื่อในพระคริสต์ / ความเชื่อในพระเยซูคริสต์.**

Three reasons:

1. **Audience.** Eremos's primary audience is Thai evangelical-Protestant; the objective-genitive reading is dominant in that tradition.
2. **Local syntax.** Where the Greek follows πίστις Χριστοῦ with a clarifying ἐπὶ τῇ πίστει (PHP 3:9b), the second πίστις unmistakably refers to the believer's faith. That is the strongest single grammatical indicator across the cluster.
3. **Corpus consistency.** Already-shipped passages (GAL 2:16, GAL 3:22) translate πίστις Χριστοῦ as **ความเชื่อในพระเยซูคริสต์** without backlash. Reversing course corpus-wide would destabilize without payoff.
