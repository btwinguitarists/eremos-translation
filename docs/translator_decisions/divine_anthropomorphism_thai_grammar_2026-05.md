# Divine anthropomorphism — Thai-grammar transfer policy

**Scope:** How the Eremos OT translation handles Hebrew divine-anthropomorphism (body-parts of God, divine-emotions, divine-bodily-actions) in Thai — preserving the Hebrew imagery in Rachasap (royal-Thai) register without flattening to abstract paraphrase, and without crossing into deity-class register collisions.

**Decided:** 2026-05-12 (Genesis end-of-book audit §J4 + Ruth-1:13 implicit rule + Jonah נִחַם validation; Genesis validates the policy a third time at scale).

**Companion docs:** `hebrew_idioms_and_metaphor_2026-05.md` (the broader idiom-preservation policy this extends), `ot_register_policy_2026-05.md` (Rachasap), `nicham_divine_relenting_2026-05.md` (the most theologically-loaded anthropomorphism), `divine_names_table_2026-05.md`.

---

## 1. The principle

Hebrew divine-anthropomorphism is **load-bearing theology**, not decorative imagery. Biblical authors used body-parts-of-God + divine-emotions + divine-bodily-actions deliberately, and the canonical theology of God's character is built on the specific imagery (the "outstretched arm," the "watching eyes," the "remembering," the "regretting/grieving"). Translation must preserve the imagery, not paraphrase it into abstract divine-attributes.

**The rule:** preserve the literal Hebrew imagery in Thai Rachasap (royal-Thai) register. Do NOT abstract ("God's power" instead of "God's hand"), do NOT soften ("God was sad" instead of "God grieved in his heart"), do NOT supply theological resolution at the surface ("God knew in his foreknowledge" instead of "God remembered").

## 2. The five categories of divine anthropomorphism

### 2.1 Body-parts of God

The Hebrew Bible's divine-body-part vocabulary maps to Thai Rachasap (พระ-prefix on body-terms):

| Hebrew | Literal English | Thai (Rachasap) |
|---|---|---|
| יָד יהוה / יְמִין יהוה ("hand of YHWH" / "right hand") | hand | **พระหัตถ์** |
| עֵין יהוה / עֵינֵי יהוה ("eye/s of YHWH") | eye | **พระเนตร** |
| פְּנֵי יהוה ("face of YHWH") | face | **พระพักตร์** / **เบื้องพระพักตร์** ("before the face of") |
| אַף / חֲרוֹן אַף יהוה ("nose / burning nose of YHWH" = divine anger) | nose / nostrils | **พระพิโรธ** (the metaphorical-emotion term) + verse-level KD names the literal "nose/nostrils" anatomical reference |
| אָזְנֵי יהוה ("ears of YHWH") | ear | **พระกรรณ** |
| פֶּה יהוה ("mouth of YHWH") | mouth | **พระโอษฐ์** |
| לֵב / לֵבָב יהוה ("heart of YHWH") | heart | **พระทัย** / **พระหฤทัย** |
| רוּחַ יהוה ("Spirit / breath of YHWH") | spirit / breath | **พระวิญญาณ** (already an established corpus-lock) |
| זְרוֹעַ יהוה ("arm of YHWH" — strength/might imagery) | arm | **พระกร** ("royal arm") |
| רֶגֶל יהוה ("feet of YHWH" — esp. Ex 24:10, Isa 66:1 "earth is my footstool") | feet | **พระบาท** |

**Locking precedent — Genesis:** Gen 3:8 (the sound of YHWH-God walking in the garden in the breeze of the day) — **พระสุรเสียง** "voice" + walking — anthropomorphism preserved. Gen 11:5 (YHWH "came down to see" the city and the tower) — **เสด็จลงมาทอดพระเนตร** preserves the literal action + body-part. Gen 19:16 (the angels grasping Lot's hand — **พระหัตถ์** for the angelic-divine hand).

### 2.2 Divine emotions

The OT uses a rich emotional-vocabulary for God:

| Hebrew | Literal English | Thai (Rachasap) |
|---|---|---|
| נָחַם (Niph'al, divine-grief context) | be sorry, regret, be moved | **ทรงเสียพระทัย** (per `nicham_divine_relenting_2026-05.md` sub-sense B) |
| נָחַם (Niph'al, divine-relenting context) | change one's mind, relent | **ทรงเปลี่ยนพระทัย** (per `nicham_divine_relenting_2026-05.md` sub-sense A) |
| חָרָה / וַיִּחַר אַף יהוה | burn (anger), become hot | **ทรงพระพิโรธ** / **พระพิโรธของพระองค์พลุ่งขึ้น** |
| חָמַל / חוּס | have pity, spare | **ทรงเมตตา** / **ทรงสงสาร** |
| חָפֵץ ("delight in") | take pleasure, will | **ทรงพอพระทัยใน** |
| אָהַב | love | **ทรงรัก** |
| שָׂנֵא | hate | **ทรงเกลียดชัง** |
| קִנְאָה ("jealousy" — covenant-protective sense) | jealousy / zeal | **พระทัยที่หวงแหน** / **ทรงหวงแหน** |
| זָכַר ("remember") | remember (covenant-recall) | **ทรงจดจำ / ทรงระลึกถึง** |
| שָׁכַח ("forget" — rhetorical, in laments) | forget | **ทรงลืม** (preserve the lament's protest-rhetoric) |

**Locking precedent — Genesis:** Gen 6:6 (וַיִּתְעַצֵּב אֶל־לִבּוֹ "and he was grieved to his heart") → **ทรงเสียพระทัยในส่วนลึกของพระหฤทัย** — preserve the "to his heart" depth-anchor. Gen 8:1 (וַיִּזְכֹּר אֱלֹהִים אֶת־נֹחַ "and God remembered Noah") → **พระเจ้าทรงระลึกถึงโนอาห์** — the covenant-recall sense. Gen 22:12 (the YHWH-of-the-Akedah saying "now I know" יָדַעְתִּי) — the divine-knowing as relational, not omniscient-static.

### 2.3 Divine bodily-actions

The OT depicts God walking, descending, ascending, going-up, going-down, hovering, etc.:

| Hebrew | Thai (Rachasap) |
|---|---|
| יָרַד ("come/go down" — divine descent) | **เสด็จลงมา** |
| עָלָה ("go up" — divine ascent, often post-theophany) | **เสด็จขึ้นไป** |
| הָלַךְ ("walk" — Gen 3:8, Lev 26:12) | **ทรงดำเนิน** / **เสด็จดำเนิน** |
| בּוֹא ("come" — theophanic arrival) | **เสด็จมา** |
| יָצָא ("go out" — divine warrior, Ex 15:3, Hab 3) | **เสด็จออก** |
| רָחַף ("hover" — Gen 1:2 Spirit hovering over waters; Deut 32:11 eagle hovering) | **ทรงปกอยู่เหนือ** / **โฉบเฉี่ยวอยู่เหนือ** |
| יָשַׁב ("sit/dwell/enthroned") | **ประทับ** / **ประทับนั่ง** |

**Locking precedent — Genesis:** Gen 1:2 (וְרוּחַ אֱלֹהִים מְרַחֶפֶת עַל־פְּנֵי הַמָּיִם) → **พระวิญญาณของพระเจ้าทรงปกอยู่เหนือน้ำ** — the rare "hover" verb preserved. Gen 18:20–21 (YHWH "going down to see" Sodom — אֵרְדָה־נָּא וְאֶרְאֶה) → **เราจะลงไปทอดพระเนตร** — first-person divine descent.

### 2.4 Divine-speech anthropomorphisms

When YHWH "speaks" (אָמַר / דִּבֶּר), the speech-act itself is a divine-anthropomorphism. The corpus already locks **ตรัส** (royal-Thai "to speak") for divine speech across the OT — this doc confirms that the speech-act anthropomorphism is preserved without paraphrase ("God communicated" / "God revealed"). Hebrew **YHWH said** → Thai **องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้าตรัส**.

The `ot_register_policy_2026-05.md §1.3` prophetic-oracle voice locks the **`องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้าตรัสดังนี้ว่า`** quotative formula. This doc cross-references that lock.

### 2.5 Divine-sensory anthropomorphisms

God "sees" (רָאָה), "hears" (שָׁמַע), "smells" (רוּחַ — Gen 8:21), "knows" (יָדַע). The sensory-verbs map to Thai Rachasap:

| Hebrew | Thai (Rachasap) |
|---|---|
| רָאָה | **ทอดพระเนตร** / **ทรงเห็น** |
| שָׁמַע | **ทรงสดับ** / **ทรงฟัง** |
| יָדַע | **ทรงทราบ** (covenant-knowing) |
| הֵרִיחַ ("smell" — Gen 8:21 the burnt-offering's pleasing aroma) | **ทรงดมกลิ่น** (preserve the bold anthropomorphism) |

**Locking precedent — Genesis:** Gen 8:21 (וַיָּרַח יהוה אֶת־רֵיחַ הַנִּיחֹחַ "and YHWH smelled the pleasing aroma") → **องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้าทรงดมกลิ่นหอมที่หวานน่าพอใจ** — the most-vivid divine-sensory anthropomorphism in the OT; preserve it.

## 3. What we deliberately reject

- **Abstract-paraphrase substitutions** that flatten the imagery ("God's power" for "God's arm/hand"; "God's awareness" for "God's eyes"; "God's anger" for "God's burning nose" — the metaphorical-anger term is OK but the verse-level KD must name the literal "nose" reference).
- **Theological-resolution overlays** at the surface ("God allowed it" instead of "God hardened Pharaoh's heart"; "God knew from the beginning" instead of "God said: now I know"). The theology happens in the reader's interpretive layer, not in the translation surface.
- **Deity-class register collisions** — never use เทพ-class vocabulary for YHWH's body-parts or actions (see `pagan_deities_2026-04.md` + `spiritual_beings_hierarchy_2026-05.md`).
- **Softening for "modern sensibility"** — Hebrew anthropomorphism is **the** vehicle for OT theology; softening it betrays the source.

## 4. Genesis validation — the three classes that validate the policy

Genesis exercises all three classes (after Ruth's body-parts and Jonah's נִחַם validation):

1. **Body-parts:** Gen 3:8 (walking-in-the-garden), 16:7 (the Angel of YHWH "finding" Hagar), 19:16 (the angels grasping Lot's hand), 32:30 (Jacob "seeing God face-to-face at Peniel"), 49:25 ("the Almighty who will bless you with blessings of breasts and womb")
2. **Emotions:** Gen 6:6 (grief; locking precedent for sub-sense B of nicham), 22:12 ("now I know"), 8:1 (remembered Noah), 8:21 (smelled the pleasing aroma)
3. **Bodily-actions:** Gen 1:2 (hovering), 11:5 (came-down-to-see), 18:21 (going-down-to-see Sodom), 35:13 (going-up from Jacob at Bethel)

The third validation at Genesis scale confirms the policy is corpus-stable and ready for the heavy anthropomorphism load of Exodus 7 (the plagues — God's outstretched arm), Deuteronomy 4 + 32 (Mosaic theology of God's hand/eye/face), Psalms (the corpus's anthropomorphism-densest book), and the prophets.
