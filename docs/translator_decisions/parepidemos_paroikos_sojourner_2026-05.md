# παρεπίδημος / πάροικος / διασπορά / παροικία — Petrine sojourner-exile cluster

**Decision date:** 2026-05-03 (formalized after 1 Peter end-of-book review)
**Anchor passages:** 1 Pet 1:1 (letter-opening identity), 1 Pet 2:11 (παροίκους καὶ παρεπιδήμους doublet)
**Status:** LOCK. The cluster frames the whole epistle and reuses standard OT-Israel diaspora-sojourner vocabulary, so the renderings need to be fixed before Hebrews 11:13 + 13:14 ship.

## The four lemmas + their renderings

| Greek lemma | Sense | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| **παρεπίδημος** | the *passing-through* sojourner (Heb. גֵּר, Gen 23:4 LXX) | **คนต่างถิ่น** |
| **πάροικος** | the *resident-alien* with limited rights | **คนพำนักชั่วคราว** (or **คนต่างถิ่น** when paired-and-collapsed in a doublet) |
| **διασπορά** | the dispersion (people / collective place) | **กระจัดกระจาย** (matching JAS 1:1 corpus precedent) |
| **παροικία** | abstract noun "sojourning-time" | verbal-paraphrase **เวลาที่ ... พำนักอยู่ในต่างถิ่น** |

## Verse-level evidence (1 Peter)

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| 1:1 | ἐκλεκτοῖς **παρεπιδήμοις διασπορᾶς** | **ผู้ที่ทรงเลือกสรรและเป็นคนต่างถิ่นกระจัดกระจายอยู่** |
| 1:17 | τὸν τῆς **παροικίας** ὑμῶν χρόνον | **ตลอดเวลาที่ท่านพำนักอยู่ในต่างถิ่น** |
| 2:11 | **παροίκους καὶ παρεπιδήμους** | **คนต่างถิ่นและคนพำนักชั่วคราว** (doublet flipped to natural Thai order) |

## Why this cluster matters theologically

Peter applies the OT-Israel diaspora-sojourner identity to **Gentile-Christians** (per uW FRONT note at 1:1; the five-province addressee-list confirms a non-Jewish primary audience). The whole epistle is structured as a letter "to-the-exiles" — making the sojourner-frame load-bearing for the letter's ethics (2:11 παροίκους-καὶ-παρεπιδήμους governs the whole 2:11–3:12 ethical block) and Christology (Christ-as-the-rejected-stone you-also-as-living-stones at 2:4-10 echoes the same outsider-identity).

The Thai needs to:
1. preserve the dual-identity (chosen + sojourners-in-dispersion) at 1:1,
2. preserve the doublet-distinction at 2:11 (where Greek pairs the two synonyms for emphasis),
3. preserve the verbal sense at 1:17 (where παροικία is an abstract-temporal noun, not a substantive),
4. carry the lexical thread through all four occurrences without flattening.

## Why "คนต่างถิ่น" + "คนพำนักชั่วคราว" over alternatives

Three Thai options were considered for παρεπίδημος:

1. **คนต่างด้าว** ("alien / foreigner") — used in formal-legal Thai (immigration documents). Rejected: too modern-administrative; mismatches the OT-pastoral register.
2. **คนเดินทาง** ("traveler") — preserves the *passing-through* sense. Rejected: loses the *exiled-from-home* connotation that the OT-διασπορά tradition carries.
3. **คนต่างถิ่น** ("foreigner / out-of-home person") — selected. Captures both the *not-from-here* and the *not-belonging-here-permanently* senses without administrative connotations. Reads naturally in religious-ethical register.

Three Thai options were considered for πάροικος (specifically at 2:11 where it pairs with παρεπίδημος):

1. **คนต่างชาติ** ("foreigner / Gentile") — Rejected: would collapse with Peter's deliberate ἔθνος vocabulary at 2:12 (`ἐν τοῖς ἔθνεσιν` → **ในหมู่คนต่างชาติ**), creating a confusing Thai-doublet.
2. **ผู้พำนัก** ("resident") — too neutral; loses the *limited-rights* sense.
3. **คนพำนักชั่วคราว** ("temporary-resident") — selected. Preserves the *limited-rights* + *transient* sense while distinguishing from the *passing-through* παρεπίδημος.

For διασπορά the corpus-precedent JAS 1:1 already locked **กระจัดกระจาย** (the verb-form of "scatter") — the same Thai is reused here for inter-textual cohesion across both Catholic Epistles.

## Cross-corpus application

This doc locks the rendering for all NT books not yet translated. Specific anticipated cases:

- **Hebrews 11:13** — `ξένοι καὶ παρεπίδημοί εἰσιν ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς` (the great patriarchs-as-sojourners passage) → **เป็นคนแปลกหน้าและคนต่างถิ่นบนแผ่นดินโลก** (preserve the doublet; ξένος → **คนแปลกหน้า** to distinguish from the παρεπίδημος → **คนต่างถิ่น** lock).
- **Hebrews 13:14** — `οὐ γὰρ ἔχομεν ὧδε μένουσαν πόλιν` — though no sojourner-lemma is used, the theological frame matches; render preserves the eschatological-not-here-yet sense.
- **Acts 7:6, 7:29, 13:17** — Stephen's speech uses πάροικος in literal-Israelite-historical contexts (Abraham + Moses + the wilderness generation) → **คนต่างถิ่น** or **คนพำนักชั่วคราว** depending on context. Already shipped; verify on next Acts pass.
- **Eph 2:19** — `οὐκέτι ἐστὲ ξένοι καὶ πάροικοι` (Gentile-believers no-longer alien/sojourners IN the church) → **ไม่เป็นคนแปลกหน้าและคนพำนักชั่วคราว** (Pauline anti-sojourner ecclesiology — preserves the lexical contrast with 1 Pet 2:11 where Christians ARE sojourners in-the-world; the two are theologically complementary, not contradictory).

## Why JAS 1:1 → 1 PET 1:1 cohesion matters

Both Catholic Epistles open with διασπορά:
- **JAS 1:1**: `ταῖς δώδεκα φυλαῖς ταῖς ἐν τῇ διασπορᾷ` → **สิบสองเผ่าที่กระจัดกระจายอยู่**
- **1 PET 1:1**: `παρεπιδήμοις διασπορᾶς` → **คนต่างถิ่นกระจัดกระจายอยู่**

The shared **กระจัดกระจาย** rendering preserves the Catholic-Epistles cohesion for Thai readers — both letters open with the same diaspora-vocabulary, which the reader experiences as a deliberate inter-textual link (matching the Greek).
