# παρουσία — subject-driven register split

**Decision date:** 2026-05-02 (formalized after PHP end-of-book external AI review)
**Anchor passages:** Philippians 1:26 (human subject), 1 Thessalonians 3:13 (Christ subject)
**Status:** LOCK. The split is not a theological imposition — it is a Thai grammatical requirement (ราชาศัพท์, royal-honorific register).

## The split

| Subject of παρουσία | Thai register | Rendering |
|---|---|---|
| **Human** (Paul, Stephanas, etc.) | Plain | **การมา** / **การกลับไป** / **การกลับมา** depending on direction |
| **Christ** (eschatological subject) | Royal (ราชาศัพท์) | **การเสด็จมา** |
| **Lawless one** (2 Thess 2:9) | Plain non-royal | **การมา** (deliberately denied royal register) |

## Why this is grammatical, not theological

Thai distinguishes between ordinary speech and royal-divine speech (ราชาศัพท์) at the level of common verbs and nouns. "เสด็จ" is a royal-only verb; using it for a non-royal subject is ungrammatical, not just stylistically marked. Conversely, using the plain register for Christ in eschatological contexts would create an asymmetry that Thai readers feel as honorific failure, not as neutrality.

Greek παρουσία does not encode this distinction — it covers "presence / arrival" generically. Thai cannot. The split is therefore not Eremos imposing theological emphasis; it is the language requiring the translator to commit to a register per occurrence.

The 2 Thess 2:9 case (the lawless one's παρουσία) reverses the register choice for the same lemma — confirming the split is subject-driven, not lemma-driven. The lawless one is given the plain register specifically to deny royal status.

## Verse-level evidence

- **PHP 1:26** GK: `διὰ τῆς ἐμῆς **παρουσίας** πάλιν πρὸς ὑμᾶς`
  TH: `เมื่อข้าพเจ้า**กลับไป**หาท่านอีก`

  Paul-as-subject; πάλιν πρὸς ὑμᾶς ("again to you") gives the directional return sense that "การกลับไป" captures. "การปรากฏตัว" or "การประทับ" would be wooden. "การเสด็จกลับ" would be ungrammatical (Paul is not royalty).

- **1 Thess 3:13** GK: `ἐν τῇ **παρουσίᾳ** τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ μετὰ πάντων τῶν ἁγίων αὐτοῦ`
  TH: `ในการ**เสด็จมา**ของพระเยซูองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้าของเราพร้อมกับบรรดาผู้บริสุทธิ์ทุกคนของพระองค์`

  Christ-as-subject + eschatological frame → royal register required.

- **2 Thess 2:9** GK: `οὗ ἐστιν ἡ **παρουσία** κατ' ἐνέργειαν τοῦ Σατανᾶ`

  The lawless one's παρουσία is rendered with plain register — deliberate denial of royal honor.

## §Petrine — ἀποκάλυψις / φανερόω as the eschatological-Christ-coming

**Added 2026-05-03 after 1 Peter end-of-book external AI review.**

1 Peter does **not** use παρουσία. It uses **ἀποκάλυψις** (revelation / unveiling) and **φανερόω** (manifest) for the same eschatological-Christ-coming event that Paul calls παρουσία. The Thai must preserve the lexical distinction.

| Greek lemma | Pauline rendering (παρουσία) | Petrine rendering (ἀποκάλυψις / φανερόω) |
|---|---|---|
| παρουσία (Christ-subject, eschatological) | **การเสด็จมา** | (not used in 1 Peter) |
| ἀποκάλυψις (Christ-subject, eschatological) | (rare in Pauline corpus for this sense — see 1 Cor 1:7; 2 Thess 1:7) | **การสำแดง** |
| φανερόω (Christ-subject, eschatological passive) | (rare in Pauline corpus for this sense) | **ทรงสำแดงพระองค์** |

### 1 Peter verse-level evidence

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| 1:7 | ἐν **ἀποκαλύψει** Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ | ใน**การสำแดง**ของพระเยซูคริสต์ |
| 1:13 | ἐν **ἀποκαλύψει** Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ | ใน**การสำแดง**ของพระเยซูคริสต์ |
| 4:13 | ἐν τῇ **ἀποκαλύψει** τῆς δόξης αὐτοῦ | ใน**การสำแดง**พระสิริของพระองค์ |
| 5:1 | τῆς μελλούσης **ἀποκαλύπτεσθαι** δόξης | พระสิริที่จะ**ทรงสำแดง** |
| 5:4 | **φανερωθέντος** τοῦ ἀρχιποίμενος | เมื่อพระผู้เลี้ยงใหญ่**ทรงสำแดงพระองค์** |

### Why the lexical distinction matters

Paul and Peter describe the same future event with different metaphors:

- **Pauline παρουσία** — "royal arrival / presence" (Hellenistic-imperial vocabulary for an emperor's visit-to-a-province). The metaphor is *the king is coming to you*. Thai **การเสด็จมา** captures *royal-arrival*.
- **Petrine ἀποκάλυψις / φανερόω** — "unveiling / manifestation" (Jewish-apocalyptic vocabulary for the disclosure of what was hidden). The metaphor is *the king who was hidden is being revealed*. Thai **การสำแดง / ทรงสำแดงพระองค์** captures *unveiling*.

Flattening both into either Thai rendering would erase the Pauline-vs-Petrine theological distinctive. The royal-prefix **ทรง-** is preserved on both sides (Christ-as-divine-subject) but the noun-stem differs (เสด็จ "arrive / proceed" vs สำแดง "reveal / manifest"), preserving the lexical-metaphor distinction.

### Cross-corpus rule

When **παρουσία** has Christ as eschatological subject → **การเสด็จมา**.
When **ἀποκάλυψις** has Christ as eschatological subject → **การสำแดง**.
When **φανερόω** (passive) has Christ as eschatological subject → **ทรงสำแดงพระองค์**.

These do not collapse. Paul's παρουσία at 1 Thess 3:13 stays **การเสด็จมา**; Peter's ἀποκάλυψις at 1 Pet 1:7 stays **การสำแดง**. When 2 Peter 1:16 uses παρουσία (the only Petrine use of that lemma), it pivots back to **การเสด็จมา** — confirming the lock is lemma-driven, not author-driven.
