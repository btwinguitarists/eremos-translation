# ἄφεσις (ἁμαρτιῶν) → การยกโทษ(บาป)

**Scope:** The Greek noun ἄφεσις when it carries the theological sense of "forgiveness / remission (of sins)." This is the dominant NT sense: ~17 occurrences including the 5 Petrine-and-Pauline Acts sermons that programmatically name the gospel-content. Distinct from the contextual "release / liberation" sense (captives, prisoners) which takes a different Thai rendering — see §Exception below.

**Decided:** 2026-04-23 during the Claude external-review pass of Luke end-of-book. Lock chosen from among the two options listed in RULES.md §4 (ἄφεσις | การยกโทษ / การอภัย | Document choice). Claude flagged cross-chapter drift inside Luke (1:77 ↔ 24:47) as the highest-priority Acts-forward risk.

**Evidence base (Luke, all ἄφεσις occurrences verified):**
- LUK 3:3: εἰς ἄφεσιν ἁμαρτιῶν → **การยกโทษบาป** (John-Baptist's programmatic baptism-message)
- LUK 4:18: αἰχμαλώτοις ἄφεσιν + ἐν ἀφέσει → **ปลดปล่อย** (Isa 61:1 quotation — "release" sense, not "forgiveness"; SEE §Exception)
- LUK 24:47: εἰς ἄφεσιν ἁμαρτιῶν → **การยกโทษบาป** (Great Commission)
- LUK 1:77: ἐν ἀφέσει ἁμαρτιῶν → **การยกโทษบาป** *(normalized 2026-04-23 from earlier การอภัย draft; Benedictus-Commission inclusio now consistent)*

---

## The rule

**ἄφεσις (ἁμαρτιῶν) → การยกโทษ(บาป)**, consistent across the NT corpus.

Apply when:
- The Greek phrase is ἄφεσις ἁμαρτιῶν or ἄφεσις τῶν ἁμαρτιῶν, or ἄφεσις alone with ἁμαρτιῶν in the immediate context (as at LUK 3:3 where it is explicit, or MRK 1:4 parallel).
- The Greek preposition varies (ἐν ἀφέσει / εἰς ἄφεσιν / κηρυχθῆναι... ἄφεσιν). Thai particles track the preposition (โดย- / เพื่อ- / เรื่อง-) but the noun-phrase stays locked.

**Do NOT apply when:**
- ἄφεσις carries the Jubilee/Isaianic "release of captives" sense (LUK 4:18 αἰχμαλώτοις ἄφεσιν). Use **ปลดปล่อย** ("release") and note the contextual shift in the verse-level rationale.

## Why การยกโทษ (and not การอภัย)

**Argument for การยกโทษ (chosen):**
- **Precise semantic match.** ἄφεσις comes from ἀφίημι ("send away, release, let go"). Thai การยกโทษ literally = "to lift/remove the penalty" — preserves the active-removal sense. การอภัย is softer-general "forgive/pardon."
- **Cross-chapter consistency already established.** LUK 3:3 (John-Baptist) and LUK 24:47 (Great Commission) both used การยกโทษ; LUK 1:77 was a single-verse outlier. Normalizing 1:77 preserves the Benedictus→Great-Commission inclusio Luke deliberately builds across his two-volume work.
- **Matches the programmatic Acts pattern.** Peter's Pentecost altar-call (Acts 2:38 εἰς ἄφεσιν τῶν ἁμαρτιῶν), Peter on Cornelius (Acts 10:43 ἄφεσιν ἁμαρτιῶν), Paul at Antioch (Acts 13:38), Paul before Agrippa (Acts 26:18), and the Sanhedrin confrontation (Acts 5:31). Five canonical evangelism-theology verses; one rendering across all.

**Argument for การอภัย (rejected):**
- More common in informal Thai Christian usage.
- Sounds warmer / pastoral.
- **Why rejected:** the NT ἄφεσις ἁμαρτιῶν phrase is technical-theological. Locking to the softer term blurs the judicial-release sense that matters for:
  - Heb 9:22 (χωρὶς αἱματεκχυσίας οὐ γίνεται ἄφεσις — "without shedding of blood there is no forgiveness")
  - Heb 10:18 (ὅπου δὲ ἄφεσις τούτων — "where forgiveness of these is")
  - Eph 1:7, Col 1:14 (ἀπολύτρωσις... τὴν ἄφεσιν τῶν παραπτωμάτων / ἁμαρτιῶν — redemption as forgiveness)
- The judicial sense (sin-as-debt-lifted) is particularly important in Pauline atonement theology where ἄφεσις pairs with ἀπολύτρωσις (redemption) and δικαίωσις (justification).

## Exception: ἄφεσις = "release" (Jubilee / prisoners)

LUK 4:18 (Isa 61:1 citation) uses ἄφεσις twice for the jubilee-release of captives, not for forgiveness of sins. Thai **ปลดปล่อย** ("release / set free"). The verse-level rationale at 4:18 documents this as an Isaianic-jubilee technical usage.

Related: LUK 4:19 — "the acceptable year of the Lord" (ἐνιαυτὸν κυρίου δεκτόν) — is the Jubilee-proclamation framing. ἄφεσις in this passage echoes LXX Lev 25 (jubilee year = ἔτος ἀφέσεως in LXX). Standard Thai jubilee-vocabulary applies.

If future ἄφεσις occurrences in this "release" sense arise (Heb 9:22's ἄφεσις clearly IS forgiveness, but watch for potential dual-sense readings in the Acts sermons), apply per context with per-verse rationale.

## ἄφημι / ἀφίημι cognate — related but separate

The cognate verb ἀφίημι ("forgive / let go") has a separate glossary rendering. Thai Christian usage typically renders:
- ἀφίημι (sins) → ยกโทษ / อภัย (verbs, not nouns). Default: ยกโทษ when paired with ἁμαρτίας as object.
- ἀφίημι (send away, dismiss) → ปล่อย / ส่ง-ไป / ละ.
- ἀφίημι (abandon, leave) → ละทิ้ง.

This doc does not lock the verb — see `glossary.json` for the ἀφίημι entry and individual-verse rationales. But aligning the verb with the noun on the "sins" axis is recommended: ยกโทษ(บาป) noun, ยกโทษ(บาป) verb.

## Forward implications

### Acts (5 programmatic sermons)
- **Acts 2:38** (Peter Pentecost): εἰς ἄφεσιν τῶν ἁμαρτιῶν ὑμῶν → **เพื่อการยกโทษบาปของพวกท่าน**
- **Acts 5:31** (Sanhedrin): δοῦναι μετάνοιαν... καὶ ἄφεσιν ἁμαρτιῶν → **การกลับใจ...และการยกโทษบาป**
- **Acts 10:43** (Cornelius's house): ἄφεσιν ἁμαρτιῶν λαβεῖν → **ได้รับการยกโทษบาป**
- **Acts 13:38** (Paul at Antioch of Pisidia): ἄφεσις ἁμαρτιῶν καταγγέλλεται → **การยกโทษบาปได้รับการประกาศ**
- **Acts 26:18** (Paul before Agrippa): τοῦ λαβεῖν αὐτοὺς ἄφεσιν ἁμαρτιῶν → **เพื่อให้พวกเขาได้รับการยกโทษบาป**

### Pauline corpus
- **Rom 3:25** (πάρεσις, related but distinct — "passing over" prior sins): distinct rendering per verse; not the same word.
- **Eph 1:7 / Col 1:14**: τὴν ἄφεσιν τῶν παραπτωμάτων / ἁμαρτιῶν → **การยกโทษการละเมิด / บาป**.

### Hebrews
- **Heb 9:22, 10:18**: both ἄφεσις → **การยกโทษ**. Theologically critical — Christ's blood-of-the-new-covenant context makes the judicial-release sense load-bearing.

### General epistles
- Not heavily used; occasional references (e.g., Jas 5:15 ἀφεθήσεται verb, not the noun; 1 John 1:9 ἀφῇ aorist-subj verb; 1 John 2:12 ἀφέωνται perfect verb). Verb choices track the related-verb policy; noun remains การยกโทษ.

## Alternatives considered

- **การยกโทษ throughout (chosen).**
- **การอภัย throughout.** Rejected — softer/pastoral register loses the judicial-release precision for Pauline atonement and Hebrews.
- **Split: การยกโทษ for Peter/Paul sermons; การอภัย for Luke/Johannine soft-pastoral.** Rejected — introduces cross-author drift the Greek doesn't mark.
- **Transliterate (อาเฟซิส).** Rejected — foreign.

## When to revisit

- If a native-Thai-reader review finds การยกโทษ feels administrative-cold rather than theological-warm, especially in Benedictus (1:77) context, revisit. Current hypothesis: Thai Christian readers parse การยกโทษ as technical-correct; warmth comes from the verse's wider register (โดย- preposition + saving-context).
- Hebrews context (chapter 9-10) will re-test this lock. If the legal/sacrificial-payment framing clashes with การยกโทษ in that chapter, escalate.
