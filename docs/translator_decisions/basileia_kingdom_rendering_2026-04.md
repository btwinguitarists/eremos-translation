# βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ / τῶν οὐρανῶν — kingdom-rendering lock

**Scope:** The kingdom-of-God / kingdom-of-heaven word group across the Gospels + Acts + epistles. Two Greek forms carry different Synoptic conventions that the Thai corpus preserves rather than flattens.

**Decided:** 2026-04 during Luke end-of-book review, formalizing a pattern already in use across Mark, Matthew, and Luke. Related MAT end-of-book entry (see `docs/MAT_END_OF_BOOK_REVIEW_2026-04-19.md` §2) documented the Matthew-internal split; this doc corpus-locks it.

**Evidence base (all NT gospel occurrences verified):**
- Matthew: ~51× βασιλεία τῶν οὐρανῶν, 2× βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ (12:28, 21:31).
- Mark: βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ exclusively (never οὐρανῶν).
- Luke: βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ exclusively; 31 verified occurrences across Luke with 0 variance.

---

## The rule

| Greek | Thai | Corpus use |
|-------|------|-----------|
| ἡ βασιλεία τῶν οὐρανῶν | **อาณาจักรสวรรค์** | Matthew (Matthean reverential periphrasis) |
| ἡ βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ | **อาณาจักรของพระเจ้า** | Mark, Luke, Matthew (12:28, 21:31), Acts, Paul, balance of NT |
| ἡ βασιλεία (bare, no gen.) | Thai rendering tracks context | varies by referent |

The Greek variation is **faithfully preserved**, not flattened. Matthew's deliberate alternation (mostly οὐρανῶν, twice θεοῦ for rhetorical emphasis) is reflected in Thai.

## Why preserve rather than flatten

**Option A (chosen): preserve the Greek οὐρανῶν / θεοῦ distinction in Thai.**
Matthew's periphrasis "of the heavens" is a recognized Jewish scribal habit (divine-name circumlocution) with theological weight. When Matthew breaks his own habit to say "of God" (12:28: εἰ δὲ ἐν πνεύματι θεοῦ ἐγὼ ἐκβάλλω; 21:31: οἱ τελῶναι καὶ αἱ πόρναι προάγουσιν ὑμᾶς εἰς τὴν βασιλείαν τοῦ θεοῦ), the break itself is rhetorical. Flattening both forms to a single Thai rendering erases this.

**Option B (rejected): flatten both Greek forms to อาณาจักรของพระเจ้า.**
Rejected — Matthean scribes and Thai readers alike can register the periphrasis; the distinction is not so obscure that it requires removal. Flattening also prevents cross-referencing a Bible-reader's observation ("why does Matthew alone say 'heavens'?").

**Option C (rejected): flatten both to อาณาจักรสวรรค์.**
Rejected — misleading outside Matthew. Mark and Luke never use the "heavens" periphrasis; forcing it onto them would misrepresent source-text convention.

## Why อาณาจักรสวรรค์ (Matthew) and not อาณาจักรของสวรรค์

Matthean τῶν οὐρανῶν is a possessive-style Greek genitive that Thai can render either as a compound (อาณาจักรสวรรค์, "heaven-kingdom") or as a prepositional phrase (อาณาจักรของสวรรค์, "kingdom of heaven").

**อาณาจักรสวรรค์ (chosen):**
- Compact; matches Thai preference for noun compounds over prepositional periphrasis.
- Reads as a proper-noun-ish phrase, which suits Matthew's stock-formula character.
- THSV and NTV use this form; familiar to Thai readers.

## Forward implications

- **Acts** (7 occurrences: 1:3, 8:12, 14:22, 19:8, 20:25, 28:23, 28:31): all τοῦ θεοῦ → **อาณาจักรของพระเจ้า**. No "heavens" form.
- **Pauline corpus** (~14 occurrences): all τοῦ θεοῦ or bare βασιλεία → context-adapted, default อาณาจักรของพระเจ้า.
- **Revelation** (2 occurrences of βασιλεία referring to God's kingdom: 1:9 Jesus's kingdom; 11:15 τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν καὶ τοῦ Χριστοῦ αὐτοῦ; 12:10 ἡ βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ ἡμῶν): all → อาณาจักรของพระเจ้า or contextually adjusted.

## Non-kingdom uses of βασιλεία

When βασιλεία refers to:
- **Political kingdoms** (Luke 4:5 αἱ βασιλεῖαι τῆς οἰκουμένης → อาณาจักรทั้งหลายในแผ่นดินโลก): use plain อาณาจักร, no "of God" phrase.
- **Divided kingdom / kingdom against kingdom** (Luke 11:17–18; 21:10): plain อาณาจักร.
- **Royal rule** as contested political role (Luke 19:12, 19:15 parable of the minas): render as การเป็นกษัตริย์ or อำนาจการเป็นกษัตริย์ (explicitly "kingship" rather than "kingdom") per the verse-level key_decisions.
- **Satan's kingdom** (Luke 11:18 implicit): plain อาณาจักร — the Thai reader registers "his kingdom" as a foil to "God's kingdom" without needing a translational signal.

## Why not transliterate (บาซิเลียอา)

Rejected as aesthetically unworkable — Thai readers have no prior exposure to βασιλεία as a loanword, unlike คริสตจักร or พระคริสต์.

## When to revisit

- If a native-Thai-reader review flags อาณาจักรสวรรค์ as sounding more like a children's-book concept than a theological one, spot-check at MAT 5:3 (Beatitudes) and MAT 13 (parables). Likely no problem — the register is church-standard.
- If a rare idiom like βασιλεία τοῦ πατρός (Matt 13:43; 26:29) warrants distinct rendering, add verse-level note.
