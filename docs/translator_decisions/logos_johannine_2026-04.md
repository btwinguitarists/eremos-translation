# λόγος — Christological Logos (พระวาทะ) vs written-revelation Word (พระวจนะ) (LOCKED)

**Status:** **LOCKED 2026-04-30** at the John end-of-book audit (§2). **Documented 2026-05-04** following the Revelation external sanity-check that surfaced REV 19:13 was operating on this unwritten lock (ChatGPT + Gemini both verdict FINE; both recommended writing this doc).

**Scope:** The Christological-Logos title (the eternal-Word who is Christ) is rendered with a distinct, royal-prefixed Thai noun reserved exclusively for that Christological-Logos use — distinguishing it from the ordinary "word of God" (written/spoken revelation) which uses a different Thai noun.

---

## The locked rule

| Greek | Context | Thai | Verses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ὁ Λόγος** (titular, Christological — JHN Prologue) | Christ as the eternal-divine-Word | **พระวาทะ** (royal-prefix divine-Word) | JHN 1:1 (×3), 1:14 |
| **ὁ Λόγος τοῦ Θεοῦ** (titular, Christological — Revelation) | Christ as warrior-Word at second-coming | **พระวาทะของพระเจ้า** | REV 19:13 |
| **ὁ λόγος τοῦ Θεοῦ** (ordinary written-revelation / spoken-prophecy) | "The word of God" came to ... / endures forever / etc. | **พระวจนะของพระเจ้า** | JHN 10:35, 17:6, 17:14, 17:17; LUK 3:2, 5:1, 8:11, 8:21, 11:28; ACT *passim*; HEB 4:12; etc. |
| **ὁ λόγος** (Christ's own teaching, message, spoken-word — ordinary use) | "If anyone keeps my word" | **พระวจนะ** / **คำ** (context) | JHN 5:24, 8:31, 8:51, 14:23; etc. |

**Key distinction:** **พระวาทะ** is reserved EXCLUSIVELY for the Christological-titular Logos (the eternal-Word-as-divine-Person). All other λόγος uses — written revelation, spoken prophecy, Christ's own messages, ordinary "word" — use **พระวจนะ** or context-appropriate alternatives.

---

## §1 — JHN 1:1 — the Christological lock

`Ἐν ἀρχῇ ἦν ὁ λόγος, καὶ ὁ λόγος ἦν πρὸς τὸν θεόν, καὶ θεὸς ἦν ὁ λόγος`
TH: `ในปฐมกาล**พระวาทะ**ทรงดำรงอยู่ และ**พระวาทะ**ทรงอยู่กับพระเจ้า และ**พระวาทะ**ทรงเป็นพระเจ้า`

The royal-prefix **พระ** + **วาทะ** ("utterance / divine-speech") creates a Thai theological-noun that names Christ specifically — distinct from the ordinary noun **พระวจนะ** which the Eremos corpus uses for written/spoken-revelation. **พระวาทะ** in Eremos is a Christological-Personal-Name, not a synonym for "the message of God."

JHN 1:14 extends the same lock: **พระวาทะ**นั้นได้ทรงรับสภาพเป็นมนุษย์ ("the Word became flesh") — the eternal-Logos becomes incarnate.

---

## §2 — REV 19:13 inherits the JHN-lock

REV 19:13 is the only post-Johannine NT instance of ὁ Λόγος as a Christological title:

- GK: `καὶ περιβεβλημένος ἱμάτιον βεβαμμένον αἵματι, καὶ κέκληται τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ ὁ Λόγος τοῦ Θεοῦ`
- TH: `และพระองค์ทรงสวมเสื้อคลุมที่ชุ่มไปด้วยเลือด และพระองค์ทรงพระนามว่า "**พระวาทะของพระเจ้า**"`

The apocalyptic-warrior-Christ context (blood-stained robe, ISA 63 echo, sword-from-mouth at 19:15) does not change the Christological-titular nature of the Word. The same eternal-Logos who was "with God in the beginning" returns as warrior-King — and the Thai uses **พระวาทะของพระเจ้า** to preserve the cross-canon Christological equation.

External reviewers (ChatGPT + Gemini, 2026-05-04) both confirmed FINE: severing the Johannine-Logos / Revelation-Logos link would fragment the NT's strongest cross-book Christological cross-reference.

---

## §3 — Distinct from JHN 10:35 + 17:6/14/17 written-revelation usage

Within John itself, the Christological **พระวาทะ** lock holds even though John uses λόγος ~40 times. The ordinary-revelation uses get **พระวจนะ**:

- JHN 10:35: `οὓς ὁ λόγος τοῦ θεοῦ ἐγένετο` → `ผู้ที่**พระวจนะของพระเจ้า**มาถึงนั้น` (the word/scripture that came to the OT recipients)
- JHN 17:6: `τὸν λόγον σου τετήρηκαν` → `**พระวจนะ**ของพระองค์` (Father's word that the disciples have kept — written-revelation)
- JHN 17:14, 17:17: same pattern

The pattern: **พระวาทะ** = Christ-as-Person; **พระวจนะ** = revelation-as-content.

---

## §4 — Cross-corpus distinct from the ordinary-message use

Outside John + REV 19:13, λόγος in the NT is overwhelmingly the ordinary-message / written-revelation / Christ's-spoken-word use. None of these are Christological-titular and none use **พระวาทะ**. Examples:

- LUK 3:2 ("the word of God came to John") → **พระวจนะของพระเจ้า**
- ACT 4:31 ("speaking the word of God boldly") → **พระวจนะของพระเจ้า**
- HEB 4:12 ("the word of God is living and active") → **พระวจนะของพระเจ้า** (NOTE: HEB 4:12 is sometimes cited as Christological by some commentators, but the Eremos rendering follows the dominant scholarly position that HEB 4:12 is the spoken/written Word, not the Logos-Person; this matches BSB/ESV/NIV/CSB)
- 1 JN 1:1 ("περὶ τοῦ λόγου τῆς ζωῆς") → cross-link to 1 John end-of-book audit (TBD); likely Christological per 1 JN 1:1's whole-clause force

---

## Why this matters

- **Cross-canon Christology.** JHN 1:1 + REV 19:13 are the NT's two strongest "Word-as-divine-Person" texts. Allowing them to drift to **พระวจนะ** would silently equate Christ-the-Logos with mere "message" — a subtle but real Christological dilution.
- **Reader trust.** Thai Christian readers familiar with THSV / NTV know **พระวาทะ** as the JHN 1:1 Christological signature. Eremos preserves that recognition.
- **Future Hebrews / 1 John handling.** When Hebrews and 1 John ship, the same lock travels: HEB 4:12 = **พระวจนะ** (active-Word, not Logos-titular per dominant exegesis); 1 JN 1:1 = **พระวาทะ** (Logos-titular per Johannine pattern).

---

## Alternatives considered

- **Use พระวจนะ for both Christological + written-revelation** — rejected: collapses the Christological signature, makes JHN 1:1 + REV 19:13 read as "the message of God" rather than "the divine-Person who is the Word."
- **Use พระวาทะ for all λόγος** — rejected: imposes royal-Christological register on contexts where the Greek means "spoken statement" or "written revelation."
- **Translate ὁ Λόγος as a transliteration "โลโกส"** — rejected: opaque to Thai readers; **พระวาทะ** is the established Thai-Christian convention.
