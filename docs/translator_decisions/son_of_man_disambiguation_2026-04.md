# Son of Man — บุตรมนุษย์ vs. บุตรของมนุษย์

**Scope:** The Christological title ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου ("the Son of Man") vs. the generic-plural υἱοὶ τῶν ἀνθρώπων ("sons of men" = humanity).

**Decided:** 2026-04 during end-of-book retro review of Mark. Rationale first established at Mark 2:10 (first occurrence) and disambiguated at Mark 3:28.

**Evidence base:** Mark 2:10, 2:28, 8:31, 8:38, 9:9, 9:12, 9:31, 10:33, 10:45, 13:26, 14:21 (×2), 14:41, 14:62 (title — 14× in Mark). Mark 3:28 (generic plural — sole Markan occurrence).

---

## The rule

- **Christological title (Dan 7:13 echo, Jesus's self-designation):** ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου → **บุตรมนุษย์** (no "ของ").
- **Generic plural (humanity, sons of men):** τοῖς υἱοῖς τῶν ἀνθρώπων → **บุตรของมนุษย์** (with "ของ", plural context).

The preposition-inclusive "ของ" marks the generic plural; without "ของ" the form is a specific title.

Rationale quoted from Mark 3:28 key_decisions:

> Semitic idiom 'sons of men' = humanity (per uW). Thai 'บุตรของมนุษย์' (sons of humanity) LITERAL form preserved — IMPORTANT: distinct from 'บุตรมนุษย์' (no 'ของ'), the Christological title for Jesus (cf. 2:10, 2:28). The preposition-inclusive 'ของ' (of) marks the generic plural; without 'ของ' the form is a specific title. Disambiguates for Thai readers.

## Why this matters

Across the NT, both forms occur:

- Title: Mark 14× + synoptic parallels ≈ 80× across gospels + Acts 7:56 + Rev 1:13, 14:14.
- Generic plural: Eph 3:5, Heb 2:6 (quoting Ps 8:4), etc.

A Thai reader who encounters the same surface-form for both uses would have to infer from context which is meant. The orthographic distinction "บุตรมนุษย์" (no ของ, title) vs. "บุตรของมนุษย์" (with ของ, generic) preserves the Christological weight of the title without ambiguity. This is the Thai-Christian-convention rendering and matches the standard established Thai Christian usage.

## Grammatical note

- บุตรมนุษย์ — noun-noun compound without preposition. Thai reads it as a fixed title, analogous to "บุตรพระเจ้า" (Son of God). The compound form signals "this is a specific, titled referent."
- บุตรของมนุษย์ — noun + "ของ" + noun. Thai reads it as a possessive/descriptive phrase: "sons [who belong to / descend from] humans." Natural for generic reference.

## Hebrews 2:6 (future-book consideration)

Heb 2:6 quotes Ps 8:4 LXX: τί ἐστιν ἄνθρωπος ὅτι μιμνῄσκῃ αὐτοῦ, ἢ υἱὸς ἀνθρώπου ὅτι ἐπισκέπτῃ αὐτόν — "or the son of man that you care for him." This is grammatically singular but generic/anthropological in Ps 8. Christian tradition has read Heb 2:6–8 Christologically (applying to Jesus), and the author of Hebrews does this in v.9. The translation decision here is contested — some render as generic "บุตรของมนุษย์," some as Christological "บุตรมนุษย์," depending on how far one lets v.9's Christological application read back into v.6's citation. Document at Heb 2:6 translation time; do not pre-commit here.

## Alternatives considered

- **Always use บุตรมนุษย์ (title-form) for every occurrence**, with context disambiguating. Rejected: loses the distinction Mark 3:28 pointedly marks.
- **Always use บุตรของมนุษย์ (generic-form) for every occurrence**, letting the capital/contextual cues mark title-use. Rejected: Thai does not have capital letters; the title form needs orthographic marking.
- **Use a transliteration "บาร์-นาชา" or "ben-adam"** for the title in all occurrences. Rejected: too foreign; established Thai Christian tradition uses the translated compound บุตรมนุษย์.
- **Use คำเทียบ ("human-one" / "บุตรแห่งมนุษย์")** — a slightly more formal-sounding alternative. Rejected: the established Thai Christian pattern is บุตรมนุษย์ without แห่ง. Deviation from established usage would confuse Thai readers who already know the title from THSV / NTV.

## When to revisit

- At Heb 2:6 (see above).
- At Rev 1:13 and 14:14 — both references are to "one like a son of man" (ὅμοιον υἱὸν ἀνθρώπου) without the article. The anarthrous form technically leaves open generic-vs-title; John's use matches the Dan 7 figure though, so title-form ("บุตรมนุษย์") will likely be right, but document at those verses.
