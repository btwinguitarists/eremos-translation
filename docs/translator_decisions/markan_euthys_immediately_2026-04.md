# Markan εὐθύς ("immediately") — context-sensitive rendering

**Scope:** εὐθύς / εὐθέως, Mark's signature pacing adverb. Occurs 42× in Mark (more than all other NT books combined for this Markan-dominant word).

**Decided:** 2026-04 during end-of-book retro review of Mark. Individual-verse rationale decisions were made throughout the book starting at Mark 1:10.

**Evidence base:** Mark 1:10, 1:12, 1:18, 1:20, 1:28, 1:29, 1:30, 1:42, 1:43, 3:6, 4:5, 4:15, 4:16, 4:17, 4:29, 5:2, 5:29, 5:30, 5:42, 6:25, 6:27, 6:45, 6:50, 6:54, 7:25, 8:10, 9:15, 9:20, 9:24, 10:52, 11:2, 11:3, 14:43, 14:45, 14:72, 15:1.

---

## The rule

**No single locked Thai rendering.** Instead: context-sensitive choice from a small, consistent set:

| Thai rendering | When used |
|----------------|-----------|
| ทันที | Default for simple "immediately" after a verb (Mark 3:6, 10:52-like). |
| ในทันที | Slightly-intensified "in that moment" — for dramatic-rapid events (Mark 4:5, 4:16, 4:17). |
| ในทันใดนั้น | Emphatic "in that very instant" — for miracle / supernatural immediacy (Mark 1:10, 5:42, 8:10). |
| ทันใดนั้น | Mid-register "just then" — for narrative-beat transitions. |
| ทันทีที่ ... ก็ ... | Used when εὐθύς opens a temporal subordinate clause (Mark 5:2, 9:15). |
| พอ + time-word | When εὐθύς combines with a time-modifier (Mark 15:1 εὐθὺς πρωΐ → พอรุ่งเช้า). |

Every occurrence has been treated as a judgment call per Mark's pacing at that point. No rendering is "canonical"; all five forms above are in the glossary.

## Why not a single locked rendering

- **Mark's 42 occurrences are not all rhetorically equal.** Some mark genuine immediacy (1:10 — the Spirit descends at Jesus's baptism). Others are near-tic-like discourse-glue between narrative beats (1:21–28 cluster, 6:45–54 cluster). A single Thai word for all would either over-emphasize the ticcy ones or under-emphasize the miraculous ones.
- **Thai rhythm doesn't tolerate Markan-level repetition.** Greek readers accept the 42 εὐθύς hits as part of Mark's stylistic fingerprint. Thai narrative prose stutters if the same adverb appears every three sentences. Variation is required for naturalness.
- **Mark himself mixes εὐθύς with εὐθέως (1 occurrence only, 1 Cor 15:30 area doesn't apply in Mark; but Mark's other quick-succession adverbs include the slightly-different εὐθέως at Mark 1:10 variant reading and εὐθύς at the standard text).** The Markan-immediacy *semantic range* is wider than any single Thai adverb can carry.

## Alternatives considered

- **Single locked rendering "ทันที" for all 42 occurrences.** Rejected. Tested mentally across Mark 1:18–45 cluster and found unreadable — too stuttering. Also loses Mark's own pacing variation.
- **Drop εὐθύς entirely in non-dramatic contexts.** Rejected. Mark's Greek rhetoric is part of what is being translated; silently deleting his signature word violates the accuracy pillar.
- **Footnote each εὐθύς to mark "this is Markan immediacy.**" Rejected. Too intrusive and defeats the purpose of a smooth Thai read.

## Preservation of Mark's stylistic fingerprint

The 42-occurrence density is visible in the Thai translation *as a set* — a Thai reader working through Mark will encounter immediacy-markers constantly, matching the Greek experience. It's the *exact word* that cannot be locked, not the experience of pacing. Mark's fingerprint survives at the discourse level even though no single Thai word is used every time.

For future corpus-level comparison (Matthew, Luke, John all have different εὐθύς density):

- Matthew: ~18 uses of εὐθύς/εὐθέως
- Luke: ~6 uses of εὐθύς/εὐθέως
- John: ~3 uses of εὐθύς/εὐθέως

The Thai rendering in each gospel should follow the same contextual set defined here. Do not differentiate Matthean/Lukan εὐθύς from Markan by a different Thai convention — the word is the same, the density just differs.

## When to revisit

- If a Thai-speaking reviewer reads Mark aloud and finds the variation too scattered (i.e., reader cannot feel Mark's pacing), consider tightening to 2–3 renderings.
- If the glossary's automated key-term consistency checker flags εὐθύς as under-documented (it currently has a few entries — the check may surface more as chapters are re-audited), expand the glossary with every rendering variant we use.
- If a doctrinal/stylistic concern emerges (e.g., that "ในทันใดนั้น" feels too formal for casual narrative transitions), adjust the register split.
