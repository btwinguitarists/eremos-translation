# OT polytheistic-surface register — Hebrew-faithful rendering with monotheistic-trajectory paratext

**Scope:** OT verses where the Hebrew uses rhetorical-comparative or pluralistic-deity language (אֵלִים, "the gods"; "God of gods"; "above all gods"; the divine-council scenes) that, on its surface, presents YHWH as one being among others rather than as the sole God. This doc locks the Thai surface to preserve the Hebrew literary register, while explaining the monotheistic-trajectory hermeneutic in Layer-2 footnotes.

**Decided:** 2026-05-13 (Ben + tri-AI review of Exodus end-of-book audit, EXO 15:11 בָּאֵלִם). Tri-AI split 2-1 in favor of preserving Hebrew-faithful surface (ChatGPT + Claude separate session) over paraphrasing to monotheistic-default (Gemini). Decision sided with majority + Claude's analysis of the OT monotheizing-trajectory.

**Companion docs:** `divine_names_table_2026-05.md`, `spiritual_beings_hierarchy_2026-05.md` (if exists), `hebrew_idioms_and_metaphor_2026-05.md`.

## 1. The decision

OT verses with rhetorical-comparative ANE-style deity language → **render Hebrew-faithfully**. The surface Thai uses "พระทั้งหลาย" / "เทพ" (lowercase, plural deities) **NOT** "พระเจ้า" (which is reserved for YHWH and the personal God of Israel). Layer-2 footnote at the verse's first occurrence in each book explains the rhetorical-incomparable form for first-time readers.

### 1.1 Locked vocabulary

| Hebrew | Thai (locked) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| אֱלֹהִים (referring to YHWH) | พระเจ้า | Standard divine name (per `divine_names_table_2026-05.md`) |
| אֱלֹהִים (referring to foreign gods in plural / generic) | พระทั้งหลาย | Lowercase, plural |
| אֱלֹהֵי הָאֱלֹהִים ("God of gods") | พระเจ้าของพระทั้งหลาย | Hebrew genitive of comparison |
| בָּאֵלִם ("among the gods", rhetorical-comparative) | ในหมู่พระทั้งหลาย | Lowercase, plural |
| אֵלִים (plural of אֵל, divine beings) | เทพ / พระทั้งหลาย | Context-dependent; lowercase |
| אֱלֹהִים אֲחֵרִים ("other gods" — covenant-forbidden) | พระอื่น / เทพอื่น | Polemic context |

### 1.2 The principle

Preserving the Hebrew rhetorical surface preserves:

1. **The literary force.** Exod 15:11 ("Who is like You, O LORD, among the gods?") is the OT's first major rhetorical-comparative declaration of incomparability. Removing "among the gods" removes the rhetorical structure.
2. **The monotheizing trajectory.** The OT does NOT begin in fully-developed philosophical monotheism. It uses ANE polytheistic vocabulary to argue for YHWH's incomparable superiority, and through that argument, builds toward exclusive-monotheism (cf. Deut 4:35, "the LORD is God; there is no other besides Him"; Isa 44-46). Paraphrasing the early rhetorical forms erases the trajectory.
3. **The exegetical seam.** Pauline Christology (1 Cor 8:5-6, "there are many 'gods' and many 'lords'... yet for us there is one God the Father") + Ephesian principalities-and-powers theology (Eph 6:12) depends on the OT's prior willingness to name supernatural beings as "gods" without elevating them to YHWH's status.

### 1.3 What this doc does NOT permit

- Calling foreign deities **พระเจ้า** ("God"). That term is reserved for the personal God of Israel + Christian Trinitarian usage. Foreign deities = พระ / พระทั้งหลาย / เทพ.
- Sliding from rhetorical-incomparable language to ontological-affirming language. The Thai must preserve the Hebrew's rhetorical posture: YHWH is *incomparably* greater, which presupposes a (rhetorical) field-of-comparison without affirming the field's ontological reality.

These verses use the "Who is like X among the gods?" or "God of gods" rhetorical form. All should use the locked Thai surface.

### 2.2 The divine-council thread

Ontologically more loaded — these scenes depict YHWH presiding over a "council" or "assembly" of subordinate divine beings. Same Thai surface; Layer-2 footnote orients first-time readers to the divine-council motif.

| Verse | Notes |
|---|---|
| Job 1:6, 2:1 | "the sons of God" (בְּנֵי הָאֱלֹהִים) presenting themselves before YHWH |
| 1 Kings 22:19-23 | Micaiah's vision of YHWH + "all the host of heaven" |
| Ps 82 | YHWH judges among "the gods" — the *locus classicus* of the divine-council motif |
| Ps 89:5-7 | the assembly of "holy ones" / "sons of God" |
| Daniel 7:9-14 | the Ancient of Days + the heavenly court |
| Isaiah 6:1-8 | seraphim around the throne |

### 2.3 NT echoes (cross-corpus protection)

- **1 Cor 8:5-6** — "many 'gods' and many 'lords'... yet for us there is one God the Father" — Paul uses scare-quote rhetoric directly grounded in the OT register.
- **Eph 6:12** — principalities, powers, world-rulers — Paul's spiritual-beings ontology.
- **Col 1:16, 2:15** — thrones / dominions / principalities, the cross-disarms-them theology.

## 3. Layer-2 footnote template (per-book first-occurrence)

When the rhetorical-incomparable or divine-council form first appears in a book, the textual_variants file should carry a Layer-2 footnote like:

```
**"ในหมู่พระทั้งหลาย"** (בָּאֵלִם / כָּל־אֱלֹהִים) — รูปวรรณกรรมแบบกวีของฮีบรู
ที่ใช้คำถามเชิงพรรณา ‘ใครเหมือนพระองค์ในหมู่พระทั้งหลาย?’ เพื่อย้ำความเหนือกว่า
และไม่มีใครเทียบได้ของพระยาห์เวห์. รูปนี้ไม่ได้ยอมรับว่ามีพระอื่นที่เป็นพระเจ้า
จริงๆ แต่เป็นภาษากวีของฮีบรูที่ใช้ ‘เทพในจินตนาการของชาติรอบข้าง’ เป็น
จุดเปรียบเทียบเพื่อสรรเสริญ. ดู docs/translator_decisions/ot_polytheistic_register_2026-05.md
```

The footnote appears once per book (at the first occurrence), not at every recurrence — readers learn the convention then carry it.
