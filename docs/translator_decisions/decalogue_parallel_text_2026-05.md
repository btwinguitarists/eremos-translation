## 1. The principle

Where the **Hebrew of Exod 20 and Deut 5 is word-identical**, the Thai surface **must be identical**. The Decalogue is the OT's most-recognizable legal text and is recited verbatim in Jewish + Christian catechesis. Two Thai surfaces for one Hebrew prohibitive sever the cross-canonical thread.

Where the Hebrew differs (Sabbath rationale, "well-with-you" expansion, coveting reorder), the Thai **must preserve the Hebrew difference**. Genuine textual divergence is honored; surface drift on identical Hebrew is not.

### 3.1 The 8th-commandment lemma lock

The Hebrew גָּנַב appears identically in Exod 20:15 and Deut 5:19. The Thai lemma must be the same. Lock: **ลักทรัพย์** (rejecting ขโมย).

**Why ลักทรัพย์, not ขโมย?**
- ลักทรัพย์ is the **legal/judicial-register** verb in Thai — appropriate for a foundational legal text.
- ขโมย is the colloquial/everyday-register verb — appropriate for narrative theft contexts.
- The Decalogue is a foundational legal text and ships with the legal register.

For narrative theft contexts elsewhere in the corpus (Joseph cup-theft narrative, etc.), ขโมย remains the natural register.

### 3.2 The 5th-commandment "well-with-you" expansion

Deut 5:16 expands the 5th commandment with כַּאֲשֶׁר צִוְּךָ ("as the LORD your God commanded you") + וּלְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ ("and that it may go well with you"). **Eph 6:2–3 quotes the DEU expanded form**, not EXO.

The locked Thai surface for Deut 5:16 (and Eph 6:2–3 NT echo) uses **ยืนยาว** ("be long-extended"), not **ยาวนาน** ("be lengthy"). EXO 20:12 keeps **ยาวนาน** as the shorter-form Hebrew rendering. This is a **principled NT-quotation alignment**: the NT-corpus surface matches the DEU expanded form because Paul quotes DEU.

| Verse | Hebrew | Thai (locked) |
|---|---|---|
| Exod 20:12 | לְמַעַן יַאֲרִכוּן יָמֶיךָ | "เพื่อวันของเจ้าจะยาวนาน…" |
| Deut 5:16 | לְמַעַן יַאֲרִיכֻן יָמֶיךָ וּלְמַעַן יִיטַב לָךְ (DEU expansion) | "เพื่อวันของเจ้าจะยืนยาว และเพื่อจะเป็นการดีกับเจ้า…" |
| Eph 6:2–3 | (quotes DEU expanded form via LXX) | matches DEU "ยืนยาว" |

This is the **principled exception** to §1's identical-Hebrew-identical-Thai rule: the NT-quotation thread takes priority where Paul cites DEU's expanded form.

## 4. NT Decalogue citation cluster

The Decalogue is cited eight times in the NT. Each citation should use the locked OT Thai surface from §3 above, unless the NT citation context requires register-shift (rare).

| NT verse | OT-quoted commandment(s) | Locked Thai surface |
|---|---|---|
| Matt 5:21 | 6th (murder) | "อย่าฆ่าคน" |
| Matt 5:27 | 7th (adultery) | "อย่าล่วงประเวณี" |
| Matt 19:18–19 // Mark 10:19 // Luke 18:20 | 6th + 7th + 8th + 9th + 5th (rich young ruler) | identical to §3 forms |
| Rom 7:7 | 10th (covet) | "อย่าโลภ" |
| Rom 13:9 | 7th + 6th + 8th + 9th + 10th (compressed list) | each per §3 |
| Eph 6:2–3 | 5th (honor parents) — quotes DEU form | "ยืนยาว" (per §3.2) |
| Jas 2:11 | 6th + 7th paired | identical to §3 forms |

**HARD-FAIL check:** if any of the above NT verses ships with a Thai surface that diverges from the §3 lock (without principled register-shift justification documented in a Layer-2 footer), the `check_phrase_consistency.py` audit must flag it.
