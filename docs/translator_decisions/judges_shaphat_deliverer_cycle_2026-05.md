# שָׁפַט (shaphat) — 3-way principled split: deliverer / courtroom / divine-judge

**Scope:** Every occurrence of the Hebrew root שׁפט and its derivatives (verb שָׁפַט, agent-noun שֹׁפֵט / שֹׁפְטִים, abstract noun מִשְׁפָּט) across Judges, Samuel, Kings, Chronicles, Ruth, Psalms, and the Latter Prophets. Also governs the OT/NT bridge to κριτής / κρίνω.

**Decided:** 2026-05-20 (Ben, post-Judges-end-of-book audit). Locked **before** 1–2 Samuel ships further (the Saul/David royal-judgment narratives) and **before** any 1–2 Kings work begins (where מִשְׁפָּט and royal-governance vocabulary begin overlapping heavily — e.g., Solomon's prayer for `לֵב שֹׁמֵעַ לִשְׁפֹּט אֶת־עַמְּךָ` "a heart to govern/judge your people", 1 Kgs 3:9).

**Trigger:** Judges end-of-book audit §Z (ChatGPT-flagged forward-protection). The shipped Judges corpus already preserves a principled 3-way split, but the lock was implicit. Without this doc, the inevitable pressure during Samuel/Kings translation will be to either (a) collapse all senses into a single Thai surface (`ตัดสิน` is the easy default) or (b) drift into ad-hoc choices per verse. This doc forecloses both failure modes.

## The 3-way lock

| Hebrew form / context | Sense | Locked Thai surface | Anchor verses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **שָׁפַט אֶת־יִשְׂרָאֵל** (verb + Israel-object) — the "judge-deliverer" formula | Lead / govern / deliver / rule Israel — the whole **office** of the Judge (deliverer, charismatic leader, tribal administrator) | **วินิจฉัยอิสราเอล** (verb); **ผู้วินิจฉัย** (agent noun, the "judge" title) | JDG 2:16, 2:17, 2:18, 3:10, 4:4, 10:2, 10:3, 12:7, 12:8, 12:9, 12:11, 12:13, 12:14, 15:20, 16:31 |
| **לַמִּשְׁפָּט / לִשְׁפֹּט בֵּין** (courtroom adjudication) — "to render legal judgment between parties" | Legal-courtroom: hearing cases, deciding lawsuits, civil-judicial sense | **ตัดสิน / ตัดสินคดี** (verb); **คำพิพากษา / คดี** (noun) | JDG 4:5 (Deborah's palm), 1 SAM 8:5–6, 1 KGS 3:9, 3:28, PSA 72:1–4 |
| **שָׁפַט יְהוָה / הַשֹּׁפֵט** (YHWH as Subject; הַשֹּׁפֵט as divine title) — "YHWH the Judge" | Divine eschatological / cosmic judgment; God-as-Judge formula | **พิพากษา** (verb); **ผู้พิพากษา** (divine title, parallel to NT κριτής when applied to God / Christ) | JDG 11:27 (יִשְׁפֹּט יְהוָה הַשֹּׁפֵט "may YHWH the Judge judge"), GEN 18:25, PSA 7:11, PSA 50:6, PSA 75:7 |

## Why the 3-way split is required (not collapsible)

1. **The judge-deliverer sense is institutionally distinct.** When Othniel `judges Israel` (JDG 3:10), he is not adjudicating lawsuits — he is delivering Israel from Cushan-Rishathaim, then ruling the tribal confederation in the resulting peace. Calling that `ตัดสิน` ("adjudicate / decide a legal case") would render the entire book of Judges as a legal-history rather than a deliverer-history. `วินิจฉัย` ("decide / govern / lead") preserves the institutional sense the Hebrew author intended.

2. **The courtroom sense is a real shipped surface in JDG 4:5.** Deborah literally sits under a tree and Israelites come to her `לַמִּשְׁפָּט` — that IS legal-courtroom adjudication, embedded inside her broader deliverer-judge office. Two senses, same person, one verse-pair (JDG 4:4 deliverer-sense `וַתִּשְׁפְּטָה אֶת־יִשְׂרָאֵל` → `วินิจฉัย`; JDG 4:5 courtroom-sense `לַמִּשְׁפָּט` → `ตัดสินคดี`). The Hebrew makes the distinction; the Thai must preserve it.

3. **The divine-judge sense is theologically load-bearing.** JDG 11:27 is the OT's clearest "YHWH the Judge" formula outside Genesis 18:25 ("Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?"). Calling YHWH `ผู้วินิจฉัย` would demote him to the same office as Othniel and Samson; calling him `ผู้ตัดสิน` would shrink him to a legal-courtroom adjudicator. **`ผู้พิพากษา`** is the Thai title that bears the full cosmic/eschatological weight — and it bridges cleanly to the NT κριτής (Jas 4:12 εἷς ἐστιν ὁ νομοθέτης καὶ κριτής "there is one Lawgiver and Judge" → already shipped in James end-of-book audit as `ผู้พิพากษา`).

## Forward-protection — what 1–2 Samuel / 1–2 Kings work must honor

- **1 SAM 7:6, 7:15–17** — Samuel `judges Israel` (deliverer-sense) → `วินิจฉัยอิสราเอล`. Samuel's annual circuit (Bethel, Gilgal, Mizpah, Ramah) IS deliverer-office circuit, not courtroom-circuit, even though courtroom-adjudication happens inside it. Same handling as Deborah.
- **1 SAM 8:5–6, 8:20** — Israel asks for a king "to judge us like all the nations" — context blurs deliverer/king-justice, leaning toward governance-judgment. Default **วินิจฉัย** for `שָׁפְטֵנוּ`; the demand IS to replace the judge-deliverer system with monarchy.
- **1 KGS 3:9, 3:11, 3:28** — Solomon's wisdom-for-judgment prayer + the two-mothers verdict. These ARE courtroom: **ตัดสิน / ตัดสินคดี**. The split between Solomon-as-king (governance) and Solomon-as-judge (specific cases) maps to the 3-way lock cleanly.
- **PSA 96:13, PSA 98:9** — "YHWH comes to judge the earth" — divine-cosmic-judge: **พิพากษา**. Forward-protect for the Psalter.
- **MAL 3:5** — "I will draw near to you for judgment" — divine-judge: **พิพากษา**.
- **NT Acts 7:7** (Stephen's speech, quoting GEN 15:14 LXX `κρινῶ ἐγώ`) — divine-judge cross-corpus alignment: should already be **พิพากษา**; flag for verification when next NT pass.

## Edge cases + ambiguous senses

- **PSA 82** (the "gods" who judge unjustly) — divine-council judgment context; lean toward **พิพากษา** as the title (since the council-members are judging in YHWH's place) + flag for separate corpus doc when Psalter chapter ships.
- **שֹׁפְטִים** as a generic noun in legal contexts (e.g., DEU 16:18 "judges and officers you shall appoint in all your gates") — these ARE courtroom-judges, not deliverer-judges → **ผู้ตัดสิน** for the courtroom-noun. Distinct from JDG's ผู้วินิจฉัย (deliverer-noun).
- **JDG 11:27 has BOTH senses in one verse:** `יִשְׁפֹּט יְהוָה הַשֹּׁפֵט הַיּוֹם בֵּין בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל וּבֵין בְּנֵי עַמּוֹן` ("may YHWH the Judge judge today between the children of Israel and the children of Ammon"). The shipped Thai correctly resolves this as `ขอองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า ผู้พิพากษา ทรงตัดสินในวันนี้ระหว่างลูกหลานของอิสราเอลและลูกหลานของอัมโมน` — the divine-title gets **ผู้พิพากษา**, and the act of arbitration between two parties (courtroom-flavor) gets **ทรงตัดสิน**. This is the single hardest case in Judges, and the shipped surface gets it right. Lock this verse as the **anchor for hybrid contexts** where YHWH himself is the courtroom-judge between two human parties.
