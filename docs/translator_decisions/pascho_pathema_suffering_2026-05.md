# πάσχω / πάθημα — subject-driven royal/plain register split

**Decision date:** 2026-05-03 (formalized after 1 Peter end-of-book external AI review)
**Anchor passages:** 1 Pet 2:21 (Christ-subject paradigm), 1 Pet 4:13 (participation κοινωνεῖτε ... παθήμασιν)
**Status:** LOCK. Like the παρουσία split, this is a Thai grammatical requirement (ราชาศัพท์, royal-honorific register) when the verb takes a divine subject.

## The split

| Subject of πάσχω / πάθημα | Thai register | Rendering |
|---|---|---|
| **Christ** (πάσχω verb) | Royal (ราชาศัพท์) | **ทรงทนทุกข์** |
| **Believers** (πάσχω verb) | Plain | **ทนทุกข์** |
| **πάθημα** (process-noun, both subjects) | Neutral substantive | **การทนทุกข์** |
| **πάσχω substantive participle** ("the one who suffered") | Plain (paradigm-figure) | **ผู้ที่ได้ทนทุกข์** |
| **πάσχω aorist** (eschatological-temporary) | Plain + temporal modifier | **ทนทุกข์เพียงชั่วครู่** |

## Why this is grammatical, not theological

Thai distinguishes between ordinary speech and royal-divine speech (ราชาศัพท์) at the level of common verbs and nouns. The royal-prefix **ทรง-** is required when a divine subject performs an action; using the plain register for Christ in passion contexts would create an asymmetry that Thai readers feel as honorific failure, not as neutrality.

Greek πάσχω does not encode this distinction — it covers "suffer / experience" generically, regardless of subject. Thai cannot. The split is therefore not Eremos imposing a Christological emphasis; it is the language requiring the translator to commit to a register per occurrence.

The 4:13 case (`κοινωνεῖτε τοῖς τοῦ Χριστοῦ παθήμασιν` → `มีส่วนร่วม...การทนทุกข์ของพระคริสต์`) is the keystone: the believers' plain-register **ทนทุกข์** is *participation in* the royal-register **ทรงทนทุกข์**. The lemma-bridge is preserved by the noun **การทนทุกข์** (which serves both subjects without royal-prefix because the noun-form is a process-substantive, not a verbal predicate).

| 4:19 | οἱ πάσχοντες κατὰ τὸ θέλημα τοῦ θεοῦ | **ผู้ที่ทนทุกข์ตามพระประสงค์ของพระเจ้า** | believer |

| 5:9 | τὰ αὐτὰ τῶν παθημάτων ... ἐπιτελεῖσθαι | **กำลังทนทุกข์อย่างเดียวกัน** | believer-corporate |
| 5:10 | ὀλίγον παθόντας | **ทนทุกข์เพียงชั่วครู่** | believer-eschatological |

The cluster is the **NT-densest πάσχω + πάθημα concentration** (15× across 5 chapters). The register-split is uniform across all 15 occurrences.

## Cross-corpus application

This doc locks the rendering for all NT books not yet translated. Specific anticipated cases:

- **2 Peter 1:14** — Peter's own approaching death; not πάσχω (uses ἀπόθεσις τοῦ σκηνώματός μου).
- **2 Peter** generally lacks πάσχω/πάθημα cluster; the lock has minimal 2 Peter touchpoints.
- **Hebrews 2:10** `διὰ παθημάτων τελειῶσαι` (Christ-subject) → **การทนทุกข์ของพระองค์** (royal-prefix when verbal: **ทรงทนทุกข์**)
- **Hebrews 2:18** `ἐν ᾧ γὰρ πέπονθεν αὐτὸς πειρασθείς` (Christ-subject) → **ทรงทนทุกข์**
- **Hebrews 5:8** `ἔμαθεν ἀφ' ὧν ἔπαθεν τὴν ὑπακοήν` (Christ-subject) → **ทรงทนทุกข์**
- **Hebrews 9:26** `ἔδει αὐτὸν πολλάκις παθεῖν` (Christ-subject, hypothetical) → **ทรงทนทุกข์**
- **Hebrews 13:12** `ἔξω τῆς πύλης ἔπαθεν` (Christ-subject) → **ทรงทนทุกข์**
- **Romans 8:17** `συμπάσχομεν ἵνα καὶ συνδοξασθῶμεν` (believer-with-Christ) → **ทนทุกข์ร่วมกัน** (συν-prefix preserved; plain register)
- **2 Cor 1:5** `τὰ παθήματα τοῦ Χριστοῦ` → **การทนทุกข์ของพระคริสต์** (process-noun)
- **Phil 3:10** `κοινωνίαν παθημάτων αὐτοῦ` → **การมีส่วนร่วมในการทนทุกข์ของพระองค์** (process-noun)
- **Col 1:24** `ἐν τοῖς παθήμασιν ὑπὲρ ὑμῶν` → **ในการทนทุกข์เพื่อท่าน** (Pauline-paradigm; process-noun)

The Pauline-corpus already has these renderings at verse-level; this doc retroactively documents the principle.
