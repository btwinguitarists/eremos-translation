# τέλος νόμου / παιδαγωγὸς εἰς Χριστόν — paired Pauline salvation-history terminus

**Scope:** Two paired Pauline constructions that together form the project's salvation-history-of-the-Law reading:
1. **Rom 10:4** τέλος νόμου Χριστὸς → **พระคริสต์ทรงเป็นจุดสิ้นสุดของธรรมบัญญัติ** (terminal-end reading)
2. **Gal 3:24** ὁ νόμος παιδαγωγὸς ἡμῶν γέγονεν εἰς Χριστόν → **ธรรมบัญญัติจึงได้กลายมาเป็นผู้ดูแลควบคุมของเรา จนกว่าจะถึงพระคริสต์** (temporal "until Christ" reading)

**Decided:** 2026-05-02 during the Romans end-of-book audit. External AI review (Gemini + ChatGPT, 2026-05-02) both independently affirmed FINE on each verse and recommended a corpus doc covering both together to prevent future editors from decoupling them.

## Evidence base

### Rom 10:4 (terminal-end)

> Greek: τέλος γὰρ νόμου Χριστὸς εἰς δικαιοσύνην παντὶ τῷ πιστεύοντι.
> Thai: เพราะพระคริสต์ทรงเป็น**จุดสิ้นสุด**ของธรรมบัญญัติ สู่ความชอบธรรมแก่ทุกคนที่เชื่อ

τέλος carries two well-attested readings:
- (a) **Termination-end** — "Christ is the END of the Law as a means of righteousness." Reformation-Lutheran-Reformed default. BSB / ESV / NIV / CSB. Eremos rendering: **จุดสิ้นสุด** ("end-point").
- (b) **Goal-fulfillment** — "Christ is the GOAL/aim that the Law was pointing to." N.T. Wright, Dunn, New-Perspective-Pauline scholarship. Would be **เป้าหมาย** or **จุดมุ่งหมาย**.

Eremos chose (a) per RULES §0 evangelical-Protestant default.

### Gal 3:24 (temporal "until Christ")

> Greek: ὥστε ὁ νόμος παιδαγωγὸς ἡμῶν γέγονεν εἰς Χριστόν.
> Thai: ดังนั้น ธรรมบัญญัติจึงได้กลายมาเป็นผู้ดูแลควบคุมของเรา **จนกว่าจะถึงพระคริสต์**.

εἰς + accusative carries two readings:
- **Temporal** — "until [the time] Christ [arrives]." Salvation-historical-end reading. Eremos chose this.
- **Directional** — "to / leading to Christ." The pedagogue's job was to escort the child to a destination. THSV1971 used the directional reading.

Eremos chose **temporal** at Gal 3:24, which together with Rom 10:4's terminal-end forms a coherent salvation-history reading.

### External AI review affirmation (Gemini + ChatGPT, 2026-05-02)

- Gemini: "Rom 10:4 as **จุดสิ้นสุด** (termination) and Gal 3:24 as **จนกว่าจะถึงพระคริสต์** (temporal limit) operate together to form a highly coherent, traditional Reformed/evangelical salvation-historical timeline. Shifting to 'goal' (เป้าหมาย) would force the corpus into a New Perspective framework, violating RULES §0."
- ChatGPT: "**จุดสิ้นสุด** is defensible because Paul's immediate contrast is righteousness pursued through Torah versus righteousness received by faith. The temporal reading of Gal 3:24 coheres with this: the Law's custodial function reaches its endpoint with Christ."

## The rule

### Pauline salvation-history terminus

The Mosaic Law had a **definite temporal-functional terminus at Christ's coming**. This terminus is expressed by Paul in two paired constructions:

| Verse | Greek | Thai | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rom 10:4 | τέλος ... νόμου Χριστὸς | **พระคริสต์ทรงเป็นจุดสิ้นสุดของธรรมบัญญัติ** | TERMINAL — Christ ends Law-as-means-of-righteousness |
| Gal 3:24 | παιδαγωγὸς ... εἰς Χριστόν | **จนกว่าจะถึงพระคริสต์** | TEMPORAL — Law's custodial-function reaches its endpoint with Christ |

The two readings are **internally consistent**:
- Christ TERMINATES the Law's role-as-means-of-righteousness (Rom 10:4).
- The Law's custodial-disciplinary role reaches its TIME-LIMIT at Christ's coming (Gal 3:24).
- Together: Paul reads the Mosaic Law as a salvation-historical ECONOMY that had a definite end-point in Christ.

### Why this paired reading (and not the alternatives)

The alternatives form an alternative coherent pair:
- (a-alt) Rom 10:4 GOAL — "Christ is the GOAL that Law was pointing to" (continuing-fulfillment).
- (b-alt) Gal 3:24 DIRECTIONAL — "the Law leads us TO Christ" (continuing-pedagogical-pointer).

The alternative pair (goal + directional) reads Law-points-to-Christ-continuously — a NEW PERSPECTIVE-style continuing-fulfillment.

The Eremos lock (terminal + temporal) is principled, RULES §0 evangelical-Protestant aligned, BSB/ESV/NIV/CSB aligned, and Reformation-mainstream. Future editors should not decouple Rom 10:4 from Gal 3:24 — shifting one without the other creates an internally-incoherent reading.

## Forward implications

### Other Pauline τέλος / νόμος-end constructions

- 1 Tim 1:5 τέλος δὲ τῆς παραγγελίας ἀγάπη — "the goal/aim of the commandment is love" — here τέλος is **GOAL-sense** (not the same as Rom 10:4 terminal-sense), since the commandment is the great-love-command and the relationship between Law-and-love is one of fulfillment-not-termination. **Render as เป้าหมาย** at 1 Tim 1:5 — different lemma-context. Document at 1 Tim end-of-book.
- Rom 6:21–22 τὸ τέλος (the end-result, of sin → death; of holiness → eternal life) — "end-result" sense, **render as ผลสุดท้าย** or **ปลายทาง**. Different lemma-context.

### Other εἰς Χριστόν constructions

- Phil 1:23 εἰς τὸν Χριστὸν εἶναι — "to be with Christ" — purpose, not temporal. Different.
- Eph 4:13 εἰς ἄνδρα τέλειον (forward) — "to/until a mature man" — temporal/maturational. Apply temporal reading.
- 1 Cor 4:15 εἰς Χριστόν — varies; review at 1 Cor end-of-book.

### Hebrews 7-10 (forward)

Hebrews's "the law made nothing perfect" (7:19), "set aside" (7:18), "first covenant obsolete" (8:13) language harmonizes with the Rom 10:4 terminal reading. Apply the same theology when Hebrews ships.

## When to revisit

- If a native-Thai-reader review finds **จุดสิ้นสุด** at Rom 10:4 reads as harshly-supersessionist, consider softer **ปลายทาง** ("destination / end-point") or **ความสำเร็จ** ("fulfillment / completion") — but only if Ben endorses the theological shift.
- If a future Pauline scholar (esp. NT-Wright-aligned) reviewer convinces the project to shift toward the New-Perspective continuing-fulfillment reading, BOTH Rom 10:4 AND Gal 3:24 must shift together. Document the shift here.
- If 1 Tim 1:5 native-Thai-review prefers จุดสิ้นสุด (mistakenly extending this lock), explicitly distinguish — the 1 Tim 1:5 τέλος is goal-sense, NOT terminal.
