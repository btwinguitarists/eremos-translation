# νόμος → ธรรมบัญญัติ (Mosaic-Torah) / กฎ (Pauline-rhetorical principle)

**Scope:** All Pauline νόμος (Greek "law") occurrences. The lock applies a **two-rendering policy**: ธรรมบัญญัติ for Mosaic-Torah / Pentateuch / law-as-canonical-text; กฎ for Pauline-rhetorical νόμος-of-X principle / governing-pattern / inner-norm.

**Decided:** 2026-05-02 during the Romans end-of-book audit, after external AI review (Gemini + ChatGPT, 2026-05-02) both independently CONCERN'd that the GAL audit's single-rendering recommendation would render Paul's argument unreadable in Thai at Rom 7:21–8:2.

---

## Evidence base

**The decisive test case — Rom 7:25 (νόμος in two senses, same Greek sentence):**

> Greek: ἄρα οὖν αὐτὸς ἐγὼ τῷ μὲν νοῒ δουλεύω **νόμῳ θεοῦ**, τῇ δὲ σαρκὶ **νόμῳ ἁμαρτίας**.
> Thai: ฉะนั้น ข้าพเจ้าเองก็รับใช้**ธรรมบัญญัติของพระเจ้า**ด้วยจิต แต่รับใช้**กฎแห่งบาป**ด้วยเนื้อหนัง.

νόμῳ θεοῦ ("law of God" — Mosaic Torah affirmed even in struggle) vs νόμῳ ἁμαρτίας ("law of sin" — the inner-principle-of-corruption-in-the-flesh) require different Thai renderings to be intelligible. Single-rendering ธรรมบัญญัติ for both would imply Paul is contrasting two Mosaic laws (incoherent).

**The Rom 7:21–8:2 5-νόμος cluster (Pauline three-laws doctrine):**

| Verse | Greek | Thai | Sense |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rom 7:21 | τὸν νόμον | กฎนี้ | the principle being discovered |
| Rom 7:23a | ἕτερον νόμον | กฎอีกอันหนึ่ง | another principle (in the members) |
| Rom 7:23b | τῷ νόμῳ τοῦ νοός | กฎแห่งจิต | the law of the (renewed) mind |
| Rom 7:23c | τῷ νόμῳ τῆς ἁμαρτίας | กฎแห่งบาป | the law of sin |
| Rom 7:25a | νόμῳ θεοῦ | ธรรมบัญญัติของพระเจ้า | **MOSAIC TORAH** (affirmed even in struggle) |
| Rom 7:25b | νόμῳ ἁμαρτίας | กฎแห่งบาป | the law of sin |
| Rom 8:2a | ὁ νόμος τοῦ πνεύματος τῆς ζωῆς | กฎของพระวิญญาณแห่งชีวิต | the law of the Spirit of life |
| Rom 8:2b | τοῦ νόμου τῆς ἁμαρτίας καὶ τοῦ θανάτου | กฎแห่งบาปและความตาย | the law of sin and death |

**External AI review affirmation (Gemini + ChatGPT, 2026-05-02):**
- Gemini: "The two-rendering split is exegetically mandatory for Romans; flattening Rom 7:21–8:2 to a single term would render Paul's argument incomprehensible in Thai."
- ChatGPT: "The Romans split is exegetically right. Rom 7:25 is the decisive test case: νόμῳ θεοῦ and νόμῳ ἁμαρτίας occur side by side. Both AIs converge that Romans should set the corpus default."

## The rule

### Default rendering by sense

| Sense | Thai | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Mosaic Torah / law-code (Sinai-covenant) | **ธรรมบัญญัติ** | "the law" (definite, salvation-historical context); ὑπὸ νόμον; ἔργα νόμου; νόμος θεοῦ; "law of Moses" |
| Pentateuch / law-as-canonical-text | **ธรรมบัญญัติ** | "the law and the prophets"; "as the law says"; OT-Pentateuch citations |
| Inner-principle / governing-pattern | **กฎ** | νόμος-of-X-genitive constructions where X is NOT God-as-covenant-giver (νόμος ἁμαρτίας / θανάτου / πνεύματος τῆς ζωῆς / νοός / Χριστοῦ — the "law of Christ" being the pivotal case) |
| The principle-being-discovered (Rom 7:21) | **กฎ** | νόμος used adjectively/abstractly where context names a discovered-pattern, not a code |

### Critical: do NOT collapse to single-rendering

The GAL audit §3 recommendation to lock single-rendering ธรรมบัญญัติ across all senses is **explicitly reversed by this lock** based on the Rom 7:25 test case. The Pauline punning that single-rendering preserves (one Greek word, multiple senses) is outweighed by the loss of argument-readability when "law of sin" reads as "Mosaic Torah of sin" in Thai.

### Handling νόμος-of-Christ constructions

| Verse | Greek | Pre-revision Thai | Post-revision Thai |
|---|---|---|---|
| GAL 6:2 | τὸν νόμον τοῦ Χριστοῦ | ธรรมบัญญัติของพระคริสต์ | **กฎของพระคริสต์** ✓ revised 2026-05-02 |
| 1 Cor 9:21 (forward) | ἔννομος Χριστοῦ | (not yet translated) | **อยู่ภายใต้กฎของพระคริสต์** (or equivalent) |

The "law of Christ" in Pauline thought is the new-covenant cruciform-loving-pattern (cf. GAL 5:14, 6:2), NOT a re-imposition of Mosaic Torah. Rendering as ธรรมบัญญัติ ("Mosaic Torah") would imply a covenant-substitution reading; rendering as กฎ ("principle / governing-norm") preserves the Pauline POSITIVE-reframing.

### Exception 1: ambiguous mixed-sense uses

When a single νόμος occurrence covers Torah-as-text AND principle simultaneously (e.g., GAL 4:21b: νόμον οὐκ ἀκούετε — "do you not LISTEN to the law?" where "the law" = the Pentateuch as canonical-text being-heard), default to **ธρρμบัญญัติ** since the Pentateuch-as-canon sense is primary. The principle-sense is secondary in such uses.

### Exception 2: the 1 Cor 14:34 / 1 Cor 9:8–9 OT-citation pattern

When νόμος introduces an explicit Pentateuch quotation (e.g., 1 Cor 9:8 ἢ καὶ ὁ νόμος ταῦτα οὐ λέγει citing Deut 25:4), default to **ธρρμบัญญัติ** since the canonical-text sense dominates.

## Why two-rendering (and not single)

### Argument for the two-rendering split (chosen)

- **Argument-readability in Rom 7:21–8:2.** Rom 7:25 alone forces the split — the same verse contrasts νόμῳ θεοῦ and νόμῳ ἁμαρτίας; Thai readers must see different concepts.
- **Pauline POSITIVE-reframing of "law" preserved.** The "law of Christ" / "law of the Spirit of life" / "law of the mind" are NOT Mosaic-Torah; rendering them as กฎ preserves Paul's deliberate semantic-stretching of νόμος beyond the Sinai-covenant code.
- **External AI consensus.** Both Gemini and ChatGPT independently argued for this split in the Romans external review.
- **No glossary collision.** ธรรมบัญญัติ remains the ONLY rendering for Mosaic-Torah νόμος; กฎ does NOT collide with any other locked Greek lemma in the corpus (γνώμη, ἐντολή, etc. have their own renderings).

### Argument for single-rendering ธρρμบัญญัติ (rejected — was GAL audit recommendation)

- **Pauline punning preserved.** GAL 4:21 "under the law / do you not LISTEN to the law" preserves the Greek's deliberate semantic-stretching.
- **Why rejected.** Romans' rhetorical density (74 occurrences across 38 verses, esp. the 5-νόμος cluster at 7:21–8:2) makes the punning-preservation cost too high. Thai readers cannot disambiguate "law of sin" from "law of God" if both render as ธรรมบัญญัติ. The GAL preservation worked for GAL because GAL has fewer adjacent-multi-sense uses; Romans does not afford the same.

## Forward implications

### Rest of Romans (already locked)

All 74 νόμος occurrences in Romans already follow this two-rendering policy as of book-romans-v1 audit shipping (2026-05-02).

### Galatians retroactive revision

| Verse | Original | Revised |
|---|---|---|
| GAL 6:2 | ธρρμบัญญัติของพระคริสต์ | **กฎของพระคริสต์** ✓ (2026-05-02) |

Other GAL νόμος occurrences (~33 of 34) are Mosaic-Torah-sense and remain ธρρμบัญญัติ — no revision needed.

### 1 Corinthians

- 1 Cor 9:8–9 (Deut citation) — ธρρμบัญญัติ (Pentateuch-as-canon sense)
- 1 Cor 9:20 (ὑπὸ νόμον — "under the law") — ธρρμบัญญัติ (Mosaic Torah)
- 1 Cor 9:21 (ἔννομος Χριστοῦ) — **กฎของพระคριสต์** (principle / governing-norm)
- 1 Cor 14:34 (καθὼς καὶ ὁ νόμος λέγει) — ธρρμบัญญัติ (Pentateuch-as-canon)
- 1 Cor 15:56 (νόμος as power-of-sin context) — ธρρμบัญญัติ (Mosaic Torah)

### Hebrews

Hebrews 7:12, 7:16, 7:19, 7:28; 8:4, 8:10; 9:19, 9:22; 10:1, 10:8, 10:16, 10:28 contrast Mosaic-νόμος with new-covenant. All Mosaic-νόμος uses → ธρρμบัญญัติ. The "writing the law on hearts" (8:10, 10:16) is a **principle**-rendering candidate but the OT citation context (Jer 31:33 LXX) likely demands ธρρμบัญญัติ for OT-quotation faithfulness. Revisit at Hebrews end-of-book.

### James 2:8–12

The "royal law / law of liberty" cluster (νόμος βασιλικός / νόμος ἐλευθερίας) is the closest non-Pauline νόμος-of-X construction. Likely **กฎ** for the "law of liberty" (parallel to Pauline "law of Christ") + ธρρมบัญญัติ for the Decalogue-citation portions. Revisit at James end-of-book.

## When to revisit

- If a native-Thai-reader review finds **กฎ** reads as "rule / regulation" with insufficient theological weight in Pauline contexts, consider **หลัก** (principle) or **บทบัญญัติ** (statute) as alternatives.
- If a Greek-text scholar argues the Pauline three-laws cluster (Rom 7-8) is theologically a single-νόμος (Torah-itself-functioning-differently-in-different-contexts) rather than three principles, revisit the policy.
- If 1 Cor 9:21 ἔννομος Χριστοῦ ships and translation reviewers find กฎของพระคริสต์ awkward in the apostle-as-bondservant context, revisit.
