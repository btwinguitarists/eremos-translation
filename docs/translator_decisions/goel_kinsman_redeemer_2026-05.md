# גֹּאֵל (Goel) — Kinsman-Redeemer / Avenger-of-Blood

**Status:** LOCKED 2026-05-15
**Triggered by:** Numbers end-of-book audit, Decision 5 (protect Ruth)
**Cross-AI consensus:** ChatGPT, Gemini 2026-05-15
**Scope:** Pentateuch + Ruth + Job + Psalms + Prophets — any text where גָּאַל / גֹּאֵל appears

---

## 1. The semantic field

The Hebrew root גאל is a **single root** that fans out into three distinct ritual/legal/theological roles. **The Eremos Thai must preserve the single-root identity** — readers should be able to recognize that "kinsman-redeemer" (Ruth), "avenger of blood" (Num 35), and "God my Redeemer" (Job 19:25) all derive from the same conceptual core.

| Role | Hebrew context | Locked Thai |
|---|---|---|
| Kinsman-redeemer (property/levirate) | Lev 25; Ruth 2-4 | **ผู้ไถ่ที่เป็นญาติ** ("kinsman who redeems") |
| Avenger-of-blood (capital crime response) | Num 35; Deut 19; Josh 20 | **ผู้แก้แค้นเลือด** ("avenger of blood") |
| Divine Redeemer (theological) | Job 19:25; Isa 41:14, 43:14, 44:6; Ps 19:14 | **พระผู้ไถ่** ("divine Redeemer") |
| Verb גָּאַל "to redeem/buy back" | Lev 25:25 etc. | **ไถ่** ("redeem/buy back") |

## 2. Why one locked root matters

The most important biblical-theology connection in this word is:

> **Boaz as גֹּאֵל of Ruth (Ruth 3:9, 4:1-10) → Christ as our גֹּאֵל (Job 19:25; Isa 43:14)**

If Ruth's Boaz is rendered ผู้ไถ่ที่เป็นญาติ but Job 19:25's "my Redeemer" is rendered with a different term (e.g., พระผู้ช่วย "savior"), the typology breaks **at the Thai surface**.

**Locked rule:** The Thai for Boaz-as-goel in Ruth must include **ผู้ไถ่** as the root word, and Job 19:25's "my Redeemer" must also use **ผู้ไถ่** (specifically: **พระผู้ไถ่ของข้าพระองค์** — "my Redeemer" with the same morphology lock as the standalone Adonai vocative).

## 4. Forward protection — Ruth

Ruth uses goel **23 times** (Ruth 2:20; 3:9, 12, 13 (×2); 4:1, 3, 4 (×3), 6 (×3), 8, 14). The full sequence will use **ผู้ไถ่ที่เป็นญาติ** when introduced, then **ผู้ไถ่** when the relational context is established. The Boaz-as-goel passages MUST recognizably share the Thai root with **Lev 25 redemption** and **Job 19:25 / Isa 43 / Isa 53 Christ-typology**.

**Boaz's specific designation (Ruth 3:12):**
- Hebrew: גֹאֵל אַחֵר קָרוֹב מִמֶּנִּי "another goel closer than I"
- Locked Thai: **ผู้ไถ่ที่เป็นญาติอีกคนหนึ่งใกล้กว่าข้าพเจ้า**

## 5. Forward protection — Job 19:25

Job 19:25: וַאֲנִי יָדַעְתִּי גֹּאֲלִי חָי "But as for me, I know my Redeemer lives."
**Locked Thai:** **ส่วนข้าพระองค์ ข้าพระองค์รู้ว่าพระผู้ไถ่ของข้าพระองค์ทรงพระชนม์อยู่.**

The phrase **พระผู้ไถ่ของข้าพระองค์** is the **canonical Thai for divine-Redeemer-as-personal-Redeemer**. This will appear identically (or with morphology adjustment for context) in:
- Isa 41:14, 43:14, 44:6 (קְדוֹשׁ יִשְׂרָאֵל גֹּאֲלוֹ)
- Ps 19:14 (יְהוָה צוּרִי וְגֹאֲלִי)
- Isa 59:20 (וּבָא לְצִיּוֹן גּוֹאֵל)

## 6. Forward protection — Isaiah 53

Isa 53 does not use the word גֹּאֵל, but the theological substrate is the same. The Eremos Thai for Isa 53:5-6 should be **register-compatible** with the goel locks above so readers experience the cross-passage typology.

## 7. Edge cases / exceptions

| Hebrew | Context | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| גָּאַל nipfal "to be polluted" (Mal 1:7, 12; Isa 59:3; Lam 4:14) | DIFFERENT ROOT (גאל II). NOT the redeemer root. | **เป็นมลทิน** / context-specific. **Do not** render with ไถ่. |

## 8. Reviewer pathway

This is one of the most theologically-loaded translation locks. Native Thai theological reviewers may flag wording but the **root-identity invariant** (§2) is non-negotiable. Any proposed lock-change must demonstrate the typology still works across Ruth + Job 19 + Isaiah 43-53.
