# ψυχή vs. πνεῦμα — human anthropology distinction

**Scope:** The Greek words ψυχή ("soul") and πνεῦμα ("spirit") when referring to **human inner-personhood** — NOT when πνεῦμα refers to the Holy Spirit (separate glossary lock: πνεῦμα ἅγιον → พระวิญญาณบริสุทธิ์) or to unclean spirits (πνεῦμα ἀκάθαρτον → ผีโสโครก / วิญญาณชั่ว).

**Decided:** 2026-04 during Luke end-of-book review. Flagged by Gemini 2.5 Pro external review (2026-04-23) as an Acts-forward compounding risk; locked here before Acts/Pauline translation work deepens.

**Evidence base (Luke, pattern established):**
- Luke 1:46 (Magnificat): ἡ ψυχή μου → **จิตวิญญาณ** ของข้าพเจ้า
- Luke 1:47 (Magnificat, parallel to 1:46): τὸ πνεῦμά μου → **จิต** ของข้าพเจ้า — explicitly distinguished from v.46 per key_decisions rationale
- Luke 1:80 (standalone, of John's growth): πνεύματι → **จิตวิญญาณ** (character-formation sense, not Holy-Spirit)
- Luke 10:27 (Shema citation): τῇ ψυχῇ → **จิตวิญญาณ**
- Luke 12:19–20 (Rich Fool): ψυχή → **ชีวิต** (life-force sense, not inner-person)
- Luke 23:46 (Jesus at cross): τὸ πνεῦμά μου → **จิตวิญญาณ** (death-commendation)

## The rule

| Greek (anthropological sense) | Thai default | When |
|---|---|---|
| ψυχή | **จิตวิญญาณ** | Inner-person / whole-self / seat of emotion and will. Thai Christian standard; matches Shema Deut 6:5 tradition. |
| ψυχή | **ชีวิต** | "Life" (animating life-force: LUK 12:19–20 Rich Fool; LUK 9:24 parallel; often in cost-of-discipleship sayings). |
| πνεῦμα (in direct parallelism with ψυχή) | **จิต** | Inner-soul / inner-spirit — Thai distinguishes the two in Hebraic-parallel doublets (LUK 1:46–47). |
| πνεῦμα (standalone, anthropological) | **จิตวิญญาณ** | General inner-spirit / character-formation / death-commendation (LUK 1:80; 23:46). Thai practically overlaps with ψυχή here; that overlap is natural-Thai. |
| πνεῦμα (Holy Spirit) | **พระวิญญาณบริสุทธิ์** | LOCKED glossary-level. Not anthropological. |
| πνεῦμα (unclean spirit / demon) | **วิญญาณชั่ว** / **ผี** | LOCKED glossary-level. Not anthropological. |

### The non-negotiable axis

The Hebraic-parallel doublet pattern **ψυχή | πνεῦμα** (LUK 1:46–47; 1 Thess 5:23; Heb 4:12; likely Jas 2:26) **must be rendered with two distinct Thai words** when the parallelism is rhetorical. Flattening both to จิตวิญญาณ collapses the poetic-theological distinction.

- LUK 1:46: ψυχή → จิตวิญญาณ
- LUK 1:47: πνεῦμα → **จิต** ← distinct from v.46
- 1 Thess 5:23: πνεῦμα + ψυχή + σῶμα → **จิต + จิตวิญญาณ + กาย** (anticipated corpus lock; apply when 1 Thess ships)
- Heb 4:12: ψυχή + πνεῦμα division → **จิตวิญญาณ + จิต** (anticipated; apply when Hebrews ships)

## Why this split (and why it looks uneven)

**Argument for the split (chosen):**
- ψυχή in Thai Christian tradition maps most naturally to จิตวิญญาณ (the Shema convention, THSV/NTV standard). Keeping it stable there is Tier 1 corpus hygiene.
- πνεῦμα has a narrower anthropological sense that can tolerate a narrower Thai rendering (จิต) in contexts where parallelism demands distinction.
- When πνεῦμα stands alone *without* a ψυχή partner (LUK 1:80; 23:46), pressing จิต would sound clipped and unnatural — Thai naturalness pulls toward จิตวิญญาณ there. The Greek has no nearby ψυχή-pair to require differentiation, so no semantic loss.

**Argument against (considered and rejected): lock πνεῦμα → จิต universally in anthropology.**
- Would produce awkward Thai at LUK 1:80 ("ทารกเติบใหญ่เข้มแข็งขึ้นในจิต" reads as "strong in mind" — narrower than the Greek's character-formation scope).
- Would produce heavy Thai at LUK 23:46 ("ข้าพระองค์ขอฝากจิตของข้าพระองค์ไว้ในพระหัตถ์ของพระองค์" — less numinous than จิตวิญญาณ for a death-commendation).

**Argument against: lock ψυχή → จิต universally.**
- Breaks Thai Christian Shema tradition (LUK 10:27 and MAT 22:37 already use จิตวิญญาณ and cannot reasonably be changed).
- Would collapse into the Rich Fool's ชีวิต sense, losing the soul/life distinction Luke 12 already preserves.

**Conclusion:** keep ψυχή anchored to จิตวิญญาณ as default (with ชีวิต as documented contextual variant); keep πนεῦมา's anthropological rendering context-sensitive — จิต when in parallel with ψυχή, จิตวิญญาณ when standalone. The only firm rule is: **never render a parallelism's two halves with the same Thai word**.

## Forward implications

### Acts
Acts uses πνεῦμα heavily but almost exclusively in the **Holy Spirit** sense (locked rendering พระวิญญาณบริสุทธิ์). Rare anthropological uses:
- Acts 7:59 (Stephen, "receive my spirit"): τὸ πνεῦμά μου → **จิตวิญญาณ** of Stephen (death-commendation, parallel to Luke 23:46).
- Acts 17:16 (Paul's spirit was provoked): τὸ πνεῦμα αὐτοῦ → **จิต** of Paul (inner-agitation, narrow-sense).

### Pauline corpus (anticipated)
- **1 Thess 5:23**: πνεῦμα + ψυχή + σῶμα triad → **จิต + จิตวิญญาณ + กาย**. Thematic-trichotomy preserved.
- **1 Cor 2:11, 5:3, 5:5, 14:14**: Paul's anthropological πνεῦμα uses cluster. When in parallel with ψυχή (e.g., 1 Cor 15:44–46 ψυχικόν/πνευματικόν — adjective pair): **จิตวิญญาณ / จิต** or distinct descriptive forms per verse.
- **Rom 8:16**: "the Spirit [αὐτὸ τὸ πνεῦμα] witnesses to our spirit [τῷ πνεύματι ἡμῶν]" — Holy Spirit / human spirit contrast. Thai: **พระวิญญาณ** + **จิต** of ours. The Holy-Spirit lock handles the first; this doc handles the second.

### Hebrews
- **Heb 4:12**: "dividing soul and spirit" ψυχή + πνεῦμα → **จิตวิญญาณ + จิต** (preserve two-word distinction per the parallelism-rule above).
- **Heb 12:23**: πνεύμασι δικαίων τετελειωμένων → **จิตวิญญาณของคนชอบธรรม...** (standalone, no ψυχή partner).

### James
- **Jas 2:26**: σῶμα χωρὶς πνεύματος → **กายที่ไม่มีจิตวิญญาณ** (standalone).
- **Jas 4:5**: πρὸς φθόνον ἐπιποθεῖ τὸ πνεῦμα — ambiguous (Holy Spirit or human spirit? Paul-of-James question); consult translation-decision at the verse.

### Revelation
- **Rev 22:6**: ὁ θεὸς τῶν πνευμάτων τῶν προφητῶν → preserves human-spirits plural; Thai plural construction via เหล่าจิตวิญญาณ.

## Alternatives considered

- **Single Thai term for all anthropological ψυχή + πνεῦμα** (flatten per modern-Thai-Christian-informal usage). Rejected — breaks 1 Thess 5:23 and Heb 4:12 in advance.
- **Transliterate** (ψุ้ค์เช / ปンึวมา). Rejected — foreign, breaks naturalness.
- **Always-two-word compounds** (ψυχή → จิตวิญญาณ, πνεῦμα → วิญญาณ) to enforce contrast. Rejected — วิญญาณ alone in Thai often connotes "ghost/spirit of the dead," wrong register for human-anthropological πνεῦμα in living persons.
