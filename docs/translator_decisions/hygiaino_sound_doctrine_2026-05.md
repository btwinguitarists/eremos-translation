# ὑγιαίνω → ถูกต้อง (sound-doctrine metaphor)

**Scope:** ὑγιαίνω + ὑγιής family applied to teaching/words/doctrine in the Pastorals (the "sound-teaching" metaphor) — distinct from the literal medical-healing usage in the Gospels. Pastoral occurrences: 1 Tim 1:10; 6:3; 2 Tim 1:13; 4:3; Tit 1:9, 13; 2:1, 2.

**Decided:** 2026-05-03 during 2 Timothy end-of-book audit. Doc-creation gates `book-titus-v1` ship per the audit's "remaining items before tag" list and 1TI end-of-book §L #3 priority.

**Evidence base:** All 4 shipped Pastoral occurrences (1 Tim 1:10; 6:3; 2 Tim 1:13; 4:3) consistent at `ถูกต้อง` per de-facto corpus practice. 4 Titus occurrences pending (Tit 1:9, 13; 2:1, 2). Gospel occurrences (literal-healing sense) all use distinct renderings (`หายเป็นปกติ`, `แข็งแรง`, etc.) — cleanly separated semantic field.

---

## The rule

**ὑγιαίνω + ὑγιής + ὑγιαινούσῃ + cognates applied to teaching/words/doctrine → `ถูกต้อง`** uniformly across the Pastorals.

| Pastoral occurrence | Greek phrase | Thai |
|---------------------|--------------|------|
| 1 Tim 1:10 | τῇ ὑγιαινούσῃ διδασκαλίᾳ | คำสอนอัน**ถูกต้อง** |
| 1 Tim 6:3 | ὑγιαίνουσι λόγοις | ถ้อยคำอัน**ถูกต้อง** |
| 2 Tim 1:13 | ὑγιαινόντων λόγων | ถ้อยคำอัน**ถูกต้อง** |
| 2 Tim 4:3 | τῆς ὑγιαινούσης διδασκαλίας | คำสอนอัน**ถูกต้อง** |
| Tit 1:9 | τῇ διδαχῇ τῇ ὑγιαινούσῃ | คำสอนอัน**ถูกต้อง** (to apply) |
| Tit 1:13 | ὑγιαίνωσιν ἐν τῇ πίστει | (to apply — adverbial; render contextually with `ถูกต้อง` as core) |
| Tit 2:1 | τῇ ὑγιαινούσῃ διδασκαλίᾳ | คำสอนอัน**ถูกต้อง** (to apply) |
| Tit 2:2 | ὑγιαίνοντας τῇ πίστει | (to apply — predicate; render contextually with `ถูกต้อง`) |

The pattern: when ὑγιαίνω family modifies διδασκαλία / λόγοι / πίστις in a Pastoral context, render with `ถูกต้อง`.

---

## Why "ถูกต้อง" over a literal "healthy/sound" metaphor

The Pauline-Pastoral metaphor is **medical-soundness**: ὑγιαίνω = "be-healthy / be-without-disease." Applied figuratively to teaching, the sense is: doctrine that is **uncorrupted, free of distortion, structurally intact** — like a healthy body free of illness.

Three Thai options were considered:

1. **Literal medical metaphor preserved**: `คำสอนที่ถูกสุขลักษณะ` / `คำสอนที่แข็งแรง` ("hygienic" / "strong" doctrine). Rejected: imports a confusing register (Thai medical vocabulary doesn't transfer to doctrine naturally; "hygienic teaching" reads as comically incongruous in Thai religious register).

2. **Doctrinal-soundness adjective**: `คำสอนอันถูกต้อง` ("correct/right teaching"). Selected: captures the medical-soundness frame's *meaning* (uncorrupted, structurally sound) without the *vehicle* of the metaphor. Naturally Thai-readable. Pastoral-doctrinal register intact.

3. **Strong-conviction adjective**: `คำสอนที่ดีงาม` ("good/excellent teaching"). Considered. Rejected: too generic; loses the structural-soundness specificity (could mean any "good teaching" without the implicit contrast against "diseased/corrupted teaching").

Option 2 is the chosen rendering. The medical-metaphor's **target sense** (doctrine free of corruption) is preserved; the **vehicle** (medical-soundness imagery) is flattened because the vehicle does not transfer cleanly into Thai. This is consistent with the broader "metaphor-flattening principle" articulated in `pastoral_corpus_locks_2026-05.md` §J: when a Greek metaphor's vehicle fails in Thai but its target sense is recoverable, prefer target-faithfulness over vehicle-preservation.

## Distinction from medical-healing usage in the Gospels

In the Gospels, ὑγιαίνω + ὑγιής almost always refer to **literal physical healing** (Matt 12:13; Mark 5:34; Luke 5:31; 7:10; 15:27; John 5:6, 9, 14). These usages render naturally with `หายเป็นปกติ`, `แข็งแรง`, `หายดี` — context-dependent literal-healing vocabulary. **Do NOT carry "ถูกต้อง" into the Gospel passages**; the metaphor is Pauline-Pastoral, not synoptic.

| Synoptic occurrence | Sense | Thai |
|---------------------|-------|------|
| Matt 12:13 ἀπεκατεστάθη ὑγιὴς | physical healing | กลับเป็นปกติหายดี |
| Mark 5:34 ἴσθι ὑγιὴς ἀπὸ τῆς μάστιγός σου | physical healing | จงหายจากโรคของเจ้า |
| Luke 5:31 οἱ ὑγιαίνοντες | physically healthy persons | คนแข็งแรง |
| Luke 7:10 ὑγιαίνοντα | physical healing | หายเป็นปกติแล้ว |
| Luke 15:27 ὑγιαίνοντα | safely / unharmed | อย่างปลอดภัย |
| John 5:6, 9, 14 ὑγιὴς γενέσθαι | physical healing | หายเป็นปกติ |

The sound-teaching metaphor is **a Pastoral-Pauline coinage** that the Gospels do not use. The semantic-field separation is clean.

## Why this lemma needs a corpus doc

ὑγιαίνω applied to doctrine is **Pastoral-distinctive**:
- 8 of the 12 NT occurrences of the lemma in figurative-doctrinal sense are in the Pastorals (1 Tim 1:10, 6:3; 2 Tim 1:13, 4:3; Tit 1:9, 13; 2:1, 2)
- It is one of the **5 most-Pastoral-distinctive vocabulary items** identified in the 1 Tim end-of-book audit (alongside πιστὸς ὁ λόγος, εὐσέβεια, σωτήρ-Pastoral-shift, ἐπιφάνεια)
- Without a corpus doc, the de-facto rendering could drift across Titus's 4 forthcoming occurrences — which would re-create exactly the kind of intra-corpus inconsistency that triggered the ἐπιφάνεια revision

This doc locks the rendering before Titus ships.

## Forward applicability to Titus

When Titus ch.1 ships, apply:

- **Tit 1:9 τῇ διδαχῇ τῇ ὑγιαινούσῃ** → `คำสอนอันถูกต้อง` (matches 1 Tim 1:10 + 6:3 + 2 Tim 1:13 + 4:3 pattern verbatim)
- **Tit 1:13 ὑγιαίνωσιν ἐν τῇ πίστει** → "have a sound faith" — adverbial-construction; render with the noun core `ความถูกต้อง` or the adjectival modifier in apposition to `ความเชื่อ`. Contextual rendering at translation time, but the core lemma `ถูกต้อง` should be preserved.
- **Tit 2:1 τῇ ὑγιαινούσῃ διδασκαλίᾳ** → `คำสอนอันถูกต้อง` (verbatim parallel to 1 Tim 1:10)
- **Tit 2:2 ὑγιαίνοντας τῇ πίστει** → predicate-adjective construction; render the core lemma as `ถูกต้อง` modifying `ความเชื่อ` (the elder-men instruction)

## When to revisit

- If a Thai-speaking NT scholar argues that `ถูกต้อง` is too neutral and loses the specifically-medical force the metaphor depends on (e.g., the implicit contrast with "diseased doctrine" in 2 Tim 2:17 γάγγραινα — gangrene), reconsider. The corpus-pattern of pairing ὑγιαίνω-doctrine with disease-imagery elsewhere in the Pastorals could justify a more medical-explicit rendering.
- If a future Pauline-corpus-distinctive-doctrine doc emerges that wants to unify "sound-teaching" across Pastorals + Hebrews-pseudo-Pauline (HEB 5:11-14 milk/solid-food doctrine maturity), the rendering may need extension. Currently HEB 5 doesn't use ὑγιαίνω, so no overlap.
