**Scope:** Revelation's six interlocking divine-self-titles establishing the divine-eternity / divine-ultimacy axis. All applied to **BOTH Father and Son** at climactic moments — the strongest Christological claims in the NT corpus.

## §1 — Two-fold form at 11:17 + 16:5 deliberately drops "coming"

Both verses are post-final-trumpet (11:15ff) / post-bowls (16:1ff) — the eschaton has arrived. The Greek deliberately omits ὁ ἐρχόμενος ("the coming one") because the future-coming has broken in. The Thai preserves this shift explicitly: **ผู้ทรงดำรงอยู่และผู้ทรงเคยดำรงอยู่** — no "ผู้ที่จะเสด็จมา" appended.

This is **not drift** — it is principled tracking of the Greek's two-fold-form / three-fold-form alternation.

---

## §2 — Christ assumes the Father's titles (the Christological equation)

| Verse | Title | Speaker | Force |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1:17 | πρῶτος-ἔσχατος | Christ (post-resurrection vision) | Originally a YHWH-title (ISA 41:4 LXX); applied to Christ |
| 2:8 | πρῶτος-ἔσχατος | Christ (Smyrna letter) | Confirmed Christ-claim |
| 22:13 | Ἄλφα/Ὦ + πρῶτος/ἔσχατος + ἀρχή/τέλος | Christ | Climactic triple-self-declaration |

The Thai uses the **same forms** whether the speaker is Father or Son — preserving the theological equation (Christ shares the Father's divine-eternity and divine-ultimacy).

---

## §3 — REV 22:13 climactic triple-declaration (synonym-pair distinction)

REV 22:13 deploys all three pairs in succession:

- GK: `ἐγὼ τὸ Ἄλφα καὶ τὸ Ὦ, ὁ πρῶτος καὶ ὁ ἔσχατος, ἡ ἀρχὴ καὶ τὸ τέλος`
- TH: `เราคือ**อัลฟาและโอเมกา** เป็น**เบื้องต้นและเบื้องปลาย** เป็น**ปฐมและอวสาน**`

The Thai uses **two distinct synonym-pairs** (เบื้องต้น/เบื้องปลาย vs ปฐม/อวสาน) — preserving the lexical-precision that πρῶτος-ἔσχατος and ἀρχή-τέλος are two different ways of saying the same divine-ultimacy claim, while preventing monotonous repetition that would dull the climactic force.

External reviewers (ChatGPT + Gemini, 2026-05-04) both verdicted this as FINE — defensible because the Greek itself uses three lexically-distinct pairings to create the cumulative effect of ultimacy; the Thai mirrors that strategy.

---

## Why this matters

- **Theological load.** These six titles are Revelation's strongest Christological claims; any drift between Father-rendering and Son-rendering would silently dilute the equation.
- **Cross-book inheritance.** ISA 41:4 LXX πρῶτος-ἔσχατος → เบื้องต้นและเบื้องปลาย is a YHWH-title; Revelation's application to Christ is one of the strongest NT divine-Christology evidences.
- **Eschatological signaling.** The two-fold / three-fold shift at 11:17 + 16:5 is structurally meaningful — preserving it prevents the Thai from reading as if Christ's "coming" were still future at the moment of the seventh trumpet / final bowl.

---

## Alternatives considered

- **Single Thai pair at 22:13** (e.g., เบื้องต้น/เบื้องปลาย repeated) — rejected: collapses the Greek's deliberate lexical-distinction between πρῶτος-ἔσχατος and ἀρχή-τέλος.
- **Translate Ἄλφα/Ὦ as "ตัวต้น/ตัวสุดท้าย" rather than transliteration** — rejected: loses the iconic Greek-letter signaling and breaks reader-recognition for cross-checking with English/Thai standard versions.
- **Restore "coming" at 11:17 + 16:5** — rejected: violates the Greek's deliberate two-fold form (post-eschaton context).
