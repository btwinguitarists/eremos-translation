**Scope:** The שַׂר־צְבָא־יְהוָה ("commander of the army of YHWH") figure in Joshua 5:13–15 and any downstream identification with that figure (Daniel 8:11, 8:25, 10:13, 10:21, 12:1 — שַׂר־הַצָּבָא + שַׂר־צְבָא־הַמְּרוֹמִים; Zechariah 14:5 — קְדֹשִׁים עִמָּךְ).

**Decided:** 2026-05-18 (Ben, post-Joshua-end-of-book audit). Locked **before** any Daniel or Zechariah chapter ships.

**Trigger:** Joshua end-of-book audit §3 + 3-way external AI review (Grok / Gemini / ChatGPT) — all three independently returned **FINE** on the shipped JOS 5:13–15 rendering and asked for an explicit doc so the convention does not drift in Former Prophets / Daniel.

The two verses use slightly different Hebrew (singular vs. plural shoe; אַדְמַת־קֹדֶשׁ "ground of holiness" vs. קֹדֶשׁ "holiness"), but the *theological lock* is the formal echo: when the Thai reader meets JOS 5:15, the EXO 3:5 phrasing must be obviously the same scene-type. **The Thai must preserve the verbal echo to the precision the Hebrew allows.** When EXO 3 ships, the parallel JOS 5:15 surface is the anchor; any divergence is flagged.

## Why ambiguity-preserved, not Christophany-explicit

The figure in JOS 5:13–15 has three classical interpretations:

1. **Christophany** (pre-incarnate Christ) — supported by: he accepts worship (v.14 וַיִּשְׁתָּחוּ; he does not refuse the way the angel of Rev 19:10, 22:9 does); the holy-ground formula matches the YHWH-theophany at the burning bush; possibly identified with the מַלְאַךְ־יְהוָה of Exo 23:20–23.
2. **Angelic commander** (high angel, perhaps the same figure as Dan 10:13/10:21/12:1's שַׂר־הַצָּבָא) — supported by: the title שַׂר is used of high angels in Daniel; some traditions identify him with Michael.
3. **Theophany proper** — the figure speaks as YHWH in v.15's holy-ground formula but is distinguished from YHWH by the שַׂר־צְבָא־יְהוָה construct (the genitive marks him as belonging to YHWH, not being YHWH).

The shipped Eremos rendering — **ผู้บัญชาการกองทัพขององค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า** — is the literal Hebrew surface: it preserves the genitive construct (commander *of* the army *of* YHWH) without overriding into pre-incarnate-Son or angel terminology.

**This is the right level of specificity** because:

- Hebrew does not say בֵּן (son), מָשִׁיחַ (Messiah), or מַלְאַךְ (angel/messenger) here; it says שַׂר (commander/prince) + צְבָא (host/army) + YHWH. Forcing the Thai into any of those would *over*translate.
- Three external AI reviewers (Grok / Gemini / ChatGPT) independently chose **FINE** and recommended locking as-is.
- Future Daniel and Zechariah occurrences must read the same way so a Thai reader can recognize the figure as recurring. A heavy-handed Christophany surface in JOS would either need to be matched in Dan/Zech (creating a doctrinal over-commitment in books where the figure is more obscure) or break — both options worse than the literal genitive.

Interpretive discussion (Christophany-vs-angel) belongs in **notes** + **textual_variants** footnotes, NOT in the main Thai surface text.

## Boundary — distinguished from `malak_yhwh_2026-05.md`

- **מַלְאַךְ־יְהוָה (the angel of YHWH)** — covered by `malak_yhwh_2026-05.md`. Distinct grammatical construct (מַלְאַךְ ≠ שַׂר); distinct theological tradition. The two figures *may* be identical in some interpretations (Justin Martyr, Calvin) but the Hebrew surfaces are different and must not be conflated in the Thai.
- **Christological NT typology** — Heb 4:8 (Joshua/Jesus typological pun), 1 Cor 10:4 (the spiritual Rock following Israel), Rev 19:11–16 (the rider on the white horse with armies of heaven) all gesture at this figure, but those are NT interpretive moves and belong in the *destination* book's footnotes, not the Joshua surface.
