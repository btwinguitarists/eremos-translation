# Job's mediator / witness / Redeemer thread — the register asymmetry

**Scope:** The four-point thread in Job where the sufferer reaches for a third party between himself and God — the **arbiter** (9:33), the **witness/guarantor** (16:19), the **Redeemer** (19:25), and the **mediating angel** (33:23) — and why Eremos renders three of them in **plain** register but 19:25 in **royal/divine** register.

## Why the asymmetry is correct (not drift)

The three plain-register figures are **hypothesized or absent**:

- **9:33** explicitly laments the *absence* of an arbiter ("there is **no** umpire between us"). Applying divine register to a figure Job says does not exist would violently over-read the Hebrew and break the logic of the complaint.
- **16:19** is a witness/guarantor *in heaven* whose **divine identity is left open** in the immediate text. Job affirms the witness exists but does not (yet) confess him as God; plain register preserves that restraint.
- **33:23** is **Elihu's** hypothetical ("**if** there be an angel … a mediator"), a contingent figure in a thousand, not a confessed divine person. (Note: 33:24's ทรงเมตตา / ตรัส is correctly royal — there the subject is **God** responding, not the angel.)

**19:25 is different in kind.** גֹּאֲלִי חָי is Job's **positive confession** of a living heavenly vindicator — the book's faith-apex. Royal/divine register (พระผู้ไถ่, ทรงพระชนม์, พระองค์, ทรงยืนขึ้น) correctly captures that confession and aligns with the locked `goel_kinsman_redeemer_2026-05.md` rendering of גֹּאֵל as **พระผู้ไถ่ของข้าพระองค์**.

## The principle (for forward protection)

> **Hypothesized / absent / identity-open third-party → plain register. Confessed divine vindicator → royal register.**

This is the same hermeneutic restraint applied at Daniel 7:13 vs 10:5–6 (see `son_of_man_disambiguation_2026-04.md`): mark the divine register only where the text grants the figure explicit divine identity or dominion; otherwise preserve the interpretive ambiguity the Hebrew leaves intact. Translators reaching the Servant Songs (Isa 42, 49, 50, 52–53) and other mediator/intercessor figures inherit this principle.

**No verse edits required** — all four verses are already shipped with the register documented here.
