# Speech verbs when adversaries address divine Jesus — ทูล convention

**Scope:** Thai narrator-voice speech verbs (ทูล / กล่าว / พูด / ร้อง / ร้องตะโกน) used to introduce what a speaker says, when the speaker is an **adversary** (Satan, tempters, hostile Sanhedrin, Pilate, mockers) and the addressee is **divine Jesus**. Distinct from the speaker's own character-voice register (see `narrator_vs_character_voice_2026-04.md` and `vocative_kyrie_didaskale_register_2026-04.md`).

**Decided:** 2026-04 during Luke end-of-book review. Flagged in Gemini 2.5 Pro external review (2026-04-23); locked here as a corpus convention after confirming it is already a documented per-verse choice in both Matthew and Luke.

**Evidence base:**
- MAT 4:3, 4:6, 4:9 (temptation dialogue, already shipped in `book-matthew-v1`)
- LUK 4:3, 4:6, 4:9 (temptation dialogue)
- LUK 4:34, 4:41 (demons screaming while possessed)

---

## The rule

**When the narrator introduces the speech of an adversary addressing divine Jesus, the speech verb tracks the *mode* of speech, not the speaker's stance:**

| Speech mode | Thai verb | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Controlled dialogue (argument, temptation, question, challenge) directed AT a divine addressee | **ทูลพระองค์ว่า** | The devil at temptation; Pilate at trial; Sanhedrin questioning Jesus; Judas's betrayal-kiss speech |
| Uncontrolled exclamation / scream / shriek (demonic outburst, crowd frenzy, mob-cry) | **ร้องว่า / ร้องตะโกนว่า / ร้องเสียงดังว่า** | Demon-possessed speech; mob cries ("Crucify him"); spontaneous shrieks |
| Quiet mockery / sneer / whispered remark | **กล่าว / พูด** (plain) | Scribes murmuring; crucifixion-scene mockers |

## Why ทูล for adversarial dialogue

Thai ทูล is **addressee-marking**, not speaker-marking. Historically and in modern Thai Christian usage, ทูล signals that the *addressee* is royal/divine; it does not require the *speaker* to be humble or submissive. Thai narrative convention uses ทูล universally when someone speaks TO a royal or divine figure — regardless of whether the speaker is a petitioner, an adversary, a servant, or a king of another realm addressing this one.

**Gemini 2.5 Pro external-review concern (2026-04-23):** using ทูล for Satan at LUK 4:3/6/9 "grants the Devil a posture of legitimate, reverent submission." This reading weights ทูล as speaker-humility.

**Counter (what we preserve):** Thai Christian translation convention weights ทูล as addressee-honor. In both THSV and NTV, ทูล is routinely used for adversaries addressing Jesus or God. Changing to plain กล่าว would semantically *downshift the addressee's divine status* — a bigger theological cost than the tonal ambiguity Gemini flagged.

**Resolution:** The narrator's speech verb keeps addressee-honor (ทูล) visible. The *speaker's* own stance (hostile, mock-respectful, arrogant) is expressed inside the quoted speech via pronouns (ท่าน not พระองค์; เรา for self — both at LUK 4:3, 4:6, 4:9). Two axes stay distinct:

- **Outer axis (narrator's verb)** — is the addressee divine? → ทูล yes, neutral no.
- **Inner axis (speaker's pronouns in the quote)** — what is the speaker's stance? → Mock/hostile speakers never use พระองค์, always use ท่าน.

This split is what Thai readers actually parse. Flattening either axis would mislead.

## When ทูล does NOT apply even with divine addressee

- **Screaming / shrieking** (LUK 4:34 demon crying out; LUK 4:41 demons screaming) → ร้องว่า / ร้องตะโกนว่า. The mode is involuntary, not measured; ทูล would misrepresent as composed speech.
- **Crowd mockery** (LUK 23:35–37 mockers; LUK 23:39 thief-railing) → plain กล่าว / พูด, since the address is often third-person-reference not direct-to-Jesus, and the speech-register is mock-derisive, not formally-addressed.
- **Judas's traitor-kiss** (MAT 26:49 "ท่านอาจารย์!" — see vocative doc) → the speech verb may still be ทูล but the vocative choice inside the speech (ท่านอาจารย์ not องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า) marks Judas's register-flatness.

## Forward implications

### Acts
- **Sorcerers / hostile authorities addressing apostles or the risen Christ**: Paul before Agrippa (Acts 26), Felix (Acts 24), Festus (Acts 25). Apostles are not divine, so the standard human-register speech verbs apply (กล่าว / พูด / ตอบ). Only adversaries addressing the *risen Christ in vision* (none in Acts) would get ทูล.
- **Simon Magus to Peter** (Acts 8:19): apostle is not divine; use กล่าว. But if Simon had been addressing Christ directly, ทูล would apply.
- **Demon-possessed slave girl** (Acts 16:17 — ironically orthodox confession from a demonic source): uses ἔκραζεν / κράζουσα "she was crying out" — speech verb should be ร้องตะโกนว่า (mode: shriek).

### Pauline corpus
Mostly epistolary; narrator-voice speech verbs rarely occur. Exception: Pastoral Epistles' descriptive passages (1 Tim 1:20 handing over Hymenaeus; 2 Tim 2:17–18 Hymenaeus and Philetus; 2 Tim 4:14 Alexander). These are about adversaries, but they aren't addressing divine Jesus directly — standard human-register speech verbs.

### Revelation
Many adversary-to-divine dialogues (the beast; Babylon personified; the dragon speaking). Apply this rule's default: **ทูล when an adversary addresses a divine figure in measured speech; ร้อง when screaming; plain กล่าว when mocking from a distance**.

## Alternatives considered

- **Always use plain กล่าว / พูด when the speaker is adversarial** (Gemini's implicit recommendation). Rejected — flattens addressee-divinity across the corpus; would require changes at MAT 4:3/6/9, MAT 26:63 (Caiaphas), MAT 27:11 (Pilate), MRK 14:61, LUK 4:3/6/9, LUK 20:21, LUK 22:67–70, LUK 23:3, and forward. Would cascade into JHN's trial scenes and beyond. Enormous cost for a single-axis clarity gain.
- **Use ทูล always regardless of speech mode.** Rejected — screaming demons (LUK 4:34, 4:41) cannot be ทูล'd without misrepresenting involuntary outcry as measured speech.
- **Invent a new Thai verb for "adversarial address to divine".** Rejected — no such verb exists in Thai; would be revisionist and fail the naturalness pillar (RULES §1).

## When to revisit

- If a native-Thai-reader review finds Satan's ทูล at LUK 4:3/6/9 (or MAT 4:3/6/9) genuinely hears as "reverent submission" that misreads the scene, revisit. Current working assumption: standard Thai Christian readers parse ทูล as addressee-marking, not speaker-marking.
- If the Acts apocalyptic confrontations (Stephen at 7; Paul on Damascus road at 9:4 — but Saul is not yet adversary-to-divine in that moment — he's persecutor-who-hears-voice) force a refinement.
