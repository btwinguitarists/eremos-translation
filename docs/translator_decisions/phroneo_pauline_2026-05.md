# φρονέω — semantic-branch split, not single-gloss

**Decision date:** 2026-05-02 (formalized after PHP end-of-book external AI review)
**Anchor book:** Philippians (10 occurrences — the Pauline-signature usage)
**Forward applicability:** Romans 8:5–7, Romans 12:3, Romans 12:16, Romans 14:6, Romans 15:5, Colossians 3:2
**Status:** LOCK the three-branch split. A single Thai gloss across all occurrences would distort the Greek lemma's semantic range, not preserve it.

## The split

φρονέω covers a wider semantic range than any single Thai verb:

| Branch | Thai | Sense | PHP examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cognitive disposition** | **คิด / ความคิด** | mindset, what one focuses on / values | PHP 1:7, 2:2, 3:15 (×2), 3:19, 4:2 |
| **Christ-shaped settled disposition** | **จิตใจ** | inner orientation, "mind of Christ" | PHP 2:5 |
| **Active concern / care** | **ห่วงใย** | practical caring-for someone | PHP 4:10 (×2) |

## Why a single gloss would fail

φρονέω in PHP 1:7 ("τοῦτο φρονεῖν ὑπὲρ πάντων ὑμῶν") is "to think this way about you all" — cognitive disposition.

φρονέω in PHP 2:5 ("τοῦτο φρονεῖτε ἐν ὑμῖν ὃ καὶ ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ") is the load-bearing transition from "think among yourselves" to "the mind of Christ" — the Greek elevates from cognitive disposition to settled inner orientation. Thai **จิตใจ** captures this elevation; **คิด** would feel flat.

φρονέω in PHP 4:10 ("ἀνεθάλετε τὸ ὑπὲρ ἐμοῦ φρονεῖν, ἐφ' ᾧ καὶ ἐφρονεῖτε") is "your concern for me has revived, in which indeed you were concerned" — practical caring, expressed materially via the gift in 4:14–18. Thai **ห่วงใย** is the natural rendering; **คิด** would lose the active-care semantic.

A single gloss (whether all-คิด or all-จิตใจ or all-ห่วงใย) would force one of these readings onto the others. The Greek lemma carries the unity; Thai needs three forms to preserve it.

## Forward applicability

When these passages ship, validate against this doc:

- **Rom 8:5–7** — "those who live according to the flesh **φρονοῦσιν** the things of the flesh" — settled-disposition sense. Likely **จิตใจ** branch (not just "think about" but "are oriented toward"). Distinct from PHP 2:5's Christ-shaped disposition; same branch, different content.
- **Rom 12:3** — "not to **φρονεῖν** more highly of yourself than you ought" — cognitive-disposition sense (self-assessment). **คิด** branch.
- **Rom 12:16** — "**φρονοῦντες** the same toward one another" — cognitive-unity sense, very close to PHP 2:2. **คิด** branch.
- **Rom 14:6** — "the one who **φρονεῖ** the day, **φρονεῖ** for the Lord" — cognitive disposition + religious-observance sense. **คิด** branch (likely ถือว่า / ให้ความสำคัญ as needed).
- **Rom 15:5** — "to **φρονεῖν** the same among one another according to Christ Jesus" — same as 12:16. **คิด** branch.
- **Col 3:2** — "**φρονεῖτε** the things above, not the things on the earth" — settled-disposition sense, parallel to Rom 8:5. **จิตใจ** branch likely.

The decision principle for forward instances: **what is the subject doing — thinking *about* something, *being oriented toward* something, or *caring for* someone?** Map to the matching Thai branch. Don't mechanically copy PHP's lemma-by-lemma renderings.
