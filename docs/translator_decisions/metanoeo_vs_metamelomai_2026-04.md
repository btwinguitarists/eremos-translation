# μετανοέω vs. μεταμέλομαι — Thai distinction lock

**Scope:** The two Greek "change-of-mind" verbs that the NT carefully distinguishes. μετανοέω (~34× NT) carries salvific-conversion weight; μεταμέλομαι (6× NT) is narrower — remorse, regret, reconsidering — without intrinsic salvific connotation.

**Decided:** 2026-04 during Matthew end-of-book review (see `docs/MAT_END_OF_BOOK_REVIEW_2026-04-19.md` §3).
**Evidence base:** Matt 3:2, 4:17, 11:20–21, 12:41 (μετανοέω, 5×); Matt 21:29, 21:32, 27:3 (μεταμέλομαι, 3×); 2 Cor 7:10 (the locus classicus juxtaposing both verbs); Heb 7:21, 12:17.

---

## The rule

| Greek | Thai | Sense |
|-------|------|-------|
| **μετανοέω** | **กลับใจ** (or **กลับใจใหม่**) | Salvific conversion — turning from sin toward God |
| **μεταμέλομαι** (3rd-party regret about a past action) | **เปลี่ยนใจ** | Change of mind — non-salvific reconsidering |
| **μεταμέλομαι** (Judas-style remorse without conversion) | **เสียใจ** | Sorrow / remorse — emphasizes affect, no turning |

The doublet **กลับใจใหม่** is interchangeable with **กลับใจ** for μετανοέω (style-driven). Either is fine; do not vary within a single context unless aural rhythm requires it.

## Why three Thai renderings for two Greek verbs

Because μεταμέλομαι itself splits semantically:
- In contexts of *re-deciding to do/not-do* (the parable of the two sons, Matt 21:29 + 32), the focus is on the will/decision change. → **เปลี่ยนใจ**.
- In contexts of *grief over consequence* (Judas, Matt 27:3), the focus is on the emotional response without resulting salvific turning. → **เสียใจ**.

Greek does not split this lexically; Thai *does* split it lexically and the distinction tracks the contextual reading. Splitting the Thai is faithful to the *function* of the verb in each context.

## Why not collapse μεταμέλομαι into กลับใจ

Tempting because Thai กลับใจ is the standard "change of heart" idiom. But:

1. 27:3 explicitly avoids กลับใจ — and the verse-level note there (existing pre-this-doc) calls the distinction "CRUCIAL" because Judas's μεταμέλομαι precisely does *not* lead to repentance unto life. If we collapse 21:29/32 to กลับใจ, the verb-level distinction disappears in Thai while remaining in Greek.
2. 2 Cor 7:10 is coming. Paul there contrasts ἡ κατὰ θεὸν λύπη (godly grief → μετάνοιαν εἰς σωτηρίαν) with ἡ τοῦ κόσμου λύπη (worldly grief → death). If Matthew's corpus blurs the distinction by using กลับใจ for μεταμέλομαι, 2 Cor 7:10 in Thai loses the lexical anchor that lets the reader recover Paul's contrast.
3. Heb 7:21 (οὐ μεταμεληθήσεται — "will not change his mind", of YHWH's oath) needs **เปลี่ยนพระทัย** (royal-register), not กลับใจ. The Hebrews translator needs the precedent locked.
4. Heb 12:17 (Esau, μετανοίας γὰρ τόπον οὐχ εὗρεν — "found no place for repentance"): μετανοία here is salvific. If μετανοέω = กลับใจ corpus-wide, that verse renders cleanly.

## Implementation history

- **Pre-2026-04 in Matthew:** drift. 21:29 used กลับใจ for μεταμέλομαι; 21:32 used ไม่กลับใจ. The 21:29 verse-rationale even admitted "Thai does not distinguish readily." Yet 27:3 used เสียใจ with a note calling the distinction "CRUCIAL."
- **2026-04-20 fix:** 21:29 and 21:32 retranslated to use **เปลี่ยนใจ** (preserves the parable's "change-of-mind" force without claiming salvific conversion). 27:3's เสียใจ stays. The Matthean corpus now keeps the distinction in three lexical buckets.

## Forward implications

For the Pauline + Hebrews translators:
- **2 Cor 7:8–10** — μεταμέλομαι (vv.8 twice) → เสียใจ (Paul's sorrow about the letter, then his lack of subsequent regret); μετάνοια (v.9 + v.10) → การกลับใจ. The contrast in v.10 (godly grief → repentance unto salvation; worldly grief → death) MUST preserve the lexical distinction.
- **Heb 7:21** — μεταμεληθήσεται (of YHWH) → เปลี่ยนพระทัย (royal: divine subject; cf. RULES §3).
- **Heb 12:17** — μετανοία → การกลับใจ (Esau sought salvific repentance and found no place).

For Acts: μετανοέω (Acts 2:38, 3:19, 17:30, 26:20) → กลับใจ throughout — the apostolic preaching uses the salvific verb consistently; this lock holds.

## Alternatives considered

- **Single rendering for μεταμέλομαι (เสียใจ throughout).** Rejected — at 21:29 the son *did the work*, which means his change was effective, not merely emotional. เสียใจ alone misses the will-change.
- **เปลี่ยนความคิด ("change one's thinking").** Considered for 21:29/32. Rejected — too cognitive, misses the affective + volitional dimensions Greek carries.
- **Same Thai for both Greek verbs (collapse).** Rejected — see "Why not collapse" above. Would damage 2 Cor 7:10 + Heb 7:21 + 12:17 in Thai.

## When to revisit

- If 2 Cor 7:10 translation surfaces a better Thai rendering (the locus classicus may force a refinement).
- If Heb 7:21's reflexive use of μεταμέλομαι (of God's oath) requires a different royal-register verb.
- If a Thai Christian theological reviewer questions the เปลี่ยนใจ choice on naturalness grounds.
