# Cities of refuge + blood-avenger — locked Thai surfaces

**Scope:** Every occurrence of עִיר מִקְלָט (city of refuge) and גֹּאֵל הַדָּם (blood-avenger / kinsman-redeemer of blood) across the Pentateuch, the Former Prophets, and any NT typological echo (notably Heb 6:18).

**Decided:** 2026-05-18 (Ben, post-Joshua-end-of-book audit). Locked **before** Judges ships and **before** any 1–2 Samuel / 1 Kings refuge-narrative work begins.

**Trigger:** Joshua end-of-book audit §5 + 3-way external AI review (Grok / Gemini / ChatGPT) flagged JOS 20–21 shipping `เมืองหลบภัย` + `ผู้แก้แค้นโลหิต` against the already-shipped NUM 35 base. ChatGPT additionally flagged DEU 19:6, 19:12 sub-drift `ผู้แก้แค้นแทนเลือด` (the inserted particle แทน is theologically wrong: the avenger avenges blood, does not substitute for it).

---

## The lock

| Hebrew | Locked Thai | Anchor |
|---|---|---|
| עִיר־מִקְלָט / מִקְלָט (city of refuge) | **เมืองลี้ภัย** | NUM 35:6, 11, 13, 14 |
| מִקְלָט (place of refuge, predicate) | **ที่ลี้ภัย** | NUM 35:12, 15 |
| גֹּאֵל הַדָּם (avenger of blood) | **ผู้แก้แค้นเลือด** | NUM 35:19, 21, 24, 25, 27 |

**Rejected surfaces (the drift that triggered this lock):**

| Rejected | Where it appeared | Why rejected |
|---|---|---|
| เมืองหลบภัย | JOS 20:2, 9 + JOS 21:13, 21, 27, 32, 38 (pre-audit) | หลบ = "duck / hide from immediate danger" — weaker than ลี้ภัย ("seek asylum / take refuge"); the מִקְלָט institution is legal asylum, not stealth concealment |
| ที่หลบภัย | JOS 20:3 (pre-audit) | Same — paired downgrade |
| ผู้แก้แค้นโลหิต | JOS 20:3, 5, 9 (pre-audit) | โลหิต is the more formal-medical surface; the NUM 35 lock uses เลือด (the everyday surface) and Joshua should not diverge from its own Pentateuch base |
| ผู้แก้แค้นแทนเลือด | DEU 19:6, 19:12 (pre-audit) | The inserted particle แทน ("instead of / on behalf of") theologically inverts the role — the avenger avenges *for* the dead, does not *substitute* for them. Translation error, not a deliberate variant |

## Why the NUM 35 base wins over the JOS surfaces

1. **Numbers 35 establishes the institution.** It is the longest single passage on refuge-cities + blood-avenger law (32 verses) and was shipped before Joshua. Joshua 20 is explicitly the *implementation* of the NUM 35 legislation (cf. JOS 20:2 "as I spoke to you by Moses") — diverging from its own statutory base would have broken the legal-thread traceability that the deuteronomic-history genre depends on.
2. **Downstream traceability.** The same lexical pair recurs in 2 Sam 3:27, 14:11 (Joab + Abner; the wise woman of Tekoa); 1 Kgs 2:5, 31 (Joab's death); and is picked up typologically in Heb 6:18 (καταφυγόντες κρατῆσαι τῆς προκειμένης ἐλπίδος — "we who have fled for refuge"). A single Thai thread is required for those forward references to land cleanly.
3. **External AI consensus.** Three independent reviews (Grok / Gemini / ChatGPT) all chose path-(a): normalize JOS to NUM 35 over either rewriting NUM or leaving the divergence documented. Recommended action was unanimous: lowest-cost forward protection.

## Boundary — not covered by this lock

- **דָּם as bloodguilt** (e.g., דָּמוֹ בְּרֹאשׁוֹ "his blood is on his head") — keep existing renderings; that is a *bloodguilt* idiom, not the *blood-avenger* technical term.
- **גֹּאֵל in the kinsman-redeemer sense** (Ruth 2:20, 3:9, 4:1ff; Lev 25:25–55; Isa 41:14, 43:14, 44:6) — see `goel_kinsman_redeemer_2026-05.md`. That is a *property/family redemption* lock, separate from גֹּאֵל הַדָּם.
- **NT καταφυγή / πρόσφυγος** terminology — locked separately under NT typological renderings; Heb 6:18 is the only direct OT-refuge echo and renders as `หลบหนีไปยังที่ลี้ภัย` (taking ลี้ภัย from this lock).
