# ποιμήν / ἀρχιποίμην / ποίμνη / ἐπίσκοπος — Christological-divine vs ecclesiastical-office register split

**Decision date:** 2026-05-03 (formalized after 1 Peter end-of-book external AI review)
**Anchor passages:** 1 Pet 2:25 (Christological ἐπίσκοπον τῶν ψυχῶν), 1 Pet 5:1-4 (ecclesiastical πρεσβύτεροι + ἐπισκοποῦντες + ἀρχιποίμην)
**Status:** LOCK. The split mirrors the παρουσία + πάσχω logic — Thai requires register-commitment per occurrence, but here the determining factor is *referent* (Christ vs church-office) rather than *subject*.

## The split

| Greek lemma | Christological-divine context | Ecclesiastical-office context |
|---|---|---|
| **ποιμήν** | **ผู้เลี้ยง** (or **ผู้เลี้ยงแกะ** when sheep-context is explicit) | **ผู้เลี้ยง** (verbal **เลี้ยงดู** for ποιμαίνω) |
| **ἀρχιποίμην** | **พระผู้เลี้ยงใหญ่** (royal-prefix + comparative; eschatological-Christ-title) | N/A — only Christological |
| **ἐπίσκοπος** | **ผู้ดูแล** (bare; Christ-as-Overseer-of-souls) | **ผู้ปกครองดูแล** (compound; office-title, e.g. 1 Tim 3:2 + Tit 1:7) |
| **ἐπισκοπέω** (verb) | (rare in Christological context) | **ดูแล** (1 Pet 5:2; ecclesiastical-elder action) |
| **ποίμνη / ποίμνιον** | (rare in Christological-direct context) | **ฝูงแกะ** (1 Pet 5:2-3; the church-as-flock) |

## Why this is grammatical, not theological

Thai distinguishes between divine-titles and human-office-titles at the level of compound-vs-bare nouns. **พระ-** prefix + comparative-suffix **ใหญ่** is reserved for divine-eschatological titles; bare **ผู้ดูแล** vs compound **ผู้ปกครองดูแล** distinguishes the *unique-Christological-overseer-of-souls* from the *plural-ordainable-church-office*.

Greek does not encode this register-split at the lemma level — ποιμήν and ἐπίσκοπος each carry both senses and the reader infers the referent from context. Thai cannot leave the inference to context for honorific terms; the translator must commit per occurrence.

The 1 Pet 2:25 case is the keystone: `τὸν ποιμένα καὶ ἐπίσκοπον τῶν ψυχῶν ὑμῶν` is a *unique-Christological dual-title* (Christ-as-Shepherd-Overseer-of-souls). Rendering this as **ผู้เลี้ยงและผู้ดูแลจิตวิญญาณ** preserves the Christological-divine-title sense without conflating it with the *plural-ordainable* ecclesiastical-office vocabulary at 5:2 (ἐπισκοποῦντες → **ดูแล**) and the Pastorals (ἐπίσκοπος → **ผู้ปกครองดูแล** at 1 Tim 3:2 + Tit 1:7).

|---|---|---|---|

| 5:2 | **ποιμάνατε** τὸ ἐν ὑμῖν **ποίμνιον** τοῦ θεοῦ | จง**เลี้ยงดู**ฝูงแกะ**ของพระเจ้าที่อยู่ในหมู่พวกท่าน** | ecclesiastical (verbal + flock) |

The 2:25 KD names the principle:

> ποιμήν → ผู้เลี้ยง — corpus-precedent JHN 10:2 (slightly-shortened from ผู้เลี้ยงแกะ to fit the dual-title context with 'ผู้ดูแล'). ἐπίσκοπος → ผู้ดูแล — DIVINE-application context (Christ-as-Overseer-of-souls), distinct from the ecclesiastical-office sense at 1 Tim 3:2 ('ผู้ปกครองดูแล'). Render bare 'ผู้ดูแล' for the Christological-divine title; alternative ผู้ปกครองดูแล considered but here the ecclesiastical-office sense doesn't fit (Christ is THE overseer-of-souls, not a church-office).

## Why "ผู้ดูแล" bare vs "ผู้ปกครองดูแล" compound

Thai compound **ผู้ปกครองดูแล** literally = "ruler-overseer" — the **ปกครอง** ("rule / govern") component carries the *ordainable-church-office* connotation (matching the ecclesiastical-office sense the Pastorals require). For the Christological-Overseer-of-souls at 1 Pet 2:25, **ปกครอง** would be theologically discordant: Christ does not "govern" souls in the *administrable-office* sense — he *watches-over / cares-for* them in the unique-divine sense.

Bare **ผู้ดูแล** ("the one who watches over / takes care") preserves the unique-Christological sense without the office-administrative overlay. This is the same pattern as κύριος-narrator-divine vs κύριος-as-human-master (`vocative_kyrie_didaskale_register_2026-04.md`) — Thai requires the translator to commit per occurrence to avoid honorific failure.

## Why "พระผู้เลี้ยงใหญ่" for ἀρχιποίμην

Three Thai options were considered:

1. **หัวหน้าผู้เลี้ยงแกะ** ("chief-shepherd" — head-of-shepherds construction). Rejected: too administrative; the ἀρχι-prefix in Greek + the eschatological context at 1 Pet 5:4 (when-the-chief-shepherd-appears) requires divine-honorific register.
2. **พระผู้เลี้ยงสูงสุด** ("supreme shepherd"). Considered. Rejected: superlative **สูงสุด** is too triumphalist; the comparative **ใหญ่** ("great" / "big") matches the ἀρχι- semantics more naturally.
3. **พระผู้เลี้ยงใหญ่** ("the Great Shepherd") — selected. **พระ-** prefix marks divine; **ใหญ่** is the natural Thai comparative that preserves ἀρχι-rank without superlative-overcommitment. Reads with the same register-weight as **พระผู้ช่วยให้รอด** ("the Savior") and **พระผู้สร้าง** ("the Creator") — divine-titles for unique offices.

## Cross-corpus application

This doc locks the rendering for all NT books not yet translated. Specific anticipated cases:

- **Hebrews 13:20** — `τὸν ποιμένα τῶν προβάτων τὸν μέγαν` (the great-shepherd-of-the-sheep) → **ผู้เลี้ยงแกะใหญ่** (Christological — preserves the **ใหญ่** rank-marker matching ἀρχιποίμην at 1 Pet 5:4; the Hebrews phrase has the explicit τῶν προβάτων so include **แกะ** in Thai). Cross-reference: Hebrews 13:20 + 1 Pet 5:4 are the two NT-cluster Christological-shepherd titles.
- **John 10:11, 10:14** — `ἐγώ εἰμι ὁ ποιμὴν ὁ καλός` → **เราเป็นผู้เลี้ยงแกะที่ดี** (already shipped per JHN translation). The **καλός** there is a quality-adjective, not a rank-marker; **ที่ดี** captures the moral-vs-hireling contrast (10:12).
- **Revelation 7:17** — `τὸ ἀρνίον ... ποιμανεῖ αὐτούς` → **พระเมษโปดก ... จะทรงเลี้ยงเขา** (the lamb-shepherd typology; royal-prefix **ทรง-** required because the lamb is the Christological subject performing the action).
- **1 Tim 3:2; Tit 1:7** — already shipped; ἐπίσκοπος → **ผู้ปกครองดูแล** (compound, office-title). Verify the lock holds when 2 Peter / Hebrews recur with ecclesiastical contexts.
- **Acts 20:28** — `ποιμαίνειν τὴν ἐκκλησίαν τοῦ θεοῦ ... ἐν ᾧ ὑμᾶς τὸ πνεῦμα τὸ ἅγιον ἔθετο ἐπισκόπους` (Paul's farewell to the Ephesian elders) → **เลี้ยงดูคริสตจักรของพระเจ้า ... ที่พระวิญญาณบริสุทธิ์ได้ทรงตั้งท่านทั้งหลายไว้เป็นผู้ปกครองดูแล**. Already shipped; verifies the ecclesiastical-office side of the lock holds in Acts.
