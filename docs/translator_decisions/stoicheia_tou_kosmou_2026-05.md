# στοιχεῖα τοῦ κόσμου — basic-principles default

**Decision date:** 2026-05-02 (formalized after COL end-of-book external AI review; long-deferred from GAL audit)
**Anchor passages:** Galatians 4:3, 4:9; Colossians 2:8, 2:20
**Status:** LOCK. Three Greek sense-clusters exist; the corpus default is **(1) basic principles**, with (2) and (3) named explicitly so future verses can deviate when context demands.

## The polyseme

στοιχεῖα τοῦ κόσμου admits three readings in NT scholarship:

1. **Basic principles / elementary religious teachings of the world** — ESV / NIV / NASB / CSB / BSB default. Reads the phrase as the rudimentary religious instruction that the Galatian and Colossian believers had previously been under (whether Jewish-ceremonial or pagan-cultic).
2. **The physical elements (earth, water, fire, air)** — ancient Greek philosophical sense, occasionally invoked by some early commentators. Marginal in modern scholarship.
3. **Elemental spirits / cosmic powers** — held by Wright / Witherington / some 20th-century critical commentators. Reads the phrase as personified hostile cosmic powers, often connected to angelic-power language elsewhere in COL/GAL.

## Project default

**(1) Basic principles → หลักการพื้นฐานของโลก.**

Reasons:

1. **Audience.** Eremos's primary audience is Thai evangelical-Protestant; reading (1) is the dominant mainstream-evangelical rendering in English Bibles those readers cross-reference (NIV, ESV, CSB, BSB).
2. **Galatians local context.** GAL 4:3 explicitly equates the στοιχεῖα with the legalistic religious instruction the Galatians had been "enslaved to" (δεδουλωμένοι); GAL 4:9 calls them "weak and beggarly" (ἀσθενῆ καὶ πτωχά) — both more naturally fitting "elementary religious principles" than "elemental spirits."
3. **Colossians wider regulation context.** COL 2:20–22 explicitly grounds the στοιχεῖα in human regulations: "Do not handle, do not taste, do not touch — referring to things that all perish with use, according to human commandments and teachings." Regulations and ceremonial principles are the operative referent; spiritual-power language in 2:10 + 2:15 is adjacent but not the same.
4. **Corpus consistency.** GAL already shipped with หลักการพื้นฐานของโลก; reversing course in COL would create cross-book inconsistency for the same Greek phrase.

## Where the elemental-spirits reading has weight

COL 2 uniquely stacks the spiritual-power vocabulary alongside στοιχεῖα:

- 2:8 — μὴ τις ὑμᾶς ἔσται ὁ συλαγωγῶν διὰ τῆς φιλοσοφίας καὶ κενῆς ἀπάτης κατὰ τὴν παράδοσιν τῶν ἀνθρώπων, **κατὰ τὰ στοιχεῖα τοῦ κόσμου**, καὶ οὐ κατὰ Χριστόν.
- 2:10 — ὅς ἐστιν ἡ κεφαλὴ πάσης **ἀρχῆς καὶ ἐξουσίας**.
- 2:15 — ἀπεκδυσάμενος **τὰς ἀρχὰς καὶ τὰς ἐξουσίας** ἐδειγμάτισεν ἐν παρρησίᾳ.
- 2:18 — θρησκείᾳ τῶν ἀγγέλων.
- 2:20 — εἰ ἀπεθάνετε σὺν Χριστῷ ἀπὸ **τῶν στοιχείων τοῦ κόσμου**.

The clustered cosmic-power language in 2:10–18 makes reading (3) more atmospheric in COL than in GAL. **The verse-text rendering stays at (1)**, but the doc names the elemental-spirits reading explicitly so future readers / reviewers / theologians cross-checking the corpus can trace the choice. Footer-note at COL 2:8 was considered (Claude's suggestion) and declined (only 1-of-3 AIs wanted it; the doc covers it).
