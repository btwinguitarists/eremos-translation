# Aramaic transliterations in the Thai text

**Scope:** Aramaic words that Mark embeds in the Greek text for dramatic effect. Mark provides a Greek translation for each; our Thai translation preserves the transliteration AND the Thai translation, matching Mark's own "transliterate then translate" rhetorical move.

**Decided:** 2026-04 during end-of-book retro review of Mark.
**Evidence base:** Mark 3:17 (Βοανηργές), 5:41 (Ταλιθα κουμ), 7:11 (κορβᾶν), 7:34 (ἐφφαθά), 14:36 (αββα), 15:22 (Γολγοθᾶ), 15:34 (Ελωΐ Ελωΐ λεμὰ σαβαχθανί).

---

## The rule

**Preserve the Aramaic transliteration in Thai script, then provide Mark's own Greek-to-Thai translation.** Do not drop the transliteration even though it feels redundant.

Instances:

| Verse | Aramaic | Thai transliteration | Thai gloss (Mark's own Greek translation, rendered) |
|-------|---------|----------------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| 3:17 | Βοανηργές | โบอาเนอเกส | "บุตรแห่งเสียงฟ้าร้อง" (sons of thunder) |
| 5:41 | Ταλιθα κουμ | ทาลิธา คูม | "เด็กหญิง เราบอกเจ้าว่า จงลุกขึ้น" |
| 7:11 | κορβᾶν | โครบาน | "สิ่งที่ถวายแด่พระเจ้า" |
| 7:34 | ἐφφαθά | เอฟฟาธา | "จงเปิดออก" |
| 14:36 | αββα | อับบา | "พระบิดา" |
| 15:22 | Γολγοθᾶ | กลโกธา | "สถานที่กะโหลกศีรษะ" |
| 15:34 | Ελωΐ Ελωΐ λεμὰ σαβαχθανί | เอโลอี เอโลอี ลามา ซาบัคทานี | "พระเจ้าของข้าพระองค์ ..." |

Rationale quoted from Mark 5:41 key_decisions:

> Following Mark's deliberate strategy: transliterate then translate. The Aramaic word is preserved as a foreign word in the Thai text — modern Thai readers will recognize this as 'a word in the language Jesus spoke.'

And from Mark 3:17 for Βοανηργές:

> HAPAX LEGOMENON. Aramaic/Hebrew loanword transliterated into Greek by Mark. Per uW translation-transliterate guidance: preserve as transliteration, since Mark himself translates the meaning in the same verse.

## Why preserve the double form

- **Mark's rhetorical device.** Mark clearly wants his Greek-reading audience to *hear* the Aramaic — it is the language Jesus actually spoke. Flattening to Thai-only translation drops a layer Mark specifically built.
- **Thai reader benefits.** A Thai reader who sees "ทาลิธา คูม" followed by the Thai gloss gets the same experience Mark's Greek reader got: the foreign sound of the Saviour's actual voice, followed by intelligibility.
- **Consistency with Thai Christian convention.** Existing Thai translations (THSV, NTV) preserve these transliterations. Our rendering matches the established expectation.

## Thai transliteration spelling

The Thai spellings above match Thai Christian tradition where one exists (ทาลิธา คูม, อับบา, กลโกธา, เอโลอี). For Βοανηργές (rare — only Mark 3:17), we use โบอาเนอเกส following phonetic conventions; verify with Thai Christian bibles if variants exist. Locked in the glossary.

## Alternatives considered

- **Drop the Aramaic transliteration; render only the Thai gloss.** Rejected — loses Mark's device.
- **Use an Aramaic-script insertion (אִמָּא in Hebrew script).** Rejected — unreadable by Thai audience and not what Mark did (he used Greek script for the Aramaic). Thai-script transliteration is the faithful parallel.
- **Italicize or bracket the transliteration to mark its foreignness.** Rejected — Thai does not use italics in the same way; brackets [ ] are reserved for textual-variant marking per `RULES.md §5`. The foreignness is already signaled by the unfamiliar Thai spelling.

## When to revisit

- **Matt 5:22 (ῥακά and μωρέ)** — Matthew's use of ῥακά (Aramaic insult) plus μωρέ (Greek, but LXX-style Semitizing) is a related-but-distinct case. Decide at Matt 5 translation whether to transliterate ρακά as ราคา or rendering as a Thai insult.
- **Matt 27:46 (Ηλι Ηλι λεμα σαβαχθανι)** — Matthew's Hebraizing spelling of the same cry. Expect the Thai transliteration to differ slightly from the Markan Ελωΐ form (เอลี เอลี lema sabakhthani). Document at that verse.
- **Romans 8:15 / Galatians 4:6 (αββα ὁ πατήρ)** — Paul uses αββα ὁ πατήρ. The Mark 14:36 precedent (อับบา + พระบิดา) travels directly.
- **John's brief transliterations** (Rabbouni at 20:16, Messiah at 1:41 and 4:25) — apply the same pattern.
- If a Thai-speaking reviewer finds specific transliterations look unnatural, revise per-transliteration. The glossary entries here are starting points, not frozen.
