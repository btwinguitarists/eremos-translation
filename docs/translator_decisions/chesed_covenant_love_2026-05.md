# חֶסֶד (chesed) — Covenant-love corpus lock

**Scope:** The Hebrew lemma חֶסֶד (chesed) — covenant-faithfulness, loyal love, steadfast mercy — across the entire OT corpus. One of the OT's most theologically loaded lemmas (~250 occurrences in MT). This doc locks the rendering before Psalms work begins so the lemma-thread is preserved across Ruth → Jonah → Psalms → Joel → Nahum → Nehemiah and the Exod 34:6–7 attribute formula's many recurrences.

**Decided:** 2026-05-09 (Ben + cross-AI review, Gemini + ChatGPT consensus). **Triggered by Jonah end-of-book audit cross-AI review.**

**Companion docs:** `divine_names_table_2026-05.md`, `ot_register_policy_2026-05.md`, `exod_34_attribute_formula_2026-05.md` (sibling), `leitwort_handling_policy_2026-05.md` (governing principle).

## 1. The lock

| Hebrew | Default Thai | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| חֶסֶד (chesed, masc.sg.) | **ความรักมั่นคง** | Default rendering — applies in nearly all contexts |
| חַסְדֵי (chasdei, construct sg./pl.) | **ความรักมั่นคงของ-** | Construct chain, e.g. חַסְדֵי יהוה → ความรักมั่นคงขององค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า |
| חֲסָדִים (chasadim, masc.pl.) | **บรรดาความรักมั่นคง** OR **พระเมตตาทั้งปวง** | Plural, often listing acts of chesed (Isa 63:7; Ps 89:1, 49) |
| רַב־חֶסֶד (rav-chesed) | **บริบูรณ์ด้วยความรักมั่นคง** | Construct + adjective; foundational Exod 34:6 attribute |
| חֶסֶד וֶאֱמֶת (chesed wĕ-ʾemet) | **ความรักมั่นคงและความซื่อสัตย์** | Word-pair (Gen 24:27, 49 etc.; ~25 occurrences); preserve as paired |
| חַסְדּוֹ לְעוֹלָם (chasdo lĕ-ʿolām) | **ความรักมั่นคงของพระองค์ดำรงนิรันดร์** | Refrain in Ps 136 (26×); preserve as identical refrain |

## 2. Why this lock matters

### 2.1 Cross-corpus traceability

חֶסֶד is the OT's primary covenant-love word. It appears:
- In Ruth (3 occurrences: 1:8, 2:20, 3:10) — established lock from Ruth's pilot.
- In Jonah (2 occurrences: 2:9, 4:2) — Jonah's prayer + the Exod 34 quotation.
- In the Psalms (~127 occurrences) — the most-frequent OT theological vocabulary in the Psalter.
- In the Exod 34:6–7 attribute formula and its ~10 OT recitations (Num 14:18; Ps 86:15, 103:8, 145:8; Joel 2:13; Nah 1:3; Neh 9:17; 2 Chr 30:9; cf. Mic 7:18).
- In the prophetic corpus (Hos 2:19; 6:6; 10:12; 12:6; Jer 9:24; 16:5; 32:18; etc.).

**If the rendering drifts, the cross-corpus lemma-thread is lost.** Thai readers cannot recognize that the Exod 34 formula is being recited in Joel or Nahum if the chesed-rendering varies. The Eremos translation must use the same Thai phrase at every chesed occurrence.

### 2.2 Why ความรักมั่นคง

- **ความรัก** ('love') captures the affectionate-relational core of chesed.
- **มั่นคง** ('steadfast, firm, faithful, enduring') captures the covenant-fidelity dimension.
- The compound is established in Thai Christian vocabulary (THSV, NTV, TNCV all converge on "ความรักมั่นคง" or close variants).
- Distinguishes from generic mercy (พระเมตตา = רחמים) and grace (พระคุณ = חן).

### 2.3 What we deliberately reject

- **พระเมตตา** alone — flattens chesed into generic mercy, losing the covenant-fidelity dimension. Used elsewhere for רחמים (rachamim, "compassion") which is a sibling but distinct lemma.
- **พระคุณ** — collides with חן (chen, "grace, favor"). חֶסֶד is relational-covenantal; חן is gratuitous-favor.
- **พระเมตตาเชิงพันธสัญญา** ('covenantal mercy') — the qualifier "เชิงพันธสัญญา" front-loads interpretation into the lexical choice rather than letting the recurring formula teach the concept canonically. Also breaks lexical-recurrence — readers can't trace the lemma if every occurrence has different qualifying material.
- **ความเมตตามั่นคง** — a near-variant; we lock to ความรักมั่นคง for consistency.

## 3. Register handling

### 3.1 With divine subject

When chesed describes God's covenant-faithfulness to his people:

- Standard: **ความรักมั่นคงขององค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า**
- With ราชาศัพท์ (Rachasap) verbs: **ทรงสำแดงความรักมั่นคง** ('show chesed') vs **มี/อยู่กับ-** ('have/be with')
- In Exod 34 formula contexts: **ทรงบริบูรณ์ด้วยความรักมั่นคง** (locked in JON 4:2; will recur in Num 14:18, Ps 86:15, 103:8, 145:8, Joel 2:13, Nah 1:3, Neh 9:17, 2 Chr 30:9)

### 3.2 Human-to-human chesed

When chesed describes inter-human loyalty (Naomi/Ruth, David/Jonathan, etc.):

- Standard: **ความรักมั่นคง** (no royal-Thai prefix)
- E.g., Ruth 1:8 (יַעַשׂ יהוה עִמָּכֶם חֶסֶד כַּאֲשֶׁר עֲשִׂיתֶם עִם־הַמֵּתִים — "may YHWH show chesed to you as you have shown chesed to the dead") — first occurrence shows divine + human chesed in one verse, both rendered ความรักมั่นคง.

### 3.3 Word-pair forms

- חֶסֶד וֶאֱמֶת ('chesed and faithfulness') → **ความรักมั่นคงและความซื่อสัตย์** — preserve as a unit; both Thai words always appear together when the Hebrew pair appears.
- חֶסֶד וְרַחֲמִים ('chesed and compassion') → **ความรักมั่นคงและพระเมตตา** — preserves the lemma distinction.

## 4. The two corrections that triggered this doc

The Jonah end-of-book audit cross-AI review (Gemini + ChatGPT, 2026-05-09) identified two chesed verses in Jonah where the rendering had drifted from Ruth's lock:

| Verse | BEFORE (drift) | AFTER (locked) | Hebrew |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jonah 2:9 | ทอดทิ้ง**พระเมตตา**ที่พวกเขาควรได้รับ | ทอดทิ้ง**ความรักมั่นคง**ที่พวกเขาควรได้รับ | חַסְדָּם יַעֲזֹבוּ |
| Jonah 4:2 | ทรงบริบูรณ์ด้วย**พระเมตตาเชิงพันธสัญญา** | ทรงบริบูรณ์ด้วย**ความรักมั่นคง** | וְרַב־חֶסֶד |

Both AI reviewers flagged these as MAJOR CONCERN / CONCERN — the Jonah-specific renderings were breaking the cross-corpus lock established in Ruth. Without correction, future Psalms work would inherit the drift and the corpus-wide lemma-thread would be unreadable to Thai readers.

**Status:** Corrections applied to both Jonah verses on 2026-05-09 (this doc's authoring date). All future occurrences must use ความรักมั่นคง.

## 5. The discourse-function escape hatch

For **rare** cases where the verse-level discourse function genuinely demands a contextual qualifier (e.g., a wisdom-instruction context where "covenant-love" needs explicit unpacking for the wisdom-instructor's audience), the qualifier goes into the `key_decisions` rationale or `thai_summary`, **not** into the main `thai` field. This preserves the lemma-thread in the surface text while letting the reader-edition layer carry the contextual nuance.

**Example:** if a verse in Proverbs needs to gloss chesed for the wisdom-instruction context, render in main text as ความรักมั่นคง but add to thai_summary: "ในบริบทของบทพระธรรมนี้ ความรักมั่นคงหมายถึง..." Do not change the main-text rendering.

## 7. When to revisit

- **Reader feedback at Psalms-end** — if Thai-language reviewer flags ความรักมั่นคง as too literary or unfamiliar in modern Thai. Migration to a different Thai compound is permitted via project-wide find-replace.
- **Discovery of a new chesed compound** that doesn't pattern (e.g., a unique idiom in a specific verse) — document in `key_decisions`, append to this doc.
- **NT cross-corpus regression** — chesed has no direct NT equivalent (NT uses ἔλεος "mercy," χάρις "grace," ἀγάπη "love" depending on context); but if NT-citation work uncovers regression, audit immediately.
