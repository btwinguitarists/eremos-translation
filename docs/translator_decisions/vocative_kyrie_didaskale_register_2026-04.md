# Character-voice vocative address to Jesus — Κύριε, Διδάσκαλε

**Scope:** In-narrative character speech addressing Jesus with the vocative Κύριε ("Lord!") or Διδάσκαλε ("Teacher!"). Distinct from narrator-voice κύριος (see `kyrios_narrator_voice_luke_acts_2026-04.md`).

**Decided:** 2026-04 during Luke end-of-book review. Extends `narrator_vs_character_voice_2026-04.md` by adding a vocative-address register rule.

**Evidence base (Luke):** 26 Κύριε vocatives across 24 chapters; 12 Διδάσκαλε vocatives. Distribution verified against `output/translations/luke_*.json`.

---

## The rule

Thai vocative rendering of Κύριε and Διδάσκαλε **varies by speaker-class** rather than being locked to one form. The variation is principled, not drift.

### Κύριε (26 Luke occurrences)

| Speaker class | Thai vocative | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Disciples and confessing believers (reverent, divine-sovereign register) | **องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า** (17 verses) | Peter 5:8; leper 5:12; "Lord, Lord" sayings 6:46; James/John 9:54; would-be followers 9:59, 9:61; Son's praise 10:21; "Lord, teach us to pray" 11:1; Peter 12:41; blind man 18:41; Zacchaeus 19:8; Peter 22:33; disciples 22:38, 22:49 |
| Humble-intimate petitioners (centurion, Martha, seventy's report) | **พระองค์เจ้าข้า** (3 verses) | 7:6 (centurion — "I am not worthy"); 10:17 (seventy — submission report); 10:40 (Martha — domestic complaint) |
| Parabolic servants addressing a master (non-divine context) | **นายท่าน / นายเจ้าข้า** (6 verses) | 13:8 (gardener → landowner); 13:25 (closed-door parable); 14:22 (great banquet servant); 19:16, 19:18, 19:20, 19:25 (minas parable) |

### Διδάσκαλε (12 Luke occurrences)

| Speaker class | Thai vocative | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Neutral-polite individual inquirers | **ท่านอาจารย์** (6) | tax collectors 3:12; Simon the Pharisee 7:40; father of epileptic 9:38; lawyer 10:25; lawyer 11:45; rich ruler 18:18 |
| Crowd + sympathetic Pharisee register | **พระอาจารย์** (2) | crowd 12:13; sympathetic Pharisee 19:39 |
| Testing / disputing speakers (Jerusalem temple sequence) | **อาจารย์เจ้าข้า** (4) | spies 20:21; Sadducees 20:28; scribes 20:39; disciples w/ temple question 21:7 |

## Why not flatten to a single rendering

**Option A (chosen): speaker-class vocative variation.**
The Thai honorific system distinguishes:
- องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า — confessing faith, divine sovereignty.
- พระองค์เจ้าข้า — intimate humble petition (servant-class or personal distress).
- นายท่าน / นายเจ้าข้า — master-servant in a non-divine context (parable).
- ท่านอาจารย์ — neutral-polite teacher-address.
- พระอาจารย์ — elevated respectful teacher-address.
- อาจารย์เจ้าข้า — formal testing-address.

Greek lets the same vocative carry all these colorations; Thai cannot. Translating all Κύριε as องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า would put confessional register in the mouth of a parabolic servant or a testing opponent, flattening the narrative's social texture.

**Option B (rejected): always องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า for Κύριε, always ท่านอาจารย์ for Διδάσκαλε.**
Rejected — misreads parabolic contexts (13:8; 14:22; 19:16–25 minas) as theological address and erases the register-escalation in Luke 20's Jerusalem debate sequence.

**Option C (rejected): transliterate (κยριε → คีริเอ).**
Rejected — foreign, no Thai register-to-register mapping.

## Worked examples

- **Luke 5:8** (Peter): ἐξελθε ἀπ᾽ ἐμοῦ, κύριε → "ขอพระองค์เสด็จไปจากข้าพระองค์เถิด องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า" — Peter in spontaneous confession. Divine-sovereign register.
- **Luke 7:6** (centurion via friends): κύριε, μὴ σκύλλου → "พระองค์เจ้าข้า ขอทรงอย่าเหนื่อยพระองค์เลย" — humble self-lowering register ("I am not worthy").
- **Luke 13:8** (vineyard-worker): κύριε, ἄφες αὐτὴν → "นายท่าน ขอปล่อยไว้อีกหนึ่งปี" — parable interior; the κύριος addressed is a human vineyard-owner, not Jesus-as-Lord.
- **Luke 19:16** (minas servant): κύριε, ἡ μνᾶ σου δέκα προσηργάσατο μνᾶς → "นายเจ้าข้า เงินมินาของท่านได้กำไรสิบมินา" — parable master-servant.
- **Luke 20:21** (spies): διδάσκαλε, οἴδαμεν ὅτι ὀρθῶς λέγεις → "อาจารย์เจ้าข้า พวกเราทราบว่าท่านพูดและสอนถูกต้อง" — formal-testing; register signals irony the Greek vocative alone doesn't.

## Forward implications

**Acts vocative κύριε (many occurrences):**
- Believer-speech (Peter, Stephen, Ananias, Cornelius): **องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า**.
- Non-believer respectful address (Agrippa, Festus, centurions to Paul — not to Jesus): **ท่าน / นายท่าน** per speaker-class; these are human κύριος addresses, not Jesus-κύριος.
- Vocative prayer to the risen Jesus (Paul on Damascus road, Acts 9:5, 22:8, 26:15): **องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า** — divine-sovereign register, matching Luke disciples.

**Pauline epistles** have few vocative κύριε forms in narrative prose; where present (e.g., Rom 10:16 citing Isa), treat as OT-quotation.

## When to revisit

- If a native-Thai reader's review finds the speaker-class distinction too subtle to register in reading, consider adding a verse-level note at each class-transition so the reader catches the register intent.
- Parable vocatives (นายเจ้าข้า) should track consistently across Matthew, Mark, Luke parables — cross-check against MAT 20:8; 21:30; 25:11; 25:20; 25:22; 25:24; MRK 12:9 et al.
