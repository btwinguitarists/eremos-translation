# Evil-spirit-from-YHWH (רוּחַ רָעָה מֵאֵת יְהוָה) — 1 Samuel companion lock

**Scope:** Every occurrence in 1–2 Samuel where a *malevolent* or *troubling* spirit is sent by YHWH against a human agent — distinct from the Spirit-of-YHWH **empowerment** cluster locked in `spirit_of_yhwh_empowerment_2026-05.md`. The two clusters share Hebrew root vocabulary (רוּחַ + occasionally צָלַח) but carry opposite theological/narrative valence, so the Thai surface must mark the distinction without leakage.

**Decided:** 2026-05-23 (Ben, post-1 Samuel end-of-book external AI review). Locked **before** any further 1–2 Samuel chapters ship, and **retroactively-applied** to the already-shipped 1 Samuel corpus during the 2026-05-23 lock-violation revision pass.

**Trigger:** Two-AI external review of 1 Samuel (ChatGPT + Gemini, 2026-05-23). Both reviewers flagged that the Spirit-of-YHWH empowerment lock had drifted in shipped 1 Samuel **and** that the evil-spirit cluster sat awkwardly outside the empowerment lock without its own principled treatment — leading to internal verb inconsistency (e.g., 18:10 used the same צָלַח verb as 10:6, blurring the empowerment/affliction distinction).

---

## The lock

### 1. Divine register

The evil spirit is **from** YHWH but is **not** the Spirit-of-YHWH. The Thai surface must mark this:

| Hebrew | Thai (locked) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| רוּחַ יְהוָה (Spirit-of-YHWH, empowerment) | **พระวิญญาณขององค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า** | Per `spirit_of_yhwh_empowerment_2026-05.md` |
| רוּחַ אֱלֹהִים (Spirit-of-God, empowerment) | **พระวิญญาณของพระเจ้า** | Per `spirit_of_yhwh_empowerment_2026-05.md` |
| **רוּחַ רָעָה מֵאֵת יְהוָה** (evil spirit from YHWH) | **วิญญาณชั่วจากองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า** | **No พระ prefix** — the spirit is not the divine Spirit |
| **רוּחַ אֱלֹהִים רָעָה** (evil spirit from God) | **วิญญาณชั่วจากพระเจ้า** | Same principle |
| **רוּחַ יְהוָה רָעָה** (evil Spirit-of-YHWH) | **วิญญาณชั่วจากองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า** | Genitive of source, not identification |

**Principle:** The Thai `พระ` honorific marks the divine Spirit (the third person of the Trinity, retrospectively read; the empowering Spirit at the OT surface). The evil/troubling spirit is an agent **sent by** YHWH, not YHWH's own Spirit — so the Thai must not honour it with `พระ`.

### 2. Verb surface

The evil-spirit cluster does **not** receive the `อย่างทรงพลัง` adverb from the empowerment lock, even when the Hebrew verb is צָלַח. The adverb has acquired positive empowerment resonance from Judges 14–15 + 1 Samuel 10–16; extending it to the evil-spirit cluster would blur the theological contrast.

| Hebrew | Locked Thai surface |
|---|---|
| צָלַח עַל + רוּחַ רָעָה (1 Sam 18:10, evil spirit "rushes" on Saul) | **วิญญาณชั่ว…ก็มาเหนือ** (plain `มาเหนือ`, **no** `อย่างทรงพลัง`) |
| הָיָה אֶל + רוּחַ רָעָה (1 Sam 19:9, evil spirit "came upon" Saul) | **วิญญาณชั่ว…มาเหนือ** |
| בָּעַת + רוּחַ רָעָה (1 Sam 16:14b, evil spirit "tormented" Saul) | **วิญญาณชั่ว…เริ่มทรมาน** (verb tracks Hebrew בָּעַת) |
| נָפַל + רוּחַ רָעָה (variant in 1 Sam 16:23) | **วิญญาณชั่ว…มาเหนือ / จากไป** (context-dependent) |

**Why no `อย่างทรงพลัง` for 18:10:** 1 Sam 18:10 deliberately echoes the empowerment-verb צָלַח from 10:6 / 10:10 / 11:6 / 16:13. The Hebrew author is signaling typological inversion — Saul's empowerment-Spirit-rushes have been replaced by evil-spirit-rushes. The Thai must preserve that **structural** echo (same verb root `มาเหนือ`) while **withholding** the empowerment adverb (`อย่างทรงพลัง`) so the contrast is not erased by surface flattening *and* not over-amplified by extending positive resonance to a negative event.

## 3. Corpus-verified shipped forms (post-revision 2026-05-23)

After the 2026-05-23 revision pass on 1 Samuel:

```
1 SAM 16:14a (סוּר, Spirit-of-YHWH departs)
  → 'พระวิญญาณขององค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้าจากซาอูลไปแล้ว'  (empowerment-lock, with พระ)

1 SAM 16:14b (בָּעַת, evil spirit torments)
  → 'วิญญาณชั่วจากองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้าก็เริ่มทรมานเขา'  (evil-spirit-lock, no พระ, verb=ทรมาน)

1 SAM 18:10 (צָלַח, evil spirit rushes)
  → 'วิญญาณชั่วจากพระเจ้าก็มาเหนือซาอูล'  (evil-spirit-lock, no พระ, no อย่างทรงพลัง)

1 SAM 19:9 (הָיָה, evil spirit comes upon)
  → 'วิญญาณชั่วจากองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้ามาเหนือซาอูล'  (evil-spirit-lock, no พระ, plain มาเหนือ)
```

The structural echo between 18:10 (`มาเหนือ`) and 10:6 / 16:13 (`มาเหนือ…อย่างทรงพลัง`) is preserved — Thai readers comparing the two see the same verb root, with the empowerment-adverb present/absent flagging the empowering vs afflicting valence.

## 4. Edge cases + forward-watch

- **רוּחַ מַשְׁחִית** (destroying spirit; 1 Kgs 22:21–23, the lying spirit) — separate idiom, separate Thai surface, not in scope of this doc.
- **רוּחַ שֶׁקֶר** (lying/deceiving spirit; same 1 Kgs 22 cluster) — separate idiom.
- **NT Synoptic-Gospels πνεῦμα ἀκάθαρτον / πνεῦμα πονηρόν** (unclean/evil spirits) — semantically related but distinct corpus, distinct lock; do not collapse into this OT lock.
- **Forward-watch for 2 Samuel:** No further evil-spirit-from-YHWH occurrences are expected in 2 Samuel. If one appears, apply this lock.
