# הַשָּׂטָן / שָׂטָן — corpus-wide mapping (the celestial accuser vs. the common-noun adversary)

**Scope:** Every OT occurrence of the root שׂטן as a noun — the article-marked celestial figure הַשָּׂטָן in the divine-council scenes (Job 1–2, Zechariah 3), the anarthrous שָׂטָן of 1 Chronicles 21:1, and the ordinary common-noun "adversary" elsewhere. Locks which occurrences render **ซาตาน** and which do not.

**Decided:** 2026-05-30, on JOB end-of-book external AI review (Gemini 2.5 Pro + ChatGPT, convergent **CONCERN**: rendering הַשָּׂטָן as the proper name ซาตาน flattens the Hebrew definite-article role-sense and sets a corpus precedent heading into Zechariah 3:1–2 — needs formal documentation + reader transparency).

**Companion docs:** `ot_register_policy_2026-05.md` §3.1 (adversary register), `adversary_speech_register_2026-05.md`, `spiritual_beings_hierarchy_2026-05.md`, `divine_names_table_2026-05.md`.

## The grammatical fact

In Hebrew, **הַשָּׂטָן** ("ha-satan") carries the **definite article** — literally "*the* accuser / *the* adversary." It is a **title designating a court/council role**, not yet a fixed personal name. The proper-name reading "Satan" is a later (Second-Temple → NT) theological identification. The one place the word appears **anarthrous** (no article) as a likely proper name is **1 Chronicles 21:1** (שָׂטָן).

## The decision

| Occurrence class | Hebrew | Thai | Disclosure |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Celestial accuser, council scenes** | הַשָּׂטָן (article-marked) — Job 1:6, 7, 8, 9, 12; 2:1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7; Zech 3:1, 2 | **ซาตาน** | Layer-2 footnote at the **book's first occurrence** noting the article + role-sense "ผู้กล่าวหา / ปฏิปักษ์" |
| **Anarthrous, proper-name reading** | שָׂטָן (no article) — 1 Chr 21:1 | **ซาตาน** | already shipped as ซาตาน; consistent with this lock |
| **Common-noun "an adversary"** (human or angelic opponent, NOT the celestial figure) | שָׂטָן — Num 22:22, 32 (the angel *as* an adversary); 1 Sam 29:4; 2 Sam 19:23; 1 Kgs 5:18; 11:14, 23, 25; Ps 109:6 | **ปฏิปักษ์ / ผู้เป็นปฏิปักษ์ / ศัตรู** (per context) — **NOT** ซาตาน | internal `key_decisions` note only |

**Rationale for rendering ซาตาน (not "ผู้กล่าวหา") in the council scenes:** reader-recognition continuity. Thai church readers, like English readers of ESV/NIV, recognize ซาตาน as the same figure across Job → Zechariah → 1 Chronicles → NT; translating the surface as "ผู้กล่าวหา" would fracture that recognition. The article's role-sense is preserved as **transparency** (footnote) rather than as a different surface word — the same mechanism the project uses for the Tetragrammaton (see `divine_names_table_2026-05.md`).

**Rationale for the common-noun exception:** rendering 1 Kgs 11:14's שָׂטָן (Hadad raised up "as an adversary" against Solomon) as ซาตาน would be a clear mistranslation. The lock therefore applies **only** to the celestial-figure occurrences; the ordinary common noun keeps its plain Thai rendering.

## Register (cross-ref, unchanged)

This doc governs the **lexical mapping** only. The celestial accuser's **register** is already locked in `ot_register_policy_2026-05.md` §3.1: plain narrator verbs (ตอบ, กล่าว — no honorific), deferential address to YHWH (ข้าแต่องค์เจ้านาย / องค์เจ้านาย), เจ้า to the human Job. No change.

## Forward protection

- **Zechariah 3:1–2** (next celestial-accuser scene to ship): render הַשָּׂטָן → ซาตาน; add the Layer-2 first-occurrence role footnote (mirror Job 1:6). The wordplay in Zech 3:2 (יִגְעַר יְהוָה בְּךָ הַשָּׂטָן, "YHWH rebuke you, O accuser") should preserve the rebuke-of-the-accuser sense.
- **1 Kings 11:14, 23, 25** (שָׂטָן = human adversary): render ปฏิปักษ์/ศัตรู, never ซาตาน.
- Any future anarthrous שָׂטָן that context marks as the celestial figure: render ซาตาน + `key_decisions` note.
