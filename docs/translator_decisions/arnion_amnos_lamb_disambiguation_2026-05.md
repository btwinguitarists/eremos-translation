# ἀρνίον (apocalyptic-Christological Lamb) vs ἀμνός (Paschal-Lamb metaphor) — Thai disambiguation (LOCKED)

**Status:** **LOCKED 2026-05-04** after end-of-book external sanity-check (ChatGPT + Gemini both verdict FINE; both recommended formalizing this doc).

**Scope:** Two Greek "lamb" lemmas with distinct theological registers, rendered with two distinct Thai compounds — plus one principled exception at REV 13:11 where the simile-vehicle reverts to the generic noun.

---

## The locked rule

| Greek lemma | Theological register | Thai | Primary verses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ἀρνίον** | Apocalyptic-Christological proper-title (Revelation's signature) | **พระเมษโปดก** (royal-prefix "lamb-of-God") | REV 5:6, 5:8, 5:12, 5:13, 6:1, 6:16, 7:9, 7:10, 7:14, 7:17, 12:11, 13:8, 14:1, 14:4, 14:10, 15:3, 17:14, 19:7, 19:9, 21:9, 21:14, 21:22, 21:23, 21:27, 22:1, 22:3 (26 verses) |
| **ἀμνός** | Paschal-Lamb metaphor (sacrificial-substitute / suffering-servant) | **ลูกแกะ** (plain "young-sheep") | JHN 1:29, 1:36; ACT 8:32; 1 PE 1:19 |
| **ἀρνίον** (simile-vehicle ONLY) | Generic-young-animal as the vehicle of a comparison | **ลูกแกะ** | REV 13:11 (the ONE deliberate exception — see §3) |

---

## §1 — ἀρνίον → พระเมษโปดก (apocalyptic-Christological)

In Revelation, ἀρνίον is *the* Christological signature — 24-26 occurrences across the book naming Christ as the slain-yet-standing Lamb (5:6), the seven-eyes / seven-horns Lamb (5:6), the Bridegroom of the wedding-feast (19:7-9), the throne-co-occupant (22:1, 3), the temple-replacement (21:22), the lamp of the New Jerusalem (21:23).

The royal-prefix **พระ** + the Thai-Christian-standard "lamb-of-God" compound **เมษโปดก** preserves the exalted-titular register. Apostrophic uses (e.g., 5:6 climactic-introduction, 21:22 incidental-temple-replacement) all share the same form — preserving the Christological-uniformity the Greek deliberately constructs.

External reviewers (ChatGPT + Gemini, 2026-05-04) both verdicted FINE: uniform **พระเมษโปดก** correctly preserves the apocalyptic-Lamb signature; varying it would dissolve the book-level Christological pattern.

---

## §2 — ἀμνός → ลูกแกะ (Paschal-Lamb metaphor)

The Paschal-Lamb / suffering-servant register uses the distinct lemma **ἀμνός**:

- JHN 1:29: `Ἴδε ὁ ἀμνὸς τοῦ θεοῦ ὁ αἴρων τὴν ἁμαρτίαν τοῦ κόσμου` → `ดูเถิด **ลูกแกะ**ของพระเจ้า ผู้ทรงรับเอาความบาปของโลกไป`
- JHN 1:36: same form, John the Baptist's repeated declaration → **ลูกแกะ**ของพระเจ้า
- ACT 8:32 (citing ISA 53:7 LXX, Philip + Ethiopian eunuch): ὡς ἀμνὸς ἐναντίον τοῦ κείραντος → `ลูกแกะ`
- 1 PE 1:19: ὡς ἀμνοῦ ἀμώμου καὶ ἀσπίλου → `ลูกแกะ` (sacrificial-blood imagery)

The distinction: **ἀρνίον** = exalted-Christological-Lamb (Revelation's enthroned Lamb); **ἀμνός** = sacrificial-Paschal-Lamb metaphor (the Lamb who is led to slaughter). Both refer to Christ; the registers differ.

The Thai preserves the Greek's lexical-distinction with **พระเมษโปดก** (royal, titular) vs **ลูกแกะ** (plain, sacrificial).

---

## §3 — REV 13:11 — the simile-vehicle exception

REV 13:11 contains the ONE deliberate exception in Revelation:

- GK: `Καὶ εἶδον ἄλλο θηρίον ἀναβαῖνον ἐκ τῆς γῆς, καὶ εἶχεν κέρατα δύο **ὅμοια ἀρνίῳ**, καὶ ἐλάλει ὡς δράκων`
- TH: `และข้าพเจ้าได้เห็น**สัตว์ร้าย**อีกตัวหนึ่งขึ้นมาจากแผ่นดิน มันมีเขาสองเขาเหมือนเขาของ**ลูกแกะ** และพูดเหมือนมังกร`

The lemma is ἀρνίῳ (dative singular of ἀρνίον), but the **context is a simile** (`ὅμοια ἀρνίῳ` = "like a lamb"). The vehicle of the simile is a generic young animal — the Beast LOOKS like an innocent lamb. Using **พระเมษโปดก** here would make the Beast appear to be the Christological-Divine-Lamb itself, **distorting Revelation's parody-imagery**: the Beast is the wolf-in-sheep's-clothing (MAT 7:15) — innocent-looking on the outside, dragon-voiced on the inside.

The Thai therefore reverts to **ลูกแกะ** at 13:11 — preserving the parody by using the generic "young-sheep" register.

**Future-proofing:** A future editor scanning ἀρνίον occurrences may attempt to "correct" 13:11 to **พระเมษโปดก** for corpus-uniformity. **DO NOT.** The 13:11 exception is principled and tracks the Greek's simile-vehicle context, not the lemma surface. This corpus-doc is the immune-system marker.

External reviewer Gemini (2026-05-04 §Z) flagged this exact future-proofing concern — though Gemini operated under the assumption that REV 13:11 currently uses **พระเมษโปดก** (it does not; the translator already made the simile-vehicle distinction). Either way, the action — explicit documentation — is the same.

---

## Why this matters

- **Christological-signature preservation.** The 26 uniform **พระเมษโปดก** occurrences form Revelation's strongest book-level Christological-pattern. Lemma-drift (e.g., decaying to **ลูกแกะ** in casual contexts) would dilute the signature.
- **Cross-canon Lamb-Christology.** The JHN 1:29 / 1 PE 1:19 Paschal-Lamb metaphor (Christ as **ἀμνός**, sacrificed-for-sin) and the Revelation-Christological-Lamb (Christ as **ἀρνίον**, slain-yet-reigning) are two complementary NT theological lenses. The Thai distinction preserves both.
- **Parody-imagery preservation at 13:11.** Without the simile-vehicle exception, the Beast's "horns like a lamb" would read as "horns like the Divine Lamb" — exactly the wrong theological signal.

---

## Alternatives considered

- **Uniform พระเมษโปดก for ALL ἀρνίον occurrences (including 13:11)** — rejected: distorts the parody at 13:11.
- **Uniform ลูกแกะ for ἀρνίον AND ἀμνός** — rejected: collapses the Christological-apocalyptic vs Paschal-metaphor distinction.
- **Translate ἀρνίον as "พระเมษ" without the โปดก suffix** — rejected: **พระเมษโปดก** is the established Thai-Christian convention; truncating breaks reader-recognition.
