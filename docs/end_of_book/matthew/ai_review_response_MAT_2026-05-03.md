## Item A: ἐκκλησία → คริสตจักร

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** Matt 16:18 and 18:17 are proleptic, but they are Jesus's own forward-looking community language, not a secular assembly context. The Christian/secular split is principled: Matt 16:18; 18:17 → คริสตจักร, Acts 19 civic assembly → ที่ประชุม.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is; document Christian-vs-secular ἐκκλησία split.

---

## Item B: μετανοέω vs μεταμέλομαι

**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** The inconsistency is real. Matt 21:29 and 21:32 use μεταμέλομαι, not μετανοέω; rendering them กลับใจ collapses the same distinction preserved at Matt 27:3 with Judas. The theological contrast matters especially for later 2 Cor 7:8–10.

**Recommended action:** Spot-revise Matt 21:29 and 21:32 to **เปลี่ยนใจ**; keep Matt 27:3 as เสียใจ or equivalent regret/remorse language. Write `metanoeo_vs_metamelomai_2026-04.md`.

---

## Item C: βασιλεία τῶν οὐρανῶν vs τοῦ θεοῦ

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** Matthew's 51× βασιλεία τῶν οὐρανῶν against 2× βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ is a Matthean lexical signal, not noise. Matt 12:28 and 21:43 are precisely the places where preserving อาณาจักรของพระเจ้า helps readers notice the Greek-side variation.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is.

---

## Item D: Inclusion variants

**Verdict:** MAJOR CONCERN

**Reasoning:** Silent omission of Matt 17:21 and 18:11 while bracketing 23:14 is policy-inconsistent. If the project's stated rule is reader-visible inclusion variants, then absent verses that many Thai readers expect should not simply disappear.

**Recommended action:** Revise Matt 17:21 and 18:11 to bracketed-included verses, matching 23:14. Pre-decide Johannine Comma treatment now; likely Tier 3 or bracket + extended note, not silent-skip.

---

## Item E: ἀμὴν λέγω ὑμῖν drift

**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** Matt 5:18, 5:26, and 13:17 create unnecessary formula drift against the Synoptic lock. The singular σοι at 5:26 should remain singular in Thai, but the formula can still align.

**Recommended action:** Spot-revise 5:18 and 13:17 to **เราบอกความจริงแก่พวกท่านว่า**; revise 5:26 to **เราบอกความจริงแก่ท่านว่า**. No need for "ท่านผู้นี้."

---

## Item F: Aramaic / foreign-name transliterations

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** Matt 6:24 μαμωνᾷ is not functioning like Emmanuel, Golgotha, or Eli/Eli; semantic rendering as เงินทอง preserves intelligibility and the God-vs-wealth contrast. Matt 5:22 ‹ราคา› is acceptable as a one-off foreign insult marker, but the convention should be documented.

**Recommended action:** Lock 6:24 as-is. Write a brief convention note for ‹…›: foreign insult / direct-speech residue only, not all loanwords.

---

## Item G: παρουσία → การเสด็จมา

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** In Matt 24 all four παρουσία occurrences refer to the Son of Man's eschatological coming, so การเสด็จมา is appropriate. Human παρουσία in Paul should not inherit royal register; Thai must be subject-sensitive even when Greek lemma is the same.

**Recommended action:** Lock Matthew as-is. Document παρουσία split: Christological/eschatological → **การเสด็จมา**; human arrival/presence → **การมา**, **การมาเยี่ยม**, or **การอยู่ด้วย** depending on context. Phil 2:12 should likely be "presence/being with you," not "arrival."
