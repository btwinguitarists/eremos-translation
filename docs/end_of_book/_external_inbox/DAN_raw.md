## GEMINI

## Item A — Foreign-monarch register: full ราชาศัพท์ for four Gentile emperors

**Verdict:** MAJOR CONCERN
**Reasoning:** The application of full Thai royal honorifics (ราชาศัพท์) to Gentile emperors in Daniel (like Nebuchadnezzar at 1:1 and Belshazzar at 5:1) accurately reflects the narrator's objective reporting of their earthly sovereignty, which makes God's ultimate supremacy over them even more theologically striking. However, having Ezra strip commoner register from the exact same Persian kings (e.g., Ahasuerus/Xerxes) that Esther and Daniel exalt with full `ทรง` creates a jarring canonical whiplash that undermines the integrity of the Ezra–Nehemiah–Esther–Daniel corpus.
**Recommended action:** Normalize Ezra to match the established Nehemiah/Esther/Daniel policy. Upgrade Ezra's Persian kings to full ราชาศัพท์ in the narrator's voice, and tag the four books together as a unified block.

## Item B — "One like a son of man" (7:13) gets light divine register, while the "man clothed in linen" (10:5–6) is kept deliberately plain

**Verdict:** FINE
**Reasoning:** The asymmetry is textually justified: Daniel 7:14 explicitly grants the "one like a son of man" everlasting dominion and a kingdom, warranting the light divine register (`เสด็จ`/`ทรง`) and the locked NT title `บุตรมนุษย์`. Conversely, the "man clothed in linen" (10:5–6) lacks these explicit dominion markers in the immediate text, meaning a plain-register translation accurately preserves the historical christophany-vs-angelophany ambiguity without forcing an exegetical hand.
**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Maintain the lexical distinction between `บุตรมนุษย์` (the messianic title in 7:13) and `บุตรแห่งมนุษย์` (the mortal idiom in 8:17), and keep the 10:5–6 figure plain.

## Item C — The seventy weeks: מָשִׁיחַ rendered generically as "ผู้ถูกเจิม" (anointed one), not "พระเมสสิยาห์/พระคริสต์"

**Verdict:** FINE
**Reasoning:** Translating מָשִׁיחַ as `ผู้ถูกเจิม` respects the project's optimal equivalence philosophy by translating the Hebrew noun literally in its Old Testament context rather than back-porting the Greek New Testament proper title into the verse surface. The project is acting consistently here: using a descriptive footnote perfectly bridges the gap for evangelical readers without compromising translation integrity, exactly mirroring the descriptive approach taken for doctrinal traditions at John 1:34.
**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Keep `ผู้ถูกเจิม` in the text of 9:25–26 and rely on the existing footnote to acknowledge the Christological reading.

## Item D — The Greek-version Additions to Daniel are excluded with no reader-facing note

**Verdict:** CONCERN
**Reasoning:** While excluding the Apocryphal additions (Prayer of Azariah, Susanna, Bel and the Dragon) is the correct canonical decision per the project's MT-base rules, silent omission is inconsistent with the transparency granted to Esther. Because these additions are massive, widely known, and fundamentally alter the chapter structures in other traditions (e.g., Catholic/Orthodox canons), failing to document their exclusion leaves readers unanchored, especially at the famous 3:23/3:24 seam.
**Recommended action:** Write a reader-facing disposition note at Daniel 1:1 (mirroring the Esther 1:1 note) explicitly stating that this translation follows the Masoretic Text and excludes the Greek additions. Add a Tier 3 (large block) footer note at the Daniel 3:23/3:24 seam noting the Greek insertion of the Prayer of Azariah and the Song of the Three.

## CHATGPT

## Item A: Foreign-monarch register

**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** Daniel's treatment is internally coherent: Nebuchadnezzar, Belshazzar, Darius, and Cyrus are royal figures in court narrative, and Thai narrator-voice ราชาศัพท์ marks rank, not moral approval. Belshazzar's sacrilege in 5:2–4 does not itself require down-registering; Nebuchadnezzar's beast-humiliation in 4:30 is a stronger status-collapse trigger.

**Recommended action:** Ratify Daniel/Nehemiah/Esther as corpus rule; normalize Ezra's Persian kings to match; tag Ezra–Nehemiah–Esther–Daniel together.

## Item B: Son of Man vs. man clothed in linen

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** The asymmetry is warranted. Daniel 7:13–14 is enthronement language: cloud-coming, approach to the Ancient of Days, universal service, everlasting dominion. Daniel 10:5–6 has theophanic imagery but remains identity-ambiguous, so plain register preserves interpretive restraint. `บุตรมนุษย์` for 7:13 and `บุตรแห่งมนุษย์` for 8:17 is a useful lexical disambiguation.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is.

## Item C: Seventy weeks "anointed one"

**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** `מָשִׁיחַ` in Dan 9:25–26 is lexically "anointed one," and the syntax does not require the later title "the Messiah/Christ" on the verse surface. So `ผู้ถูกเจิม` is defensible. The concern is reader-facing: this is a high-salience evangelical prooftext, and the current note may feel too thin compared with the more canonically committal handling of 7:13.

**Recommended action:** Keep `ผู้ถูกเจิม` in text; expand the note to state that many Christian interpreters understand this as messianic and connect 9:26 with Christ's death, while the translation keeps the Hebrew surface generic.

## Item D: Additions to Daniel excluded silently

**Verdict:** MAJOR CONCERN

**Reasoning:** Excluding Prayer of Azariah/Song of the Three, Susanna, and Bel and the Dragon is correct for an MT-based Protestant Daniel. Silent exclusion is weaker than Esther's documented handling and will confuse readers comparing Catholic/Orthodox Daniel, especially at the 3:23–3:24 seam.

**Recommended action:** Add a reader-facing note at Daniel 1:1 or book intro, plus a seam note at 3:23/3:24. State that Eremos follows the Masoretic Hebrew/Aramaic text and therefore does not include the Greek additions.
