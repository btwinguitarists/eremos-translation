## Item B: Translation of דָּת (Law/Decree) and the "Irrevocable" Motif

**Verdict:** CONCERN
**Reasoning:** While tiering `דָּת` based on immediate context (statute vs. specific edict) is functionally sound, failing to harmonize the "irrevocable" motif at 1:19 and 8:8 breaks a critical structural hinge of the book. The narrative relies heavily on the irony that the king cannot undo his own laws; `เปลี่ยนแปลงไม่ได้` and `เพิกถอนไม่ได้` obscure the deliberate mirroring in the Hebrew text.
**Recommended action:** Harmonize the irrevocability motif at 1:19 and 8:8 to a single, identical Thai phrasing (e.g., settle on `เพิกถอนไม่ได้`). Review the five `דָּת` variants to see if they can be tightened into a smaller cluster of 2-3 terms, but prioritize fixing the 1:19/8:8 motif.
