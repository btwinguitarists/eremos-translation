Without making the Trinitarian grammar explicit, the Thai rendering แด่พระเจ้า ... โดยทางพระเยซูคริสต์ risks being flattened into a subordinationist reading by typical readers. Surfacing the deliberate contrast between Jude's Christological-mediation structure and the Granville-Sharp structure (Christ as God and Savior) in 2 PET 1:1 and TIT 2:13 clarifies the Trinitarian theology (Father as Savior, Son as mediator) without compromising Christ's divinity.

Item C: ἐπαγωνίζομαι at JUD 1:3 → ต่อสู้อย่างเข้มแข็ง: Jude's programmatic statement

Verdict: CONCERN
Reasoning: Simplifying the verb to merely ต่อสู้ (as in THSV) strips the morphologically marked ἐπ- prefix and weakens the programmatic urgency of Jude's command to "contend earnestly." However, อย่างเข้มแข็ง ("strongly") feels slightly generic for the specific agonistic imagery in view. The prefix carries an active, relentless pursuit on behalf of the faith.
Recommended action: Spot-revise JUD 1:3 to ต่อสู้อย่างไม่ลดละ ("unrelentingly"). This preserves the grammatical intensifier while matching the urgent, agonistic Pauline register (like 2 TIM 4:7) far better than the current rendering.

Item E: Catholic-Epistle πληθυνθείη greeting verb cross-corpus split

Verdict: CONCERN
Reasoning: While neither rendering is incorrect, the lack of harmonization across the Catholic Epistles' distinctive opening formula is an unnecessary inconsistency. The Greek πληθυνθείη carries a direct multiplicative-passive force ("may it be multiplied"). ทวีคูณ captures this specific nuance more accurately than the comparative/incremental ทวียิ่งขึ้น.
Recommended action: Harmonize to Option A. Change 1 PET 1:2 to ทวีคูณ to match 2 PET 1:2 and JUD 1:2. This locks the Catholic-Epistle opening formula to the tighter lexical equivalent and requires only one verse edit.
