## Item A: Tit 1:5 + 1:7 πρεσβύτερος = ἐπίσκοπος equivalence

**⚠ DIVERGENCE — concern level only.** Both AIs agree on the action (lock main text + add 1:7 thai_summary clarifying same-office identity). They differ only in whether the unannotated text reads cleanly enough on its own (ChatGPT skeptical; Gemini confident).

### ChatGPT
**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** The shared stem in **ผู้ปกครอง** / **ผู้ปกครองดูแล** probably preserves enough lexical relationship, while still reflecting the Greek's two-term pattern in 1:5 and 1:7. The real risk is reader inference: without a note, Thai readers may assume two offices.

**Recommended action:** Keep main text as-is; add Tit 1:7 `thai_summary` explicitly identifying ἐπίσκοπος as the same office as πρεσβύτερος in v.5, with emphasis on oversight function.

### Gemini
**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** Using two distinct lexemes that share a stem (**ผู้ปกครอง** / **ผู้ปกครองดูแล**) perfectly balances structural equivalence with lexical distinctness. It mirrors the Greek's deliberate use of two distinct terms (πρεσβυτέρους / ἐπίσκοπον) in the same paragraph without flattening them into a single word, which would erase textual data.

**Recommended action:** Lock the text as-is. Add the proposed `thai_summary` to Tit 1:7 to explicitly connect the two offices for the reader, preventing ecclesiological confusion while maintaining the lexical integrity of the translation.

## Item B: Tit 1:11 / 1:12 / 1:13 / 2:3 §J metaphor-flattening cluster

**⚠ DIVERGENCE on Tit 1:11 only.** ChatGPT FINE on all four; Gemini wants **ห้ามไม่ให้พูด → ปิดปาก** spot-revision at 1:11 to preserve the ἐπιστομίζω mouth-imagery. Items 1:12, 1:13, 2:3 ratified by both. Ben to decide on 1:11.

### ChatGPT
**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** The four renderings preserve the semantic force without importing awkward or misleading Thai imagery. For 1:11, **ห้ามไม่ให้พูด** is accurate, though **ปิดปาก** would preserve more vehicle force; however, it may sound harsher or more colloquial than intended. For 1:12, **คนตะกละและเกียจคร้าน** rightly preserves the moral stereotype without making the line cartoonish.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Optionally document 1:11 as "vehicle intentionally flattened despite possible **ปิดปาก** alternative."

### Gemini
**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** While 1:12, 1:13, and 2:3 cleanly pass the §J test by avoiding incongruous or cartoonish imagery in Thai, 1:11's ἐπιστομίζω ("muzzle") flattens the metaphor slightly too much. Moving to **ปิดปาก** ("shut the mouth" / "silence") preserves the localized "mouth" imagery and rhetorical force better than the purely abstract **ห้ามไม่ให้พูด**, bridging the gap between literal physical restraint and pure concept.

**Recommended action:** Spot-revise Tit 1:11 to **ปิดปาก**. Lock 1:12, 1:13, and 2:3 as-is.
