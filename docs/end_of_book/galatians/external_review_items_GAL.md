## Item A — πίστις Χριστοῦ: objective vs subjective genitive (Galatians is the entry-point for the entire Pauline corpus)

**The pattern:** Galatians is the FIRST FULL Pauline epistle the project has shipped. The genitive in `πίστις Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ` (and its variants `πίστις Χριστοῦ`, `πίστις τοῦ υἱοῦ τοῦ θεοῦ`) is the most-debated single phrase in modern Pauline scholarship — objective ("faith IN Christ", traditional Reformed/Lutheran reading; NIV/ESV/CSB) vs subjective ("faith/faithfulness OF Christ", N.T. Wright / Richard Hays / Douglas Campbell / New Perspective). The translation chose **OBJECTIVE GENITIVE uniformly** across all 6 GAL occurrences:

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| GAL 2:16a | διὰ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ | **โดยความเชื่อในพระเยซูคริสต์** |
| GAL 2:16b | ἐκ πίστεως Χριστοῦ | **โดยความเชื่อในพระคริสต์** |
| GAL 2:20 | ἐν πίστει ζῶ τῇ τοῦ υἱοῦ τοῦ θεοῦ | **ด้วยความเชื่อในพระบุตรของพระเจ้า** |
| GAL 3:22 | ἐκ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ | **โดยทางความเชื่อในพระเยซูคริสต์** |
| GAL 3:26 | διὰ τῆς πίστεως ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ | **โดยทางความเชื่อในพระเยซูคริสต์** |

The 2:16 verse-level decision explicitly names both readings + chooses objective on RULES §0 evangelical-Protestant alignment grounds. **No corpus doc exists yet** locking the policy.

**Cleanest counterexample for the subjective reading — GAL 2:20:**
- GK: `ἐν πίστει ζῶ τῇ τοῦ υἱοῦ τοῦ θεοῦ τοῦ ἀγαπήσαντός με καὶ παραδόντος ἑαυτὸν ὑπὲρ ἐμοῦ`
- TH (current, objective): `ข้าพเจ้าดำเนินด้วย**ความเชื่อในพระบุตรของพระเจ้า** ผู้ทรงรักข้าพเจ้า และทรงสละพระองค์เองเพื่อข้าพเจ้า`
- Subjective alternative: `ข้าพเจ้าดำเนินด้วย**ความซื่อสัตย์ของพระบุตรของพระเจ้า** ...` ("by the faithfulness of the Son of God who loved me and gave himself for me")

The grammatical relative clause (ὁ ἀγαπήσας με, ὁ παραδοὺς ἑαυτόν) describes Christ's own faithful self-giving — strong contextual support for the subjective reading specifically at 2:20.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the project's objective-genitive default sound for a CC0 evangelical-Protestant Thai Bible? (Specifically: should at least 2:20 be marked as a possible exception, since the immediately following relative clauses describe Christ's own faithful action, not the believer's faith-IN-him?)
2. Should there be a corpus-doc + footer-note treatment that acknowledges the New Perspective subjective-reading without requiring re-translation? Critically: this needs locking BEFORE Romans 3:22, 3:26, Phil 3:9 — the same πίστις-Χριστοῦ-genitive recurs in dense theological clusters.

## Item B — δικαιόω → ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม: forensic-declarative vs Catholic-leaning "made righteous"

**The pattern:** GAL has **9 δικαιόω + 4 δικαιοσύνη** uses, all uniformly rendered with the explicitly forensic-declarative Thai construction **ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** ("be DECLARED righteous") rather than the older THSV1971 / Catholic-leaning **ทำให้ชอบธรรม** ("MADE righteous", suggesting infused-righteousness). This is a Reformational doctrinal-translation choice as load-bearing as ἐκκλησία → คริสตจักร.

**The 5-syllable rendering used in Pauline justification verses:**
- GAL 2:16 GK: `οὐ δικαιοῦται ἄνθρωπος ἐξ ἔργων νόμου ἐὰν μὴ διὰ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ`
- TH: `มนุษย์ไม่ได้**ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม**โดยการประพฤติตามธรรมบัญญัติ แต่โดยความเชื่อในพระเยซูคริสต์`
- GAL 3:11 GK: `ἐν νόμῳ οὐδεὶς δικαιοῦται παρὰ τῷ θεῷ`
- TH: `ไม่มีผู้ใด**ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม**ต่อพระพักตร์พระเจ้าโดยธรรมบัญญัติ`
- GAL 5:4 (conative present preserved): GK `οἵτινες ἐν νόμῳ δικαιοῦσθε` → TH `พวกท่านที่กำลังพยายาม**ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม**โดยธรรมบัญญัติ`

**Active form (God as subject):** GAL 3:8 `ἐκ πίστεως δικαιοῖ τὰ ἔθνη ὁ θεὸς` → `พระเจ้าจะ**ทรงประกาศให้คนต่างชาติเป็นผู้ชอบธรรม**โดยความเชื่อ`. Abstract noun δικαιοσύνη → **ความชอบธรรม** uniformly (2:21, 3:21, 5:5).

**Two questions:**
1. Is **ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** (5 syllables, Reformational forensic-declarative) the right Pauline Thai — or is the compact THSV-style **นับว่าเป็นผู้ชอบธรรม** (4 syllables) preferable for reading flow?
2. Lift to corpus doc before Rom 3:21–4:25 (12+ δικαιόω uses in 35 verses)?

## Item C — νόμος multi-sense single-rendering (ธรรมบัญญัติ across all four Pauline senses)

**The pattern:** GAL has **34 νόμος occurrences across 26 verses** — by far the densest νόμος-text in the NT, with at least 4 distinct Pauline senses, **all rendered ธรรมบัญญัติ uniformly** to preserve Paul's deliberate punning power.

| Sense | Greek (GAL example) | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| Mosaic Torah (Sinai law-code) | 3:17 (430 years after promise) | **ธรรมบัญญัติ** |
| Pentateuch / Torah-as-canonical-text | 4:21b (`τὸν νόμον οὐκ ἀκούετε`) | **ธรรมบัญญัติ** |
| Principle / governing-pattern (positive Christ-pattern) | 6:2 (`νόμον τοῦ Χριστοῦ`) | **ธรรมบัญญัติของพระคริสต์** |
| Implicit "principle of" (witty closer) | 5:23 (`κατὰ τῶν τοιούτων οὐκ ἔστιν νόμος`) | **ธรรมบัญญัติย่อมไม่มี...** |

**Cleanest evidence of the deliberate punning — GAL 4:21:**
- GK: `Λέγετέ μοι, οἱ ὑπὸ νόμον θέλοντες εἶναι, τὸν νόμον οὐκ ἀκούετε;`
- TH: `จงบอกข้าพเจ้าเถิด พวกท่านที่ต้องการอยู่ภายใต้**ธรรมบัญญัติ** พวกท่านไม่ได้ฟัง**ธรรมบัญญัติ**นั้นหรือ?`

In one sentence νόμος occurs twice — first as **Mosaic-law-system to be subjected to**, then as **Pentateuch-text to listen to**. The single rendering preserves the rhetorical-pivot.

**Reframing — GAL 6:2:** GK `ἀναπληρώσετε τὸν νόμον τοῦ Χριστοῦ` → TH `ทำให้**ธรรมบัญญัติของพระคริสต์**สำเร็จลุล่วง` (same word, now Christ-pattern of self-giving love).

**Question:** Is the single-rendering ธรรมบัญญัติ across all 4 senses the right call for the Pauline corpus? It preserves Paul's punning, but a Thai reader cannot SURFACE-distinguish "the Mosaic-yoke" (5:18) from "the law of Christ" (6:2) without context. Alternative: mark the Christ-positive sense (**พระธรรมบัญญัติของพระคริสต์** royal-prefixed at 6:2, or paraphrastic **หลักการของพระคริสต์**) — both lose the lexical-link Paul deliberately makes. **Critical for forward-compounding into Romans 7:21–8:2 (5 νόμος senses cluster in 2 verses).**

## Item D — στοιχεῖα τοῦ κόσμου = หลักการ (basic principles, not elemental spirits)

**The textual question:** GAL 4:3, 4:9, and the συστοιχέω cognate at 4:25 use the famously-contested στοιχεῖα τοῦ κόσμου. Three exegetical readings:
1. **Basic principles** — elementary religious systems (Jewish-law and pagan ritual alike). BSB / ESV / NIV / NASB majority.
2. **Physical elements** — the four elements of Greek philosophy.
3. **Elemental spirits** — personified spiritual powers (angelic beings) governing pre-Christian humanity. N. T. Wright, J. L. Martyn, increasingly favored in apocalyptic-Paul readings.

The translation chose **option (1)**:

- GAL 4:3 GK: `οὕτως καὶ ἡμεῖς, ὅτε ἦμεν νήπιοι, ὑπὸ τὰ στοιχεῖα τοῦ κόσμου ἤμεθα δεδουλωμένοι`
- TH: `เช่นเดียวกัน เมื่อเราทั้งหลายยังเป็นเด็ก เราก็ถูกครอบงำให้เป็นทาสภายใต้**หลักการพื้นฐานของโลก**`
- GAL 4:9 GK: `πῶς ἐπιστρέφετε πάλιν ἐπὶ τὰ ἀσθενῆ καὶ πτωχὰ στοιχεῖα`
- TH: `เหตุใดท่านจึงหันกลับไปยัง**หลักการที่อ่อนแอและไร้ประโยชน์**เช่นนั้นอีก`

**The polemical pivot at 4:9** ("weak and beggarly στοιχεῖα") works smoothly with หลักการ ("principles") — basic-principles can be weak-and-beggarly. But option (3) "elemental spirits" would also work polemically — and would match the chapter's CONTEXT better (vv.8 Galatians once enslaved to "things which by nature are not gods"; v.10 they observe special days/months/seasons — exactly the kind of practice ancient peoples associated with elemental-spirits-governing-time).

**Two questions:**
1. Is **หลักการ** (option 1: basic principles) the right Pauline-corpus default, OR should this letter trigger reconsideration toward option (3) elemental-spirits given the immediate context (vv.8-10 pagan-deity-worship → calendrical-observance)?
2. Either way, this needs a corpus-level lock before Colossians 2:8 + 2:20 (where στοιχεῖα τοῦ κόσμου recurs in a Christ-vs-cosmic-powers polemic that some commentators argue REQUIRES the elemental-spirits reading).

## Item E — GAL 6:16 "the Israel of God" — three-way exegetical fork preserved as ambiguous

**The textual question:** GAL 6:16 is one of the most-debated single phrases in modern Pauline-Israel-theology:

- GK: `καὶ ὅσοι τῷ κανόνι τούτῳ στοιχήσουσιν, εἰρήνη ἐπ’αὐτοὺς καὶ ἔλεος, **καὶ ἐπὶ τὸν Ἰσραὴλ τοῦ θεοῦ**`
- TH: `และทุกคนที่ดำเนินตามมาตรฐานนี้ ขอสันติสุขและพระเมตตาจงมีแก่พวกเขา **และแก่อิสราเอลของพระเจ้า**`

Three interpretations, hinging on the function of the second καί:

1. **Epexegetic** ("namely, the Israel of God") — believers (Jew + Gentile alike) ARE "the Israel of God" = Israel-redefined-around-Christ. Calvin, Luther.
2. **Additive** ("and ALSO upon the Israel of God") — distinguishes cross-followers AND a separate "Israel of God" (= ethnic Jewish-Christians OR ethnic Israel per Rom 11:25-26). Dispensationalist tradition; N. T. Wright (modified).
3. **Adversative** (rare) — implicit prayer-of-mercy on still-unbelieving ethnic Israel.

The current Thai **และ** preserves the Greek ambiguity. The 6:16 verse-level Notes acknowledge "FAMOUSLY-DEBATED PHRASE."

**Two questions:**
1. Is the deliberately-ambiguous-Thai (และ matching the Greek καί's flexibility) the right strategy here? Or should the project pick one reading + add a footer-note acknowledging the alternative?
2. If picking: which reading aligns with RULES §0 (evangelical-Protestant) for a CC0 Thai Bible? The traditional Reformed/Lutheran reading is (1) epexegetic; the dispensationalist reading is (2) additive-distinguishing.

## Item F — σάρξ multi-sense single-rendering (เนื้อหนัง across 6 distinct Pauline senses)

**The pattern:** GAL has 17 σάρξ uses across 13 verses, all rendered uniformly **เนื้อหนัง** ("flesh"). This is the entry-point for the dominant Pauline ethical-negative sense (σάρξ = sinful-nature opposed to Spirit) — distinct from the Johannine anthropological + eucharistic senses. The single-rendering preserves the Greek lemma-link but loses surface-distinguishability between very different Thai senses:

| Sense | GAL example | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| Hebraic idiom "flesh-and-blood" (= human beings) | 1:16 (`σαρκὶ καὶ αἵματι`) | **เนื้อหนังและเลือด** |
| Anthropological-neutral "in the flesh" (bodily existence) | 2:20 (`νῦν ζῶ ἐν σαρκί`) | **ในเนื้อหนัง** |
| Bodily illness (Paul's affliction) | 4:13 (`ἀσθένειαν τῆς σαρκός`) | **ทางเนื้อหนัง** |
| Outward-physical (circumcision marker) | 6:13 (`ἐν τῇ ὑμετέρᾳ σαρκὶ καυχήσωνται`) | **ในเนื้อหนัง** |
| Mode-of-birth contrast (κατὰ σάρκα vs δι' ἐπαγγελίας) | 4:23, 4:29 | **ตามเนื้อหนัง** |
| **Ethical-negative "the flesh" (sinful nature opposing Spirit)** | 5:13, 5:16, 5:17 (×2), 5:19, 5:24, 6:8 | **เนื้อหนัง** |

**The polemical Spirit-vs-flesh climax — GAL 5:17:**
- GK: `ἡ γὰρ σὰρξ ἐπιθυμεῖ κατὰ τοῦ πνεύματος, τὸ δὲ πνεῦμα κατὰ τῆς σαρκός, ταῦτα γὰρ ἀλλήλοις ἀντίκειται`
- TH: `เพราะ**เนื้อหนัง**มีความปรารถนาขัดกับพระวิญญาณ และพระวิญญาณก็ขัดกับ**เนื้อหนัง** ทั้งสองนี้ต่างขัดแย้งกันและกัน`

Versus the neutral-bodily 2:20:
- GK: `ὃ δὲ νῦν ζῶ ἐν σαρκί, ἐν πίστει ζῶ τῇ τοῦ υἱοῦ τοῦ θεοῦ`
- TH: `ชีวิตที่ข้าพเจ้ากำลังดำเนินอยู่**ในเนื้อหนัง**นี้ ข้าพเจ้าดำเนินด้วยความเชื่อในพระบุตรของพระเจ้า`

**Same Thai word, opposite theological valence.** Greek itself does this; Thai mirrors it.

**Question:** Is the single-rendering policy (เนื้อหนัง everywhere) the right call for the Pauline corpus going forward to Romans 7-8 (the densest σάρξ text in NT, with the "I" of Rom 7 famously oscillating between neutral-anthropological and ethical-negative σάρξ within consecutive verses)? Or should the project mark the ethical-negative use distinctively (e.g., **เนื้อหนังที่ตกในบาป** "sinful flesh") at the dominant Pauline-ethical occurrences (5:13/16/17/19/24, 6:8) to help Thai readers track the polemical Spirit-vs-flesh contrast?

## Item G — παιδαγωγός εἰς Χριστόν: temporal vs directional (3:24)

**The textual question:** GAL 3:24's `ὁ νόμος παιδαγωγὸς ἡμῶν γέγονεν εἰς Χριστόν` has a famous εἰς + accusative ambiguity:

1. **Temporal** ("until Christ [arrives]") — the law's pedagogue-role TERMINATED at Christ's coming. NIV/ESV/CSB.
2. **Directional** ("to Christ" / "leading us to Christ") — the law's pedagogue-role escorted us toward Christ. KJV / older Reformed expository tradition. THSV1971.

The translation chose **temporal**:
- GK: `ὥστε ὁ νόμος παιδαγωγὸς ἡμῶν γέγονεν εἰς Χριστόν, ἵνα ἐκ πίστεως δικαιωθῶμεν`
- TH (current, temporal): `ดังนั้นธรรมบัญญัติจึงได้กลายมาเป็น**ผู้ดูแลควบคุมของเรา จนกว่าจะถึงพระคริสต์** เพื่อเราจะได้ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรมโดยความเชื่อ`
- Directional alternative: `... ผู้ดูแลควบคุมของเรา **เพื่อนำเราไปถึงพระคริสต์** ...`

The 3:24 KD acknowledges both readings. The cultural-context (Greco-Roman παιδαγωγός = household-slave who ESCORTED a child to school) suggests the directional reading is the more vivid match to the metaphor's source-domain. But the salvation-historical argument-arc (3:23 "before faith came" → 3:25 "now that faith has come, no longer under παιδαγωγός") clearly favors the temporal reading.

**Question:** Is the temporal reading the right Pauline-corpus default? It will compound into the related εἰς + accusative ambiguity at Romans 10:4 (`τέλος γὰρ νόμου Χριστὸς εἰς δικαιοσύνην παντὶ τῷ πιστεύοντι` — "Christ is the τέλος of the law" — where τέλος itself has temporal "end" vs teleological "goal" ambiguity).

## Item H — divine vs human πατήρ register split (the 4:1-7 adoption climax)

**The pattern:** GAL has 7 πατήρ-cluster occurrences with a principled register split: divine πατήρ → **พระบิดา** (royal pระ-), human-paternal in Greco-Roman analogy → **บิดา** (plain). Same letter, both registers:

- GAL 1:1: `θεοῦ πατρός` → **พระเจ้าพระบิดา** (divine; royal)
- GAL 1:4: `τοῦ θεοῦ καὶ πατρὸς ἡμῶν` → **ของพระเจ้าและพระบิดาของเรา** (Granville Sharp; royal)
- GAL 4:2: `ἄχρι τῆς προθεσμίας τοῦ πατρός` → **จนกว่าจะถึงเวลาที่บิดากำหนดไว้** (Greco-Roman household-father analogy; plain)
- GAL 4:6: `Αββα ὁ πατήρ` → **อับบา พระบิดา** (Aramaic transliteration + royal Greek apposition)

**Question:** Is the divine/human register split the right call for the Pauline corpus? The 4:2 plain-register **บิดา** is critical — the analogy is to a normal Greco-Roman father setting a date in a will, NOT the Father of the Trinity. If marked with royal pระ-, the analogy collapses (and the chapter's pivot at v.6 from "minor heir under guardians" to "child crying Abba Father" loses its rhetorical climax). Anything sounding off-register for a Thai-evangelical-Pauline-reader-ear in the broader letter — narrator-κύριος (5×), divine/human πατήρ, ἀδελφοί (11×), or the Pauline epistolary register generally?
