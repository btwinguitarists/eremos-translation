## Item A — δικαιόω in James 2:21/24/25 vs the Pauline forensic-declarative lock

**The pattern:** James 2:14–26 contains the famous faith-and-works passage. δικαιόω occurs **3×** in 2:21, 2:24, 2:25, plus the cognate λογίζομαι quoting Gen 15:6 LXX at 2:23. The current Thai uses **นับว่าชอบธรรม** (the **นับ** verb-stem) for δικαιόω — **but the locked Pauline-corpus rendering is ทรงประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** (the **ประกาศ** verb-stem).

| Verse | Greek | Thai (current) | Comply with Pauline lock? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2:21 | οὐκ ἐξ ἔργων ἐδικαιώθη | **ทรงได้รับการนับว่าชอบธรรมโดยการกระทำ ... มิใช่หรือ** | ✗ would be **ทรงถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** under Pauline lock |
| 2:23 | ἐλογίσθη αὐτῷ εἰς δικαιοσύνην (Gen 15:6 LXX, λογίζομαι not δικαιόω) | **ทรงนับให้เป็นความชอบธรรมแก่ท่าน** | ✓ — λογίζομαι correctly uses **นับ** |
| 2:24 | ἐξ ἔργων δικαιοῦται ἄνθρωπος καὶ οὐκ ἐκ πίστεως μόνον | **คนเราได้รับการนับว่าชอบธรรมโดยการกระทำ และไม่ใช่โดยความเชื่ออย่างเดียว** | ✗ would be **ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** |
| 2:25 | οὐκ ἐξ ἔργων ἐδικαιώθη | **ก็ได้รับการนับว่าชอบธรรมโดยการกระทำของนาง ... มิใช่หรือ** | ✗ would be **ถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** |

**The locked Pauline rule** (`docs/translator_decisions/dikaioo_dikaiosyne_family_2026-05.md`, decided 2026-05-01):

> Default: passive δικαιόω → **ทรงถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** (forensic-declarative).
>
> **Critical: do NOT use นับ (count/reckon) for δικαιόω.** `นับ` is reserved for **λογίζομαι** (Pauline-bookkeeping-vocabulary). Collapsing δικαιόω into the same Thai verb destroys Paul's deliberate two-step argument structure (δικαιόω = courtroom-act / λογίζομαι = accounting-act).

The James 2:21 verse-level KD cites the pre-2026-05-01 corpus-precedent ACT 13:38, GAL 2:16, Tit 3:7 with **นับ** — but those verses have all been re-locked to **ประกาศ** under the Pauline forensic-declarative doc. James was translated before the lock was formalized; the audit caught the divergence.

**The crux:** James is **non-Pauline**. The lock's scope says "All **Pauline** δικαιόω-verb." Two stances:

- **Option A — Apply the lock to James** (relock 2:21, 2:24, 2:25 to **ทรงถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม**)
  - **Pro:** Lexical consistency. Same Greek lemma → same Thai verb across the entire NT in identical syntax (passive divine-subject, soteriological frame).
  - **Pro:** Preserves the JAS 2:23 (λογίζομαι → นับ) vs 2:24 (δικαιόω → ประกาศ) lexical distinction across the same paragraph — exactly the structure the Pauline lock protects in Romans 4.
  - **Pro:** Most modern English translations (NIV/ESV/NLT/CSB) DO render δικαιόω uniformly across Paul + James as "justify."
  - **Con:** Makes the Paul/James apparent-tension MORE acute for the lay reader. Both Paul (Rom 3:28 "justified by faith apart from works of law") and James (2:24 "justified by works and not faith alone") become "ประกาศว่าชอบธรรม" with opposite predicates — direct theological clash visible in the same Thai verb.

- **Option B — Keep James as ทรงนับว่าชอบธรรม** (current); document as principled non-Pauline exception
  - **Pro:** James's δικαιόω is doing different rhetorical work — closer to OT/Jewish-wisdom "be vindicated / be reckoned-righteous" (cf. JAS 2:23 quoting Gen 15:6 with ἐλογίσθη). The 2:21/24/25 + 2:23 cluster deliberately echoes Genesis-15 — the Thai นับ-stem unifies that echo.
  - **Pro:** Matches the historic Reformation harmonization-strategy (Paul: forensic-declarative; James: demonstrative-vindication) at the lexical level.
  - **Con:** Two Thai verbs for one Greek lemma based on book-of-origin is a hermeneutical commitment, not a lexical distinction.
  - **Con:** James's Gen 15:6 OT-citation at 2:23 ALSO uses **นับ** (for λογίζομαι) — so within JAS 2:21–25, both lemmas collapse into the same Thai verb. Loses the structural distinction the Pauline lock preserves in Romans 4.

**Two questions:**
1. Should the locked Pauline forensic-declarative rendering (**ทรงถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม**) extend to non-Pauline James? Most modern English translations preserve uniform "justify" across both Paul and James — letting the Greek tension stand for the reader. Or should James be a principled exception (current state) preserving the historic Reformation Paul/James harmonization through the Thai lexical split?
2. If Option A (extend the lock): the JAS 2:21–25 cluster would have **ประกาศ** (δικαιόω at 2:21, 2:24, 2:25) and **นับ** (λογίζομαι at 2:23 quoting Gen 15:6) lexically distinct — exactly the Romans 4 structure. If Option B (preserve current): the JAS 2:21–25 cluster would have **นับ** uniformly — all four occurrences using the same Thai verb. Which preserves James's argument-structure more faithfully?

## Item B — νόμος uniform-ธรรมบัญญัติ across all 12 JAS occurrences vs the Pauline two-rendering split

**The pattern:** JAS has **12 νόμος occurrences** spanning multiple semantic registers, all uniformly rendered as **ธรรมบัญญัติ**:

| Verse | Greek | Thai | Sense |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1:25 | νόμον τέλειον τὸν τῆς ἐλευθερίας | **ธรรมบัญญัติอันสมบูรณ์ คือธรรมบัญญัติแห่งเสรีภาพ** | "perfect law of liberty" |
| 2:8 | νόμον ... βασιλικόν | **ธรรมบัญญัติแห่งพระมหากษัตริย์** | "royal law" |
| 2:9 | ὑπὸ τοῦ νόμου ὡς παραβάται | **ถูกธรรมบัญญัติพิพากษาว่าเป็นผู้ละเมิด** | Mosaic Torah |
| 2:10 | πάντα τὸν νόμον τηρήσῃ | **รักษาธรรมบัญญัติทั้งหมด** | Mosaic Torah |
| 2:11 | παραβάτης νόμου | **ผู้ละเมิดธรรมบัญญัติ** | Mosaic Torah |
| 2:12 | διὰ νόμου ἐλευθερίας | **โดยธรรมบัญญัติแห่งเสรีภาพ** | "law of freedom" |
| 4:11 | καταλαλεῖ νόμου ... κρίνει νόμον (×4 in this verse) | **พูดใส่ร้ายธรรมบัญญัติ ... พิพากษาธรรมบัญญัติ ... ผู้ปฏิบัติตามธรรมบัญญัติ** | the law-as-norm |

**The locked Pauline rule** (`docs/translator_decisions/nomos_pauline_2026-05.md`, decided 2026-05-02):

> ธρρρμ for Mosaic-Torah / Pentateuch / law-as-canonical-text;
> **กฎ** for Pauline-rhetorical νόμος-of-X principle / governing-pattern / inner-norm.
>
> "νόμος-of-X-genitive constructions where X is NOT God-as-covenant-giver (νόμος ἁμαρτίας / θανάτου / πνεύματος τῆς ζωῆς / νοός / Χριστοῦ)" → **กฎ**.

By the lock's criterion, **3 of the 12 JAS νόμος occurrences are precisely Pauline-style "νόμος-of-X-genitive" constructions** that should be **กฎ** under the lock:

- **1:25 + 2:12: νόμος (τῆς) ἐλευθερίας** ("law of freedom") → would be **กฎแห่งเสรีภาพ**
- **2:8 νόμος βασιλικός** ("royal law") — adjectival not genitive, but the same Pauline-style "law-characterized-by-X" semantics

**The crux:** The current James rendering (uniform **ธρρρμบัญญัติ** everywhere) represents a **monolithic-Mosaic stance** — James is read as treating the actual Mosaic Torah throughout, with attributive modifiers ("the perfect Torah, namely the Torah-of-liberty") rather than abstract Pauline-style principles.

- **Option A — Apply the Pauline two-rendering split to James** (relock 1:25, 2:8, 2:12 to **กฎ**)
  - **Pro:** "Law of LIBERTY" (1:25) is rhetorically very close to Paul's "law of the SPIRIT of LIFE" (Rom 8:2) — both are "law-as-internalized-principle."
  - **Pro:** Lexical consistency with the locked Pauline split.
  - **Con:** James is Jewish-Christian wisdom-genre addressing the Diaspora (1:1) and presupposes Mosaic-Torah-validity (2:8–12 explicitly works through Decalogue commands). Reading "law of liberty" as **กฎ** ("principle") rather than **ธρρρμ** ("Torah") may cut against James's Jewish-Torah-positive register.

- **Option B — Keep James as uniform ธρρρμ** (current); document as principled non-Pauline exception
  - **Pro:** Reflects James's wisdom-genre stance: the Torah IS the perfect-law-of-liberty for the Jewish-Christian community living-under-grace. There is one law, with attributive modifiers, not multiple "laws."
  - **Con:** The "νόμος βασιλικός" at 2:8 read as **ธρρρμบัญญัติแห่งพระมหากษัตริย์** ("law of the divine-king") may be pleonastic — any law of God's is by-definition a "King's law"; the attribution dilutes the rhetorical pivot.

- **Option C (hybrid)**: Keep `νόμος-of-X` constructions in James as **ธρρρμ-แห่ง-X** (preserving the perfect-Torah-with-attribute reading) BUT document the principle in a new corpus-doc `nomos_jacobean_2026-05.md` that explicitly distinguishes James's monolithic-Mosaic νόμος from Paul's rhetorical-multivalent νόμος.

**Two questions:**
1. Should the locked Pauline two-rendering split (ธρρρμ for Mosaic-Torah / กฎ for νόμος-of-X-principle) extend to non-Pauline James? The James-distinctive "perfect law of liberty" (1:25) and "royal law" (2:8) and "law of freedom" (2:12) are stylistically close to the Pauline "law-of-X-quality" patterns — but James's overall theological frame is Jewish-Torah-positive rather than Pauline-rhetorical-multivalent.
2. Forward-pertinent: when 1 Pet, 2 Pet, Jude, Hebrews ship, should νόμος default to ธρρρμ across the General Epistles + Hebrews (since they share the Jewish-Christian register), or follow the Pauline split case-by-case? The James decision sets the precedent.

## Item C — πειρασμός deliberate lemma-ambiguity preserved across testing/temptation senses

**The pattern:** JAS 1:2–15 contains the most-concentrated πειρασμ-stem cluster in the NT — the lemma occurs **6×** in 14 verses. The lemma SHIFTS senses between v.2–3 (TEST/TRIAL) and v.13–14 (TEMPTATION). The translator chose to preserve the lemma-link with single Thai rendering **การทดลอง / ทดลอง** across both senses:

| Verse | Greek | Thai | Sense |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1:2 | πειρασμοῖς ποικίλοις περιπέσητε | **เผชิญการทดลองต่าง ๆ** | trials (external test of faith) |
| 1:3 | τὸ δοκίμιον ὑμῶν τῆς πίστεως | **การทดสอบความเชื่อของท่าน** | testing-process (cognate δοκίμιον → **ทดสอบ**) |
| 1:12 | μακάριος ἀνὴρ ὃς ὑπομένει πειρασμόν | **คนที่อดทนต่อการทดลอง** | trial endurance |
| 1:13 | μηδεὶς πειραζόμενος λεγέτω ὅτι Ἀπὸ θεοῦ πειράζομαι | **เมื่อถูกทดลอง อย่าให้ผู้ใดกล่าวว่า "พระเจ้ากำลังทดลองข้าพเจ้า"** | temptation (NOT trial) |
| 1:13 | ὁ θεὸς ἀπείραστός ἐστιν κακῶν, πειράζει δὲ αὐτὸς οὐδένα | **พระเจ้าไม่อาจถูกทดลองให้ทำชั่ว และพระองค์เองก็ไม่ทรงทดลองผู้ใดเลย** | God-untemptable, untempting |
| 1:14 | ὑπὸ τῆς ἰδίας ἐπιθυμίας πειράζεται | **ถูกตัณหาของตนเองชักออกไป** | desire-tempts (verb form) |

The 1:13 KD names the principle:

> "πειρασμός lemma starts at the trial/test-of-faith sense (will shift to temptation-by-desire sense at v.13 — same Greek lemma, same Thai 'การทดลอง,' context disambiguates)."

**The CHALLENGE — James's argumentative-structure depends on the wordplay:**

James argues that **the same EVENT can be a trial-of-faith from God's perspective (vv.2–3) AND a temptation-toward-sin from the desire's perspective (vv.13–14); the moral responsibility lies with the desire, not with God**. Single Thai rendering preserves this argumentative-structure visibly.

The English-translation tradition is split:
- **NIV / NLT / CSB / ESV / NRSV:** "trials" (vv.2–3, 12) + "tempt / temptation" (vv.13–14) — TWO English words reflecting two senses
- **NASB / KJV:** uniform "tempt / temptation" — preserves the lemma-link English-side

**Two questions:**
1. Is single Thai rendering **การทดลอง / ทดลอง** (preserving the πειρασμός-cluster wordplay across trial→temptation senses) the right call for the Eremos corpus, or should JAS 1:13–14 use a distinct Thai term (e.g., **การล่อลวง** "enticement-temptation" or **การประจญ** "trial-by-confrontation") to mark the sense-shift?
2. Should JAS 1:13 add an explicit **thai_summary** clarifying the lemma-shift for Thai readers who may not catch the wordplay without exegetical-help? (Currently no thai_summary on 1:13. Verse-level Notes acknowledge the shift but the user-facing Thai does not.)

## Item D — βασιλικὸς νόμος at JAS 2:8: "royal law" interpretive commitment

**The textual question:** James 2:8 — what does "νόμον βασιλικόν" mean?

- 2:8 GK: `Εἰ μέντοι **νόμον τελεῖτε βασιλικὸν** κατὰ τὴν γραφήν Ἀγαπήσεις τὸν πλησίον σου ὡς σεαυτόν, καλῶς ποιεῖτε`
- 2:8 TH (current): `อย่างไรก็ตาม ถ้าท่านปฏิบัติตาม**ธรรมบัญญัติแห่งพระมหากษัตริย์** ดังที่พระคัมภีร์กล่าวไว้ว่า "จงรักเพื่อนบ้านของท่านเหมือนรักตนเอง" ท่านก็ทำดีอยู่`

The 2:8 verse-level KD names the dual-possible-meaning:

> "Per uW figs-metonymy on βασιλικός: dual-possible-meaning (1) law-of-the-king's-kingdom, (2) law-given-by-the-divine-king. Render 'ธρρρμบัญญัติแห่งพระมหากษัตริย์' — preserves the king-divine-law-source sense, leaving the kingdom-of-God interpretation accessible without forcing it."

**Four readings of βασιλικός in James 2:8:**

- (a) **"royal law"** = the Torah given by the divine-King (current Thai; preserves source-attribution)
- (b) **"the king's law"** = the law of the kingdom-of-God / kingdom-ethic (eschatological-kingdom reading)
- (c) **"the supreme/preeminent law"** = the law that takes-precedence-over-all-others (intensifier reading; cf. NIV "royal law" implying paramount-importance)
- (d) **"the law of the King"** = the law given by Jesus-as-King (Christological reading; cf. Jesus's "love your neighbor" teaching at MAT 22:39)

**Two questions:**
1. Is **ธρρρμบัญญัติแห่งพระมหากษัตริย์** ("law of the divine-king") the right Eremos commitment? It picks readings (a) + (d), and forecloses (c). Alternatives: **กฎอันสูงส่ง** ("supreme/exalted law" — preserves (c)); **ธρρρμบัญญัติของกษัตริย์** ("law of the king" — slightly more neutral). The English NIV/ESV "royal law" preserves the ambiguity by translating **βασιλικός** as an adjective; Thai requires a noun-phrase commitment.
2. Is the construction **ธρρρμบัญญัติแห่งพระมหากษัตริย์** pleonastic? Any law God gives is by-definition a "divine-king's law" — the attribution may dilute James's rhetorical pivot. The James-distinctive force of βασιλικός is "this law [neighbor-love] is the SUPREME / KING-OVER-OTHER-LAWS one." Should the rendering capture that supremacy-register more directly?

## Item E — μοιχαλίδες explicit "พระเจ้า" disambiguation at JAS 4:4

**The textual question:** James 4:4 contains the strongest single condemnation-of-worldliness in the NT, opening with the OT-prophetic-marriage-metaphor:

- 4:4 GK: `**μοιχαλίδες**, οὐκ οἴδατε ὅτι ἡ φιλία τοῦ κόσμου ἔχθρα τοῦ θεοῦ ἐστιν;`
- 4:4 TH: `ท่านทั้งหลาย**ผู้นอกใจพระเจ้า**เอ๋ย ท่านไม่รู้หรือว่า การเป็นมิตรกับโลกย่อมเป็นการเป็นศัตรูกับพระเจ้า`

The translator made a distinctive interpretive choice — added explicit **"พระเจ้า"** to disambiguate the OT-prophetic-marriage-metaphor:

> "μοιχαλίς 'adulteress' as OT-prophetic-metaphor for unfaithfulness-to-God (HOS, JER 3:8, EZK 16, etc.). Render 'ผู้นอกใจพระเจ้า' — preserves the spiritual-unfaithfulness sense; explicit 'พระเจ้า' added to disambiguate the metaphor (Thai readers may not immediately catch the OT-prophetic-marriage-metaphor without it)."

**Three rendering alternatives:**
- **(current) ผู้นอกใจพระเจ้า** ("those-unfaithful-to-God") — explicit metaphor-explanation; access for Thai-readers without OT-background
- **ผู้ล่วงประเวณีทั้งหลาย** ("adulterers/adulteresses") — preserves literal but loses the OT-prophetic figurative-unfaithfulness sense; readers without OT-background may take it as literal sexual-immorality (the corpus standard for π1ορνεία cluster)
- **คนเหลาะแหละ** ("faithless ones") — losses the marital-covenant register entirely

**Two questions:**
1. Is the explicit-"พระเจ้า" disambiguation at 4:4 (**ผู้นอกใจพระเจ้า**) the right call? It supplies the implicit complement that makes the OT-prophetic-marriage-metaphor accessible — but commits to a specific interpretive stance (the μοιχαλίδες ARE the same Christian-believers later named in v.8 as "double-minded sinners"). Some commentators read μοιχαλίδες more narrowly (literal-female-adulterers in James's audience) — the explicit "พระเจ้า" closes that reading.
2. The trade-off: Thai-reader access vs. metaphor-richness preserved. What is the right balance for an evangelical-Protestant Thai Bible without external commentary support?

## Item F — πνεῦμα at JAS 4:5: the difficult-verse interpretive cube

**The textual question:** JAS 4:5 is among the most difficult-to-translate verses in the entire NT. Major commentaries identify a multi-dimensional interpretive cube:

- 4:5 GK: `ἢ δοκεῖτε ὅτι κενῶς ἡ γραφὴ λέγει· **Πρὸς φθόνον ἐπιποθεῖ τὸ πνεῦμα ὃ κατῴκισεν ἐν ἡμῖν;**`
- 4:5 TH: `หรือท่านคิดว่าพระคัมภีร์กล่าวอย่างเปล่าประโยชน์หรือว่า "**วิญญาณที่พระองค์ทรงให้สถิตในเราโหยหาด้วยความริษยา**"`

**The interpretive cube:**

1. **Subject of ἐπιποθεῖ:**
   - (i) τὸ πνεῦμα (the Spirit/spirit) — "the Spirit yearns ..."
   - (ii) God (with πνεῦμα as object) — "[God] yearns toward the spirit ..."

2. **Identity of πνεῦμα:**
   - (a) Holy Spirit (divine-Spirit dwelling-in-us)
   - (b) human-spirit (the human spirit-that-God-caused-to-dwell-in-us at creation)

3. **Sense of πρὸς φθόνον:**
   - (α) negative-jealousy/envy (the human-spirit yearns-with-evil-envy → vice critique)
   - (β) positive-divine-jealousy (the divine-Spirit longs-jealously for our complete devotion → divine-jealousy parallel to OT)

**The Thai locks reading (i)+(b)+(α):** "**the [human-]spirit-that-God-caused-to-dwell-in-us yearns with [evil] envy**" — NIV-aligned.

| English Translation | Reading |
|---|---|
| **NIV** | "the spirit he caused to dwell in us envies intensely" — (i)+(b)+(α) ✓ matches Thai |
| **ESV** | "He yearns jealously over the spirit that he has made to dwell in us" — (ii)+(b)+(β) |
| **NASB** | "He jealously desires the Spirit which He has made to dwell in us" — (ii)+(a)+(β) |
| **CSB** | "The spirit he made to dwell in us envies intensely" — (i)+(b)+(α) ✓ matches Thai |

**The Thai uses วิญญาณ** (without royal **พระ-** prefix) — leaves the divine-vs-human-spirit reading slightly open (Thai **วิญญาณ** is used for both contextually; the absence of the royal-prefix slightly leans toward (b) human-spirit but does not foreclose (a)).

**Two questions:**
1. Is the NIV-aligned (i)+(b)+(α) reading — currently locked in Thai — the right Eremos commitment for this difficult verse? The ESV/NASB read (ii)+(β) where God is the subject yearning-jealously; that is exegetically defensible and theologically richer (parallel to OT divine-jealousy themes Ex 20:5, Deut 4:24). Alternatives:
   - Keep current (i)+(b)+(α) — preserve NIV-aligned reading
   - Re-render to (ii)+(β) — "พระเจ้าทรงโหยหา**ด้วยความหึงหวง**ต่อวิญญาณที่ทรงให้สถิตในเรา" (preserves divine-jealousy positive register)
   - Add a verse-footer-note (Tier 2) acknowledging that ESV/NASB take a different reading
2. The choice of **ความริษยา** ("envy" — negative connotation) vs **ความหึงหวง** ("jealousy/zeal" — register-ambiguous, can be positive divine-zeal or negative human-jealousy): which Thai term better preserves the πρὸς φθόνον ambiguity? The current choice **ริษยา** locks reading (α) negative-envy; **หึงหวง** would preserve both (α) and (β).

## Item G — anointing-with-oil at JAS 5:14: sacramental-vs-medicinal interpretive ambiguity

**The textual question:** JAS 5:14 is the **locus classicus** for the sacrament of anointing-the-sick (Catholic Extreme-Unction / Eastern Orthodox Holy-Unction) AND for charismatic / Pentecostal healing-prayer practice. The current Thai preserves both readings:

- 5:14 GK: `ἀσθενεῖ τις ἐν ὑμῖν; προσκαλεσάσθω τοὺς πρεσβυτέρους τῆς ἐκκλησίας, καὶ προσευξάσθωσαν ἐπ' αὐτὸν **ἀλείψαντες αὐτὸν ἐλαίῳ** ἐν τῷ ὀνόματι τοῦ κυρίου`
- 5:14 TH: `ในพวกท่านมีใครป่วยหรือ ผู้นั้นจงเชิญผู้ปกครองของคริสตจักรมา ให้เขาทั้งหลายอธิษฐานเผื่อเขา และ**เจิมด้วยน้ำมัน**ในพระนามขององค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า`

**Key translation choices:**
1. πρεσβυτέρους τῆς ἐκκλησίας → **ผู้ปกครองของคริสตจักร** ("elders of the church") — corpus-locked from 1 Tim 5:19 + Tit 1:5
2. ἀλείψαντες αὐτὸν ἐλαίῳ → **เจิมด้วยน้ำมัน** ("anoint with oil") — uses **เจิม** (the Christian-vocabulary anointing verb, OT-priestly + prophetic + kingly use); does NOT pre-decide sacramental vs medicinal

The 5:14 KD acknowledges the dual-function:

> "Per uW translate-unknown: oil-anointing dual-function: medical + consecration. Render 'ด้วยน้ำมัน' — preserves the oil-anointing practice (transfers naturally to Thai-Christian practice)."

The 5:15 σώσει τὸν κάμνοντα → **ช่วยผู้ป่วยให้หาย** ("heal the sick") — uses physical-healing **ให้หาย** rather than the soteriological **ให้รอด**. This commits to the physical-healing reading of σῴζω here (one of the verb's senses, attested for healing-context elsewhere in the NT — Mark 5:23, Mark 5:34, etc.).

**Two questions:**
1. Is **เจิมด้วยน้ำมัน** ("anoint-with-oil") the right rendering for ἀλείψαντες αὐτὸν ἐλαίῳ? It is theologically-neutral and uses the established Thai-Christian anointing-verb. Alternative: **ทาน้ำมัน** ("apply oil") — more medicinal-leaning but loses the consecration-register. The current rendering preserves both readings — is that the right call?
2. Should σώσει τὸν κάμνοντα at 5:15 → **ช่วยผู้ป่วยให้หาย** (physical-healing-only) be modified to preserve ambiguity with the spiritual-healing reading (which is also live in the verse — note the 5:15 ἁμαρτίας ... ἀφεθήσεται "sins will be forgiven" pairing)? Alternatives include rendering as **ช่วย ... ให้รอด** (soteriological) or compound **ช่วย ... ให้รอดและหาย** (preserve both).

## Item H — δίκαιος at JAS 5:6: generic-righteous-person vs Christological "Righteous One"

**The textual question:** JAS 5:6 is the climax of the prophetic-imprecation-against-rich-oppressors:

- 5:6 GK: `κατεδικάσατε, ἐφονεύσατε **τὸν δίκαιον**, οὐκ ἀντιτάσσεται ὑμῖν`
- 5:6 TH (current): `ท่านได้ตัดสินลงโทษและฆ่า**ผู้ชอบธรรม** เขามิได้ต่อต้านท่านเลย`

**Two well-attested readings:**

- (a) **Generic "the righteous person"** — singular-collective, like Greek τὸν δίκαιον. The rich oppress and condemn-to-death poor-just-people (legal-injustice + economic-violence pattern of 5:1–6). Modern-commentary majority. Current Thai **ผู้ชอบธรรม** without honorifics → reads generic.
- (b) **Christological "the Righteous One" (Christ).** ACT 3:14 (τὸν δίκαιον ἠρνήσασθε), 7:52 (τὴν ἔλευσιν τοῦ δικαίου), 22:14 (τὸν δίκαιον) all use **τὸν δίκαιον** as a Messianic title for the crucified-Jesus. Reading 5:6 Christologically: "you condemned and murdered **THE RIGHTEOUS ONE [= Jesus]**" — the rich-oppressors' crime climaxes in the corporate-rejection of Jesus-as-the-archetypal-righteous-victim.

**The Thai locks reading (a):** **ผู้ชอบธรรม** without **องค์**-honorific or Messianic-title-marker. To render Christologically would require **องค์ผู้ชอบธรรม** (ACT 3:14 corpus-pattern).

**Two questions:**
1. Is the generic reading (current) the right Eremos default for JAS 5:6? Modern commentary leans generic (the verse is the climax of the social-justice invective, not Christological); but the Christological reading has patristic support and is a live minority-reading in evangelical commentary. The corpus-precedent ACT 3:14 + 7:52 + 22:14 provides the **องค์ผู้ชอบธรรม** template for the Christological-Messianic-title reading.
2. Should the verse have a footer-note acknowledging the Christological-reading possibility (Tier 2 chapter footer)? The verse's interpretive density warrants it; the alternative is letting the bare-Thai stand and let the reader resolve.
