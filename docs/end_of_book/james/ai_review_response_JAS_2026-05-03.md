## Item A: δικαιόω in James 2

**⚠ DIVERGENCE on action — both CONCERN, opposite recommendations.** This is the most consequential decision in the packet. ChatGPT wants James 2:21/24/25 spot-revised to forensic-declarative language to align with the Pauline lock; Gemini wants James locked as-is to preserve the deliberate Gen 15:6 lexical chain and to formalize a principled non-Pauline exception. Ben to decide.

### ChatGPT
**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** James 2:21/24/25 should not collapse δικαιόω into the same Thai stem as λογίζομαι in 2:23. The paragraph itself benefits from keeping ἐδικαιώθη / δικαιοῦται distinct from ἐλογίσθη, especially because James explicitly quotes Gen 15:6 and then argues from it.

**Recommended action:** Spot-revise JAS 2:21, 2:24, 2:25 to **ประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** language; keep 2:23 as **นับให้เป็นความชอบธรรม**.

### Gemini
**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** Changing James to match Paul's forensic **ทรงถูกประกาศว่าชอบธรรม** obscures James's deliberate lexical linkage with Gen 15:6 (นับ / ἐλογίσθη) in verse 23 and forces a Pauline theological category onto James's distinct wisdom argument. However, retaining **นับว่าชอบธรรม** technically violates the strict Pauline lock that reserves นับ for λογίζομαι. Since Eremos prioritizes optimal equivalence, we should recognize that James's usage is demonstrative/vindicatory, not declarative, and he is building a specific rhetorical chain.

**Recommended action:** Lock James as-is (Option B) and write a specific `dikaioo_jacobean_2026-05.md` document formally establishing the principled non-Pauline exception based on the Gen 15:6 internal linkage.

## Item D: βασιλικὸς νόμος (JAS 2:8)

**Both AIs CONCERN.** Both flag **แห่งพระมหากษัตริย์** as overcommitting / pleonastic; both defer to Ben on the final wording. Strong cross-validation that a tweak is warranted. Suggested alternatives: **ธรรมบัญญัติของกษัตริย์** (Gemini) or simpler supremacy-register phrasing (ChatGPT).

### ChatGPT
**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** **ธรรมบัญญัติแห่งพระมหากษัตริย์** overcommits to "law of the divine King" and weakens the "royal/supreme" force of βασιλικός in 2:8. Since the quoted content is Lev 19:18, James's point is not merely source but status: this command has royal/preeminent weight.

**Recommended action:** Ben to decide between keeping current with documentation or revising toward a supremacy-register phrase. I would lean spot-revise away from **แห่งพระมหากษัตริย์** if natural Thai allows.

### Gemini
**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** Rendering βασιλικὸς as **แห่งพระมหากษัตริย์** borders on pleonasm in Thai, as any divine law is inherently from the King. It arguably dilutes James's rhetorical pivot, which is to emphasize that the command to love your neighbor is the *supreme* or governing law over all others.

**Recommended action:** Ben to decide. Present the option of shifting to a slightly more neutral but exalted phrase like **ธรรมบัญญัติของกษัตริย์** (law of the king) or **ธรรมบัญญัติสูงสุด** (supreme law) to capture the supremacy register better than the current heavy-handed source attribution.

## Item F: πνεῦμα at James 4:5

**Both AIs CONCERN — Gemini elevates to MAJOR CONCERN.** Strongest cross-validated revision recommendation in the packet. Both want **ความริษยา → ความหึงหวง** to restore the πρὸς φθόνον ambiguity; both want a footer note acknowledging the ESV/NASB "God yearns jealously over the spirit" alternative reading.

### ChatGPT
**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** The current rendering strongly locks the NIV/CSB reading: human spirit + negative envy. That is defensible, but **ความริษยา** removes much of the ambiguity in πρὸς φθόνον, while the verse is famously difficult and ESV/NASB take God as the jealous subject.

**Recommended action:** Keep the current main-text reading only if Eremos is comfortable with NIV/CSB alignment; otherwise revise **ความริษยา** to a less closed term such as **ความหึงหวง** and add a footer note acknowledging the alternate "God yearns jealously over the spirit" reading.

### Gemini
**Verdict:** MAJOR CONCERN

**Reasoning:** Locking in **ความริษยา** forces a purely negative (human envy) reading, entirely foreclosing the well-supported, contextually appropriate, and theologically rich ESV/NASB interpretation of positive divine jealousy. Using a more bivalent Thai term like **ความหึงหวง** would preserve the ambiguity of πρὸς φθόνον much better, allowing for both the human-envy and divine-zeal readings.

**Recommended action:** Spot-revise **ความริษยา** to **ความหึงหวง** to restore the register-ambiguity. Add a Tier 2 verse-footer note explicitly acknowledging the alternative ESV/NASB structural reading (God as subject yearning jealously).

### ChatGPT
**Verdict:** FINE with one minor concern

**Reasoning:** **เจิมด้วยน้ำมัน** is the right call for 5:14; **ทาน้ำมัน** would over-medicalize the act and lose the ecclesial/prayer context. In 5:15, **ช่วยผู้ป่วยให้หาย** is acceptable because κάμνοντα and the prayer-for-the-sick context are physical, though the forgiveness clause keeps spiritual restoration nearby.

**Recommended action:** Lock 5:14 as-is. For 5:15, keep **ให้หาย** unless you want a slightly fuller rendering; do not use plain **ให้รอด** here.

### Gemini
**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** **เจิมด้วยน้ำมัน** perfectly straddles the line between medicinal application and sacramental/consecration practice, matching the natural ambiguity of the Greek. For 5:15, **ให้หาย** (heal) fits the immediate physical context of the sickbed, while the following clause ("sins will be forgiven") naturally handles the spiritual/soteriological dimension without needing to overload the verb σῴζω.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. It successfully maintains the dual-function ambiguity without over-translating into a narrow theological corner.
