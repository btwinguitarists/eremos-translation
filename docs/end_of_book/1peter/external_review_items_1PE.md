## Item A — 1 Pet 3:19 Christ's preaching to the spirits in prison: lexical-ambiguity preservation strategy

**The textual question:** 1 Peter 3:19 is the **single most-contested passage in the entire NT** for interpretation. Three mainstream readings (per BDAG, Davids, Achtemeier, Schreiner, Jobes commentaries):

> 1PE 3:18-19 GK: `... θανατωθεὶς μὲν σαρκὶ ζῳοποιηθεὶς δὲ πνεύματι· **ἐν ᾧ καὶ τοῖς ἐν φυλακῇ πνεύμασιν πορευθεὶς ἐκήρυξεν**,`

> TH (current): `... ทรงถูกประหารในเนื้อหนัง แต่ทรงได้ชีวิตในพระวิญญาณ **ในพระวิญญาณนั้น พระองค์ได้เสด็จไปประกาศแก่บรรดาวิญญาณที่อยู่ในที่กักขัง**`

**Three interpretations:**
- (1) **Fallen-angels-Genesis-6** preached-to between death-and-resurrection (Calvin / Kelly / Achtemeier; cf. 2 Pet 2:4 + Jude 6 + 1 Enoch 6-21) — Christ proclaims-judgment over the Genesis-6 "sons-of-God" demonic-spirits
- (2) **Dead-humans-from-flood** preached-to between death-and-resurrection (patristic; Roman-Catholic-traditional postmortem-evangelism — would conflict with HEB 9:27)
- (3) **Pre-incarnate-Christ-preached-through-Noah** to Noah's contemporaries while the ark was being built (Augustinian-classical-Reformed; Wayne Grudem's preferred reading)

**Editorial strategy chosen — LEXICAL-AMBIGUITY PRESERVATION:** The translator deliberately renders Greek-grammar-faithfully with no interpretive narrowing:
- **ἐν ᾧ** ("in which") → **ในพระวิญญาณนั้น** ("in that Spirit") — preserves the antecedent ambiguity (πνεύματι 3:18b OR ζῳοποιηθεὶς 3:18b)
- **πορευθεὶς ἐκήρυξεν** → **ได้เสด็จไปประกาศ** (royal-prefix; preserves the *journey-and-proclaim* sequence without specifying *where*)
- **τοῖς ἐν φυλακῇ πνεύμασιν** → **บรรดาวิญญาณที่อยู่ในที่กักขัง** — preserves the spirits=angels-vs-spirits=human-souls ambiguity

**The CONTRAST with 1 Pet 4:6 (next item):** At 4:6 the translator did the OPPOSITE — *narrowed* the interpretive ambiguity by inserting "**บัดนี้**" ("now") to rule out postmortem-evangelism. The two adjacent passages thus have OPPOSITE editorial strategies.

**Two questions:**
1. Is **lexical-ambiguity preservation** the right corpus-default for irreducibly-multi-interpretable Petrine eschatological passages? Or should one of the three readings be made explicit (e.g., adding **"ในยุคของโนอาห์"** = "in the days of Noah" to commit to the pre-incarnate-Christ-through-Noah Reformed reading)?
2. Should there be an explicit footer-note flagging the three interpretations for Thai pastor-readers? (RULES §0 evangelical-Protestant alignment + the irreducibility of the Greek would seem to support footer-note + ambiguous main-text — current strategy.)

## Item B — 1 Pet 4:6 gospel preached to "the dead": explicit disambiguation strategy (opposite of 3:19)

**The textual question:** Adjacent in the letter to 3:19's spirits-in-prison passage, but the **translator chose explicit disambiguation** here in deliberate contrast:

> 1PE 4:6 GK: `εἰς τοῦτο γὰρ καὶ **νεκροῖς εὐηγγελίσθη** ἵνα κριθῶσι μὲν κατὰ ἀνθρώπους σαρκὶ ζῶσι δὲ κατὰ θεὸν πνεύματι.`

> TH (current): `เพราะเหตุนี้เอง ข่าวประเสริฐจึงได้ถูกประกาศแก่**ผู้ที่บัดนี้ตายไปแล้ว**ด้วย เพื่อพวกเขาจะถูกพิพากษาตามมนุษย์ในเนื้อหนัง แต่จะมีชีวิตตามพระเจ้าในวิญญาณ`

**The interpretive-narrowing:** The translator inserted "**บัดนี้**" ("now") into the Thai to convert what reads in Greek as ambiguous (νεκροῖς = "to the dead" — *when?* could be: postmortem-preaching OR preaching-to-now-dead-people-while-they-were-alive) into an explicit "**those-who-are-NOW-dead**" reading. This commits to the mainstream-evangelical reading consistent with HEB 9:27 ("it is appointed for men to die once, then comes judgment" — which forecloses postmortem-evangelism).

**The KD names the reasoning:**
> Per uW figs-explicit: 'dead-ones' refers to those-who-heard-the-gospel-while-alive but have-since-died. Render with explicit 'ผู้ที่บัดนี้ตายไปแล้ว' — disambiguates from a postmortem-evangelism reading (which would contradict HEB 9:27).

**Why this is striking:** The strategy at 3:19 (preserve ambiguity) and 4:6 (narrow to mainstream-evangelical reading) are **opposite**. Both are defensible, but consistency would either preserve-ambiguity at both OR disambiguate at both. The current mixed strategy matches mainstream-evangelical English translations (NIV/ESV/CSB) and reflects an *implicit principle*: **preserve ambiguity when multiple-readings are exegetically-defensible; disambiguate when one-reading is theologically-incompatible-with-the-rest-of-Scripture**.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the **mixed strategy** (preserve at 3:19 / narrow at 4:6) the right Pauline-Petrine-corpus default? It matches mainstream English translations and the project's RULES §0 alignment.
2. Should the implicit principle be made explicit in a corpus doc (`docs/translator_decisions/petrine_eschatological_disambiguation_2026-05.md`) — *preserve when irreducible, disambiguate when one reading is incompatible with broader Scripture*? This would also govern future Hebrews + 2 Peter + Jude eschatological-disambiguation calls.

## Item C — 1 Pet 3:21 baptism: ἀντίτυπον (typology) + ἐπερώτημα (pledge vs appeal) framing

**The textual question:** 1 Peter 3:21 is the **clearest NT non-regenerative-baptism formulation** — Peter explicitly disclaims that baptism is salvific-by-physical-cleansing. Two HAPAX renderings carry significant theological weight:

> 1PE 3:21 GK: `ὃ καὶ ὑμᾶς **ἀντίτυπον** νῦν σῴζει βάπτισμα, οὐ σαρκὸς ἀπόθεσις ῥύπου ἀλλὰ **συνειδήσεως ἀγαθῆς ἐπερώτημα** εἰς θεόν, δι' ἀναστάσεως Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ,`

> TH (current): `**บัพติศมาซึ่งเป็นภาพแทนของน้ำนั้น** บัดนี้ก็ช่วยท่านทั้งหลายให้รอด ไม่ใช่การล้างความสกปรกออกจากร่างกาย แต่เป็น**คำสัญญาของจิตสำนึกที่ดีต่อพระเจ้า** โดยทางการคืนพระชนม์ของพระเยซูคริสต์`

**HAPAX 1 — ἀντίτυπον → ภาพแทน:**
- **ἀντίτυπον** (NT-hapax-here, "antitype / corresponding-figure / counterpart") → **ภาพแทน** ("image-substitute / corresponding-image"). Preserves the Petrine flood-water-as-type / baptism-as-antitype typology.
- Alternatives: **ภาพคู่ขนาน** ("parallel-image") or **ตัวแทน** ("representative") — both lose the type-and-antitype theological weight.

**HAPAX 2 — ἐπερώτημα → คำสัญญา:**
- **ἐπερώτημα** (NT-hapax-here) has TWO well-attested readings:
  - (a) **"pledge / formal-promise"** (rabbinic / legal-Greek sense — a covenantal-vow) — *current Thai* **คำสัญญา** ("formal-promise"); mainstream-evangelical (NIV: "pledge of a clear conscience").
  - (b) **"appeal / petition / inquiry"** (classical Greek sense — an asking-of-God) — alternative **คำขอวิงวอน** ("petition / appeal"); older NRSV ("an appeal to God for a good conscience").
- The translator chose (a) consistent with mainstream-evangelical interpretation.

**The 3:21 KD names the choice:**
> HAPAX ἐπερώτημα (only here NT, 'appeal / pledge / inquiry') → คำสัญญา — preserves the formal-promise sense (one of the contested-meanings; render with the pledge-reading consistent with mainstream-evangelical interpretation). Per uW Translation Issues: Peter is NOT teaching baptismal-regeneration — the explicit 'not removal-of-dirt-from-flesh' qualifier + the 'through-resurrection-of-Christ' basis make clear that faith-in-Christ-confessed-at-baptism saves, not the rite itself.

**Two questions:**
1. Is **ἐπερώτημα → คำสัญญา (pledge/promise)** the right Pauline-Petrine-corpus default? It is mainstream-evangelical but theologically-loaded (turns baptism into a *credal-pledge act* rather than *interrogative-vow*). The alternative **คำขอวิงวอน** preserves more of the Greek interrogative-semantic.
2. Is **ἀντίτυπον → ภาพแทน** the right rendering? Should Peter's explicit denial of baptismal-regeneration ("not removal-of-dirt-from-flesh") get a footer-note for Thai-Catholic and Thai-Eastern-Orthodox readers who might bring different baptismal-theologies to the verse?

## Item D — 1 Pet 2:9 βασίλειον ἱεράτευμα = "royal priesthood": single-concept vs two-distinct-titles

**The textual question:** 1 Peter 2:9 is the strongest NT-Petrine ecclesiology — a composite citation of Exod 19:6 LXX + Isa 43:20-21 LXX, applying the four-fold Sinai/Israel formula to the Christian church:

> 1PE 2:9 GK: `Ὑμεῖς δὲ **γένος ἐκλεκτόν**, **βασίλειον ἱεράτευμα**, **ἔθνος ἅγιον**, **λαὸς εἰς περιποίησιν** ...`

> TH (current): `แต่ท่านทั้งหลายเป็น "**ชนชาติที่ทรงเลือกสรร** เป็น**ปุโรหิตหลวง** เป็น**ชนชาติบริสุทธิ์** เป็น**ประชากรเป็นของพระองค์เอง**" ...`

**The βασίλειον ἱεράτευμα crux:**
- (a) **Single-concept "royal priesthood"** — *current Thai* **ปุโรหิตหลวง** ("royal-priesthood" — adjective-modifying-noun). Reads βασίλειον *adjectivally* (a royal-kind-of-priesthood). Mainstream English (NIV/ESV/CSB/NASB).
- (b) **Two distinct titles "royal-house, priesthood"** — alternative **ราชอาณาจักร และปุโรหิต** ("a royal-realm + priesthood"). Reads βασίλειον as a *substantive* (a royal-house / royal-realm — cf. βασίλειον in classical Greek for "palace"). Earlier NRSV; some Catholic tradition.

The HAPAX βασίλειον (only here in NT as a stand-alone neuter-noun) is genuinely ambiguous in Greek. The 2:9 KD names the choice:

> HAPAX βασίλειον (only here NT, neuter-noun 'royal-something') — per uW: either (1) royal-priesthood as appositional (Exod 19:6 LXX βασίλειον ἱεράτευμα), or (2) two distinct titles 'a royal-realm + priesthood.' Render 'ปุโรหิตหลวง' — preserves the royal-priesthood single-concept reading (mainstream evangelical).

**The CHALLENGE forward — Revelation 1:6 / 5:10 / 20:6** uses the SAME Exod 19:6 root with explicit dual-construction: **ἐποίησεν αὐτοὺς βασιλείαν, ἱερεῖς** ("he made them a kingdom, priests" — βασιλείαν as accusative-noun + ἱερεῖς as plural separate noun). The Revelation parallel-passages would naturally render **ราชอาณาจักร และปุโรหิต** (two distinct nouns), preserving the dual-construction. If 1 Pet 2:9 reads as single-concept (current) and Revelation 1:6 reads as dual-construction (forward), Thai readers will see the lexical-distinction across passages — defensible since the Greek itself differs between the passages.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the **single-concept "ปุโรหิตหลวง"** rendering the right corpus-default for 1 Pet 2:9 βασίλειον ἱεράτευμα? It matches mainstream-evangelical English alignment, but βασίλειον *as substantive* would honor the HAPAX more literally.
2. How should Revelation 1:6 / 5:10 / 20:6 βασιλείαν, ἱερεῖς be rendered to preserve consistency with the 1 Pet 2:9 choice — same single-concept "ราชอาณาจักรปุโรหิต" or dual-construction "ราชอาณาจักร และปุโรหิต"? The Greek there is unambiguously dual.

## Item E — 1 Pet 5:13 Βαβυλών: transliteration (current) vs explicit-Rome (alternative)

**The textual question:** 1 Peter 5:13 contains the most-famous NT-coded-place-language: "Babylon" = Rome. The translator preserved the transliteration:

> 1PE 5:13 GK: `ἀσπάζεται ὑμᾶς ἡ ἐν **Βαβυλῶνι** **συνεκλεκτὴ** καὶ Μᾶρκος ὁ υἱός μου.`

> TH (current): `**คริสตจักรที่อยู่ในบาβิโลน** ผู้ที่ทรงเลือกสรรร่วมกับท่านทั้งหลาย ฝากความระลึกถึงท่าน และมาระโกบุตรของข้าพเจ้าก็เช่นกัน`

**Two editorial choices flagged:**

**§A — Βαβυλών → บาβิโลน (transliteration, current) vs. explicit-Rome:**
- (a) **Transliteration "บาβิโลน"** — *current*. Preserves authorial-coded-language (Peter wrote "Babylon" as code, not "Rome"); the Rome-metaphor is explained in `thai_summary`. Matches NIV/CSB/NASB main-text.
- (b) **Footnote-clarified "บาβิโลน (หมายถึงโรม)"** — adds explicit "(refers to Rome)" in main-text. Matches some explanatory-Bibles.
- (c) **Direct "โรม"** — uses Rome explicitly in main-text. Loses the authorial-coded-language; theologically distorts the OT-typological-resonance (Babylon = the great-pagan-empire; Peter's coded-language deliberately rhymes with Revelation 14:8 / 17:5 / 18:2).

**§B — συνεκλεκτή (HAPAX) → คริสตจักร (insertion) — church-reading vs Peter's-wife-reading:**
- The Greek **συνεκλεκτή** is feminine-singular ("the [feminine] co-elect-one"). Two readings:
  - (a) **The Roman church (collective-feminine for ἐκκλησία)** — *current Thai* with explicit insertion **คริสตจักร** + appositional rendering **ผู้ที่ทรงเลือกสรรร่วมกับท่านทั้งหลาย**. Mainstream-evangelical (NIV/ESV/CSB).
  - (b) **Peter's wife** — supported by 1 Cor 9:5 ("Cephas and the brothers of the Lord" — confirming Peter had a wife). Some early-patristic-readings.

The 5:13 KD chose (a):
> HAPAX συνεκλεκτή (only here NT, fem. 'chosen-together-with') — refers to the Roman-church (collective-feminine for ἐκκλησία). Render 'คริสตจักร ... ผู้ที่ทรงเลือกสรรร่วมกับท่านทั้งหลาย' — preserves the implicit-feminine + co-election sense.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the **transliteration บาβิโลน + summary-explanation** the right Pauline-Petrine-Revelation-corpus default for OT-coded-language? It preserves authorial-intent and the OT-typological-resonance to Revelation, but may obscure the metaphor for Thai-readers unfamiliar with NT-coded-language. Should the main-text gain a footer-note **(หมายถึงโรม)** = "(refers to Rome)"?
2. Is the **คริสตจักร explicit-insertion** for συνεκλεκτή the right rendering? It commits to (a) the church-reading; the wife-reading (b) is mainstream-minority. Worth preserving the Greek-feminine-singular ambiguity (e.g., **ผู้ที่ทรงเลือกสรรร่วมกับท่าน** without **คริสตจักร** insertion)?

## Item F — 1 Pet 3:7 σκεῦος = wife: cross-reference with 1 Thess 4:4 σκεῦος = body DECIDE

**The textual question:** 1 Peter 3:7 contains a σκεῦος ("vessel") metaphor where the referent is unambiguously the **wife** (γυναικεῖος = "of-the-female"):

> 1PE 3:7 GK: `Οἱ ἄνδρες ὁμοίως συνοικοῦντες κατὰ γνῶσιν, ὡς **ἀσθενεστέρῳ σκεύει τῷ γυναικείῳ** ἀπονέμοντες τιμήν, ὡς καὶ συγκληρονόμοις χάριτος ζωῆς, εἰς τὸ μὴ ἐγκόπτεσθαι τὰς προσευχὰς ὑμῶν.`

> TH (current): `บรรดาสามี เช่นเดียวกัน จงอยู่กินกับภรรยาของท่านอย่างเข้าใจ โดยให้เกียรติแก่นางในฐานะ**ภาชนะที่อ่อนแอกว่า** และในฐานะผู้รับมรดกร่วมแห่งพระคุณคือชีวิต เพื่อคำอธิษฐานของท่านจะไม่ถูกขัดขวาง`

**The cross-corpus σκεῦος contrast:**

| Verse | σκεῦος referent | Current Thai | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 Thess 4:4 | BODY (translator's choice) — debated | **ครอบครองร่างกายของตนเอง** ("control one's own body") | (a) BODY — modern majority |
| 1 Pet 3:7 | WIFE (lexically explicit — γυναικεῖος in apposition) | **ภาชนะที่อ่อนแอกว่า** ("the weaker vessel") | unambiguous WIFE |

**The 1TH 4:4 audit (already shipped) flagged σκεῦος as a DECIDE item** — two readings (BODY vs WIFE) — translator chose BODY. At 1 Pet 3:7 the same Greek lemma is used in the **explicit WIFE-as-vessel** sense. The translator preserved the metaphor (**ภาชนะที่อ่อนแอกว่า**) rather than periphrasing.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the **literal preservation of the σκεῦος metaphor** at 1 Pet 3:7 (**ภาชนะที่อ่อนแอกว่า** = "the weaker vessel") the right corpus-default? It reads cross-culturally awkward (modern Thai readers may find "vessel" demeaning to women). Alternatives: **ในฐานะที่อ่อนแอกว่า** ("in the role of the weaker [partner]" — drops the vessel-metaphor); **เพราะนางเป็นเพศที่อ่อนแอกว่า** ("because she is the weaker sex" — Greek-faithful but more interpretive).
2. How should the σκεῦος metaphor be handled across the corpus — preserve at 1 Pet 3:7 (current) + body-rendering at 1 Thess 4:4 (current), or unify the rendering across the two passages? The Greek lexeme is the same; the contextual-referents differ.

## Item G — Petrine πάσχω/πάθημα suffering theology: register-split (Christ-royal vs believer-plain)

**The pattern:** 1 Peter has the densest πάσχω + πάθημα cluster per chapter in the entire NT — **15× across 5 chapters** (πάσχω 8×, πάθημα 7×). The translator implements a register-split that preserves the Petrine-Christological-paradigm:

| Greek | Subject | Thai | Verses |
|---|---|---|---|
| πάσχω (verb) | Christ | **ทรงทนทุกข์** (royal-prefix) | 2:21, 2:23, 3:18, 4:1 |
| πάσχω (verb) | believers | **ทนทุกข์** (plain) | 2:19, 2:20, 3:14, 3:17, 4:19 |
| πάθημα (noun) | both | **การทนทุกข์** | 1:11, 4:13, 5:1, 5:9 |

**The KEY THEOLOGICAL MOVE — 1 Pet 4:13:**
> καθὸ **κοινωνεῖτε** τοῖς τοῦ Χριστοῦ **παθήμασιν**

> ตามที่ท่านมี**ส่วนร่วม**ใน**การทนทุกข์**ของพระคริสต์

The believers' plain-register **ทนทุกข์** is *participatory* in the royal-register **ทรงทนทุกข์** of Christ. The Thai register-split lets readers experience the participatory-Christology lexically.

**The 2:21 cleanest evidence:**
> 1PE 2:21 GK: `εἰς τοῦτο γὰρ ἐκλήθητε, ὅτι καὶ Χριστὸς **ἔπαθεν** ὑπὲρ ὑμῶν, ὑμῖν ὑπολιμπάνων ὑπογραμμὸν ἵνα ἐπακολουθήσητε τοῖς ἴχνεσιν αὐτοῦ`

> TH: `เพราะท่านได้รับการทรงเรียกมาเพื่อสิ่งนี้ เพราะพระคริสต์ก็**ทรงทนทุกข์**เพื่อท่านทั้งหลาย ทรงทิ้งแบบอย่างไว้แก่ท่าน เพื่อท่านจะเดินตามรอยพระบาทของพระองค์`

**Two questions:**
1. Is the **register-split** (Christ-royal **ทรงทนทุกข์** vs believer-plain **ทนทุกข์**) the right Pauline-Petrine-corpus default? It preserves the participatory-Christology elegantly, but the alternative — **uniform plain register** for all human-suffering-context πάσχω uses (including Christ's redemptive-suffering, since the Christ-as-Servant-suffering at 2:21-25 echoes Isa 53's plain-Hebrew register) — is also defensible.
2. Should this be lifted to a corpus doc before 2 Peter and Hebrews ship? Hebrews 2:10 + 5:8 + 9:26 (Christ's sufferings) and 13:12 (outside-the-camp suffering) will compound the editorial weight; locking the register-split here is forward-pertinent.

## Item H — Petrine σωτηρία ψυχῶν + Petrine "now-dead" disambiguation: anthropological consistency check

**The textual question:** 1 Peter has a distinctive ψυχή usage pattern at 1:9 + 1:22 + 2:11 + 2:25 + 3:20 + 4:19 — six occurrences with two distinct senses:

| Verse | Greek | Sense | Thai |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1:9 | σωτηρίαν ψυχῶν | salvation-of-souls (eschatological) | **ความรอดของจิตวิญญาณ** |
| 1:22 | Τὰς ψυχὰς ὑμῶν ἡγνικότες | purify-souls (ethical-process) | **ชำระจิตวิญญาณของท่านให้บริสุทธิ์** |
| 2:11 | στρατεύονται κατὰ τῆς ψυχῆς | war-against-soul (anthropological-conflict) | **ทำสงครามต่อจิตวิญญาณ** |
| 2:25 | ποιμένα καὶ ἐπίσκοπον τῶν ψυχῶν | Shepherd-of-souls (Christological) | **ผู้เลี้ยงและผู้ดูแลจิตวิญญาณ** |
| 3:20 | ὀκτὼ ψυχαί | "eight souls" = eight people (Hebraic-idiom) | **แปดคน** |
| 4:19 | παρατιθέσθωσαν τὰς ψυχὰς αὐτῶν | entrust-souls (entrustment) | **มอบจิตวิญญาณของตน** |

**The principled split:**
- ψυχή (anthropological-soul, the locus-of-salvation) → **จิตวิญญาณ** (5 occurrences, locked corpus-rendering)
- ψυχή (Hebraic-idiomatic "person, individual") → **คน** (1 occurrence at 3:20; Genesis-Noah-flood family)

This is perfectly compliant with `psyche_vs_pneuma_anthropological_2026-04.md`. The 3:20 **คน** rendering matches Acts 27:37 ("souls in the boat" → people).

**Two questions:**
1. Is the **principled idiom-split** (locked **จิตวิญญาณ** for theological-anthropological uses + **คน** for Hebraic-idiomatic counting-souls) the right corpus-default? It already exists in Acts; 1 Pet ratifies it.
2. The 4:19 **มอบจิตวิญญาณของตนแก่ผู้สร้างที่สัตย์ซื่อ** ("entrust their souls to a faithful Creator") echoes Luke 23:46 (Jesus' last words, παρατίθεμαι τὸ πνεῦμά μου). ## Item I — Petrine ἀποκάλυψις/φανερόω as the eschatological-Christ-coming (vs Pauline παρουσία)

**The pattern:** 1 Peter does NOT use **παρουσία** — the Pauline-corpus standard for Christ's eschatological return. Instead, 1 Peter uses **ἀποκάλυψις** (revelation / unveiling) and **φανερόω** (manifest) for the same eschatological-event. Five occurrences, all rendered **การสำแดง / ทรงสำแดง**:

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| 1:7 | ἐν ἀποκαλύψει Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ | **ในการสำแดงของพระเยซูคริสต์** |
| 1:13 | ἐν ἀποκαλύψει Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ | **ในการสำแดงของพระเยซูคริสต์** |
| 4:13 | ἐν τῇ ἀποκαλύψει τῆς δόξης αὐτοῦ | **ในการสำแดงพระสิริของพระองค์** |
| 5:1 | τῆς μελλούσης ἀποκαλύπτεσθαι δόξης | **พระสิริที่จะทรงสำแดง** |
| 5:4 | φανερωθέντος τοῦ ἀρχιποίμενος | **เมื่อพระผู้เลี้ยงใหญ่ทรงสำแดงพระองค์** |

**The lexical-distinction the project's `parousia_christou_2026-05.md` doc should acknowledge:**
- Pauline-corpus **παρουσία** → **การเสด็จมา** (royal-เสด็จ verb-stem; "the coming/arrival")
- Petrine-corpus **ἀποκάλυψις / φανέρωσις** → **การสำแดง / ทรงสำแดง** (revelation / manifestation)

The two Thai-renderings preserve the *Greek-lexical-distinction* even though they refer to the *same eschatological-event*. This is principled — Pauline emphasizes the *coming/arrival* (royal-arrival imagery from Hellenistic civic-reception); Petrine emphasizes the *unveiling/revealing* (apocalyptic-disclosure imagery).

**Two questions:**
1. Is the **lexical-distinction preserved in Thai** (Pauline **การเสด็จมา** vs Petrine **การสำแดง**) the right corpus-default? It honors the Greek-lexemes' theological-emphases. Alternative: unify all eschatological-Christ-coming renderings under **การเสด็จมา** to streamline cross-corpus reading.
2. Should `parousia_christou_2026-05.md` be amended with a **§Petrine-ἀποκάλυψις** sub-section noting the rendering-distinction? Forward-relevant for 2 Peter 1:16 (παρουσία first occurrence in Petrine corpus — would test whether 2 Pet 1:16 + 3:4, 12 παρουσία render with Pauline **การเสด็จมา** or with the Petrine **การสำแดง**).

## Item J — 1 Pet 5:8 διάβολος ὡς λέων ὠρυόμενος: distinctive Petrine adversary-imagery

**The textual question:** 1 Peter 5:8 contains the corpus's most-distinctive personal-devil imagery — the *roaring-lion-prowling-to-devour*:

> 1PE 5:8 GK: `νήψατε, γρηγορήσατε. ὁ **ἀντίδικος** ὑμῶν **διάβολος** ὡς **λέων ὠρυόμενος** περιπατεῖ ζητῶν τινα **καταπιεῖν**`

> TH (current): `จงมีสติและตื่นตัว **ปฏิปักษ์**ของท่านคือ**มาร** เดินวนเวียนไปมาดุจ**สิงโตคำราม** เสาะหาผู้ที่จะ**กลืนกิน**`

**The triple-imagery preserved:**
1. **ἀντίδικος → ปฏิปักษ์** (legal-adversary; "opponent-in-court") — preserves the Job 1:6-12 / Zech 3:1-2 court-prosecutor metaphor.
2. **διάβολος → มาร** (corpus-locked Thai-Christian standard; distinct from **ซาตาน** transliterated-Satan).
3. **λέων ὠρυόμενος → สิงโตคำราม** ("roaring lion") — echoes Ps 22:13 LXX (ὡς λέων ὁ ἁρπάζων) + Ps 7:2 LXX (lion-pursuer).
4. **καταπιεῖν → กลืนกิน** ("swallow / devour completely") — preserves the *consume-utterly* sense (cf. 1 Cor 15:54 catapinō Christological-victory-over-death).

**Editorial assessment:** The Thai preserves all four lexical-images cleanly. Per `adversary_speech_register_2026-05.md` and `spiritual_beings_hierarchy_2026-05.md`, the plain register on the adversary correctly avoids royal-prefixing. The triplet **มาร + ปฏิปักษ์ + สิงโตคำราม** reads with appropriate menace-rhetoric without exceeding the Greek's intensity.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the **plain register** on διάβολος (**มาร** unprefixed) the right corpus-default for the Petrine-and-corpus-wide adversary-imagery? It correctly avoids royal-prefixing per the adversary-speech-register doc.
2. The **กลืนกิน** rendering (consume-utterly) is the strongest devour-verb in Thai. Should it be unified with the corpus's other catapinō uses (1 Cor 15:54 — the SAME verb in Christological-victory context, "death is swallowed up in victory") for cross-corpus lexical-link? Or is the Petrine-pejorative καταπιεῖν (devil-as-devourer) sufficiently distinct from the Pauline-victorious καταπιεῖν (death-as-devoured) to merit a different Thai rendering?
