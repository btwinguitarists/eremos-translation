## Item A — καυχάομαι / καύχησις cluster (chs 10–12) rendered uniformly as ภาคภูมิใจ

**The pattern:** 2 Corinthians has THE densest καυχάομαι-family concentration in the NT — about 29 occurrences across chs 10–12 (the so-called "fool's speech"). The translator renders the family **uniformly** as **ภาคภูมิใจ** ("be proud, take pride") regardless of valence:

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| 10:8 | ἐάν τε γὰρ περισσότερόν τι **καυχήσωμαι** περὶ τῆς ἐξουσίας ἡμῶν | ถึงแม้ว่าข้าพเจ้าจะ**ภาคภูมิใจ**เกินไปในเรื่องสิทธิอำนาจของเรา |
| 10:17 | Ὁ δὲ **καυχώμενος** ἐν κυρίῳ **καυχάσθω** | "แต่ผู้ที่**ภาคภูมิใจ** ก็ให้เขา**ภาคภูมิใจ**ในองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้าเถิด" (Jer 9:24 cite) |
| 11:18 | πολλοὶ **καυχῶνται** κατὰ σάρκα, κἀγὼ **καυχήσομαι** | คนเป็นจำนวนมาก**ภาคภูมิใจ**ตามเนื้อหนัง ข้าพเจ้าก็จะ**ภาคภูมิใจ**เช่นกัน |
| 11:30 | τὰ τῆς ἀσθενείας μου **καυχήσομαι** | ข้าพเจ้าก็จะ**ภาคภูมิใจ**ในสิ่งที่แสดงความอ่อนแอของข้าพเจ้า |
| 12:9 | **καυχήσομαι** ἐν ταῖς ἀσθενείαις μου | ข้าพเจ้าจะ**ภาคภูมิใจ**ในความอ่อนแอของข้าพเจ้า |

**The interpretive crux:** καυχάομαι in Paul carries a **double valence** — *pejorative* when subject is opponents/flesh (10:12; 11:12, 18; "boast" in the bad sense), *redeemed* when subject is "in the Lord" or "in weakness" (10:17; 11:30; 12:9). The translator's uniform **ภาคภูมิใจ** preserves the Pauline rhetorical-inversion (the same verb is BOTH the disease AND the cure across the fool's speech) but reads as more even-keeled than the Greek polemic. A pejorative **อวด** ("show off, brag, vaunt") would land harder at 11:18; ภาคภูมิใจ is uniformly positive in Thai ("take pride, be proud").

**Question:** Is the uniform **ภาคภูมิใจ** the right Pauline-corpus default — preserving the Pauline rhetorical-pun across the fool's speech — or should the translator distinguish pejorative-καυχάομαι (e.g., 11:18 of opponents) with a more pointed **อวด**, accepting the loss of lexical-link with the redeemed-καυχάομαι at 12:9? Will recur Rom 2:17, 2:23, 5:2–3, 5:11; Eph 2:9; Phil 3:3.

## Item B — 2 Cor 4:4 ὁ θεὸς τοῦ αἰῶνος τούτου → เทพเจ้าแห่งยุคนี้ (extending the pagan-deity rule to Satan)

**The pattern:** 2 Cor 4:4 is the only NT verse where Greek **ὁ θεός** (with the standard biblical-divine article-construction) is used metaphorically of **Satan**. The translator applies the locked corpus rule for pagan deities ("**พระเจ้า** is reserved for the biblical God only; non-biblical-god references → **เทพเจ้า**") to this metaphorical reference:

- 4:4 GK: ἐν οἷς **ὁ θεὸς τοῦ αἰῶνος τούτου** ἐτύφλωσεν τὰ νοήματα τῶν ἀπίστων εἰς τὸ μὴ αὐγάσαι τὸν φωτισμὸν τοῦ εὐαγγελίου
- 4:4 TH: ในพวกเขานั้น **เทพเจ้าแห่งยุคนี้**ได้ทำให้ความคิดของผู้ที่ไม่เชื่อตาบอด เพื่อไม่ให้พวกเขาเห็นความสว่างแห่งข่าวประเสริฐ

The verse-internal contrast with v.6 is preserved cleanly:
- 4:6 GK: ὁ θεὸς ὁ εἰπών· Ἐκ σκότους φῶς λάμψει
- 4:6 TH: เพราะ**พระเจ้า**ผู้ตรัสว่า 'ความสว่างจะส่องออกจากความมืด'

**Why this is interpretive-load-bearing:** Most published Thai translations preserve **พระเจ้า** at 4:4 (e.g., THSV, TNCV) — they read the Greek ὁ θεός as a standard construction whose referent (Satan) is established by τοῦ αἰῶνος τούτου, not by lexical-register. The Eremos choice **เทπเจ้า** lexicalizes the Christian/Satanic boundary into the Thai noun itself — preserving the project's strict "พระเจ้า ↔ biblical God" lexical lock at the cost of a slightly-marked rendering that no other Thai Bible takes.

The locked `pagan_deities_2026-04.md` doc was written for *literal* pagan deities (Acts 14 Zeus/Hermes; Acts 17 Athenian "gods"; Acts 19 Artemis). It does not explicitly cover NT *polemical / metaphorical* "god" references — Satan (2 Cor 4:4); the belly (Phil 3:19 ὧν ὁ θεὸς ἡ κοιλία); the antichrist (2 Thess 2:4 ὁ ἀντικείμενος καὶ ὑπεραιρόμενος ἐπὶ πάντα λεγόμενον θεόν). The translator extended the principle, correctly in the project's terms, but the corpus-doc needs amendment to make the extension explicit and forward-binding.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the **เทพเจ้าแห่งยุคนี้** rendering at 4:4 right? It strictly preserves the project's "พระเจ้า reserved for biblical God only" lock, but it's a *minority reading* in the published Thai-Bible corpus. Should it stand for `book-2corinthians-v1`, or shift to **พระเจ้าแห่งยุคนี้** to match Thai-tradition?
2. Should the corpus-doc (`pagan_deities_2026-04.md`) be amended to make the extension to NT *polemical/metaphorical* "god" references *explicit*, locking the rendering for Phil 3:19 (ὁ θεὸς ἡ κοιλία) and 2 Thess 2:4 (the antichrist's self-deification claims) ahead of those books?

## Item C — 2 Cor 12:4 παράδεισος → เมืองบรมสุข (Thai-Buddhist Pure-Land cultural resonance)

**The pattern:** 2 Cor 12:4 is one of three NT παράδεισος occurrences (Luke 23:43; 2 Cor 12:4; Rev 2:7). The translator uses **เมืองบรมสุข** ("city of supreme bliss"):

- 12:4 GK: ὅτι ἡρπάγη εἰς τὸν παράδεισον καὶ ἤκουσεν ἄρρητα ῥήματα
- 12:4 TH: ว่าเขาถูกฉวยขึ้นไปยัง**เมืองบรมสุข** และได้ยินถ้อยคำที่พรรณนาไม่ได้

Cross-corpus check at Luke 23:43 (already shipped + corpus-locked):
- Luke 23:43 TH: วันนี้เจ้าจะอยู่กับเราใน**เมืองบรมสุขเกษม**

Both renderings use **บรมสุข** ("supreme bliss") — the key Buddhist-resonant element. **บรมสุข / สุขาวดี** is in Thai-Buddhist-cultural register the standard term for the Pure Land / heavenly destination, with strong Mahāyāna-Amitābha-Pure-Land associations. The Greek παράδεισος itself is a Persian-loanword ("walled garden, royal park") that LXX uses for Eden (Gen 2:8) and Second-Temple Judaism for the eschatological-blessed-realm.

**The cultural-resonance question:** **เมืองบรมสุข** is theologically-correct AND culturally-engaged — Paul's third-heaven mystical experience lands in a Thai context where "supreme-bliss-realm" is a living religious category. But it imports a Buddhist-eschatological referent into the Pauline text. Alternatives:
- **สวรรค์** ("heaven"; Christian-marked but generic — overlaps with the locked οὐρανός rendering)
- **สถานที่บรมสุข** (place-of-supreme-bliss; descriptive)
- **อุทยาน** (garden/park; literal, preserves the Persian-loanword "walled royal garden" sense)
- transliteration **พาราดีซัส**

**Question:** Is **เมืองบรมสุข / เมืองบรมสุขเกษม** the right corpus-default for παράδεισος — accepting the Thai-Buddhist Pure-Land cultural resonance as a *bridge* (Paul's Greek itself imports a Persian-loanword), or is the resonance too dense (importing a Buddhist eschatological referent into a Christian-eschatological Pauline text)? Will recur Rev 2:7 (τοῦ παραδείσου τοῦ θεοῦ) — cross-corpus consistency required.

## Item D — ὑπερλίαν ἀπόστολοι at 11:5, 12:11 marked with curly quotes ('อัครทูตชั้นยอด')

**The pattern:** Paul's sarcastic-honorific reference to his Corinthian opponents is rendered with **curly quotes** marking the embedded-sarcasm:

- 11:5 GK: λογίζομαι γὰρ μηδὲν ὑστερηκέναι τῶν **ὑπερλίαν ἀποστόλων**
- 11:5 TH: เพราะข้าพเจ้าถือว่าข้าพเจ้าไม่ได้ด้อยกว่า **'อัครทูตชั้นยอด'** เหล่านั้นเลยแม้แต่น้อย

- 12:11 GK: οὐδὲν γὰρ ὑστέρησα τῶν **ὑπερλίαν ἀποστόλων**, εἰ καὶ οὐδέν εἰμι
- 12:11 TH: เพราะข้าพเจ้าไม่ได้ด้อยกว่า **'อัครทูตชั้นยอด'** เหล่านั้นเลยแม้แต่น้อย แม้ว่าข้าพเจ้าจะไม่ได้เป็นอะไรเลย

This **parallels the 1 Cor "Corinthian-slogan" curly-quote convention** (1 Cor 7:1, 8:4, 10:23) which the 1 Cor end-of-book audit (Item C) flagged for corpus-doc lift. Both books mark *sarcastic Pauline embedded-quotation of opponents' self-styling* with curly quotes — a consistent corpus convention worth formalizing.

The 2 Cor "super-apostles" case is **stronger than 1 Cor's slogan-quotation** because the **ὑπερλίαν** ("exceedingly, super-") modifier itself is unambiguously sarcastic in Pauline usage — Paul is mocking the term *as the opponents apply it to themselves*. The NIV/ESV/CSB use italics or quote-marks similarly here. The Eremos choice of curly-quotes is consistent with the 1 Cor pattern.

**Question:** Should the corpus formalize the rule "mark Pauline sarcastic-embedded-quotation of opponents (slogans + self-styling honorifics) with curly quotes" so future books (Romans diatribe sections; Galatians 2:6 οἱ δοκοῦντες "those reputed to be"; Phil 3:2 κατατομή "the mutilation" sarcastic-pun) are consistent? Or does the curly-quote convention cross from translation into light-commentary, and should it be reserved for the most-unambiguous cases only?

## Item E — δοξάζω at 9:13: ถวายเกียรติ vs locked สรรเสริญ — corpus-consistency spot-check

**The pattern:** The locked corpus rule (per `divine_object_praise_verbs_2026-04.md`):

> δοξάζω / εὐλογέω / αἰνέω / αἶνον δίδωμι (object = God) → **สรรเสริญ** (single Thai default)

But at 2 Cor 9:13 the translator uses **ถวายเกียรติ** ("ascribe glory") rather than สรรเสริญ:

- 9:13 GK: διὰ τῆς δοκιμῆς τῆς διακονίας ταύτης **δοξάζοντες τὸν θεὸν** ἐπὶ τῇ ὑποταγῇ τῆς ὁμολογίας ὑμῶν
- 9:13 TH: โดยบทพิสูจน์ของการรับใช้นี้ พวกเขาจะ**ถวายเกียรติแด่พระเจ้า** เพราะการเชื่อฟัง...

Other 2 Cor δόξα-cluster occurrences use the noun **พระเกียรติ** consistently (1:20 πρὸς δόξαν → **เพื่อพระเกียรติสิริ**; 4:15 εἰς τὴν δόξαν → **เพื่อพระเกียรติของพระเจ้า**). **The 9:13 case is the only verb-form (δοξάζω) with God-as-object in 2 Cor.**

**The case for the deviation:** ถวายเกียรติ ("ascribe / offer glory") is contextually-fitting at 9:13 — the participial construction is "[they] glorifying God *for* the obedience of your confession", which is more naturally a *cognitive ascription of glory* than a *vocal praise-act*. The locked สรรเสริญ leans toward vocal-doxological praise. ถวายเกียรติ may license here for the same reason that 1:20 + 4:15 use the noun-phrase พระเกียรติ (which is the doxological-noun cognate of ถวายเกียรติ, not of สรรเสริญ).

**Question:** Is the 9:13 ถวายเกียรติ deviation from the locked สรรเสริญ defensible on cognitive-vs-vocal-praise grounds (linking it lexically to the noun-form พระเกียรติ used in 1:20 + 4:15), or should it be revised to สรรเสริญ to comply strictly with the lock? If the deviation is principled, the corpus-doc needs an explicit sub-rule for "δοξάζω-as-cognitive-ascription (typically participial, with διά / ἐπί / περί + cause) → ถวายเกียรติ; δοξάζω-as-vocal-praise → สรรเสริญ."

## Item F — καταλλαγή / καταλλάσσω at 5:18–20 → คืνดี cluster (corpus-doc lift confirmation)

**The pattern:** The 5:18–20 cluster is the FIRST DENSE NT καταλλαγή occurrence — the Pauline locus of "ministry of reconciliation" theology:

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| 5:18 | τοῦ **καταλλάξαντος** ἡμᾶς ἑαυτῷ διὰ Χριστοῦ καὶ δόντος ἡμῖν τὴν διακονίαν τῆς **καταλλαγῆς** | ผู้ทรงให้เรา**คืνดี**กับพระองค์โดยทางพระคริสต์ และทรงประทานพันธกิจแห่ง**การคืνดี**ให้แก่เรา |
| 5:19 | θεὸς ἦν ἐν Χριστῷ κόσμον **καταλλάσσων** ἑαυτῷ ... τὸν λόγον τῆς **καταλλαγῆς** | พระเจ้าได้ทรงให้โลก**คืνดี**กับพระองค์ในพระคริสต์ ... ถ้อยคำแห่ง**การคืνดี** |
| 5:20 | **καταλλάγητε** τῷ θεῷ | จง**คืνดี**กับพระเจ้าเถิด |

Verb form → **คืνดี** ("be reconciled; restore relationship"); noun form → **การคืνดี** ("the [act of] reconciliation"). **คืνดี** is the everyday Thai idiom for "make peace / restore" between estranged parties — Thai-natural, theologically-load-bearing.

**The forward-corpus stake:** καταλλαγή / καταλλάσσω will recur densely in Rom 5:10–11 (the central "while we were still enemies" passage) and Rom 11:15 (Israel-Gentile reconciliation); ἀποκαταλλάσσω in Eph 2:16 + Col 1:20–22 (the cosmic-reconciliation passages). The 2 Cor 5:18–20 rendering effectively LOCKS the choice for the Pauline corpus.

**Question:** Is **คืνดี** / **การคืνดี** the right corpus-default for the καταλλαγή cluster? The Thai-natural everyday-relational sense lands well, but it may UNDER-COMMUNICATE the theological-cosmic scope (Rom 11:15 "the reconciliation of the world"; Col 1:20 "to reconcile to himself all things"). Should the corpus-doc lock **คืνดี** uniformly, or distinguish a more-formal **การคืνดีกับพระเจ้า** for cosmic-Pauline contexts? And how should the cognate ἀποκαταλλάσσω (Col 1:20 + Eph 2:16; intensified prefix) be handled — same Thai (**คืνดี**) or marked differently (e.g., **คืνดีอย่างสมบูρณ์**)?

## Item G — διαθήκη at 3:6, 14 → พันธสัญญα + Old/New Testament canon-naming pun

**The pattern:** 2 Cor 3:6–18 is the densest NT διαθήκη theological-cluster outside Hebrews — Paul's "ministry of the new covenant" passage with the Mosaic-veil typology:

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| 3:6 | διακόνους **καινῆς διαθήκης**, οὐ γράμματος ἀλλὰ πνεύματος | ผู้รับใช้แห่ง**พันธสัญญาใหม่** ไม่ใช่ของตัวอักษร แต่ของพระวิญญαณ |
| 3:14 | ἐπὶ τῇ ἀναγνώσει **τῆς παλαιᾶς διαθήκης** | ในขณะอ่าน**พันธสัญญาเดิม** |

**พันธสัญญα** ("bond / covenant") is the standard Thai Bible-tradition rendering. Cross-corpus consistent with Galatians 4:24 (δύο διαθῆκαι → **พันธสัญญαสองแบบ**).

**The deliberate pun:** **พันธสัญญαเดิม / ใหม่** are *also* the canonical Thai *names* for the Old/New Testament. Paul's covenant-theological argument in 3:14 (ἀνάγνωσις τῆς παλαιᾶς διαθήκης = "reading of the Old Covenant") becomes in Thai, by happy lexical-coincidence, also "reading of the Old Testament" — the very canon-formation conclusion the Pauline argument supports. The translator's choice preserves this *non-trivial Thai-language theological pun* by accident-or-design.

**The forward-corpus stake:** Hebrews has ~17 διαθήκη occurrences; the rendering will cement the Pauline-Hebrews covenant-theology bridge. Plus μεσίτης διαθήκης ("mediator of the covenant") at Heb 8:6, 9:15, 12:24.

**Question:** Is **พันธสัญญα** the right corpus-default for διαθήκη, with the **เดิม / ใหม่** modifiers preserving the Old/New Testament canon-naming pun? Should the corpus-doc explicitly note the pun as a *deliberate feature* (so future translators don't break it by varying the descriptor)? And how should μεσίτης διαθήκης (Heb 8:6, etc.) render — **คนกลางแห่งพันธสัญญα** (literal mediator) or with a more covenantal-juridical term?

## Item H — ἀρραβών τοῦ πνεύματος (1:22, 5:5) → มัดจำ commercial-deposit metaphor

**The pattern:** ἀρραβών ("earnest, deposit, down-payment") is a Pauline distinctive — it occurs only in 2 Cor 1:22, 5:5 + Eph 1:14 in the entire NT. The Greek itself is a Hebrew/Phoenician commercial-loanword (עֵרָבוֹן) — its source register is the marketplace, not the temple. Translator preserves the commercial register:

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| 1:22 | ὁ καὶ σφραγισάμενος ἡμᾶς καὶ δοὺς τὸν **ἀρραβῶνα τοῦ πνεύματος** ἐν ταῖς καρδίαις ἡμῶν | พระองค์ได้ทรงประทับตราเราไว้ และทรงประทานพระวิญญาณเป็น**มัดจำ**ในใจของเรา |
| 5:5 | ὁ δοὺς ἡμῖν τὸν **ἀρραβῶνα τοῦ πνεύματος** | พระองค์ทรงเป็นผู้ประทานพระวิญญาณเป็น**มัดจำ**ให้แก่เรα |

**มัดจำ** ("deposit, earnest, down-payment") is the Thai commercial idiom — exactly the Greek metaphor's source register. A Thai reader hears the marketplace echo (the Spirit as the "down-payment" guaranteeing the rest of the inheritance). Reads precisely.

**Question:** Is **มัดจำ** the right corpus-default for ἀρραβών — preserving the Greek's commercial-loanword register at the cost of a slightly-mundane Thai feel — or should it shift to a more-elevated **เครื่องประกัน** ("guarantee, surety") to match the theological-eschatological force? Will recur at Eph 1:14 (the third and final NT occurrence, where the same metaphor crowns the Ephesian-blessing benediction). Cross-corpus consistency required.
