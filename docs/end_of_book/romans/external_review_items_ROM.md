## Item A — νόμος Pauline two-rendering policy (ธรรมบัญญัติ vs กฎ — first-systematized at Rom 7:21–8:2)

**The pattern:** Romans has 74 occurrences of νόμος across 38 verses — the densest νόμος-text in the entire NT. Galatians (the prior Pauline-letter shipped) used **single-rendering ธรรมบัญญัติ** across all 4 senses (Mosaic Torah / Pentateuch / principle / governing-pattern). Romans introduces a **principled two-rendering split**:

| Sense | Thai | Romans evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Mosaic Torah / Pentateuch (the law-code or law-as-canonical-text) | **ธรรมบัญญัติ** | Rom 2:12–27, 3:19–31, 4:13–16, 5:13–20, 6:14–15, 7:1–14 (most), 7:22, 7:25a, 8:3–7, 9:31, 10:4–5, 13:8–10 — ~50 occurrences |
| Inner-principle / governing-pattern (Pauline-rhetorical νόμος-of-X-genitive) | **กฎ** | Rom 7:21, 7:23 (×3 — three-laws cluster), 7:25b, 8:2 (×2) |

**Cleanest exegetical proof of the two-rendering split — Rom 7:25, both senses in the same Greek sentence:**

- Rom 7:25 GK: `ἄρα οὖν αὐτὸς ἐγὼ τῷ μὲν νοῒ δουλεύω **νόμῳ θεοῦ**, τῇ δὲ σαρκὶ **νόμῳ ἁμαρτίας**`
- Rom 7:25 TH: `ฉะนั้น ข้าพเจ้าเองก็รับใช้**ธรรมบัญญัติของพระเจ้า**ด้วยจิต แต่รับใช้**กฎแห่งบาป**ด้วยเนื้อหนัง`

The same noun νόμος, in adjacent dative-genitive constructions, gets two Thai renderings: **ธรรมบัญญัติ** for the Mosaic-Torah sense (νόμῳ θεοῦ) and **กฎ** for the inner-principle sense (νόμῳ ἁμαρτίας).

**The Rom 7:21–8:2 5-νόμος cluster:**

- Rom 7:21 GK: `Εὑρίσκω ἄρα τὸν **νόμον** τῷ θέλοντι ἐμοὶ ποιεῖν τὸ καλὸν` → TH: `ฉะนั้น ข้าพเจ้าพบ**กฎ**นี้`
- Rom 7:23 GK: `βλέπω δὲ ἕτερον **νόμον** ἐν τοῖς μέλεσίν μου ἀντιστρατευόμενον τῷ **νόμῳ τοῦ νοός** μου καὶ αἰχμαλωτίζοντά με ἐν τῷ **νόμῳ τῆς ἁμαρτίας**` → TH: `แต่ข้าพเจ้าเห็น**กฎ**อีกอันหนึ่ง...ทำสงครามต่อต้าน**กฎแห่งจิต**...จับข้าพเจ้าเป็นเชลยใน**กฎแห่งบาป**`
- Rom 8:2 GK: `ὁ γὰρ **νόμος τοῦ πνεύματος τῆς ζωῆς** ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ ἠλευθέρωσέν σε ἀπὸ τοῦ **νόμου τῆς ἁμαρτίας καὶ τοῦ θανάτου**` → TH: `เพราะ**กฎของพระวิญญาณแห่งชีวิต**...ได้ปลดปล่อยท่านจาก**กฎแห่งบาปและความตาย**`

**Galatians precedent (single-rendering ธรรมบัญญัติ) — the apparent contradiction:**

- GAL 6:2 GK: `ἀλλήλων τὰ βάρη βαστάζετε,καὶ οὕτως ἀναπληρώσετε τὸν **νόμον τοῦ Χριστοῦ**` → TH: `จงแบกภาระของกันและกัน...ทำให้**ธรรมบัญญัติของพระคริสต์**สำเร็จ`

GAL 6:2 used ธรรมบัญญัติ-of-Christ for the same νόμος-of-X compound construction. By the Romans logic, this would be **กฎของพระคริสต์** ("the principle / law of Christ" — emphasizing the new-covenant-norm sense).

**Forward implications:** 1 Cor 9:20–21 has the densest non-Romans νόμος cluster (`τοῖς ὑπὸ νόμον ὡς ὑπὸ νόμον... τοῖς ἀνόμοις ὡς ἄνομος... ἀλλ' **ἔννομος Χριστοῦ**`). Hebrews 7:12, 7:16, 8:10 contrast Mosaic vs new-covenant νόμος. The corpus needs a single locked policy.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the Romans two-rendering split (ธρρมบัญญัติ for Mosaic-Torah; กฎ for inner-principle / νόμος-of-X-Pauline-rhetorical) the right corpus default, OR should the GAL single-rendering (ธρρมบัญญัติ everywhere) be retroactively imposed on Romans? The tradeoff: two-rendering preserves Pauline argument-clarity (different things by different words) but loses Pauline-rhetorical-punning (one Greek word, multiple senses, same Thai word). Single-rendering preserves the punning but flattens the Romans 7-8 three-laws-doctrine.
2. If endorsing the Romans two-rendering split, should GAL 6:2 (ν. τοῦ Χριστοῦ) be revised from **ธรรมบัญญัติ** to **กฎ** to match the principle-of-Christ inner-norm sense? And should 1 Cor 9:21 (ἔννομος Χριστοῦ) follow the same principle? Critically: this needs a `docs/translator_decisions/nomos_pauline_2026-05.md` doc before 1 Corinthians 9 ships.

## Item B — Rom 9:5 christological doxology — punctuation/syntax crux

**The verse — most-debated single Pauline-Christology proof-text:**

- Rom 9:5 GK: `ὧν οἱ πατέρες,καὶ ἐξ ὧν ὁ χριστὸς τὸ κατὰ σάρκα,**ὁ ὢν ἐπὶ πάντων,θεὸς εὐλογητὸς εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας·ἀμήν**.`
- Rom 9:5 TH: `ของพวกเขาเป็นบรรพบุรุษ — และจากพวกเขาเองที่พระคริสต์ทรงสืบเชื้อสายตามเนื้อหนัง — **ผู้ทรงเป็นพระเจ้าเหนือทุกสิ่ง ผู้ทรงเป็นที่ถวายสาธุการตลอดกาลเป็นนิตย์ อาเมน**`

**Two well-attested readings (the Greek is unpunctuated; punctuation is editorial):**
- (a) **Christological:** "...from whom is Christ according to the flesh, **who is over all, God blessed forever. Amen.**" — Christ identified as "God-blessed-forever." Pauline-Christology proof-text par excellence. BSB / NIV / ESV / CSB / NASB / Eremos all chose this.
- (b) **Doxology-to-Father:** "...from whom is Christ according to the flesh. **God who is over all be blessed forever. Amen.**" — A separate doxology to the Father, syntactically detached from "Christ." NRSV (older), RSV-original-1946, NEB chose this.

**The 9:5 KD acknowledges the choice and follows (a) per RULES §0:**

> MAJOR INTERPRETIVE CRUX. Greek ambiguous-syntax: (a) referring-to-Christ ('Christ-who-is-God-over-all-blessed-forever' — most-evangelical-Protestant: BSB, NIV, ESV); (b) doxology-to-Father ('and-God-who-is-over-all-be-blessed' — some scholars: NEB, RSV-original). Following (a) per RULES §0 + BSB. ... The κατὰ σάρκα qualifier in 9:5a ('Christ according to flesh') would be POINTLESS without a complementary 'according to God-side' — supporting reading (a). Doxology-to-Father reading lacks this contextual-pointer.

**Editorial weight:** This is one of two verses in Romans where Pauline-Christology depends entirely on the editor's choice of comma/period (the other is Rom 1:3–4 — and Romans 1:3-4 is uncontested in Eremos). Thai punctuation forces a choice; **ผู้ทรงเป็นพระเจ้าเหนือทุกสิ่ง** is unambiguously Christological in Thai — there is no syntax-preserving rendering possible.

**Three questions:**
1. Is the Christological reading (a) defensible per RULES §0 + BSB, or does the Greek's syntactic ambiguity warrant a footer-note acknowledging (b)?
2. The κατὰ σάρκα-implies-complementary-divine-nature argument — is this exegetically load-bearing, or could the doxology-to-Father reading (b) account for the κατὰ-σάρκα qualifier through a different syntactic-segmentation?
3. If endorsing (a): should a footer-note manage Thai-evangelical reader expectation, given that NRSV/RSV/NEB still represent (b) in mainline scholarship? OR is the Christological reading sufficiently mainstream that no manage-the-debate apparatus is needed?

## Item C — Rom 10:4 τέλος νόμου Χριστὸς — terminal vs goal reading

**The verse:**

- Rom 10:4 GK: `**τέλος** γὰρ νόμου Χριστὸς εἰς δικαιοσύνην παντὶ τῷ πιστεύοντι.`
- Rom 10:4 TH: `เพราะพระคริสต์ทรงเป็น**จุดสิ้นสุด**ของธรรมบัญญัติ สู่ความชอบธรรมแก่ทุกคนที่เชื่อ`

**Two well-attested readings:**
- (a) **Termination-end** ("Christ is the END of the Law as a means of righteousness"). Reformation-Lutheran-Reformed default. BSB / ESV / NIV / CSB. Eremos current → **จุดสิ้นสุด** ("end-point").
- (b) **Goal-fulfillment** ("Christ is the GOAL/aim that the Law was pointing to"). N.T. Wright, Dunn, New-Perspective-Pauline scholarship. Would render **เป้าหมาย** ("goal") or **จุดมุ่งหมาย** ("aim").

**The 10:4 KD names the choice and follows (a) per RULES §0:**

> FAMOUS-INTERPRETIVE-CRUX. τέλος ambiguous: (a) 'end / termination' (Reformation-Lutheran-Reformed default — Law-functionally-ended-as-means-of-righteousness); (b) 'goal / fulfillment' (some New-Perspective: Christ as the goal Law-was-pointing-to). RULES §0 evangelical-Protestant default favors (a). 'จุดสิ้นสุด' captures the termination-sense; could also be 'เป้าหมาย' for goal-sense. Following BSB.

**Cross-cutting: Rom 10:4 + GAL 3:24 form a salvation-history pair.** GAL 3:24 (παιδαγωγὸς ... εἰς Χριστόν) was rendered **จนกว่าจะถึงพระคริสต์** ("until [the time] Christ [arrives]") — preserving the **temporal** reading. GAL audit §7 flagged this as REVIEW-before-Rom-10:4.

The two together form a coherent salvation-history reading: the Law is a TEMPORARY-disciplinarian (GAL 3:24 temporal) ENDED by Christ (Rom 10:4 terminal). Both choices are aligned. The alternative (directional GAL 3:24 + goal Rom 10:4) would shift the Pauline argument toward a "law-points-to-Christ" continuous-fulfillment reading.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the termination reading (a) at Rom 10:4 + the temporal reading at GAL 3:24 (παιδαγωγός εἰς Χριστόν → "until Christ") the right corpus default, OR should the project shift toward the goal-fulfillment / directional reading? The choice has compounding effects across Pauline soteriology.
2. Should this be locked in a single corpus doc covering both verses (`docs/translator_decisions/telos_paidagogos_christ_2026-05.md`), or kept at verse-level rationale only?

## Item D — Rom 11:25–26 "all Israel will be saved" — three-way exegetical fork

**The verses:**

- Rom 11:25 GK: `πώρωσις ἀπὸ μέρους τῷ Ἰσραὴλ γέγονεν ἄχρι οὗ τὸ πλήρωμα τῶν ἐθνῶν εἰσέλθῃ`
- Rom 11:25 TH: `ความใจแข็งกระด้างได้เกิดขึ้นกับอิสราเอล**บางส่วน** จนกว่าความครบบริบูรณ์ของคนต่างชาติจะเข้ามา`
- Rom 11:26 GK: `καὶ οὕτως **πᾶς Ἰσραὴλ** σωθήσεται·καθὼς γέγραπται·Ἥξει ἐκ Σιὼν ὁ ῥυόμενος,ἀποστρέψει ἀσεβείας ἀπὸ Ἰακώβ.`
- Rom 11:26 TH: `และอย่างนี้**อิสราเอลทั้งสิ้น**จะได้รับความรอด ดังที่เขียนไว้ว่า ‘ผู้ทรงช่วยให้พ้นจะเสด็จมาจากศิโยน — พระองค์จะทรงขจัดความอธรรมจากยาโคบ’`

**Three competing exegetical readings of πᾶς Ἰσραήλ:**
- (a) **All-elect-of-ethnic-Israel** (Reformed-mainstream — elect-remnant cumulated across history; Calvin, Berkhof)
- (b) **Future end-time mass conversion of ethnic Israel** (Premillennial / Dispensational; Schreiner, Moo)
- (c) **Israel-and-the-Church-together as the new-Israel** (typologically-redefined; N.T. Wright)

**The 11:26 KD acknowledges all three and preserves the ambiguity:**

> FAMOUS-INTERPRETIVE-CRUX. πᾶς Ἰσραήλ 'all Israel' — three-readings: (a) all-elect-of-ethnic-Israel (Reformed-mainstream); (b) corporate-Israel-as-a-future-end-time-mass-conversion (Premillennial); (c) Israel-and-the-Church-together as the new-Israel (some-Reformed). Eremos preserves 'อิสราเอลทั้งสิ้น' literally — leaving the reader to discern.

**Editorial assessment:** The Thai **อิสราเอลทั้งสิ้น** ("all Israel") is the most literal possible rendering and PRESERVES the ambiguity exactly as the Greek does. Critics from any of the three camps will read this through their denominational frame and may want a more-committed rendering.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the ambiguity-preservation strategy (อิสราเอลทั้งสิ้น literal) the right approach, given that the Greek itself is ambiguous and Thai protestant-evangelical readers span all three readings? Or should the project pre-commit to one reading via either rendering choice or footer-note?
2. The OT citation at 11:26 (Ἥξει ἐκ Σιὼν ὁ ῥυόμενος) follows LXX Isa 59:20 with adaptation (LXX has ἕνεκεν Σιὼν "for the sake of Zion"; Paul has ἐκ Σιὼν "from Zion") + likely composite with Jer 31:34 (ἀφαίρεσις ἁμαρτίας). The Thai **ผู้ทรงช่วยให้พ้นจะเสด็จมาจากศิโยน** uses the Pauline-modified ἐκ-Σιὼν form correctly. Should the verse-level NOTE flag the LXX/MT/Pauline-modification more explicitly, or is the current OT citation flagging sufficient?

## Item E — Rom 16:1 Phoebe διάκονον — generic servant vs formal deaconess office

**The verse:**

- Rom 16:1 GK: `Συνίστημι δὲ ὑμῖν Φοίβην τὴν ἀδελφὴν ἡμῶν,οὖσαν καὶ **διάκονον τῆς ἐκκλησίας** τῆς ἐν Κεγχρεαῖς`
- Rom 16:1 TH: `ข้าพเจ้าขอฝากเฟบีพี่น้องหญิงของเรากับพวกท่าน ผู้เป็น**ผู้รับใช้ของคริสตจักร**ที่เมืองเคนเครีย`

**Two readings:**
- (a) **Generic servant** ("Phoebe, a servant of the church") — preserves NT-Pauline-διάκονος-fluidity (same word for Christ at Rom 15:8, apostles at 1 Cor 3:5, Pauline missionary co-workers at Eph 6:21, Col 1:7). Conservative-evangelical-mainstream. Eremos current → **ผู้รับใช้**.
- (b) **Formal office (deaconess / female deacon)** ("Phoebe, a deacon of the church") — formal church-office. Some-Reformed and progressive traditions; transliteration **มัคนายก** (formal deacon-title used in Thai church-polity) or **ดีคอน**.

**Cross-cutting:** Same word at Rom 15:8 for **Christ** as διάκονος (→ **ผู้รับใช้**, plain-register). 1 Tim 3:8–13 (forward) — the female-deaconess question. The Romans-lock will propagate to 1 Tim before Eremos translates the Pastorals.

**The 16:1 KD names the crux:**

> διάκονος here-translation-debated: (a) 'servant' (generic; Eremos current); (b) 'deaconess' (formal-office). NT-Pauline-term-fluidity: same-word used of Christ (Rom 15:8) and apostles (1 Cor 3:5). Conservative-evangelical mainstream: 'servant' (preserves ambiguity); some-Reformed traditions read formal-office (= deacon). Eremos preserves the ambiguous-Greek by 'ผู้รับใช้.'

**Two questions:**
1. Is the generic-servant rendering **ผู้รับใช้** the right corpus default for Pauline διάκονος (preserving the lexical fluidity across Christ + apostles + co-workers + Phoebe)? Or should Phoebe's διάκονος-of-a-specific-church-at-Cenchreae warrant the formal-office rendering **มัคนายก** because the syntactic frame διάκονον τῆς ἐκκλησίας τῆς ἐν Κεγχρεαῖς is a titular-of-X-church construction, distinct from the generic-διάκονος uses?
2. The forward-pertinent 1 Tim 3:11 γυναῖκας (women) in the διάκονοι-section — if Phoebe is locked at **ผู้รับใช้**, 1 Tim 3:11 should follow; if Phoebe is locked at **มัคนายก**, 1 Tim 3:11 women-deaconesses-question is pre-committed. Which direction does the project want to lock?

## Item F — Rom 16:7 Junia / Junias + ἐπίσημοι ἐν τοῖς ἀποστόλοις — gender + apostolic-status

**The verse:**

- Rom 16:7 GK: `ἀσπάσασθε Ἀνδρόνικον καὶ **Ἰουνίαν** τοὺς συγγενεῖς μου καὶ συναιχμαλώτους μου,οἵτινές εἰσιν **ἐπίσημοι ἐν τοῖς ἀποστόλοις**,οἳ καὶ πρὸ ἐμοῦ γέγοναν ἐν Χριστῷ.`
- Rom 16:7 TH: `จงทักทายอันโดรนีคัสและ**ยูเนีย** ญาติของข้าพเจ้าและเพื่อนร่วมเป็นเชลยของข้าพเจ้า ทั้งสองคน**เป็นที่นับถือในหมู่อัครทูต** และทั้งสองได้อยู่ในพระคริสต์ก่อนข้าพเจ้า`

**Two cruxes:**

**Crux 1 — Gender:** Ἰουνίαν (acc.) is grammatically ambiguous between fem. Ἰουνία ("Junia") and hypothetical masc. Ἰουνίας ("Junias," unattested elsewhere in Greek). Modern scholarship overwhelmingly favors **feminine** (Bauckham, Epp, Wallace, Burer-Wallace). Eremos transliterates **ยูเนีย** — phonetically feminine by default in Thai, gender not lexically marked.

**Crux 2 — Apostolic status (ἐπίσημοι ἐν τοῖς ἀποστόλοις):**
- (a) **"Outstanding among the apostles"** = they ARE apostles. Patristic (Origen, Chrysostom); Bauckham, Epp, Cranfield.
- (b) **"Well-known to the apostles"** = they are NOT apostles. Burer-Wallace 2001; ESV/NIV footnote.

**Eremos current Thai เป็นที่นับถือในหมู่อัครทูต ("respected within / among the apostles") — preserves the ambiguity, with ในหมู่ ("among / within") leaning slightly toward (a).**

**The 16:7 NOTES flag the inclusive lean:**

> INTERPRETIVE CRUX: Junia (female) vs Junias (male hypothetical). Modern-scholarship overwhelmingly female. ἐπίσημοι ἐν τοῖς ἀποστόλοις: most-likely reads 'outstanding-among-the-apostles' (= they ARE-apostles, broad-sense missionaries like Barnabas). Eremos preserves slight-ambiguity in Thai while leaning-toward-inclusive.

**Cross-cutting:** Rom 16:7 is **the** most-debated single verse in the NT for female-leadership questions. Thai protestant-evangelical reviewers will engage this regardless of the rendering. The inclusive lean is not RULES §0 evangelical-Protestant default — the conservative-evangelical-mainstream has shifted toward Burer-Wallace's reading-(b) since 2001.

**Three questions:**
1. Is the inclusive lean **เป็นที่นับถือในหมู่อัครทูต** (current; reads slightly toward "outstanding-among-the-apostles" / they-ARE-apostles) defensible on grammatical-Greek grounds (ἐν τοῖς ἀποστόλοις with-locative-force = "among the group of apostles, included in"), OR is the Burer-Wallace conservative reading (ἐν + dative = "to / by the apostles," they-are-known-by) more grammatically rigorous?
2. Should the Thai rendering be revised toward stricter-conservative **เป็นที่รู้จักของอัครทูต** ("known TO the apostles" — they-are-not-apostles), OR toward stricter-inclusive **โดดเด่นในหมู่อัครทูต** ("prominent-among the apostles" — they ARE apostles), OR retained at the current ambiguous-leaning-inclusive **เป็นที่นับถือในหมู่อัครทูต**?
3. The Junia/Junias gender-question — should the Thai **ยูเนีย** be lexically marked as feminine (e.g., **ยูเนีย (สตรี)** "Junia (woman)" in a footer), OR is the phonetic-feminine-default sufficient for Thai readers?

## Item G — υἱοθεσία rendering drift (GAL การรับเป็นบุตร → ROM สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร)

**The lemma:** υἱοθεσία occurs 5× in NT; 4 are Pauline. Romans 8:15, 8:23, 9:4 are the densest cluster.

**The drift — same lemma, two renderings:**

| Verse | Greek | Thai | Phase |
|---|---|---|---|
| GAL 4:5 | ἵνα τὴν υἱοθεσίαν ἀπολάβωμεν | **เพื่อเราจะได้รับ**การรับเป็นบุตร** | early-Pauline (2026-04) |
| Rom 8:15 | ἐλάβετε πνεῦμα **υἱοθεσίας** | **พระวิญญาณแห่ง**สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** | Romans (2026-04 to -05) |
| Rom 8:23 | **υἱοθεσίαν** ἀπεκδεχόμενοι, τὴν ἀπολύτρωσιν τοῦ σώματος | **รอคอย**สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** — คือการไถ่ร่างกายของเรา` | Romans |
| Rom 9:4 | ὧν ἡ **υἱοθεσία** | **ผู้ที่ทรงประทาน**สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** | Romans |

**The two renderings are not synonymous:**
- **การรับเป็นบุตร** ("the receiving-as-son [act/status]") — emphasizes the act/status of being received as a son.
- **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** ("the right/privilege of being-a-son") — adds the legal-rights component (สิทธิ์ = right / privilege / legal-claim) — sharper emphasis on the Greco-Roman-legal-adoption force the GAL §12 KD names (legal-status, inheritance rights).

The GAL 4:5 KD framed υἱοθεσία as Greco-Roman-legal-technical (full legal-status, inheritance-rights). The Romans rendering **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** is arguably more precise for the legal-technical force, but it is a quiet post-GAL revision without a corresponding decision document. The corpus-consistency-checker won't catch the drift because both renderings tokenize the same lemma differently.

**Two questions:**
1. Which rendering should be the corpus default — **การรับเป็นบุตร** (GAL pattern; emphasizes act-of-receiving) or **สิทธิ์การเป็นบุตร** (Romans pattern; emphasizes legal-rights / Greco-Roman-adoption-force)? The Romans pattern is more technically precise but loses the simpler "received-as-son" sense.
2. Should the rendering chosen be propagated retroactively — i.e., revise GAL 4:5 if Romans pattern wins, or revise Rom 8:15/23, 9:4 if GAL pattern wins? And should this be locked in `docs/translator_decisions/huiothesia_adoption_2026-05.md` before Eph 1:5 (προορίσας ἡμᾶς εἰς υἱοθεσίαν — "predestined for adoption") ships?

## Item H — Rom 12:1 λογικὴν λατρείαν — rational vs spiritual worship

**The verse:**

- Rom 12:1 GK: `παραστῆσαι τὰ σώματα ὑμῶν θυσίαν ζῶσαν ἁγίαν εὐάρεστον τῷ θεῷ,τὴν **λογικὴν λατρείαν** ὑμῶν.`
- Rom 12:1 TH: `ให้พวกท่านมอบร่างกายของพวกท่านเป็นเครื่องบูชาที่มีชีวิต ที่บริสุทธิ์ ที่พอพระทัยพระเจ้า — นี่คือ**การปรนนิบัติบูชาตามความคิด**ของพวกท่าน`

**Two readings:**
- (a) **Rational / mind-oriented** (cognate with λόγος "reason/word") — "your rational/reasonable worship" (worship that befits a thinking creature). BSB / ESV / NASB. Eremos current → **ตามความคิด** ("according to thought / reason").
- (b) **Spiritual** (in contrast to bodily/external) — "your spiritual worship" (Hellenistic-Jewish / patristic usage). NIV "true and proper worship"; CSB "true worship." 1 Pet 2:2 (λογικὸν γάλα — "spiritual milk") shows the meaning-fluidity.

**The 12:1 NOTES name the choice and follow (a):**

> 'Living-sacrifice' is the great-Pauline-paradox of new-covenant-worship. λογικός 'rational/reasonable' is interpretively-debated; Eremos preserves the rational-mind sense (cognate-with-λόγος).

**Editorial assessment:** **ตามความคิด** could read as "according-to-your-opinion" — subjective overtones. Alternatives: **ที่สมเหตุสมผล** ("reasonable"); **ที่ใช้ปัญญา** ("intellectual"); **ฝ่ายจิตวิญญาณ** ("spiritual" — collides with πνευματικός lock). Forward-pertinent: 1 Pet 2:2 λογικὸν γάλα.

**Two questions:**
1. Is **ตามความคิด** the right Thai for Pauline-cognate-with-λόγος "rational worship," or is it too subjective-sounding for a Thai-evangelical-pulpit-natural rendering? The alternatives (ที่สมเหตุสมผล / ที่ใช้ปัญญา) are smoother but lose the explicit cognate-with-λόγος force.
2. The Thai-evangelical reader-test: when read aloud, does **การปรนนิบัติบูชาตามความคิดของพวกท่าน** convey "worship that befits your mind / understanding" (the intended Pauline sense), OR does it slide toward "worship according to your own thoughts/preferences" (which inverts the verse)? If the latter is a real risk, the rendering should be revised.
