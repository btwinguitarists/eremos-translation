## Item A: πιστὸς ὁ λόγος = ถ้อยคำนี้เชื่อถือได้

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** "ถ้อยคำนี้เชื่อถือได้" captures both the lexical accuracy of πιστός and λόγος, and functions perfectly as a natural Thai citation formula across 1 Tim 1:15, 3:1, and 4:9. Shifting to "คำนี้สัตย์จริง" would over-theologize the phrase and lose the distinct "quoted maxim" feel intended in the Pastorals.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Draft a Pastoral-corpus doc establishing this string as the strict default, while formally committing to preserving contextual ambiguity for reference directions (like 4:9) where the Greek allows it.

## Item B: εὐσέβεια = ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** "ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า" accurately reflects the active, directed piety central to the Pastorals and establishes a strong corpus-level baseline. The principled departure at 1 Tim 3:16 to "ทางของพระเจ้า" is highly defensible, as the "mystery of εὐσέβεια" functions there as a comprehensive summary of the Christian faith rather than individual personal piety.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Accept the 3:16 departure as a context-specific necessity, but strictly reserve "ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า" as the default for the rest of the corpus to prevent semantic drift.

## Item C: σωτήρ-applied-to-God: Pastoral Christological-shift

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** Retaining the standard lemma "พระผู้ช่วยให้รอด" for God (1 Tim 1:1, 2:3, 4:10) preserves vital corpus-wide lexical consistency without masking the Pastoral shift in attribution. For the upcoming Titus 2:13 crux, the Granville-Sharp construction is widely recognized by modern evangelical scholarship as referring to a single subject.

**Recommended action:** Lock "พระเจ้าพระผู้ช่วยให้รอด" for 1 Tim. For Titus 2:13, Ben to decide, but strongly recommend translating the Granville-Sharp construction to affirm the high-Christological reading (Christ as God and Savior) rather than preserving unnatural ambiguity.

## Item D: ἀρσενοκοίτης at 1:10 = ผู้ชายที่ร่วมประเวณีกับผู้ชาย

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** "ผู้ชายที่ร่วมประเวณีกับผู้ชาย" masterfully preserves the etymological roots (LXX Lev 18:22/20:13) and clinical register of Paul's coined compound without importing modern socio-political LGBT connotations. The length is a necessary and worthwhile trade-off for exegetical neutrality.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Add a brief `thai_summary` note explaining the lexical derivation from the Septuagint to educate readers and prevent anachronistic readings, matching standard evangelical commentary practice.

## Item F: 1 Tim 3:11 γυναῖκας: wives-of-deacons vs women-deacons crux

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** Rendering γυναῖκας simply as "หญิง" without an added possessive pronoun perfectly mirrors the Greek's grammatical ambiguity. Forcing "wives" would be an interpretive overstep that violates the project's optimal equivalence goals.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Keep the `thai_summary` neutral but informative regarding the lack of a possessive pronoun and the ὡσαύτως parallel. Ensure Rom 16:1 (Phoebe) is translated with a similarly careful, non-over-determined rendering (e.g., ผู้ปรนนิบัติ or similar) rather than forcing an ecclesiastical office.

## Item G: 1 Tim 3:2 / 3:12 / 5:9 μιᾶς γυναικὸς ἄνδρα

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** "สามีของภรรยาเพียงคนเดียว" accurately reflects the literal Greek idiom across 1 Tim 3:2, 3:12, and 5:9 without narrowing the text to just "marital fidelity," which would eliminate the historical and literal dimensions. The feminine inversion in 5:9 gracefully maintains the deliberate structural parallelism of the Greek text.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Maintain the strictly parallel feminine inversion for 5:9 ("ภรรยาของสามีเพียงคนเดียว") to preserve the structural mirroring intended by the author.

## Item H: 1 Tim 3:16 Christ-hymn + Ὃς vs Θεός variant

**Verdict:** CONCERN

**Reasoning:** While "พระองค์ทรง..." masterfully captures the Ὃς relative pronoun and the hymn-like structure of 1 Tim 3:16, omitting a note about the Byzantine variant (Θεός) is a pastoral/trust liability for Thai readers. Because "God was manifest" is deeply entrenched in conservative Protestantism, a silent omission might cause readers to suspect theological tampering rather than textual accuracy.

**Recommended action:** Lock the main text as "พระองค์ทรง...", but Ben to decide on adding a Tier 2 footer-note (e.g., "สำเนาโบราณบางฉบับระบุว่า พระเจ้าทรงปรากฏ...") to build reader trust and transparently acknowledge the manuscript tradition.
