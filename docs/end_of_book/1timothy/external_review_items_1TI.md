## Item A — πιστὸς ὁ λόγος = ถ้อยคำนี้เชื่อถือได้: Pastoral-formula locking precedent

**The pattern:** 1 Timothy is the FIRST PASTORAL EPISTLE the project has shipped. The Pastoral-distinctive citation-formula **πιστὸς ὁ λόγος** ("trustworthy is the saying") occurs **3× in 1 Timothy** and **5× across the Pastorals total** (1 Tim 1:15, 3:1, 4:9; 2 Tim 2:11; Tit 3:8). All three 1 Tim occurrences render uniformly:

| Verse | Greek | Thai | Refers to |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1:15 | πιστὸς ὁ λόγος καὶ πάσης ἀποδοχῆς ἄξιος | **ถ้อยคำนี้เชื่อถือได้และสมควรแก่การยอมรับอย่างเต็มที่** | "Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners" |
| 3:1 | πιστὸς ὁ λόγος | **ถ้อยคำนี้เชื่อถือได้** | forward to overseer-aspiration |
| 4:9 | πιστὸς ὁ λόγος καὶ πάσης ἀποδοχῆς ἄξιος | **ถ้อยคำนี้เชื่อถือได้และสมควรแก่การยอมรับอย่างเต็มที่** | ambiguous (back to 4:8 OR forward to 4:10) |

The 3:1 KD names the corpus-locking principle:

> Formulaic phrase appearing 5x in the Pastorals (1 Tim 1:15; 3:1; 4:9; 2 Tim 2:11; Tit 3:8). Establishing 'ถ้อยคำนี้เชื่อถือได้' as the consistent Thai rendering for all five occurrences.

**Editorial assessment:** **ถ้อยคำนี้เชื่อถือได้** preserves both the literal πιστός ("trustworthy") + λόγος ("saying / utterance") and reads naturally as a Thai citation-formula introducing a quoted maxim. The 4:9 ambiguity (back-to-v.8 godliness-promise vs forward-to-v.10 God-as-Savior) is preserved by the standalone-sentence treatment.

**Two questions:**
1. Is **ถ้อยคำนี้เชื่อถือได้** the right Pastoral-corpus default for πιστὸς ὁ λόγος? It is theologically-neutral and preserves the citation-formula force. Alternative: **คำนี้สัตย์จริง** ("this word is faithful-true," more Calvinist-evangelical-register) preserves more of the πιστός ("faithful") force but loses the citation-formula function. Is the current rendering principled?
2. Should the formula be lifted to a corpus doc before 2 Tim 2:11 + Tit 3:8 ship — and should the doc commit to a default rule for the back-vs-forward reference question (e.g., 4:9 currently preserved as ambiguous; 2 Tim 2:11 unambiguously forward; Tit 3:8 unambiguously backward)?

## Item B — εὐσέβεια = ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า: Pastoral piety-virtue vocabulary

**The pattern:** εὐσέβεια + cognates is the Pastoral-corpus's **single most-distinctive virtue-term**. It occurs **10× across 1-2 Tim + Titus** with **4× in 1 Tim alone**, plus the cognates θεοσέβεια (HAPAX, 2:10) + εὐσεβέω (verb, 5:4):

| Verse | Greek | Thai | Sense |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2:2 | ἐν πάσῃ εὐσεβείᾳ | **ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า** | civic-life godliness |
| 2:10 | θεοσέβειαν (HAPAX cognate) | **ความเคารพยำเกรงพระเจ้า** | women's-adornment piety |
| 3:16 | τὸ τῆς εὐσεβείας μυστήριον | **ข้อล้ำลึกแห่งทางของพระเจ้า** | the gospel-as-godliness-mystery |
| 4:7 | γύμναζε σεαυτὸν πρὸς εὐσέβειαν | **ฝึกตัวเองในความเคร่งในพระเจ้า** | spiritual-formation goal |
| 4:8 | ἡ εὐσέβεια πρὸς πάντα ὠφέλιμός | **ความเคร่งในพระเจ้ามีประโยชน์ในทุกทาง** | comprehensive benefit |
| 5:4 | τὸν ἴδιον οἶκον εὐσεβεῖν (verb) | **แสดงความเคร่งในพระเจ้าต่อครอบครัวของตัวเอง** | family-honoring piety |
| 6:5 | πορισμὸν εἶναι τὴν εὐσέβειαν | **ความเคร่งในพระเจ้าเป็นเครื่องมือหากิน** | merchant-religion (false) |
| 6:6 | ἡ εὐσέβεια μετὰ αὐταρκείας | **ความเคร่งในพระเจ้าพร้อมด้วยความพอใจ** | true-gain godliness |

**Editorial assessment:** **ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า** ("strict-toward God") is consistently locked — preserves both the toward-God directionality (เคร่ง + ใน + พระเจ้า "strict-in + God") and the broad embodied-piety sense that εὐσέβεια carries in the Pastorals. The verbal form εὐσεβέω at 5:4 takes the natural verbal **แสดงความเคร่งในพระเจ้า** ("show / manifest εὐσέβεια").

The most-substantive editorial choice is at **3:16 εὐσεβείας μυστήριον → ข้อล้ำลึกแห่งทางของพระเจ้า** ("the mystery of *the way-of-God*") — a slight context-departure from the standard ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า lock. The 3:16 KD argues that ทางของพระเจ้า ("way-of-God") captures both the devotional and practical dimensions of εὐσέβεια as the Pastorals' near-summary term for "authentic-Christian-life-and-faith" — appropriate for the εὐσεβείας-μυστήριον near-credal-summary construction.

**Two questions:**
1. Is **ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า** the right Pastoral-corpus default for εὐσέβεια? The Thai compound preserves the toward-God directionality but risks reading slightly more legalistic ("strict") than the Greek (which is broader: piety / reverence / godly-life). Alternative: **ทางของพระเจ้า** ("way-of-God"; used at 3:16 for the mystery-construction) is broader but more idiomatic — should the default be ทางของพระเจ้า + ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า reserved for verb-cognate (εὐσεβέω) cases?
2. Is the **3:16 principled-departure** to ทางของพระเจ้า defensible — or does it create inconsistency that should be resolved by either (a) extending ทางของพระเจ้า back to all εὐσέβεια occurrences, or (b) forcing 3:16 to use ความเคร่งในพระเจ้า for uniformity?

## Item C — σωτήρ-applied-to-God: Pastoral Christological-shift

**The pattern:** The Pauline-corpus default applies σωτήρ predominantly to **Christ** (Eph 5:23; Phil 3:20). The Pastorals invert this — σωτήρ is applied **6× to God-the-Father and 4× to Christ** across 1-2 Tim + Tit. In **1 Timothy specifically, all 3 σωτήρ occurrences are God-the-Father, not Christ:**

| Verse | Greek | Thai | Subject |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1:1 | θεοῦ σωτῆρος ἡμῶν καὶ Χριστοῦ Ἰησοῦ τῆς ἐλπίδος ἡμῶν | **พระเจ้าพระผู้ช่วยให้รอดของเรา และของพระเยซูคริสต์ผู้ทรงเป็นความหวังของเรา** | God; Christ = "our hope" not Savior |
| 2:3 | ἐνώπιον τοῦ σωτῆρος ἡμῶν θεοῦ | **เฉพาะพระพักตร์ของพระเจ้าพระผู้ช่วยให้รอดของเรา** | God |
| 4:10 | θεῷ ζῶντι, ὅς ἐστιν σωτὴρ πάντων ἀνθρώπων | **พระเจ้าผู้ทรงพระชนม์ ผู้ทรงเป็นพระผู้ช่วยให้รอดของทุกคน** | God |

The 1:1 KD names the Pastoral-distinctive shift:

> Distinctive Pastoral-title for God (NOT Christ): God-as-Savior. Recurs at 1Ti 2:3, 4:10, Tit 1:3, 2:10, 3:4. The Pastoral-distinctive shifts the σωτήρ-attribution from Christ-only (typical Pauline) to Father-as-Savior in salvation-history's-broader-sense — fitting 1Tim's polemic-against-false-teachers.

**Editorial assessment:** **พระเจ้าพระผู้ช่วยให้รอดของเรา** uses the same Pauline-corpus-locked **พระผู้ช่วยให้รอด** rendering (matching Eph 5:23 of Christ) but applies it to God-the-Father in apposition. This preserves the Pastoral Christological-pattern in Thai naturally without forcing a different lemma, and **leaves Christ-as-Savior available for occurrences in Titus** (Tit 1:4 + 2:13 + 3:6) where Christ is named σωτήρ explicitly.

**The CHALLENGE forward — Tit 2:13 Granville-Sharp question:**
> Tit 2:13 GK: τοῦ μεγάλου θεοῦ καὶ σωτῆρος ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ

Two readings:
- (a) **CHRIST-AS-GOD-AND-SAVIOR** ("the great God and Savior of-us, Jesus Christ" — Granville-Sharp's-Rule + Pauline-Christology favor this; modern English: NIV / ESV / NLT / CSB)
- (b) **TWO PERSONS** ("the great God [the Father] and our Savior Jesus Christ" — older traditional + KJV-style readings)

**Two questions:**
1. Is **พระเจ้าพระผู้ช่วยให้รอด** the right rendering for σωτήρ-applied-to-God in the Pastorals? It uses the same lemma as σωτήρ-applied-to-Christ (พระผู้ช่วยให้รอด) which preserves intra-corpus consistency but loses the Pastoral-distinctive shift's lexical-marking. Alternative: **พระผู้ทรงปกป้องของเรา** ("our protector / preserver") for God-Savior contexts would mark the shift but create a lemma-split with Christ-Savior contexts.
2. How should the project pre-decide **Tit 2:13** before Titus ships? Recommend (a) Granville-Sharp Christ-as-God-and-Savior reading per modern-evangelical-majority — render with a Thai construction that captures the high-Christological reading naturally? Or (b) preserve the ambiguity literally per RULES §1?

## Item D — ἀρσενοκοίτης at 1:10 = ผู้ชายที่ร่วมประเวณีกับผู้ชาย: corpus-locked Levitical clinical-descriptive rendering

**The textual question:** 1 Tim 1:10 is one of two NT occurrences of ἀρσενοκοίτης (the other is 1 Cor 6:9):

> 1 Tim 1:10 GK: πόρνοις, **ἀρσενοκοίταις**, ἀνδραποδισταῖς, ψεύσταις, ἐπιόρκοις...
> TH: คนล่วงประเวณี **ผู้ชายที่ร่วมประเวณีกับผู้ชาย** คนค้าทาส คนโกหก คนสาบานเท็จ...

The 1:10 KD names the corpus-precedent + Levitical-derivation:

> ἀρσενοκοίτης → ผู้ชายที่ร่วมประเวณีกับผู้ชาย (corpus-precedent at 1Co 6:9). Pauline-compound coined-from LXX Lev 18:22 + 20:13 (ἄρσην + κοιμάω). Render preserves the Levitical-derivation-and-clinical-description without modern-political-coloring.

**Editorial assessment:** The Thai **ผู้ชายที่ร่วมประเวณีกับผู้ชาย** ("a man who has sexual-relations with a man") is **principled and Levitically-derived** — Paul's compound is itself coined from LXX Lev 18:22 (ἄρσενος / ἄρσην + κοίτη) and Lev 20:13. The Thai rendering preserves both the lexical compositionality (ผู้ชาย + ร่วมประเวณีกับผู้ชาย) and the clinical-descriptive register without modern-political-coloring, transliteration of contemporary Thai LGBT terminology, or euphemism. This is the most-defensible scholarly choice given the contested-modern-interpretive landscape (active-vs-passive-male-partner readings; pederasty-only vs all-male-male-relations readings).

**Two questions:**
1. Is **ผู้ชายที่ร่วมประเวณีกับผู้ชาย** the right Pauline-corpus default? It is corpus-locked from 1 Cor 6:9 (already shipped). Alternative: a more compact **ชายรักร่วมเพศ** ("homosexual man") would read naturally in modern Thai but introduces modern-political-coloring (the Thai phrase carries contemporary LGBT-discourse weight that the Greek does not). The current rendering is more defensibly-neutral but is longer — is the trade-off worth it for a CC0 evangelical-Protestant Thai Bible?
2. Should a footer-note or thai_summary acknowledge the interpretive sub-questions (active-vs-passive male partner; pederasty-only readings; etc.)? Mainstream English Bibles (BSB / NIV / ESV / CSB) generally do NOT footnote — would a Thai CC0 Bible benefit from explicit acknowledgement, given missions-context Thai readers may not have access to modern-evangelical-scholarly-resources clarifying the lexical history?

## Item E — 1 Tim 2:11–12 + 2:15: women-in-worship aggressive-ambiguity-preservation strategy

**The textual question:** The most-contested cluster in 1 Timothy. The translation deploys an aggressive **interpretive-ambiguity-preservation strategy** at FOUR points:

**1 Tim 2:11–12:**
> γυνὴ ἐν ἡσυχίᾳ μανθανέτω ἐν πάσῃ ὑποταγῇ· διδάσκειν δὲ γυναικὶ οὐκ ἐπιτρέπω, οὐδὲ αὐθεντεῖν ἀνδρός, ἀλλ' εἶναι ἐν ἡσυχίᾳ.
> TH: ให้**หญิง**เรียนด้วย**ความสงบเงียบ** และยอมเชื่อฟังอย่างเต็มที่ ข้าพเจ้าไม่อนุญาตให้**หญิงสอน** หรือ**ใช้อำนาจเหนือชาย** แต่ให้อยู่ใน**ความสงบเงียบ**

Four preservation-points (per the 2:11 + 2:12 KDs):
1. **γυνή** — generic-women OR specifically-wives → rendered **หญิง** (Thai-ambiguous between women-generally + wife-context)
2. **ἡσυχία** — peaceful-disposition OR silence → rendered **ความสงบเงียบ** (Thai compound: สงบ "calm" + เงียบ "silent" — preserves BOTH senses)
3. **αὐθεντέω** (HAPAX, only here in NT) — neutral-authority OR domineering-authority → rendered **ใช้อำนาจเหนือ** (Thai-ambiguous in same way Greek is)
4. **διδάσκειν** — teach-anyone-in-public-worship OR teach-men-specifically → rendered literal **สอน** (no object specification)

**1 Tim 2:15:**
> σωθήσεται δὲ διὰ τῆς **τεκνογονίας** (HAPAX), ἐὰν μείνωσιν ἐν πίστει καὶ ἀγάπῃ καὶ ἁγιασμῷ μετὰ σωφροσύνης.
> TH: แต่**นาง**จะได้รับความรอด**ผ่านการคลอดบุตร** ถ้า**พวกเขา**ดำรงอยู่ในความเชื่อ ความรัก และความบริสุทธิ์พร้อมด้วยความสำรวม

Singular σωθήσεται **นาง** ("she") → plural μείνωσιν **พวกเขา** ("they") shift preserved. The διά rendered **ผ่าน** ("through") preserves the four major interpretations: (1) Mary-as-Messiah-bearer, (2) women-saved-as-mothers, (3) protected-during-childbirth, (4) Eve-saved-by-Mary-the-second-Eve.

**Editorial assessment:** The strategy is principled per uW recommendations + RULES §1 (accuracy-with-ambiguity-preserved). However, this is the **single most-watched passage in the Pastoral letters for evangelical-Protestant Thai readers**. The current rendering is more conservative than mainstream English Bibles — NIV / ESV / CSB / NLT all commit to "silence + authority" for ἡσυχία + αὐθεντέω (complementarian-leaning); only NRSV / NRSVue lean closer to the Eremos-style ambiguity preservation.

**Three questions:**
1. Is the **literal-ambiguity-preservation** at FOUR points (γυνή / ἡσυχία / αὐθεντέω / διδάσκειν) the right corpus-default? It is the most-conservative option scholarly-wise but commits less than ESV / NIV / CSB. For a CC0 Thai Bible serving missions-oriented audiences, does this preserve genuine Greek ambiguity OR risk reading as evasive? Is the **ความสงบเงียบ** compound-rendering for ἡσυχία genuinely ambiguity-preserving, or does it lean toward the silence-reading?
2. Is the **2:15 σωθήσεται διὰ τῆς τεκνογονίας** rendering **ได้รับความรอดผ่านการคลอดบุตร** preserving genuine ambiguity, or does the Thai naturally read as the women-saved-as-mothers interpretation (which is the modern-majority but theologically-contested)?
3. Looking forward to **2 Tim 3:6–7** (γυναικάρια "weak women" laden-with-sins) + **Tit 2:3–5** (πρεσβύτιδας / νέας / οἰκουργούς "older women / younger women / household-keepers"), should the project lock the ambiguity-preservation strategy now (to be applied uniformly forward) — or commit to one interpretation that propagates forward consistently?

## Item F — 1 Tim 3:11 γυναῖκας: wives-of-deacons vs women-deacons crux

**The textual question:** 1 Tim 3:11 is sandwiched between male-deacon qualifications (3:8–10) and male-deacon marital-status (3:12). The Greek lacks the possessive **αὐτῶν** ("their") that would unambiguously signal "wives":

> 1 Tim 3:11 GK: γυναῖκας ὡσαύτως σεμνάς, μὴ διαβόλους, νηφαλίους, πιστὰς ἐν πᾶσιν.
> TH: **หญิง**ก็เช่นเดียวกัน ต้องเป็นผู้น่าเคารพ ไม่เป็นคนชอบใส่ร้าย รู้จักประมาณตน และซื่อสัตย์ในทุกเรื่อง

The 3:11 KD names the three-way crux:

> γυνή can mean either (a) women, (b) wives. Three main views: (1) wives of deacons, (2) women deacons/deaconesses (parallel ὡσαύτως with v.8 suggests new category; Phoebe is διάκονος in Rom 16:1), (3) women in general in the congregation. The Greek lacks any possessive pronoun (αὐτῶν 'their') that would signal 'wives.'

**Modern English handling:**
- BSB / NIV / CSB: ambiguous "the women"
- ESV: committed to "their wives"
- NLT: "the wives of deacons" (committed to wives)
- NRSV: "the women" + footnote noting deacon-interpretation

**Editorial assessment:** The Thai literal-rendering **หญิง** (no possessive, parallel to ὡσαύτως-construction) is **principled-ambiguity-preservation** per uW + RULES §1. The thai_summary at 3:11 frames the **modern-scholarship-preference** (women deacons, given (a) no αὐτῶν, (b) parallel ὡσαύτως pattern with v.8 introducing new category, (c) Rom 16:1 Phoebe-as-διάκονος) without committing.

**The CHALLENGE forward — Rom 16:1 Phoebe:**
> Rom 16:1 GK: Φοίβην τὴν ἀδελφὴν ἡμῶν, οὖσαν διάκονον τῆς ἐκκλησίας τῆς ἐν Κεγχρεαῖς

If Eremos commits 1 Tim 3:11 to the wives-of-deacons reading, then Rom 16:1 needs a corresponding rendering decision (Phoebe = διάκονος as informal-servant or formal-office?).

**Two questions:**
1. Is **หญิง** (literal-ambiguity-preserving) the right rendering for 3:11? It matches BSB / NIV / CSB / NRSV but commits less than ESV / NLT. Alternatively, **หญิงมัคนายก** ("women-deacons") would commit to the modern-scholarly-majority (option 2) and align with Rom 16:1 Phoebe-as-διάκονος. Should the corpus commit to ambiguity-preservation, or to women-deacons explicitly?
2. If ambiguity-preservation is confirmed for 3:11, should the thai_summary lean more explicitly toward the modern-scholarly-majority (women-deacons) — or remain neutral as currently? The current thai_summary lists modern-scholarship-preference but doesn't recommend one reading.

## Item G — 1 Tim 3:2 / 3:12 / 5:9 μιᾶς γυναικὸς ἄνδρα: Pastoral marital-faithfulness formula

**The textual question:** The famous Pastoral-crux occurs **3× in 1 Timothy** (3:2 overseer; 3:12 deacon; 5:9 enrolled-widow — feminine inversion) and recurs at Tit 1:6 (elder). All three 1 Tim occurrences render literally:

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| 3:2 | μιᾶς γυναικὸς ἄνδρα | **เป็นสามีของภรรยาเพียงคนเดียว** |
| 3:12 | μιᾶς γυναικὸς ἄνδρες | **เป็นสามีของภรรยาเพียงคนเดียว** |
| 5:9 | ἑνὸς ἀνδρὸς γυνή | **เป็นภรรยาของสามีเพียงคนเดียว** |

The 3:2 KD names the four-way interpretive-ambiguity:

> Literal: 'of one wife a husband.' Four main interpretations: (1) monogamous (not polygamous), (2) not divorced and remarried, (3) married only once (even after widowhood), (4) marital fidelity. Modern majority: (4) faithfulness to one's wife — but the literal Thai 'สามีของภรรยาเพียงคนเดียว' preserves the genuine ambiguity rather than committing to one reading.

**Editorial assessment:** The literal-rendering **สามีของภรรยาเพียงคนเดียว** ("husband of one wife only") is **principled per uW + RULES §1**. It refuses to commit the verse to any of the four major interpretations. The 5:9 feminine inversion **ภรรยาของสามีเพียงคนเดียว** ("wife of one husband only") maintains structural-parallelism — editorially correct since the Greek 5:9 is the deliberate feminine mirror of 3:2.

**Two questions:**
1. Is **สามีของภรรยาเพียงคนเดียว** the right rendering? It preserves all four interpretations but reads slightly literally. Alternative: **ซื่อสัตย์ต่อภรรยาคนเดียว** ("faithful to one wife") commits to the modern-evangelical-majority reading (option 4) and reads more naturally in Thai but loses interpretations (1)–(3). For a CC0 Thai Bible serving missions-context, does ambiguity-preservation outweigh natural-Thai-readability, or vice versa?
2. Should the **5:9 feminine inversion** ภรรยาของสามีเพียงคนเดียว be locked structurally-parallel to 3:2 (current) — or could it idiomatically read **ซื่อสัตย์ต่อสามีคนเดียว** for the same reason?

## Item H — 1 Tim 3:16 Christ-hymn + Ὃς vs Θεός variant

**The textual question:** The famous Pastoral-Christological-hymn (3:16) opens with the contested relative pronoun. SBLGNT reads Ὃς ("He who"); Byzantine majority reads Θεός ("God"); a minority reads ὅ (neuter "that which"):

> 1 Tim 3:16 GK (SBLGNT): καὶ ὁμολογουμένως μέγα ἐστὶν τὸ τῆς εὐσεβείας μυστήριον· **Ὃς** ἐφανερώθη ἐν σαρκί, ἐδικαιώθη ἐν πνεύματι, ὤφθη ἀγγέλοις, ἐκηρύχθη ἐν ἔθνεσιν, ἐπιστεύθη ἐν κόσμῳ, ἀνελήμφθη ἐν δόξῃ.

> TH: และตามที่ยอมรับร่วมกัน ข้อล้ำลึกแห่งทางของพระเจ้านั้นยิ่งใหญ่นัก คือ **พระองค์ทรงปรากฏในเนื้อหนัง** ทรงได้รับการพิสูจน์โดยพระวิญญาณ ทรงปรากฏแก่เหล่าทูตสวรรค์ ทรงได้รับการประกาศในหมู่ประชาชาติ ทรงเป็นที่เชื่อในโลก ทรงถูกรับขึ้นสู่พระสิริ

The 3:16 KD comprehensively names the SBLGNT-compliance + paleographic-rationale:

> External evidence strongly favors Ὃς (earliest and best mss). Paleographic explanation: ΟC (relative pronoun) → ΘC̄ (nomen sacrum abbreviation for θεός) is a plausible scribal addition of a horizontal bar; the reverse change is harder to motivate. Theologically: the Byzantine reading ('God was manifested in the flesh') makes an explicit high-Christology statement; the critical reading ('He who was manifested in the flesh') leaves the subject a relative pronoun that grammatically has no antecedent — best explained as the opening of a quoted hymn fragment about Christ. Following SBLGNT: Ὃς.

**Editorial assessment:** The Thai **พระองค์ทรง...** ("He / The-One-who...") is **principled** — preserves the Ὃς-relative-pronoun structure without committing to a Θεός high-Christological-anchor that would force พระเจ้าทรง... ("God..."). The royal-prefix ทรง is uniformly applied to all six aorist-passive verbs (the hymn's distinctive form), preserving the divine-passive structure and the Christological-subject readability.

The hymn-fragment-recognition (per the ὁμολογουμένως HAPAX introduction) is acknowledged in the notes — the Thai pronoun **พระองค์** ("He" / "The One who") works naturally as a hymn-opening with implicit-Christ-subject (just as the Greek Ὃς does).

**Two questions:**
1. Is **พระองค์ทรง...** the right Pauline-corpus-rendering for SBLGNT-compliance with the Ὃς-variant? It reads naturally as the Christological-subject of the hymn AND as the Thai-relative-pronoun-style construction. Alternative: an explicit **พระคริสต์ทรง...** ("Christ...") would supply the implicit-subject (matching the hymn's clear-referent) but would abandon the SBLGNT's deliberately-relative structure.
2. Should the verse have a **footer-note** acknowledging the Byzantine Θεός variant? RULES §0 SBLGNT-strictness + RULES §5 silent-omission would say no, and the current notes do not include footer-rendering. But for a CC0 Thai Bible serving evangelical-Protestant readers who may have grown up with KJV-tradition rendering ("God was manifest in the flesh," 1 Tim 3:16 KJV, the most-cited prooftext for Christ's-deity in some Reformed-Calvinist traditions), a brief footer-note ("Some ancient manuscripts read 'God' instead of 'He who'") would aid reader-trust without committing the corpus to the Byzantine reading.
