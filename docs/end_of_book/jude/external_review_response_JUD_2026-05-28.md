## Items in detail

### Item A — Pseudepigrapha-citation surfacing strategy at JUD 1:9 + 1:14–15
- **Gemini:** CONCERN. Leaving the Protestant canonical boundaries implicit risks hermeneutical missteps (treating 1 Enoch as canonical; rejecting Jude *because* it cites extra-canonical material). The Pauline-poet-citation parallel (Acts 17:28, Tit 1:12) perfectly frames the "citation-of-truth vs endorsement-of-source" distinction.
- **ChatGPT:** CONCERN. Same. The verse-level handling is sound, but it doesn't yet establish a reusable corpus rule. Use the frame "Scripture may cite or allude to a true statement/tradition without granting canonical authority to the whole source." Cross-reference Jude 1:9, 1:14–15, 1:6, and 2 Pet 2:4, distinguishing direct citation from tradition-stream allusion.
- **Synthesized / Action:** Write `pseudepigrapha_citations_2026-05.md` (Option B). Both AIs converge on the frame and the cross-references.

### Item B — Doxology v.25 Christological-mediation surfacing  (**Gemini: MAJOR**)
- **Gemini:** MAJOR CONCERN. JUD 1:25 is one of the most-recited doxologies in Protestant liturgical contexts. Without making Trinitarian grammar explicit, แด่พระเจ้า ... โดยทางพระเยซูคริสต์ risks being flattened into a subordinationist reading. Surface the deliberate contrast between Jude's Christological-mediation structure and the Granville-Sharp structure (Christ as God + Savior) in 2 PE 1:1 and TIT 2:13. Cascade.
- **ChatGPT:** CONCERN. Same diagnosis, slightly less alarmed. The Thai translation itself is good (โดยทางพระเยซูคริสต์ clearly marks mediation); the concern is reader-facing — ordinary Thai readers won't see why the wording is Trinitarian rather than anti-Christological. Suggested concise Thai text supplied (see raw). Cascade to 2 PE 1:1 + TIT 2:13 lightly if they lack reader-visible surfacing.
- **Synthesized / Action:** Add v.25 thai_summary naming the Father-as-Savior / Son-as-mediator structure; brief contrast with 2PE 1:1 + TIT 2:13. Light cascade to those two if their reader-layer doesn't already surface the Granville-Sharp pattern.

### Item C — ἐπαγωνίζομαι (1:3) → ต่อสู้อย่างเข้มแข็ง  (**⚠ DIVERGENT**)
- **Gemini:** CONCERN. อย่างเข้มแข็ง ("strongly") feels generic for the specific agonistic imagery. The ἐπ- prefix carries active, relentless pursuit. **Spot-revise to ต่อสู้อย่างไม่ลดละ ("unrelentingly")** — matches the urgent, agonistic Pauline register (like 2 Tim 4:7).
- **ChatGPT:** FINE. ต่อสู้อย่างเข้มแข็ง is defensible — preserves both "contend/fight for" force and intensified agonistic nuance. Dropping the adverb would lose a marked feature. Explicitly **don't change to อย่างไม่ลดละ** (may overstate persistence/aggression). Lock as-is.
- **Synthesized / Action:** **⚠ Real divergence — Ben to break the tie.** Gemini wants ต่อสู้อย่างไม่ลดละ; ChatGPT explicitly rejects that exact change and locks current. Decision factor: does Ben judge อย่างเข้มแข็ง as "generic" (Gemini) or "appropriately intense" (ChatGPT) for ἐπαγωνίζομαι's agonistic-pursuit weight?
