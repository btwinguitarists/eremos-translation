## Item A: σωτήρ + ἐπιφάνεια cluster (2 Tim 1:10)

**Verdict:** CONCERN (minor, localized to lemma consistency)

**Reasoning:** The rendering of σωτήρ → พระผู้ช่วยให้รอด applied to Christ in 2 Tim 1:10 is correct and consistent with the Pastoral pattern (cf. 1 Tim 1:1; 2:3; Tit 1:3–4). The issue is ἐπιφάνεια drift: 1 Tim 6:14 uses การเสด็จมาปรากฏ while 2 Tim 1:10; 4:1; 4:8 use การทรงปรากฏ, creating an intra-corpus inconsistency within a small, tightly-linked lexical set where semantic range (incarnation vs. parousia) can be handled contextually without lexical variation.

**Recommended action:** Standardize ἐπιφάνεια → การทรงปรากฏ corpus-wide; retroactively conform 1 Tim 6:14 unless there is a documented policy allowing eschatological intensification via compounding (if so, document explicitly).

## Item B: παραθήκη ambiguity (2 Tim 1:12)

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** สิ่งที่ฝากไว้นั้น accurately preserves the bidirectional ambiguity of τὴν παραθήκην μου (Paul→God vs. God→Paul), which is syntactically unresolved in Greek and intentionally debated. This aligns with a high-fidelity translation philosophy; forcing direction (as in most English versions) collapses a real exegetical crux. The proximity to 1:14 does not require disambiguation; Greek likewise leaves the relationship implicit rather than formally contrastive.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Optionally ensure the `thai_summary` for 1:12 explicitly states both readings to preserve the interpretive tension.

## Item C: ὀρθοτομέω (2 Tim 2:15)

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** จัดการ…อย่างตรงเที่ยง correctly reflects the functional force of ὀρθοτομέω ("handle/guide correctly") without overcommitting to an uncertain metaphor (woodcutting vs. road-building vs. cultic). This aligns with dominant modern translation practice (ESV/NIV/CSB/BSB) and avoids importing the KJV's "divide" tradition, which is not lexically demanded. The Greek's emphasis is evaluative correctness (δόκιμος / ἀνεπαίσχυντος), not imagery.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Elevate "metaphor flattening when non-determinative" to a documented corpus principle (with examples: ὑγιαίνω, ὀρθοτομέω).

## Item D: Ἰάννης + Ἰαμβρῆς (2 Tim 3:8)

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** Transliteration ยันเนส / ยัมเบรส is appropriate and consistent with Greek phonology. The explicit `thai_summary` acknowledgment of extra-canonical tradition is justified: the names are not present in Exodus, and omission of explanation creates avoidable confusion for readers without secondary resources. This mirrors responsible critical editions rather than obscuring provenance.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Adopt as corpus policy: when canonical text depends on extra-canonical tradition not visible in OT, surface briefly in summary. Pre-commit application to Jude 9 and Jude 14–15.

## Item E: πᾶσα γραφή θεόπνευστος (2 Tim 3:16)

**Verdict:** CONCERN (moderate; two distinct sub-issues)

**Reasoning:**

1. πᾶσα γραφή → ทุกตอน leans distributive and risks underrepresenting the collective sense ("Scripture as a whole"), which is the dominant syntactic reading in Koine usage and nearly universal in translation tradition. While Greek ambiguity exists, Thai ทุกตอน foregrounds segmentation more strongly than Greek requires.
2. θεόπνευστος → ได้รับการดลใจจากพระเจ้า is doctrinally standard but fully suppresses the unique "breath" metaphor of the HAPAX. Given the theological centrality of the verse, complete loss of imagery is non-trivial, though mitigated by the summary.

**Recommended action:**

- Revise ทุกตอน → ทุกส่วนของพระคัมภีร์ or พระคัมภีร์ทั้งหมด (Ben to decide based on ambiguity policy tolerance).
- Retain ได้รับการดลใจจากพระเจ้า in main text but require explicit etymological note in summary (already present). Do not introduce "ลมหายใจของพระเจ้า" into main text unless a broader policy shift toward preserving metaphoric compounds is adopted.

## Item F: Conditional hymn asymmetry (2 Tim 2:11–13)

**Verdict:** FINE

**Reasoning:** The asymmetry at v.13 is preserved: πιστὸς μένει correctly rendered as ongoing divine faithfulness, and ἀρνήσασθαι ἑαυτὸν οὐ δύναται → พระองค์ทรงปฏิเสธพระองค์เองไม่ได้ properly reflects ontological impossibility rather than incapacity. Adding an explicit causal conjunction would over-interpret the Greek parataxis; the current structure preserves rhetorical force.

**Recommended action:** Lock as-is. Consider formatting 2:11–13 as poetic/hymnic lines in display layer (not translation layer) to surface structure.
