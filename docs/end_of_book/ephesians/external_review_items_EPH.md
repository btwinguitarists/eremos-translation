## Item A — The EXPANDED Pauline Haustafel (5:22–6:9): wife-husband Christ-church typology + ὑποτάσσω lock + 5:21 mutual-submission frame

**The pattern:** EPH 5:22–6:9 is the most-debated NT-marriage-text in modern Christian-ethics discourse + the **22-verse Pauline-corpus-anchor** for marriage-and-household-code doctrine. EPH triples the COL Haustafel (COL 3:18–4:1, 6 verses) — the wife-husband section now centers on the Christ-and-church-as-marriage-archetype.

| Verse | Greek | Thai |
|---|---|---|
| 5:21 | ὑποτασσόμενοι ἀλλήλοις ἐν φόβῳ Χριστοῦ | **ยอมอยู่ใต้บังคับซึ่งกันและกัน**ในความยำเกรงพระคริสต์ |
| 5:22 | Αἱ γυναῖκες τοῖς ἰδίοις ἀνδράσιν ὡς τῷ κυρίῳ | ภรรยาทั้งหลาย จง**ยอมอยู่ใต้บังคับ**สามีของตน เหมือนยอมอยู่ใต้บังคับองค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า |
| 5:23 | ἀνήρ ἐστιν κεφαλὴ τῆς γυναικὸς ὡς καὶ ὁ Χριστὸς κεφαλὴ τῆς ἐκκλησίας, αὐτὸς σωτὴρ τοῦ σώματος | สามีเป็น**ศีรษะ**ของภรรยา เช่นเดียวกับที่พระคริสต์ทรงเป็นศีรษะของคริสตจักร พระองค์เองทรงเป็น**พระผู้ช่วยให้รอด**ของกายนั้น |
| 5:25 | οἱ ἄνδρες, ἀγαπᾶτε τὰς γυναῖκας, καθὼς καὶ ὁ Χριστὸς ἠγάπησεν τὴν ἐκκλησίαν καὶ ἑαυτὸν παρέδωκεν ὑπὲρ αὐτῆς | สามีทั้งหลาย จง**รัก**ภรรยา เช่นเดียวกับที่พระคริสต์ทรงรักคริสตจักรและทรงสละพระองค์เองเพื่อคริสตจักร |
| 5:33 | ἡ δὲ γυνὴ ἵνα φοβῆται τὸν ἄνδρα | ภรรยาก็ต้อง**เคารพยำเกรง**สามี |

Three principled choices stand out:

1. **5:21 mutual-submission preserved as participle-bridge** between Spirit-filling (vv.18–20) and the household-code. The Thai **ซึ่งกันและกัน** (literal "to one another") preserves the egalitarian-frame the Greek participle establishes. Egalitarian-vs-complementarian tension is preserved exactly as Greek leaves it.

2. **5:23 asymmetry deliberate**: husbands are NOT told to be saviour-of-wives (impossible-Christological-claim). The 5:23 KD names this. Husbands imitate Christ's-self-giving-cross-love, not Christ's-divine-status. The asymmetry-prevents the Christ-church analogy from over-mapping into wife-as-needing-salvation-from-husband.

3. **5:33 φοβέομαι → เคารพยำเกรง** (reverent-respect, not terror) — preserves the φόβος-of-Christ register from v.21.

The corpus-precedent for ὑποτάσσω → **ยอมอยู่ใต้บังคับ** matches LUK 10:17, 1Co 14:32, COL 3:18 — already-locked.

**Three questions:**
1. Is **ยอมอยู่ใต้บังคับ** ("submit-yourself-under-control") at 5:22 + 5:24 the right register for evangelical-Thai readers — corpus-precedent-aligned with COL 3:18 + the Pauline pattern? Or is it too-strong for a CC0 evangelical-Thai Bible aiming at contemporary-readability + non-coercive register? Alternative: **ยอมเชื่อฟัง** ("submit-and-obey," softer) or **ยอมตาม** ("yield-to," softer still).
2. Should the literal **ซึ่งกันและกัน** ("to one another") at 5:21 be preserved, keeping the egalitarian-mutual-submission frame visible? Or softened to **ตามลำดับ** ("in due order") which would force the complementarian reading? The CC0 evangelical-Protestant frame allows either, and the corpus-precedent for ὑποτάσσω + ἀλλήλοις at LUK 10:17 + 1Co 14:32 is mutual.
3. Is **เคารพยำเกรง** at 5:33 (reverent-respect, preserving the φόβος-of-Christ register from v.21) the right call vs. literal **เกรงกลัว** ("fear")?

EPH is the corpus-ANCHOR for Pauline marriage-doctrine; the lock cannot be revisited later without back-incompatibility into 1 Pet 2:18–3:7 + 1 Tim 2:8–15 + Tit 2:1–10.

## Item B — EPH 1:23 πᾶν τὸ πλήρωμα τοῦ τὰ πάντα ἐν πᾶσιν πληρουμένου: three-way ambiguity preserved

**The textual decision:** EPH 1:23 contains one of the most-debated single phrases in Pauline-theology — packed into the climax of the eulogia + cosmic-Christology section:

- EPH 1:23 GK: `ἥτις ἐστὶν τὸ σῶμα αὐτοῦ, **τὸ πλήρωμα τοῦ τὰ πάντα ἐν πᾶσιν πληρουμένου**`
- TH (current): `ซึ่งคริสตจักรเป็นกายของพระองค์ คือ **ความบริบูρณ์ของพระองค์ผู้ทรงเติมเต็มทุกสิ่งในทุกประการ**`

Three readings hinge on the function of the participle πληρουμένου (middle vs passive) and the referent of πᾶν τὸ πλήρωμα:

| Reading | Claim |
|---|---|
| **(a) Christ-fills-all-things-in-all-people** (active middle) — *currently most-natural in Thai* | Christ as cosmic filler; the church = his fullness as the body that displays his cosmic-presence. |
| **(b) Christ-fills-all-aspects-of-all-things** (active middle, neuter) | Christ comprehensively-saturates every-aspect of all-creation. |
| **(c) The church is the fullness through whom Christ fills the cosmos** (passive sense) | The church is the agency-of-Christ's-cosmic-filling; church-as-Christ's-extension. |

The 1:23 KD explicitly names the three-way debate: "ambiguous between (1) Christ-fills-all-things-in-all-people, (2) Christ-fills-all-aspects-of-all-things, (3) the church is the fullness through whom Christ fills the cosmos. The translation preserves the ambiguity."

**The current rendering tilts to (a)/(b):** the active-middle Thai **ทรงเติมเต็มทุกสิ่งในทุกประการ** ("fills all-things in all-respects") with Christ as subject reads most naturally as Christ-as-cosmic-filler. Reading (c) — church-as-instrument-of-Christ's-cosmic-filling — is grammatically possible but less-natural in Thai. NIV/ESV/CSB/BSB all tilt the same way.

**Forward-pertinent:** EPH 3:19 (πληρωθῆτε εἰς πᾶν τὸ πλήρωμα τοῦ θεοῦ — "be filled with all the fullness of God") + EPH 4:10 (Christ ascended ἵνα πληρώσῃ τὰ πάντα — to fill all things) + EPH 4:13 (mature-to-fullness-of-Christ) — all four πλήρωμα verses in EPH form a tight Christological-cluster that this 1:23 reading anchors.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the (a)/(b)-favoring rendering the right corpus-default — Christ-fills-all-things-in-all-people / Christ-fills-all-aspects — vs. shifting to a more-passive (c) church-as-instrument-of-cosmic-filling reading? The current Thai matches NIV/ESV/CSB/BSB but loses the (c) ecclesiology-extension reading some Pauline scholars (esp. those reading EPH ecclesiologically) prefer.
2. Should the project add a footer-note at 1:23 acknowledging the (c) church-as-Christ's-extension reading — or rely on the KD-level rationale + the verse's connection to 3:19 + 4:10 to do the disambiguation work?

## Item C — EPH 4:8 Ps 68:18 Pauline-modification: "received → gave"

**The textual decision:** EPH 4:8 is one of the most-discussed citation-modifications in Paul. The apostle changes the LXX's "received-gifts among-men" (ἔλαβες δόματα ἐν ἀνθρώπῳ) to "gave-gifts to-men" (ἔδωκεν δόματα τοῖς ἀνθρώποις):

- EPH 4:8 GK: `διὸ λέγει· Ἀναβὰς εἰς ὕψος ᾐχμαλώτευσεν αἰχμαλωσίαν, **ἔδωκεν δόματα τοῖς ἀνθρώποις**`
- TH (current): `เพราะฉะนั้นจึงมีคำกล่าวว่า ‘เมื่อพระองค์เสด็จขึ้นสู่ที่สูง พระองค์ได้ทรงนำเชลยไปเป็นเชลย และ**ทรงประทานของประทานแก่มนุษย์**’`
- Ps 68:18 LXX original (for comparison): `ἀνέβης εἰς ὕψος, ᾐχμαλώτευσας αἰχμαλωσίαν, **ἔλαβες δόματα ἐν ἀνθρώπῳ**` (received-gifts among-men)

The Thai correctly mirrors **Paul's** modified-version, NOT the LXX original. The 4:8 KD walks through the issue + the 4:8 thai_summary explains the modification + cites the Targum-tradition that supports Paul's reading.

A Thai-reader who consults a Thai OT translation (e.g., THSV1971 Ps 68:18) will see "received-gifts" in the Psalm and may wonder why Paul "changed" it. This is a CC0 evangelical-Protestant-text-handling question.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the literal-mirror-of-Paul + thai_summary disambiguation the right approach — keeping the Thai NT clean of footnotes? Or should a one-line footer-note be added at EPH 4:8 acknowledging the Pauline-modification + the Targum-tradition that supports it? More academically-honest but adds register-burden + sets a precedent for footnoting all citation-modifications across the NT.
2. Either way, should the citation be flagged in `data/nt_ot_citations.json` with the modification noted (currently it is, with the modification flagged)? Should the project develop a CC0-Bible-wide policy on Pauline-modified-citations before Romans 9–11 ships (where dozens of OT-citations recur with varying degrees of LXX/MT divergence)?

## Item D — EPH 4:9 κατώτερα μέρη τῆς γῆς: descent-to-earth vs descent-to-Hades

**The textual decision:** EPH 4:9 is the most-debated single phrase in EPH-soteriology — bound up with the Apostles'-Creed "descended-into-hell" question:

- EPH 4:9 GK: `τὸ δὲ Ἀνέβη τί ἐστιν εἰ μὴ ὅτι καὶ κατέβη εἰς **τὰ κατώτερα μέρη τῆς γῆς**`
- TH (current, literal-ambiguity-preserving): `แต่คำว่า ‘เสด็จขึ้น’ นั้นหมายความอย่างไร นอกจากว่าพระองค์ก็ได้เสด็จลงไปสู่**ส่วนที่ต่ำกว่าของแผ่นดินโลก**ด้วย?`

Two readings:

| Reading | Claim |
|---|---|
| **(a) Descent to-the-earth** (genitive of apposition: "the earth itself, as the lower-region") — *currently preserved literally* | Christ descended TO the earth in incarnation. Lutheran / Reformed mainstream + most modern English Bibles (NIV, ESV, CSB). |
| **(b) Descent below-the-earth** (genitive of place: "the lower-parts OF the earth, i.e., Sheol/Hades") | Christ descended INTO the underworld (cf. 1 Pet 3:19 "preached to spirits in prison"). Older patristic + Roman-Catholic + some-Reformed reading. |

The 4:9 KD acknowledges both readings explicitly. The Thai **ส่วนที่ต่ำกว่าของแผ่นดินโลก** ("the lower-parts of the earth") is literally-bare — it leaves both readings open in Thai exactly as in Greek. Thai-readers without commentary will probably default to (b) "descent below-the-earth" because the literal preposition reads spatially.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the literal-ambiguity-preserving rendering the right choice for the CC0 evangelical-Protestant Thai Bible, or should the rendering be shifted to **ลงมาสู่แผ่นดินโลกซึ่งเป็นที่ต่ำกว่า** ("down to the earth, which is the lower-region") to force reading (a) and match NIV/ESV/CSB/BSB — sacrificing the textual-ambiguity for evangelical-orthodox-defaultization?
2. Should the project develop a footer-note policy for cases where the bare-Thai rendering tilts the Thai-reader toward a non-mainstream-evangelical default (this verse) vs. the bare-Greek-leaves-both-readings-open principle?

## Item E — EPH 5:14 unidentified citation: source-uncertainty handling

**The textual decision:** EPH 5:14 contains the only NT-citation **without an identified OT source** in the project so far:

- EPH 5:14 GK: `διὸ λέγει· **Ἔγειρε, ὁ καθεύδων, καὶ ἀνάστα ἐκ τῶν νεκρῶν, καὶ ἐπιφαύσει σοι ὁ Χριστός**`
- TH (current): `เพราะฉะนั้นจึงมีคำกล่าวว่า ‘**จงตื่นเถิด ผู้ที่กำลังหลับ จงลุกขึ้นจากความตาย แล้วพระคริสต์จะทรงส่องแสงเหนือเจ้า**’`

Likely sources (commentator-debate):
- An early-Christian baptismal-hymn fragment (most-common modern view)
- A Pauline-paraphrase of Isa 60:1 LXX (`φωτίζου, φωτίζου, Ιερουσαλήμ ... ἥκει σου τὸ φῶς` — "Arise, arise, Jerusalem ... your light has come")
- A composite of multiple OT texts (Isa 26:19 + 51:17 + 52:1 + 60:1)

The 5:14 KD acknowledges the source-uncertainty + the 5:14 thai_summary explains the early-Christian-hymn possibility to Thai readers. The Thai-curly-quotes preserve the citation-form per RULES §5b. The HAPAX ἐπιφαύσκω → **ทรงส่องแสง** (royal-ทρρ for Christ-as-divine-subject) is principled. Not added to `nt_ot_citations.json` (no OT source to cite).

**Question:** Is the citation-marked-without-identified-source approach (Thai-curly-quotes + thai_summary explanation) the right handling for the CC0 evangelical-Protestant Thai Bible? Or should the project either (a) link to the most-likely-OT-source (Isa 60:1 LXX) with a "possibly echoing" footer-note + add to `nt_ot_citations.json` with a "likely-echo / not-direct-quotation" tag, or (b) add a one-line note at the verse acknowledging that the source is debated? Sets a precedent for any future Pauline-or-NT-citation that lacks an identifiable OT source (e.g., 1 Cor 2:9, Jas 4:5).

## Item F — EPH 5:26 λουτρόν+ῥῆμα: baptism-and-word imagery + ῥῆμα-vs-λόγος flattening

**The textual decision:** EPH 5:26 contains the most-condensed baptismal-and-word formula in the Pauline-corpus:

- EPH 5:26 GK: `ἵνα αὐτὴν ἁγιάσῃ καθαρίσας **τῷ λουτρῷ τοῦ ὕδατος ἐν ῥήματι**`
- TH (current): `เพื่อพระองค์จะทรงชำระคริสตจักรให้บริสุทธิ์ โดย**ทรงล้างด้วยการชะล้างน้ำผ่านพระวจนะ**`

The Thai **การชะล้างน้ำผ่านพระวจνะ** preserves both layers: λουτρόν → **การชะล้าง** (washing — corpus-precedent at Tit 3:5 same lemma), ὕδατος → **น้ำ**, ἐν ῥήματι → **ผ่านพระวจνะ** (through-the-word). The 5:26 KD names the baptismal-allusion + the word-of-God-in-baptismal-instruction reading.

**The flattening question:** ῥῆμα ("specific-spoken-word, utterance") is technically distinct from λόγος ("general-message, principle, content"). EPH uses ῥῆμα twice (5:26 + 6:17 "sword of the Spirit, which is ῥῆμα θεοῦ"); in both, the rendering is **พระวจνะ** — the same Thai as for λόγος (corpus-default). This **flattens** the ῥῆμα-vs-λόγος distinction.

For comparison:
- λόγος in EPH 1:13 (`τὸν λόγον τῆς ἀληθείας`) → **พระวจνะแห่งความจริง** (the gospel-message)
- ῥῆμα in EPH 5:26 → **พระวจνะ** (specific-baptismal-utterance)
- ῥῆμα in EPH 6:17 → **พระวจνะ** (specific-Spirit-empowered-word as sword)

A more-distinguishing rendering might use **ถ้อยพระดำรัส** ("spoken-word") or **คำตรัส** ("uttered-word") for ῥῆμα — preserving the specific-spoken-word vs. general-message distinction.

**Two questions:**
1. Should ῥῆμα be distinguished from λόγος in Thai (e.g., **ถ้อยพระดำรัส** for ῥῆμα at 5:26 + 6:17, vs. **พระวจνะ** for λόγος elsewhere)? The translator chose to flatten; the alternative would distinguish but adds Thai-lexical-load that may not match the Greek's actual semantic-distance.
2. Is the baptismal-allusion in 5:26's λουτρόν → **การชะล้าง** the right register for a CC0 evangelical-Protestant Thai Bible — given that Thai-evangelical baptism-vocabulary uses **บัพติศμา** (transliteration) for the ordinance? The Pauline-sacramental-allusion may be slightly-veiled to a Thai evangelical-reader who associates **การชะล้าง** with hygiene rather than baptism.

## Item G — EPH 4:11 fivefold ministry: "pastor and teacher" article-sharing + ministry-list count

**The textual decision:** EPH 4:11 contains the famous **fivefold-or-fourfold-ministry-list**:

- EPH 4:11 GK: `καὶ αὐτὸς ἔδωκεν τοὺς μὲν ἀποστόλους, τοὺς δὲ προφήτας, τοὺς δὲ εὐαγγελιστάς, τοὺς δὲ **ποιμένας καὶ διδασκάλους**`
- TH (current, fivefold-rendering): `และพระองค์เองได้ทรงประทานบางคนให้เป็น**อัครทูต** บางคนเป็น**ผู้เผยพระวจνะ** บางคนเป็น**ผู้ประกาศข่าวประเสริฐ** และบางคนเป็น**ศิษยาภิบาลและอาจารย์**`

The Greek `τοὺς δὲ ποιμένας καὶ διδασκάλους` uses a **single article** governing both ποιμένας and διδασκάλους (Granville-Sharp-style construction) — strongly suggesting a single combined office (pastor-teacher) rather than two-separate offices. The 4:11 notes flag this: "pastor and teacher share a single article in Greek ('the some-as pastors-and-teachers'), suggesting a combined-role rather than two-separate-offices."

**The Thai rendering preserves both nouns as if they were two-distinct-roles** — the most-common-evangelical-reading. ποιμήν → **ศิษยาภิบาล** is the standard Thai-Christian rendering for the modern pastor-office (vs. **ผู้เลี้ยงแกะ** "shepherd" used at MAT 26:31 — context-disambiguated).

**The fivefold-or-fourfold debate:** modern church-polity discussions split on whether this verse names 5 distinct ministries or 4 (with "pastor-teacher" as a single combined office). Pentecostal / charismatic + some Reformed traditions read fivefold (current rendering); other Reformed + Lutheran traditions read fourfold.

**Two questions:**
1. Is the four-distinct-nouns rendering (fivefold reading) the right call vs. compounding the last-pair as **ศิษยาภิบาล-อาจารย์** ("pastor-teacher" with hyphen) or **ศิษยาภิบาลผู้สั่งสอน** ("teaching-pastor") to reflect the Granville-Sharp single-article evidence (fourfold reading)?
2. Either way, should a verse-level KD note acknowledge the fivefold-vs-fourfold debate explicitly + name the polity-traditions on each side? The current KD notes the debate but doesn't lock either reading.

## Item H — EPH 6:10–17 armor-of-God: military-vocabulary register

**The pattern:** EPH 6:10–17 contains **eight NT-HAPAXES** in 8 verses, all rendered with reasonable Thai military-vocabulary equivalents:

| Verse | Greek (HAPAX) | Thai | Sense |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6:11 | πανοπλία | **ยุทโธปกรณ์ครบชุด** | full-armor / panoply |
| 6:12 | πάλη | **การต่อสู้** | wrestling / struggle |
| 6:12 | κοσμοκράτωρ | **เจ้าโลก** | cosmic-ruler |
| 6:15 | ἑτοιμασία | **ความพร้อม** | readiness / preparation |
| 6:16 | θυρεός | **โล่** | large-shield (Roman scutum) |
| 6:16 | βέλος | **ลูกศร** | arrow / dart |
| 6:18 | προσκαρτέρησις | **ความพากเพียร** | perseverance |

Plus the OT-echo cluster: 6:14 (Isa 11:5 belt-of-righteousness; Isa 59:17 breastplate); 6:15 (Isa 52:7 feet-of-good-news-bringers); 6:17 (Isa 59:17 helmet-of-salvation) — all 4 added to `nt_ot_citations.json`.

- EPH 6:11 GK: `ἐνδύσασθε **τὴν πανοπλίαν τοῦ θεοῦ** πρὸς τὸ δύνασθαι ὑμᾶς στῆναι πρὸς τὰς μεθοδείας τοῦ διαβόλου`
- TH: `จงสวม**ยุทโธปกρณ์ครบชุดของพระเจ้า** เพื่อพวกท่านจะสามารถยืนหยัดต้านทานกลอุบายของมารได้`

**The register question:** The Thai military-vocabulary may sound either too-archaic (**ยุทโธปกรณ์** Sanskrit-rooted compound + **โซ่ตรวน** "chains" at 6:20) or too-contemporary (**ลูกศรไฟ** "flame-arrow" — hard to make less-modern in Thai). Worth Thai-evangelical-reader confirmation.

**Question:** Does the Thai military-register match the EPH-evangelical-Thai-reader-ear without drifting into either fantasy-game register or historical-documentary register? Specifically:
- Is **ยุทโธปกรณ์ครบชุด** (military-equipment-full-set, Sanskrit-rooted) the right register for πανοπλία at 6:11 + 6:13 — or too-formal? Alternative: simpler **เครื่องอาวุธ** ("weapons-equipment") or **เกราะครบชุด** ("complete-armor").
- Is **โล่** the right register for θυρεός (the large Roman scutum-style shield, vs. the smaller ἀσπίς round-shield) — or should it be qualified as **โล่ใหญ่** ("large-shield") to distinguish?
- Is **เจ้าโลก** (literally "world-master") the right register for κοσμοκράτωρ — capturing the cosmic-rulership without sounding like a Thai mythology-lord? Already locked per the spiritual-beings-hierarchy doc but worth the external-reviewer check.
