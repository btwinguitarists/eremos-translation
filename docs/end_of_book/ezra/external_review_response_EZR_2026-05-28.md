## Items in detail

### Item A — Foreign-monarch honor register
- **Gemini:** FINE. The two-tier split beautifully balances theological fidelity with historical/sociological realism: withholding `ทรง` in the narrator's voice maintains the project's reservation of divine/covenant honorifics; deferential register (`กราบทูล`, `พระองค์จะทรงพบ`) inside reported correspondence (4:11, 4:15) accurately reflects in-world Persian courtly etiquette.
- **Action:** Lock as-is. **Write `foreign_monarch_register_2026-05.md`** immediately, codifying the exact two-tier rule so Nehemiah, Esther, and Daniel inherit a consistent, documented framework.

### Item B — Ezra 10:3 אֲדֹנָי
- **Gemini:** FINE. Masoretic pointing (אֲדֹנָי with qamats) is traditionally reserved for the divine title, but immediate context strongly supports the human reading "my lord" (אֲדֹנִי). v.4's pivot — Shecaniah addressing Ezra directly ("Arise, for this matter is your responsibility") — establishes that "counsel of my lord" in v.3 refers to Ezra's leadership. BSB/ESV/NIV rightly prioritize contextual coherence over later Masoretic vocalization. The translation's pre-emptive "false positive" classification is correct.
- **Action:** Lock as-is. Existing translator note is sufficient; no divine-names change needed.

### Item C — Ezra vs 1 Esdras Greek recension
- **Gemini:** FINE. 1 Esdras (Esdras A) is a distinct literary composition / recension, not a variant manuscript witness to MT Ezra. Per-verse disclosure footnotes inside the Ezra text are neither required nor appropriate. However, because 1 Esdras's canonical presence in Orthodox traditions creates a high-visibility macro-structural divergence, a macro-level disposition prevents reader confusion without cluttering the scripture text.
- **Action:** Write a single book-level disposition (either in `mt_vs_lxx_textual_variant_handling_2026-05.md` or a general Ezra introduction doc) stating: *"MT Ezra is the base; 1 Esdras is a separate Greek recension, not a textual variant of MT, so no per-verse disclosure is owed."*
- **Cross-book agreement:** 1 Kings Item B independently proposes a Tier-4 macro-structural variant policy (3 Reigns); the 1 Esdras case is a second instance of the same architectural need.

### Item D — "God of heaven" → พระเจ้าแห่งฟ้าสวรรค์
- **Gemini:** FINE. `พระเจ้าแห่งฟ้าสวรรค์` accurately captures both the transcendent theological weight and the imperial-diplomatic nuance of the title as used in the Persian era. It leverages existing locked vocabulary for heaven/sky (`ฟ้าสวรรค์`) and reads naturally in Thai, maintaining continuity across the Hebrew (1:2) and Aramaic (5:11–12) sections. Adding "Most High" modifiers would over-translate.
- **Action:** Lock as-is. **Write `god_of_heaven_persian_title_2026-05.md`** to establish this as the unalterable corpus standard before compounding into Nehemiah and Daniel.
