# Esther 8 Chapter Introduction

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### God’s protection

Yahweh is at work in this chapter preventing the possible destruction of the Jews. God used Esther and Mordecai to protect their people.

## Esther 8:1

**Quote:** בַּ⁠יּ֣וֹם הַ⁠ה֗וּא
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-newevent
This could mean one of three things: (1) A new series of events is now being described, but the story makes clear that they happened on the same day as the second banquet. While it must have been some time later in the day, the idea is that when Haman lost his life, and also his property, Esther gained what her enemy had lost. Alternate translation: [that same day]; (2) The expression could also be an idiom that means “as of that day.” This would refer to the effective date for the transfer of Haman’s property to Esther. Alternate translation: [as of that day]; (3) The term “day” could also be used to mean around the same time.

**Quote:** נָתַ֞ן הַ⁠מֶּ֤לֶךְ אֲחַשְׁוֵרוֹשׁ֙ לְ⁠אֶסְתֵּ֣ר הַ⁠מַּלְכָּ֔ה אֶת־בֵּ֥ית הָמָ֖ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
Here, **house** represents all the household and property of Haman. The story could be referring to all the property by naming one part of it, the house. Or “house” could be a metonym for “property,” meaning everything a person owns and keeps in their house, and by extension everything they own beyond the house as well. Alternate translation: [King Ahasuerus declared that everything that Haman had owned would now belong to Queen Esther]

**Quote:** צֹרֵ֣ר הַיְּהוּדִ֑ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-distinguish
This phrase gives clarifying information about Haman. Alternate translation: [the enemy of the Jews]

**Quote:** וּ⁠מָרְדֳּכַ֗י בָּ֚א לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י הַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The implication is that when the king learned how Mordecai was related to Esther, he summoned Mordecai into his presence. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this explicitly. Alternate translation: [when he learned that, the king sent for Mordecai to come into his presence]

**Quote:** לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י הַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here, **face** represents the presence of a person. This phrase indicates that Mordecai was allowed to come into the king’s presence. Alternate translation: [into his presence]

**Quote:** הִגִּ֥ידָה אֶסְתֵּ֖ר מַ֥ה הוּא־לָֽ⁠הּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-events
You can say this before saying that the king summoned Mordecai, since it happened first. See the UST. Alternate translation: [Esther told the king how Mordecai was related to her]

**Quote:** הִגִּ֥ידָה אֶסְתֵּ֖ר מַ֥ה הוּא־לָֽ⁠הּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Esther told this to the king. She would likely have explained not just how she and Mordecai were related, but that he had raised her after her parents died. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this explicitly. Alternate translation: [Esther told the king that Mordecai was her cousin and that he had been like a father to her]

## Esther 8:2

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּ֨סַר הַ⁠מֶּ֜לֶךְ אֶת־טַבַּעְתּ֗⁠וֹ & וַֽ⁠יִּתְּנָ֖⁠הּ לְ⁠מָרְדֳּכָ֑י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-symaction
Giving the ring to Mordecai showed that Mordecai could now act on the king’s own authority, and it enabled him to do that. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this explicitly. Alternate translation: [the king now gave this ring to Mordecai to show that Mordecai had the power to act on the authority of the king]

**Quote:** אֶת־טַבַּעְתּ֗⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-unknown
See how you translated this in [3:10](../03/10.md). Review the explanation there if that would be helpful. Alternate translation: [the ring that had his official seal on it]

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁ֤ר הֶֽעֱבִיר֙ מֵֽ⁠הָמָ֔ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
This means that the king had taken back his signet ring from Haman. He would have done this when he sentenced Haman to death. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say that explicitly. Alternate translation: [the king had given Haman the ring that had his official seal on it, but he had taken it back from Haman when he sentenced Haman to death, and the king was wearing it again.]

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁ֤ר הֶֽעֱבִיר֙ מֵֽ⁠הָמָ֔ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-events
If it would be helpful in your language, you could put this information first in the verse because it happened before everything else.

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יִּתְּנָ֖⁠הּ לְ⁠מָרְדֳּכָ֑י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
By giving his signet ring to Mordecai, the king gave Mordecai the authority to write important papers that people would have to obey. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this explicitly. See the UST.

**Quote:** וַ⁠תָּ֧שֶׂם אֶסְתֵּ֛ר אֶֽת־מָרְדֳּכַ֖י עַל־בֵּ֥ית הָמָֽן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
To place someone over something means to put them in charge of it. Alternate translation: [Esther appointed Mordecai to be in charge of everything that had belonged to Haman]

**Quote:** בֵּ֥ית הָמָֽן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
See the explanation of this phrase in [verse 1](../08/01.md). Alternate translation: [everything that Haman had owned] or [the household of Haman]

## Esther 8:3

**Quote:** וַ⁠תּ֣וֹסֶף אֶסְתֵּ֗ר וַ⁠תְּדַבֵּר֙ לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י הַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-newevent
Here the exact meaning of the progression of verbs is uncertain, but this verse probably begins a new event in the story separate from what happened in the previous verse. Alternate translation: [then Esther again spoke in the king’s presence]

**Quote:** לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י הַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here, **face** represents the presence of a person. Alternate translation: [while she was in his presence]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תִּפֹּ֖ל לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י רַגְלָ֑י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
Here, **falling** is an idiom referring to the action of bowing down or prostrating oneself, and **face** represents the front of a person, place, or object. Alternate translation: [and she bowed down] or [and she prostrated herself]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תִּפֹּ֖ל לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י רַגְלָ֑י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-symaction
This may actually mean that Esther put her face right on top of the feet of King Ahasuerus. This would have been an act of humility and desperation by which she showed that her need was very great and that she believed the king had great power to help her.

**Quote:** וַ⁠תִּפֹּ֖ל לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י רַגְלָ֑י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Esther’s action was probably a recognized sign of pleading in this culture. However, it would likely still have been remarkable for a queen to fall at the feet of her husband, the king. you could show this by introducing the information with a phrase like “in fact.” Alternate translation: [in fact, to show how desperately she was pleading, Esther got down and put her face right on top of his feet]

**Quote:** לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י רַגְלָ֑י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here, **face** is a metonym meaning the front of a person, place, or object. This phrase means that Esther prostrated herself in front of the feet of King Ahasuerus.

**Quote:** לְ⁠הַֽעֲבִיר֙ אֶת־רָעַת֙ הָמָ֣ן הָֽ⁠אֲגָגִ֔י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
Here, **the evil** refers to Haman’s plan. This expression means to prevent a wrong action from happening. Alternate translation: [to stop the evil plan of Haman the Agagite] or [to prevent the evil things from happening that Haman the Agagite had planned]

**Quote:** הָֽ⁠אֲגָגִ֔י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
This is the name of Haman’s people group. See how you translated this in [3:1](../03/01.md).

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֵת֙ מַֽחֲשַׁבְתּ֔⁠וֹ אֲשֶׁ֥ר חָשַׁ֖ב
Alternate translation: [and the plot that he had invented] or [and the plot that Haman invented]

## Esther 8:4

**Quote:** וַ⁠יּ֤וֹשֶׁט הַ⁠מֶּ֨לֶךְ֙ לְ⁠אֶסְתֵּ֔ר אֵ֖ת שַׁרְבִ֣ט הַ⁠זָּהָ֑ב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-symaction
As in [5:2](../05/02.md), King Ahasuerus did this to show that Esther had his favor. In that earlier episode, this indicated that he would not enforce the law that said Esther should be executed for coming into the inner court without being summoned. But since Esther was apparently already in the king’s presence on this occasion, it seems that the gesture could also be used generally to show that the king was positively disposed towards a person and would grant the person’s request. Alternate translation: [the king held out his golden scepter to Esther]

**Quote:** אֵ֖ת שַׁרְבִ֣ט הַ⁠זָּהָ֑ב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-unknown
See how you translated this in [4:11](../04/11.md). If it would be helpful, review the explanation of what this object was. Alternate translation: [golden scepter]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תָּ֣קָם אֶסְתֵּ֔ר
Alternate translation: [Esther got up off the floor]

**Quote:** לִ⁠פְנֵ֥י הַ⁠מֶּֽלֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here, **face** represents the front of a person, place, or object. This phrase means the Esther now stood facing King Ahasuerus. Alternate translation: [and stood facing the king]

## Esther 8:5

**Quote:** אִם־עַל־הַ⁠מֶּ֨לֶךְ ט֜וֹב & וְ⁠כָשֵׁ֤ר הַ⁠דָּבָר֙ לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י הַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-parallelism
These two phrases mean basically the same thing. Esther is saying very deferentially that she hopes that the king will think that her suggestion is a good idea and will, therefore, grant her request. Esther uses the repetition to emphasize the importance of what she is expressing. If it would be helpful in your language, you could combine the phrases. Alternate translation: [if what I am going to suggest seems like a good idea to you]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אִם־מָצָ֧אתִי חֵ֣ן לְ⁠פָנָ֗י⁠ו & וְ⁠טוֹבָ֥ה אֲנִ֖י בְּ⁠עֵינָ֑י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-parallelism
These two phrases mean basically the same thing. Esther is saying very deferentially that she hopes that the king is positively disposed towards her, that he thinks well of her, and will, therefore, grant her request. Esther uses the repetition to emphasize the importance of what she is expressing. If it would be helpful in your language, you could combine the phrases. Alternate translation: [if you are pleased with me]

**Quote:** אִם־עַל־הַ⁠מֶּ֨לֶךְ ט֜וֹב וְ⁠אִם־מָצָ֧אתִי חֵ֣ן לְ⁠פָנָ֗י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-123person
Here Esther addresses the king throughout in the third person as a way of showing respect. You could express the same meaning in the second person, as in the UST.

**Quote:** אִם־עַל־הַ⁠מֶּ֨לֶךְ ט֜וֹב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
This is an idiom that also appears in [1:19](../01/19.md), [3:9](../03/09.md), [5:4](../05/04.md), and [5:8](../05/08.md). A suggested translation in those cases was, “If it seems like a good idea to you, O king.” That was suitable for the deferential tone the speakers were using. But in another place, [7:3](../07/03.md), when Esther was pleading urgently for the survival of her whole people, the suggested alternate translation was, “I hope you will be willing to do what I ask” Here, Esther is also pleading, so you could use that phrase here in your translation as well. Alternate translation: [if what I ask for seems right in the king’s evaluation]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אִם־מָצָ֧אתִי חֵ֣ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
This expression also appears many times in the story. It means to gain the approval or acceptance of another person. Alternate translation: [if you are pleased with me]

**Quote:** לְ⁠פָנָ֗י⁠ו & לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י הַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here, **face** means the presence of a person. These phrases could mean “as I stand here before you.” But “face” could also be referring to the king himself by naming one part of him. What he thought and felt about Esther’s request would become evident in his face first, so it would be an appropriate part of him to use to represent all of him. Alternate translation: [you]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠עֵינָ֑י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here, **eyes** represent the action of seeing, and seeing is a metaphor for knowledge, notice, attention, or judgment. Alternate translation: [if you evaluate me and I please you] or [if you are pleased with me]

**Quote:** יִכָּתֵ֞ב לְ⁠הָשִׁ֣יב אֶת־הַ⁠סְּפָרִ֗ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The first letters that told of the plan to destroy the Jews would not be brought back to Susa physically. Rather, **bring back** is an idiom that means “cancel” or “revoke.” Alternate translation: [make a new law to cancel what Haman decreed]

**Quote:** יִכָּתֵ֞ב לְ⁠הָשִׁ֣יב אֶת־הַ⁠סְּפָרִ֗ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
You can say this with an active form. Alternate translation: [write a new letter] or [make a new law]

## Esther 8:6

**Quote:** כִּ֠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-result
Here Esther is giving the reason why she is asking King Ahasuerus to revoke the letters. Alternate translation: [the reason I am asking is that]

**Quote:** אֵיכָכָ֤ה אוּכַל֙ וְֽ⁠רָאִ֔יתִי בָּ⁠רָעָ֖ה אֲשֶׁר־יִמְצָ֣א אֶת־עַמִּ֑⁠י וְ⁠אֵֽיכָכָ֤ה אוּכַל֙ וְֽ⁠רָאִ֔יתִי בְּ⁠אָבְדַ֖ן מוֹלַדְתִּֽ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Esther is actually making a statement, but she expresses herself in question form to show how strongly she feels about what she is saying. She does not actually expect the king to tell her how she could bear seeing her people destroyed. Alternate translation: [I cannot bear to see disaster fall on the Jews] or [I cannot endure watching my relatives be killed]

**Quote:** אֵיכָכָ֤ה אוּכַל֙ וְֽ⁠רָאִ֔יתִי בָּ⁠רָעָ֖ה אֲשֶׁר־יִמְצָ֣א אֶת־עַמִּ֑⁠י וְ⁠אֵֽיכָכָ֤ה אוּכַל֙ וְֽ⁠רָאִ֔יתִי בְּ⁠אָבְדַ֖ן מוֹלַדְתִּֽ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-parallelism
These two sentences mean basically the same thing. Esther uses the repetition to emphasize the importance of what she is saying. If it would be helpful in your language, you could combine the phrases. Alternate translation: [I could not bear to see my relatives and my whole people destroyed]

**Quote:** בָּ⁠רָעָ֖ה אֲשֶׁר־יִמְצָ֣א אֶת־עַמִּ֑⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
Even though it was morally very wrong for Haman to want to destroy Mordecai’s whole people, here the abstract noun **evil** likely means “harm,” as in [7:7](../07/07.md). It means the same thing as “destruction” in the next sentence. Alternate translation: [my people being destroyed]

**Quote:** יִמְצָ֣א
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
As in [1:5](../01/05.md), **find** is an idiom that means something exists in a certain place or time, not that people were looking for it and located it. Esther is speaking of the harm that will exist for the Jews if the effect of Haman’s letters is not undone. Alternate translation: [would be destroyed]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠אָבְדַ֖ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
This abstract noun can be expressed with a verb such as “destroyed.”

**Quote:** מוֹלַדְתִּֽ⁠י
Alternate translation: [my relatives]

## Esther 8:7

**Quote:** הָ⁠עֵ֔ץ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-unknown

## Esther 8:8

**Quote:** וְ֠⁠אַתֶּם
Alternate translation: [so this is what you should do]

**Quote:** כִּתְב֨וּ עַל־הַ⁠יְּהוּדִ֜ים כַּ⁠טּ֤וֹב בְּ⁠עֵֽינֵי⁠כֶם֙
Alternate translation: [I am giving you permission to write other letters to save your people]

**Quote:** כַּ⁠טּ֤וֹב בְּ⁠עֵֽינֵי⁠כֶם֙
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here, **eyes** represent the action of seeing, and seeing is a metaphor for knowledge, notice, attention, or judgment. This phrase means that Esther and Mordecai have permission to write what they think is best. Alternate translation: [as you think is best] or [what you think is best]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠שֵׁ֣ם הַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here, **name** is a metaphor meaning authority. This phrase means Esther and Mordecai have permission to write with the authority of the King Ahasuerus.

**Quote:** בְּ⁠שֵׁ֣ם הַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-123person
King Ahasuerus refers to himself in the third person. You can have him say this in the first person. Alternate translation: [I give you permission to write with my own authority]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠טַבַּ֣עַת הַ⁠מֶּ֑לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-unknown
This is a special ring that could be used to imprint the king’s official seal on a law or decree. Alternate translation: [the ring that has my official seal on it]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠טַבַּ֣עַת הַ⁠מֶּ֑לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-123person
King Ahasuerus refers to himself in the third person. You can have him say this in the first person. Alternate translation: [the ring that has my official seal on it]

**Quote:** כִּֽי
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-result
Here, King Ahasuerus is expressing the reason why he cannot simply revoke the first letter and also why Esther and Mordecai can write another letter that people must obey. Alternate translation: [you see] or [people will obey your letter because]

**Quote:** אֵ֥ין לְ⁠הָשִֽׁיב
As in [verse 5](../08/05.md), this expression means “revoke.” Alternate translation: [no one can revoke] or [no one can nullify]

**Quote:** כְתָ֞ב & אֵ֥ין לְ⁠הָשִֽׁיב
If it would be helpful in your language, you could put this first in the verse because it explains why Ahasuerus answers Esther and Mordecai in the way that he does. See the UST.

**Quote:** כְתָ֞ב אֲשֶׁר־נִכְתָּ֣ב בְּ⁠שֵׁם־הַ⁠מֶּ֗לֶךְ וְ⁠נַחְתּ֛וֹם בְּ⁠טַבַּ֥עַת הַ⁠מֶּ֖לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The king is describing a general situation, but he has Haman’s letter specifically in mind. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say that explicitly. Alternate translation: [Haman wrote his letter with my authority, and he sealed it with the ring that has my official seal on it. No one can revoke a letter like that] (The story explained in [1:19](../01/19.md) that the king’s laws could not be changed once they had been made and proclaimed.)

## Esther 8:9

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקָּרְא֣וּ סֹפְרֵֽי־הַ⁠מֶּ֣לֶךְ
