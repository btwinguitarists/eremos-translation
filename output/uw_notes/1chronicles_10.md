## Chapter intro

# 1 Chronicles 10 General Notes

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### The death of Saul

Saul died because he disobeyed God.

---

## 1 Chronicles 10:1

**Quote:** Every man of Israel fled from before the Philistines and fell down dead on Mount Gilboa
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
It is implied that these men were Israelite soldiers. Alternate translation: [The whole army of Israel ran away from the Philistines]

**Quote:** Every man of Israel … fell down dead
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-hyperbole
This is probably a generalization. It seems that while all the soldiers did run away, not all of them died. Alternate translation: [Every man of Israel … most of them died]

## 1 Chronicles 10:4

**Quote:** thrust me through with it
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
The action of stabbing is a metonym for the result, death. Alternate translation: [kill me with it]

**Quote:** these uncircumcised will come
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-nominaladj
The nominal adjective “uncircumcised” can be stated as an adjective. Alternate translation: [these people who are uncircumcised will come] or [these uncircumcised people will come]

**Quote:** these uncircumcised
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here “uncircumcised” represents people who do not belong to Yahweh. Alternate translation: [these people who do not belong to Yahweh] or [these heathen Philistines]

**Quote:** fell on it
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Saul probably stuck the handle into the ground and leaned on the point so the sword would go through his body as he fell down. The action is a metonym for the result, death. Alternate translation: [killed himself with it]

## 1 Chronicles 10:5

**Quote:** fell on his sword
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
He probably stuck the handle into the ground and leaned on the point so the sword would go through his body as he fell down. The action is a metonym for the result, death. See how you translated this in [1 Chronicles 10:4](../10/04.md). Alternate translation: [killed himself with his sword]

## 1 Chronicles 10:6

**Quote:** and his three sons
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-ellipsis
The word “died” is understood from the previous phrase. It can be repeated. Alternate translation: [and his 3 sons died] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-numbers]])

## 1 Chronicles 10:7

**Quote:** When every man of Israel
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-hyperbole
This is probably a generalization. Alternate translation: [When the men of Israel]

**Quote:** they had fled
Alternate translation: [the Israelite soldiers had fled]

**Quote:** the Philistines came and lived in them
“the Philistines came and lived in the cities from which the Israelites had fled.” This probably happened after the events in verses 8–12.

## 1 Chronicles 10:8

**Quote:** It came about
This phrase is used here to mark an important event in the story. If your language has a way for doing this, you could consider using it here.

**Quote:** to strip the dead
Alternate translation: [to take everything of value off of the dead bodies]

**Quote:** Saul and his sons fallen
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-euphemism
Here “fallen” is a polite way to refer to someone who died in battle. Alternate translation: [Saul and his sons dead]

## 1 Chronicles 10:9

**Quote:** They stripped him
Alternate translation: [The Philistines removed everything from Saul’s body]

**Quote:** to carry the news to their idols and to the people
They told the people what had happened and praised their idols in prayer.

**Quote:** to carry the news
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
A person telling others about something that has happened is spoken of as if the person were carrying a solid object and giving it to those other people. Alternate translation: [to tell what had happened]

## 1 Chronicles 10:11

**Quote:** When all Jabesh Gilead heard of all that the Philistines
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-hyperbole
Both instances of the word “all” are generalizations. The name of the town is a metonym for the people who live in the town, and the people group name is a metonym for that people group’s soldiers. Alternate translation: [When the people of Jabesh Gilead heard what the soldiers of the Philistine army] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy]])

**Quote:** Jabesh Gilead
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
This is the name of a town in the region of Gilead.
