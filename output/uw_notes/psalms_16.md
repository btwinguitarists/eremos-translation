## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Psalm

### Land and inheritance imagery in 16:5РђЊ6.

The historical and cultural background for 16:5РђЊ6 is the inheritance of land. 16:6 uses the imagery of measuring cords (lines) which were used to measure and distribute land. These verses probably also have as their background the distribution of the Promised Land to the tribes of Israel and Yahweh promising to be the inheritance of the tribe of Levi. If it would help you to better understand and translate these verses, you can read more about the inheritance of land in Joshua 18РђЊ19 and God providing for the Levites in Numbers 18:20РђЊ24.

### Messiah

In PeterРђЎs sermon (Acts 2:14РђЊ36), he quotes Psalm 16:8РђЊ11 in 2:25РђЊ28 and then explains that David was a prophet and was speaking of the resurrection of the Messiah.
(See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/resurrection]] and [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/anoint]])

## Translation Issues in This Psalm

### What does David mean by Рђюyou will not abandon my soul to Sheol. You will not hand over your godly one to see the pitРђЮ in 16:10?

The word РђюSheolРђЮ refers to the place or location of the dead (where dead people go). The word РђюpitРђЮ also refers to the grave. In this verse David is not saying that God will keep him from ever dying. 1 Kings 2:1РђЊ10 records that David died of old age. Here David is asserting his trust that God will save him from dying an unnatural death. In other words, he trusts that God will save him from being killed by evil men. Notice that this psalm begins with a prayer for protection. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/hades]])
