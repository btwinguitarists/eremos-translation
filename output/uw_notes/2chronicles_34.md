## Chapter intro

# 2 Chronicles 34 General Notes

## Structure and Formatting

This chapter is the beginning of the story of King Josiah. (2 Chronicles 34–35)

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### Returning the people to Yahweh

Josiah worshiped Yahweh and cleaned out the idols and shrines from Judah. The people returned to worship Yahweh again.

## 2 Chronicles 34:2

**Quote:** what was right in the eyes of Yahweh
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here Yahweh’s “sight” refers to how he judges or decides on the value of something. See how you translated a similar phrase in [2 Chronicles 14:2](../14/02.md). Alternate translation: [the things that Yahweh said were right] or [the things that Yahweh considers right]

**Quote:** walked in the ways of David his ancestor
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Josiah behaving as David did is spoken of as if he walked on the same road or way as David. Alternate translation: [lived the way David his ancestor had lived] or [followed the example of David his ancestor]

**Quote:** did not turn away either to the right or to the left
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
To fully obey Yahweh is spoken of as if a person were on the correct road and never turned from it. Alternate translation: [did not do anything that would displease Yahweh] or [fully obeyed the laws of Yahweh]

## 2 Chronicles 34:31

**Quote:** stood in his place
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
This refers to the place where the king stood at the temple. Alternate translation: [stood where he was supposed to stand at the entrance to the temple]

**Quote:** walk after Yahweh
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The way a person lives is spoken of as if that person were walking on a path, and “to walk after” someone is a metonym for doing what that other person does or wants others to do. Alternate translation: [live obeying Yahweh] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy]])

**Quote:** his commandments, his regulations, and his statutes
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-doublet
These words all share similar meanings. Together they emphasize everything that Yahweh had commanded in the law.

**Quote:** with all his heart and all his soul
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The idiom “with all his heart” means “completely” and “with all his soul” means “with all his being.” These two phrases have similar meanings. Alternate translation: [with all his being] or [with all his energy] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-doublet]])

**Quote:** that were written in this book
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
This can be translated in active form. Alternate translation: [that this book contained]
