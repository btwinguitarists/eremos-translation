## Genesis 22:1

**Quote:** וַ⁠יְהִ֗י אַחַר֙ הַ⁠דְּבָרִ֣ים הָ⁠אֵ֔לֶּה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-newevent
This is the beginning of a new episode that takes place around twelve years after Abraham and King Abimelech made their treaty ([chapter 21](../21/01.md)). See how you translated “after those events” in Genesis [15:1](../15/01.md); it may be necessary to translate this phrase in a slightly different way here because of the different context. Alternate translation: [Sometime after those things happened] or [Several years later]

**Quote:** וְ⁠הָ֣⁠אֱלֹהִ֔ים נִסָּ֖ה אֶת־אַבְרָהָ֑ם
Make sure your translation of this clause does not sound like the test was over. Rather it introduces what the following episode is about. Alternate translation: [God wanted to test Abraham]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֣אמֶר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-pronouns
Make sure it is clear in your translation who is speaking here. Alternate translation: [Abraham replied to him]

**Quote:** הִנֵּֽנִ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-politeness
Abraham is responding to God with the courteous expression that a person in this culture would use to tell a superior that he had heard a summons and was ready and willing to obey. Your language may have a comparable expression that you can use in your translation. Alternate translation: [Yes, Master?] or [What is it, Lord]

## Genesis 22:2

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֡אמֶר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
Translate this quote margin in a way that fits this context well. Alternate translation: [Then God said to him] or [Then God told him]

**Quote:** קַח־נָ֠א אֶת־בִּנְ⁠ךָ֨
See how you translated “please” when God is talking to a person in Genesis [13:14](../13/14.md); [15:5](../15/05.md). Your translation of **take** here should not imply the use of force. See how you translated “took” in Genesis [11:31](../11/31.md) and [12:5](../12/05.md).

**Quote:** קַח־נָ֠א אֶת־בִּנְ⁠ךָ֨ אֶת־יְחִֽידְ⁠ךָ֤ אֲשֶׁר־אָהַ֨בְתָּ֙
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-parallelism
These two phrases mean basically the same thing. The second emphasizes the meaning of the first by repeating the same idea with different words. Hebrew poetry was based on this kind of repetition, and it would be good to show this to your readers by including both phrases in your translation rather than combining them. It may be clearer in your language to connect the phrases with a word other than **and** in order to show that the second phrase is repeating the first one, not saying something additional. Alternate translation: [Please take your son, yes, your only son whom you love]

**Quote:** וְ⁠לֶךְ־לְ⁠ךָ֔
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
It is understood that he was to take Isaac with him. If this is not clear in your language, you could make that information explicit. Alternate translation: [and go with him]

**Quote:** אֶל־אֶ֖רֶץ הַ⁠מֹּרִיָּ֑ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Alternate translation: [to the region called Moriah] or [to the Moriah Region]

**Quote:** וְ⁠הַעֲלֵ֤⁠הוּ שָׁם֙ לְ⁠עֹלָ֔ה
See how you translated a similar phrase in Genesis [8:20](../08/20.md). Alternate translation: [and offer him there to me as a burnt offering] or [There you are to burn him up on an altar as a offering to me]

**Quote:** עַ֚ל אַחַ֣ד הֶֽ⁠הָרִ֔ים
Alternate translation: [on top of one of the mountains] or [on top of a mountain]

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁ֖ר אֹמַ֥ר אֵלֶֽי⁠ךָ
Alternate translation: [that I will point out to you]

## Genesis 22:3

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּשְׁכֵּ֨ם אַבְרָהָ֜ם בַּ⁠בֹּ֗קֶר
See how you translated **got up early in the morning** in Genesis [20:8](../20/08.md). Alternate translation: [So the next morning, Abraham got up early] or [Early the next morning Abraham got up]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקַּ֞ח אֶת־שְׁנֵ֤י נְעָרָי⁠ו֙ אִתּ֔⁠וֹ וְ⁠אֵ֖ת יִצְחָ֣ק בְּנ֑⁠וֹ
Alternate translation: [He took two of his servants with him and his son Isaac] or [Then he took his son Isaac and two of his servants]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יְבַקַּע֙ עֲצֵ֣י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The reason Abraham is the only one mentioned in this clause and in the next sentence is that he is in focus. He did not travel alone and he probably did not chop the wood alone. Decide whether or not you need to make that explicit in your translation. Alternate translation: [and he split some firewood with them] or [and they chopped some firewood together]

**Quote:** עֹלָ֔ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
Some languages must translate this noun phrase using a verb. Do what is natural in your language. See what you did in [verse 2](../22/02.md). Alternate translation: [to use for the burnt offering and loaded it on the donkey] or [that they would use to burn the offering and loaded it on the donkey]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּ֣קָם וַ⁠יֵּ֔לֶךְ אֶל־הַ⁠מָּק֖וֹם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
In this context, **got up** is used as an idiom that means Abraham started an activity. It does not mean that he was sitting down and literally stood up. Alternate translation: [Then he set out with them for the place] or [Then they started traveling to the mountain]

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁר־אָֽמַר־ל֥⁠וֹ הָ⁠אֱלֹהִֽים
Alternate translation: [that God had told him to go to]

## Genesis 22:6

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקַּ֨ח אַבְרָהָ֜ם אֶת־עֲצֵ֣י הָ⁠עֹלָ֗ה

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּ֨שֶׂם֙ עַל־יִצְחָ֣ק בְּנ֔⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקַּ֣ח בְּ⁠יָד֔⁠וֹ
Alternate translation: [and he himself carried]

**Quote:** אֶת־הָ⁠אֵ֖שׁ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy

Make sure that your translation of this phrase does not mean that Abraham was holding fire or hot coals in his bare hands. Alternate translation: [a fire pot with hot embers in it] or [the other things to make a fire]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־הַֽ⁠מַּאֲכֶ֑לֶת
Alternate translation: [and a sacrifice knife]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֵּלְכ֥וּ שְׁנֵי⁠הֶ֖ם יַחְדָּֽו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-youdual
Some languages have a dual pronoun that could be used here. Alternate translation: [Then Abraham and Isaac walked on together] or [Then they continued walking toward the mountain]
