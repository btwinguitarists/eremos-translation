## Chapter intro

# 2 Chronicles 28 General Notes

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### King Ahaz’s idolatry

Judah was defeated by all of the neighboring countries because King Ahaz was worshiping all sorts of foreign gods. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/falsegod]])

---

## 2 Chronicles 28:1

**Quote:** twenty years old … sixteen years
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-numbers
“20 years old … 16 years”

**Quote:** what was right in the eyes of Yahweh
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The eyes here represent seeing, and seeing represents judgment. Yahweh saw and did not approve of Ahaz’s actions. See how you translated this in [2 Chronicles 14:2](../14/02.md). Alternate translation: [what Yahweh judged to be right] or [what Yahweh considered to be right]

---

## 2 Chronicles 28:2

**Quote:** he walked in the ways of the kings of Israel
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Walking represents behavior and actions. Alternate translation: [he acted the same way that the kings of Israel had acted] or [he did the things that the kings of Israel had done]

## 2 Chronicles 28:3

**Quote:** the Valley of Ben Hinnom
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
This is a valley in Jerusalem.

**Quote:** before the people of Israel
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The people of those nations fled as the people of Israel moved into the land. The full meaning of this statement can be made explicit. Alternate translation: [before the people of Israel who moved into the land] or [as the people of Israel moved into the land]

---

## 2 Chronicles 28:4

**Quote:** the high places and on the hills and under every green tree
These are places where the people of the other nations worshiped their false gods.

**Quote:** under every green tree
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-hyperbole
God wanted his people to offer sacrifices to him in Jerusalem. The word “every” here is an exaggeration to show how determined King Ahaz was to disobey God by offering sacrifices in many other places instead. Alternate translation: [under many green trees] or [under many green trees around the country]

---

## 2 Chronicles 28:5

**Quote:** Yahweh the God of Ahaz gave him into the hand of the king of Aram
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here the metonym “hand” represents power. God enabling the army of the king of Aram to defeat Ahaz and his army is spoken of as if God had put Ahaz in the hand of the king of Aram. Alternate translation: [Yahweh the God of Ahaz enabled the king of Aram to defeat Ahaz] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor]])

**Quote:** carried away from him
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
“carried away from Ahaz.” Here “him” represents Ahaz’s army. Alternate translation: [carried away from Ahaz’s army]

**Quote:** Ahaz was also given into the hand of the king of Israel who defeated him
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here the metonym “hand” represents power. God enabling the army of the king of Israel to defeat Ahaz and his army is spoken of as if God had put Ahaz in the hand of the king of Israel. If your language does not use the passive form in this way, you can state this in active form or in another way that is natural in your language. Alternate translation: [Yahweh also enabled the king of Israel to defeat Ahaz] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor]] and [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive]])

## 2 Chronicles 28:8

**Quote:** The army of Israel took captive from their relatives
“From their own relatives, the army of Israel took captive.” The word “their” refers to the army of Israel. The narrator speaks of the people of Judah as the relatives of the people of Israel.

**Quote:** 200,000 wives, sons, and daughters
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-numbers
“two hundred thousand wives, sons, and daughters”

## 2 Chronicles 28:9

**Quote:** Oded
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
This is the name of a man.

**Quote:** he gave them into your hand
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here the word “hand” represents power. God enabling the army of Israel to defeat the army of Judah is spoken of as if God had put the army of Judah in the hand of the army of Israel. Alternate translation: [God enabled you to defeat them] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor]])

**Quote:** you have slaughtered them in a rage that reached up to heaven
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Oded speaks of Yahweh knowing how severely the army of Israel slaughtered the army of Judah as if the rage with which the army of Israel slaughtered them were an object so large that it reached into heaven. That Yahweh knows what they have done implies that he will respond. Alternate translation: [you have slaughtered them in such great rage that Yahweh himself has noticed and will take vengeance] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit]])

---

## 2 Chronicles 28:10

**Quote:** But are you not guilty of sins of your own against Yahweh your God?
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Oded asks this rhetorical question to rebuke the army of Israel and to emphasize the positive answer that the question anticipates. Alternate translation: [But you yourselves are guilty of sins of your own against Yahweh your God.]

## 2 Chronicles 28:11

**Quote:** Yahweh’s fierce wrath is on you
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Oded speaks of Yahweh being extremely angry with the Israelite army as if Yahweh’s wrath were an object that were upon them. Alternate translation: [Yahweh is extremely angry with you]

## 2 Chronicles 28:12

**Quote:** Azariah son of Jehohanan, Berechiah son of Meshillemoth, Jehizkiah son of Shallum, and Amasa son of Hadlai
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
These are all names of men.

**Quote:** stood up against those who came back from the war
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The phrase “stood up against” is an idiom that means to confront or to oppose. Alternate translation: [confronted those who came back from the war] or [opposed those who came back from the war]

## 2 Chronicles 28:13

**Quote:** something that will bring on us sin
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The leaders speak of doing something that will cause them to be guilty of sin as if it were sin were an object that the action brings upon them. Alternate translation: [something that will cause us to be guilty of sinning]

**Quote:** there is fierce wrath against Israel
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
This phrase implies that it is Yahweh’s fierce wrath that is against Israel. The words “fierce wrath” can be translated with a verbal phrase. Alternate translation: [Yahweh is extremely angry with the people of Israel] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns]])

## 2 Chronicles 28:19

**Quote:** Yahweh brought Judah low because of Ahaz
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
The metonym “Judah” represents the people of Judah. Yahweh humiliating the people is spoken of as if he physically brought Judah low to the ground. Alternate translation: [Yahweh humiliated the people of Judah because of Ahaz] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor]])

**Quote:** sinned against Yahweh very heavily
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Ahaz sinning very much is spoken of as if sin were a very heavy object. Alternate translation: [sinned very greatly against Yahweh]

## 2 Chronicles 28:23

**Quote:** the gods of Damascus
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The Syrians believed Damascus to be the city where their gods dwelt, much like Yahweh dwelt in Jerusalem. Alternate translation: [the gods whom the Syrians worshiped in Damascus]

**Quote:** gods that had defeated him
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
This expresses Ahaz’s opinion. Because the Syrians had defeated him and his army, Ahaz attributed his defeat to the gods whom the Syrians worshiped. Alternate translation: [gods whom he believed had enabled the Syrian army to defeat him]

**Quote:** they were the ruin of him and of all Israel
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
The word “ruin” can be translated with a verb. Alternate translation: [those gods ruined him and all Israel]

**Quote:** all Israel
Here “Israel” represents the southern kingdom of Judah.

## 2 Chronicles 28:24

**Quote:** the house of God … the house of Yahweh
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here the word “house” represents the temple. Alternate translation: [the temple of God … the temple of Yahweh]

**Quote:** in every corner of Jerusalem
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
This could mean: (1) the word “corner” refers to street corners, or places where streets intersect. Alternate translation: [on every street corner in Jerusalem] or (2) the various parts of Jerusalem are spoken of as if they were the corners of the city. Alternate translation: [in every part of Jerusalem]

**Quote:** he made for himself altars
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Since Ahaz was king, he may have had his workers make these high places. Alternate translation: [he had his workers make altars for him]

## 2 Chronicles 28:25

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Since Ahaz was king, he may have had his workers make these high places. Alternate translation: [he had his workers make high places to burn sacrifices]

## 2 Chronicles 28:26

**Quote:** all his ways, first and last
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Ahaz’s actions are spoken of as if they were a path upon which he walked. The phrase “first and last” refers to those extremes and everything in between. Alternate translation: [all that he did from the beginning of his reign to the end of his reign] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-merism]])

**Quote:** see, they are written in the book … Israel
Alternate translation: [anyone can look at the book … Israel and see that they are written there]

**Quote:** they are written in the book
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
If your language does not use the passive form in this way, you can state this in active form or in another way that is natural in your language. Alternate translation: [someone has written them in the book] or [you can read of them in the book]

**Quote:** the book of the kings of Judah and Israel
This is a book that no longer exists.
