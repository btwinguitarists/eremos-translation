## Genesis 46:2

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֨אמֶר אֱלֹהִ֤ים ׀ לְ⁠יִשְׂרָאֵל֙ בְּ⁠מַרְאֹ֣ת הַ⁠לַּ֔יְלָה וַ⁠יֹּ֖אמֶר יַעֲקֹ֣ב ׀ יַעֲקֹ֑ב
God repeats Jacob’s name to communicate that he has something urgent or important to tell him. Make sure your translation of this phrase does not sound angry or critical. Also see how you translated “vision” in Genesis [15:1](../15/01.md).

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֖אמֶר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For some languages, it is more natural to put this quotation margin (**he said**) after the quote. Alternate translation: [Jacob replied to him]

**Quote:** הִנֵּֽ⁠נִי
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
Consider again how you translated the idiom **Behold me** throughout the book of Genesis. See [22:1](../22/01.md), [7](../22/07.md), [11](../22/11.md); [27:1](../27/01.md), [18](../27/18.md); [31:11](../31/11.md); [37:13](../37/13.md); [46:2](../46/02.md). Alternate translation: [Here I am, Lord] or [Yes, Master?] or [I am listening, Lord]

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy

## Genesis 46:5

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּ֥קָם יַעֲקֹ֖ב מִ⁠בְּאֵ֣ר שָׁ֑בַע
Be consistent with how you spell **Beersheba** throughout the book of Genesis. See [21:14](../21/14.md), [31–33](../21/31.md); [22:19](../22/19.md); [26:23](../26/23.md), [33](../26/33.md); [28:10](../28/10.md); [46:1](../46/01.md), [5](../46/05.md). Alternate translation: [So Jacob left the city of Beersheba] or [After that, Jacob whose other name was Israel left the city of Beersheba]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשְׂא֨וּ בְנֵֽי־יִשְׂרָאֵ֜ל אֶת־יַעֲקֹ֣ב אֲבִי⁠הֶ֗ם
“and his sons, the sons of Israel, took/transported him their father” or “and his sons, that is the sons of Israel, took/transported him” or “and his sons took/transported him.” **Israel** is the name that God gave to Jacob (Genesis [32:28](../32/28.md), [35:10](../35/10.md)). In [chapters 45](../45/01.md) and [46](../46/46.md), the text repeatedly switches back and forth between both of Jacob’s names. Make sure it is clear in your translation that these two names refer to the same person. You could include a footnote that helps readers understand what is happening. Alternate translation: [and his sons, the sons of Israel, took him their father] or [and his sons, that is the sons of Israel, took him] or [and his sons transported him]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־טַפָּ⁠ם֙ וְ⁠אֶת־נְשֵׁי⁠הֶ֔ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
See how you translated “little ones and … wives” in Genesis [45:19](../45/19.md). Alternate translation: [as well as their children and wives]

**Quote:** בָּ⁠עֲגָל֕וֹת
See how you translated **carts** in Genesis [45:19](../45/19.md), [21](../45/21.md), [27](../45/27.md).

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁר & פַּרְעֹ֖ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
For a note about how **Pharaoh** is used like a name in the Bible, see [12:15](../12/15.md). Alternate translation: [that Pharaoh the king of Egypt] or [that the king of Egypt]

**Quote:** שָׁלַ֥ח
Alternate translation: [had sent to Canaan]

**Quote:** לָ⁠שֵׂ֥את אֹתֽ⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
The pronoun **him** refers to Jacob, the head of his family, and includes Jacob’s wives and children. It may be necessary to use the plural pronoun “them” to make that clear. Alternate translation: [to transport him to Egypt] or [for them to ride on to Egypt]
