## Chapter intro

# 2 Samuel 23 Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

This chapter continues the supplemental information about David’s reign as king of Israel that the author provides at the end of the book. It presents the fourth and fifth parts of that information, a song that presents David’s last words and a description of his greatest warriors.

Some translations set each line of poetry farther to the right than the rest of the text to make it easier to read. The ULT does this with the poetry in [23:2–7](../23/02.md).

2 Samuel [23:8–39](../23/08.md) is a list of the famous people in David’s army along with some of the special deeds some of them did. Consider how you have been presenting the lists in 2 Samuel in your translation and how you might present the list in [verses 24–29](../23/24.md) similarly. You may choose to put one name, rather than two, on each line.

## 2 Samuel 23:1

**Quote:** נְאֻ֧ם דָּוִ֣ד בֶּן־יִשַׁ֗י וּ⁠נְאֻ֤ם הַ⁠גֶּ֨בֶר֙ הֻ֣קַם עָ֔ל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-parallelism
These two phrases mean basically the same thing. The second emphasizes the meaning of the first by repeating the same idea with different words. Hebrew poetry was based on this kind of repetition, and it would be good to show this to your readers by including both phrases in your translation rather than combining them. It may be clearer in your language to connect the phrases with a word other than **and** in order to show that the second phrase is repeating the first one, not saying something additional. Review from your work on [chapter 22](../22/01.md) how you decided as a team to represent Hebrew poetry in your translation. Alternate translation: [An oracle of David the son of Jesse, yes, an oracle of the man raised to height] or [An oracle of David the son of Jesse, who was a man raised to height]

**Quote:** הַ⁠גֶּ֨בֶר֙ הֻ֣קַם עָ֔ל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
The expression **raised** is a passive verbal form. If your language would not use that form, you could express the idea in active form or in another way that is natural in your language. Alternate translation: [the man whom God raised to height]

**Quote:** הַ⁠גֶּ֨בֶר֙ הֻ֣קַם עָ֔ל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor

**Quote:** מְשִׁ֨יחַ֙ אֱלֹהֵ֣י יַֽעֲקֹ֔ב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive

**Quote:** אֱלֹהֵ֣י יַֽעֲקֹ֔ב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-possession
The author is using the possessive form to describe **the God of Jacob** that is characterized by **Jacob**, meaning this is the God who chose Jacob and his descendants and who made covenant promises to them. If a speaker of your language would not use the possessive form for this, you could express this idea in another way. Alternate translation: [the God with whom the descendants of Jacob are in a covenant relationship]

## 2 Samuel 23:33
