## Chapter intro

# Genesis 1 Chapter Introduction

### Possible titles for this book:

  - “Genesis”
  - “The Book of Genesis”
  - “Genesis: The First Book By Moses”
  - “Genesis: The Book About Beginnings”
  - “The First Book That God’s Spokesman Moses Wrote: Genesis”

The English title “Genesis” is a transliteration of a Greek word that means “origin” or “beginning” or “birth”; the ancient Greek translation of the Hebrew scriptures (the Septuagint) uses this word for the first time in Genesis [2:4](../02/04.md). The Hebrew title of this book בְּרֵאשִׁית (pronounced “bereshith”) is the first Hebrew word in [verse 1](../01/01.md) and literally means “In the beginning.” The Latin translation of the Bible (the Latin Vulgate) was the first translation to use the title “The Book of Genesis.” Since that time, many other translations include “Genesis” in the book title because people are familiar with that name. Sometimes, as part of the title, translators also include the fact that Moses was the author and that he was inspired by God.

## Genesis 1:1

**Quote:** בְּ⁠רֵאשִׁ֖ית
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
The first chapter of Genesis is a true, historical narrative (which is indicated by the Hebrew language structures that are used throughout the chapter), and [verse 1](../01/01.md) records the first event in that history. This is confirmed by the wider context of the Scriptures, which teach us that God created everything out of nothing at the very beginning of the world. [Psalm 33:6](../../psa/033/006.md), [Psalm 33:9](../../psa/033/009.md); [Hebrews 11:3](../../heb/11/03.md)). Some languages must use a verb (“began”) in [verse 1](../01/01.md) rather than an abstract noun (**beginning**). Alternate translation: [At the beginning of time]

**Quote:** בָּרָ֣א
In the Hebrew Bible, the verb “create” refers to an activity that only God does, and it often implies (as it does here) that he made something out of nothing. Also, the forms of the verbs in the Hebrew text (and the conjunctions) show that [chapter 1](../01/01.md) is a narrative that tells about true history and events in the order that things actually happened. Make sure that your translation does the same thing. Alternate translation: [made]

**Quote:** אֵ֥ת הַ⁠שָּׁמַ֖יִם
Alternate translation: [the sky]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֵ֥ת הָ⁠אָֽרֶץ
This phrase refers to the planet earth.

## Genesis 1:2

**Quote:** וְ⁠הָ⁠אָ֗רֶץ
The author pauses here to describe what the earth and the heavens were like after God first created them ([verse 1](../01/01.md)). Some languages use a conjunction like **Now** to introduce that information, while other languages do not use a conjunction here. Do what is natural in your language. Also, be consistent here with how you translated **the earth** in [verse 1](../01/01.md). Alternate translation: [Now at first after that, the earth]

**Quote:** הָיְתָ֥ה תֹ֨הוּ֙ וָ⁠בֹ֔הוּ
It is not clear what the earth looked like at that time, but it did not have its present shape and order. The phrase **was formless and empty** could mean: (1) “did not have its present form, and there was nothing living on it yet” (2) “completely desolate,” which treats the text as a hendiadys. However, it is best to keep both terms separate in your translation (as they are in the Hebrew text).

**Quote:** וְ⁠חֹ֖שֶׁךְ עַל־פְּנֵ֣י תְה֑וֹם
Alternate translation: [It was completely dark, there was deep water everywhere] or [and everywhere there was deep water that was surrounded by total darkness]

**Quote:** וְ⁠ר֣וּחַ אֱלֹהִ֔ים מְרַחֶ֖פֶת
The clause **and the Spirit of God was hovering** could mean: (1) “and God’s Spirit was hovering” or (2) “and a wind from God was blowing”

**Quote:** עַל־פְּנֵ֥י הַ⁠מָּֽיִם
Throughout this chapter, the word **waters** is in the plural in the Hebrew text. For some languages it is more natural to use the singular “water.” Alternate translation: [over the surface of the water] or [above the waters]

## Genesis 1:3

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֥אמֶר אֱלֹהִ֖ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
What God says in [verse 3](../01/03.md) is a command. Consider whether or not it is best in your language to make that explicit in this quote margin. Alternate translation: [Then God commanded]

**Quote:** יְהִ֣י א֑וֹר
This is a command, not a permission or a suggestion. It is a direct quote of what God actually said, and it is usually put between quotation marks in a translation. If you use an exclamation point with this command in your translation, make sure it does not mean or imply that God was angry. Also, the way you translate **light** should refer to what shines from a source of light; it does not refer here to the source itself. Alternate translation: [Let light exist] or [I command there to be light]

## Genesis 1:4

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּ֧רְא אֱלֹהִ֛ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-words-phrases
Notice that Hebrew frequently uses a conjunction such as **And** or **Then** at the beginning of a sentence. Some languages do not use conjunctions as frequently as Hebrew uses conjunctions. Do what is accurate and natural in your language in each context. Alternate translation: [And he saw] or [He saw]

**Quote:** אֶת־הָ⁠א֖וֹר כִּי־ט֑וֹב
In the Bible, **good** is an important theme. Throughout [chapter 1](../01/01.md), it especially means that what God created was high quality, complete, and had no defects; in other words, it had all the characteristics and functions that God intended it to have. The word **good** also includes the idea that what God created was untainted by sin and reflected his good character ([Psalm 19:1](../../psa/019/001.md); [Romans 1:20](../../rom/01/20.md)). The focus here is not on how pleased or satisfied God was. Alternate translation: [that the light was good]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּבְדֵּ֣ל אֱלֹהִ֔ים בֵּ֥ין הָ⁠א֖וֹר וּ⁠בֵ֥ין הַ⁠חֹֽשֶׁךְ
Alternate translation: [Then he caused the light to have its own time and the darkness to have its own time] or [Then he separated the light from the darkness so that it would be light for a number of hours and then dark for a number of hours]

## Genesis 1:5

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקְרָ֨א אֱלֹהִ֤ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-words-phrases
Some languages omit the connecting word **And** or **Then** here. Consider what is the best way in your language to connect this sentence with the previous one. Alternate translation: [Then He called] or [God named]

**Quote:** לָ⁠אוֹר֙ י֔וֹם
Notice that in this context the word **Day** refers only to the time when the sun is shining, not to the 24-hour time period that makes up a complete day. Make sure that this is clear in your translation. Throughout [chapter 1](../01/01.md), some translations use quote marks around the names that God gives the different things he created.

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יְהִי־עֶ֥רֶב וַֽ⁠יְהִי־בֹ֖קֶר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-time-sequential
The evening and the morning are the parts of a 24-hour day that separate the hours of daytime (light) from the hours of nighttime (darkness): Evening came at the end of each daytime after God finished his work of creating. Then after about 12 hours of nighttime, morning came, which began the next 12 hours of daytime when God did more work. Make sure your translation does not sound like morning came immediately after evening. Some languages have a one-word conjunction that means “and then” or “and later” and would fit well here. Alternate translation: [Then evening came, and then morning came]

**Quote:** י֥וֹם אֶחָֽד
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-ordinal
Here, **day** refers to a full 24-hour day. Also, the Hebrew text uses a cardinal number (**one**) here, but then uses ordinal numbers (“second,” “third,” and so on) for the rest of the days of the creation week. Some languages need to use an ordinal number (“first”) here too. Other languages use cardinal numbers throughout (“day one,” “day two,” and so on). Alternate translation: [and that was day one]

## Genesis 1:11

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֣אמֶר אֱלֹהִ֗ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
Alternate translation: [Then God commanded]

**Quote:** תַּֽדְשֵׁ֤א הָ⁠אָ֨רֶץ֙ דֶּ֔שֶׁא
The Hebrew word for **vegetation** refers generally here to any kind of green plant and includes the plants and trees that are mentioned next in this verse. Also, see how you translated “Land” in [verse 10](../01/10.md). Alternate translation: [I command the ground to grow green plants] or [The ground must now be covered with green plants]

**Quote:** עֵ֚שֶׂב מַזְרִ֣יעַ זֶ֔רַע
This phrase refers to many different kinds of plants, not just one plant or one seed. Make sure that is clear in your translation. Also, these kinds of plants have soft stems and include vegetable plants, herbs and other plants that produce edible seeds, such as rice, corn, and wheat. Alternate translation: [including every kind of plant that has seeds] or [including all kinds of plants that bear seeds]

**Quote:** עֵ֣ץ פְּרִ֞י עֹ֤שֶׂה פְּרִי֙ לְ⁠מִינ֔⁠וֹ
The phrase **according to its kind** emphasizes that each different kind of tree produces its own kind of fruit and seeds, which grow and become only that kind of tree. For example, apple trees always produce apples with apple seeds, never peaches or cherries, or any other kind of fruit or seeds. Alternate translation: [and fruit trees that bear fruit, each according to its own kind] or [and every kind of fruit tree that bears its own kind of fruit] or [and all kinds of fruit trees that bear their own kind of fruit]

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁ֥ר זַרְע⁠וֹ־ב֖⁠וֹ
Alternate translation: [that has seeds inside] or [with its own kind of seeds inside it]

**Quote:** עַל־הָ⁠אָ֑רֶץ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For some languages it is clearer and more natural to put this phrase earlier in this verse and say, “Let the land sprout vegetation throughout the earth …” Alternate translation: [throughout the earth]

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יְהִי־כֵֽן
Either a period or a colon can be used here, but a colon more clearly shows that [verse 12](../01/12.md) gives the details of what happened. This is different from [verse 7](../01/07.md), where the details of what happened come before **And it was so**, and from [verse 9](../01/09.md), where no additional details are given. Alternate translation: [And it happened exactly as God commanded it to happen:]

## Genesis 1:14

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֣אמֶר אֱלֹהִ֗ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
Alternate translation: [Then God commanded]

**Quote:** יְהִ֤י מְאֹרֹת֙
These lights refer to the sun, the moon, and stars that give off light. But it is important to keep this general in your translation (like the Hebrew text does) and not specify the names of these lights. Alternate translation: [Let there be objects that give light]

**Quote:** בִּ⁠רְקִ֣יעַ הַ⁠שָּׁמַ֔יִם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicitinfo
It might seem that the expression **the expanse of the heavens** contains extra information that would not be natural to express in your language. If so, you could shorten it. The expanse is the same thing as the heavens ([verse 8](../01/08.md)), so some translations leave **the expanse of** implied here. Alternate translation: [in the space of the sky] or [in the open space of the sky] or [in the heavens]

**Quote:** לְ⁠הַבְדִּ֕יל
Alternate translation: [to divide] or [that separate]

**Quote:** וְ⁠הָי֤וּ לְ⁠אֹתֹת֙
Alternate translation: [Let those lights also be] or [They will also serve as markers] or [I also command those lights to serve as]

**Quote:** וּ⁠לְ⁠מ֣וֹעֲדִ֔ים וּ⁠לְ⁠יָמִ֖ים וְ⁠שָׁנִֽים
The sun and moon show the passing of time. For example, the 30-day month is determined by the phases of the moon, and the 365-day year is determined by the time it takes for the earth to travel around the sun. The **seasons** are regular times of the year, such as seed-planting and harvesting times, and the seasons of spring, summer, fall and winter (Genesis [8:22](../08/22.md)), as well as the special festivals which God appointed for his people to meet with him. You could put some of that information in a footnote.

## Genesis 1:16

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּ֣עַשׂ אֱלֹהִ֔ים
[Verses 16–18](../01/16.md) explain what God’s commands in [verses 14–15](../01/14.md) caused to happen. Make sure your translation of these verses does not sound like he created the lights twice. See how you translated **So God made** in [verse 7](../01/07.md). Alternate translation: [In that way God made] or [That is how he made]

**Quote:** אֶת־שְׁנֵ֥י הַ⁠מְּאֹרֹ֖ת הַ⁠גְּדֹלִ֑ים
This phrase refers to the sun and the moon, which are the two brightest objects in the earth’s sky. However, the author intentionally does not use their names, so you should not include their names in your translation either. Alternate translation: [the two big lights]

**Quote:** אֶת־הַ⁠מָּא֤וֹר הַ⁠גָּדֹל֙
The focus is on the relative brightness or strength of the lights, not on their size. Alternate translation: [the stronger light]

**Quote:** לְ⁠מֶמְשֶׁ֣לֶת הַ⁠יּ֔וֹם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-personification
The word **rule** is a figure of speech that means each light has the greatest effect during a particular time period, because it is the brightest light that is shining then. For some languages, nonliving objects like the sun and the moon cannot **rule** because only people can do that. Do what is accurate and natural in your language. Alternate translation: [to govern the day]

Alternate translation: [And it was exactly as God said it would be]

## Genesis 1:31

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּ֤רְא אֱלֹהִים֙
See how you translated “God saw …” in [verse 4](../01/04.md). Alternate translation: [Then God observed]

**Quote:** אֶת־כָּל־אֲשֶׁ֣ר עָשָׂ֔ה
This phrase refers to everything that God made during the entire week of Creation, not just on the sixth day. Consider again how you translated **made** (or “make”) in [verses 7](../01/07.md), [16](../01/16.md), [25](../01/25.md), [26](../01/26.md) and [31](../01/31.md). Compare that to how you translated a word that has a similar meaning (“created”) in [verses 1](../01/01.md), [21](../01/21.md) and [27](../01/27.md). Alternate translation: [everything that he had created]

**Quote:** וְ⁠הִנֵּה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
This word calls attention to what God is looking at and helps emphasize that what he saw was very good. Alternate translation: [and indeed]

**Quote:** ט֖וֹב מְאֹ֑ד
Consider again how you translated “good” in [verses 4](../01/04.md), [10](../01/10.md), [12](../01/12.md), [18](../01/18.md), [21](../01/21.md), [25](../01/25.md), and [31](../01/31.md). Alternate translation: [it was all very good]

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יְהִי־עֶ֥רֶב וַֽ⁠יְהִי־בֹ֖קֶר
Consider again how you translated these phrases in [verses 5](../01/05.md), [8](../01/08.md), [13](../01/13.md), [19](../01/19.md), [23](../01/23.md), and [31](../01/31.md), and see the note about that at [verse 5](../01/05.md). Alternate translation: [Then evening came, and then morning came]
