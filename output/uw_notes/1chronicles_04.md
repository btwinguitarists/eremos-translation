## 1 Chronicles 4:10

**Quote:** expand my territory
Alternate translation: [give me more land]

**Quote:** your hand will be with me
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Possible meanings are that God’s **hand:** is: (1) a metonym for his guidance, his power, or his protection. Alternate translation: [you will guide me] or [you will make me prosper] or [you will protect me] or (2) a synecdoche for himself. Alternate translation: [you will be with me] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche]])

**Quote:** granted him his prayer
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
The words “his prayer” are a metonym for what Jabez asked in the prayer. Alternate translation: [did what Jabez had asked him to do]

## 1 Chronicles 4:17

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-versebridge
# General Information:

You may want to combine the information in 1 Chronicles 4:17–18 into one verse so its meaning can be more easily understood.

**Quote:** Ezrah … Jether … Mered … Epher … Jalon … Miriam … Shammai … Ishbah … Eshtemoa
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
These are the names of men.

**Quote:** These were the sons of Bithiah
The word “these” refers to Miriam, Shammai, and Ishbah. They were the sons Bithiah bore for her husband Mered.

**Quote:** Bithiah
This is the name of a woman.

**Quote:** Jered … Gedor … Heber … Soko … Jekuthiel … Zanoah
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names

**Quote:** Mered’s Judahite wife
