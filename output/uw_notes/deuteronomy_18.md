## Chapter intro

# Deuteronomy 18 Chapter Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

This chapter continues Moses’ second speech, which began in chapter five.

1. Yahweh’s provision for the priests and all Levites (1–8)
2. Warnings against pagan religious practices (9–14)
3. Yahweh promises to send a prophet like Moses (15–22)

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### Levites

As Yahweh’s chosen priests, the Levites were to trust in Yahweh to provide for them. They did not inherit any land, but Yahweh was their inheritance, in the sense that they received the privilege of being his priests. Yahweh provided for their needs through the rest of the Israelites, because the Levites were allowed to eat portions of the sacrifices that the Israelites sacrificed to Yahweh. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/elect]], [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/priest]] and [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/trust]] and [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/inherit]])

## Translation Issues in This Chapter

### “you” and “your” singular and plural

Even though Moses is addressing all the Israelites, he uses the singular forms of “you.” If the singular forms of these pronouns would not be natural in your language, you could use the plural forms in your translation. (See: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-youcrowd]])

### Generic nouns in hypothetical situations

Verses 6–10 and 20–22 give instructions and warnings for the Israelites. In these hypothetical conditions, singular nouns like “Levite,” “maker,” and “prophet” represent those groups of people in general, not a specific person. If it would be helpful in your language, you could clarify that these terms refer to any person who fulfills the hypothetical situation. (See: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-genericnoun]] and [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical]])

## Deuteronomy 18:1

**Quote:** לֹֽא־יִ֠הְיֶה לַ⁠כֹּהֲנִ֨ים הַ⁠לְוִיִּ֜ם כָּל־שֵׁ֧בֶט לֵוִ֛י חֵ֥לֶק וְ⁠נַחֲלָ֖ה עִם־יִשְׂרָאֵ֑ל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The implication is that the Levites will not receive any **portion** or **inheritance** of land so that they cannot grow or raise their own food. See how you translated the similar phrase “Levi has no portion nor inheritance with his brothers” in [10:9](../10/09.md). Alternate translation: [All the tribe of Levi will not own a portion of land] or [The Levites will not be given a portion of land]

**Quote:** לַ⁠כֹּהֲנִ֨ים הַ⁠לְוִיִּ֜ם כָּל־שֵׁ֧בֶט לֵוִ֛י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-distinguish
Moses is not distinguishing between **priests** who are **Levites** and priests who are not Levites. All **priests** came from **the tribe of Levi** and were therefore **Levites**. However, not all people of the tribe of Levi were priests. Be sure that this is clear in your translation. Alternate translation: [People from the tribe of Levi who serve as priests and all the rest of the Levites]

**Quote:** אִשֵּׁ֧י יְהוָ֛ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-possession
Here Moses is using the possessive form to describe **gifts** that the Israelites would offer to **Yahweh**. If your language would not use the possessive form for this, you could use a comparable expression for describing an offering. Alternate translation: [the food that the other Israelites give as an offering to Yahweh]
