## Chapter intro

# 2 Kings 12 Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

This chapter continues the story of King Joash of Judah. It tells how he ensured that the temple of Yahweh was repaired and maintained.

## 2 Kings 12:4

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁר־יוּבָ֤א
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
The expression **is brought** is a passive verbal form. If your language would not use that form, you could express the idea in active form or in another way that would be natural in your language. Alternate translation: [that people bring]

**Quote:** בֵית־יְהוָה֙
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
See how you translated the similar expression in [5:18](../05/18.md). Alternate translation: [into the temple of Yahweh]

**Quote:** כֶּ֣סֶף עוֹבֵ֔ר אִ֕ישׁ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
Joash is using **the silver passing over a man** as a common expression of his culture to mean the money that each Israelite male would pay during a census for the maintenance of the temple. (This was commanded in the law of Moses in [Exodus 30:11–16](../../exo/30/11.md) and originally supported the worship in the tabernacle.) If this phrase does not have that meaning in your language, you could use a comparable expression from your language that does have that meaning, or you could state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [the silver collected during a census] or [census money]

**Quote:** כֶּ֥סֶף נַפְשׁ֖וֹת עֶרְכּ֑⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Joash is referring to **silver** that someone would pay to redeem a person or animal that he had dedicated to Yahweh, as described in [Leviticus 27:1–13](../../lev/27/01.md). You could indicate this explicitly in your translation if that would be helpful to your readers. Alternate translation: [silver paid to redeem people or animals dedicated to Yahweh]

**Quote:** כָּל־כֶּ֗סֶף אֲשֶׁ֤ר יַֽעֲלֶה֙ עַ֣ל לֶב־אִ֔ישׁ לְ⁠הָבִ֖יא בֵּ֥ית יְהוָֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Joash is speaking as if **silver** could actually go **up onto the heart of a man**. He is using the heart to represent a person’s desires. If it would be helpful in your language, you could state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [all of the silver that a person desired to bring into the temple of Yahweh]
