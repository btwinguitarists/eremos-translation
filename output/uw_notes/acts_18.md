## Chapter intro

# Acts 18 Chapter Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

  - Verses 1–17 describe how Paul proclaimed the gospel in the city of Corinth.
  - Verses 18–23 describe how Paul returned to Antioch and then started out on another missionary journey.
  - Verses 24–28 describe how a man named Apollos became an effective preacher of the gospel.

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### The baptism of John

Some Jews who lived far away from Jerusalem and Judea had heard of John the Baptist and followed his teachings. They had not yet heard about Jesus. One of these Jews was Apollos. He followed John the Baptist, but he did not know that the Messiah had come. John had baptized people to show that they were sorry for their sins, but that kind of baptism was different from Christian baptism. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/faithful]] and [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/christ]] and [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/repent]])

## Acts 18:6

**Quote:** ἐκτιναξάμενος τὰ ἱμάτια
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-symaction
This symbolic action was an expression of strong rejection in this culture. It showed that someone did not want even the dust of a place, in this case the synagogue, to remain on them. If there is a similar gesture in your culture, you could consider using it here in your translation. See what you did with the similar gesture in [13:51](../13/51.md).

**Quote:** τὸ αἷμα ὑμῶν ἐπὶ τὴν κεφαλὴν ὑμῶν
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy

**Quote:** τὴν κεφαλὴν ὑμῶν

**Quote:** καθαρὸς ἐγώ

## Acts 18:18

**Quote:** ὁ δὲ Παῦλος ἔτι προσμείνας ἡμέρας ἱκανὰς
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-contrast

**Quote:** τοῖς ἀδελφοῖς
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor

**Quote:** Κενχρεαῖς
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
The word **Cenchreae** is the name of a city. This city was the seaport for the larger inland city of Corinth.

**Quote:** κειράμενος ἐν Κενχρεαῖς τὴν κεφαλήν, εἶχεν γὰρ εὐχήν
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-symaction
Paul cut the hair off **his head** to show that he had fulfilled a vow that he had made to God. If this would not be clear to your readers, you could explain the significance of this action. It may be helpful to begin a new sentence here. Alternate translation: [Paul cut off his hair in Cenchrea to show that he had fulfilled a vow that he had made to God]

**Quote:** εἶχεν γὰρ εὐχήν
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure

**Quote:** εἶχεν γὰρ εὐχήν
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-extrainfo
Luke tells us nothing further about why Paul made this vow or what he did to carry it out, so it would not be appropriate to add any information in your translation about why Paul made it.

**Quote:** εὐχήν
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-unknown
This **vow** was a promise that a person in this culture made to God that he would not drink alcohol or cut his hair until the end of a set period of time. A person might do this to express his sincerity about something he was praying for, to acknowledge something that God had done for him, or to consecrate himself to deeper devotion to God for a time. In your translation, you may be able to use the word for a comparable practice in your culture. Alternate translation: [made a special promise to God not to cut his hair for a time in order to be more devoted]
