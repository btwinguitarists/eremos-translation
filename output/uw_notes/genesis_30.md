## Genesis 30:13

**Quote:** בְּ⁠אָשְׁרִ֕⁠י
Although many translations have “happy” in this verse instead of **blessed**, the Hebrew noun (and the verb it is based on) is more general and has to do with a person’s blessed state (which results in joy and happiness). The same Hebrew word is used in [Psalm 1:1](../../psa/001/001.md), and the equivalent Greek word is used in [Luke 1:48](../../luk/01/48.md) and [Matthew 5:3–11](../../mat/05/03.md). Alternate translation: [I am truly blessed!] or [God has greatly blessed me!]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תִּקְרָ֥א אֶת־שְׁמ֖⁠וֹ
Alternate translation: [So she named him] or [So she gave him the name]

**Quote:** אָשֵֽׁר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
The word **Asher** is the name of a Leah’s baby boy. If you include the meaning of Asher’s name in your translation or in a footnote, make sure it matches the way you translated **blessed** earlier in this verse.

**Quote:** כִּ֥י אִשְּׁר֖וּ⁠נִי בָּנ֑וֹת
Alternate translation: [Now other women will say that I am blessed by God] or [Now other women will say that God has blessed me]
