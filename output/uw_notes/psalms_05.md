## Chapter intro

# Psalm 5 Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

  1. Appeal (1–2)
  2. Precedent (3–7)
  3. Petition (8–11)
  4. Resolution (12)

## About the Psalm

  1. Purpose: to petition the divine King and Judge for justice from enemies
  2. Content: Yahweh, act justly! Banish the evildoers! Shelter the righteous!
  3. Message: Yahweh is a just ruler, and he blesses the just.

## Translation Issues in This Psalm

  1. The first word of the content portion of the psalm starts with the first letter of the Hebrew alphabet and the final word starts with the last letter of the Hebrew alphabet. This symbolizes completeness; the author’s appeal to Yahweh is complete.
  2. There is a parallel structure between verses 3–7 and verses 8–11:

A. Verses 3 and 8 start with an address to Yahweh, who is also the subject.

B. Verses 4 and 9 start with “For” followed by a word of negation. They use a verbless clause in Hebrew, and the wicked are the topic.

C. Verses 7 and 11 contrast with what is immediately before, and they speak of being with Yahweh.

D. There is also progression between each earlier verse and its related later verse either in specificity or time.

## Psalms 5:2

**Quote:** מַלְכִּ֥⁠י וֵ⁠אלֹהָ֑⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-possession
The psalmist is not referring to two people; both of these terms refer to Yahweh. He is also not using the phrase **my king** or **my God** to mean that he possesses or controls Yahweh, but rather to emphasize that Yahweh is the God he worships. See the introduction to Psalms for further discussion. Alternate translation: [you are the king and God whom I worship]

## Psalms 5:8

**Quote:** נְחֵ֬⁠נִי בְ⁠צִדְקָתֶ֗⁠ךָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The author speaks of **righteousness** as if it were a path, and of teaching as guiding. If it would be helpful in your language, you could state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [teach me to do what is righteous as you are righteous] or [teach me to do what is righteous as you do what is righteous]

**Quote:** שׁוֹרְרָ֑⁠י
Alternate translation: [those who watch for a chance to attack me]

**Quote:** הַיְשַׁ֖ר לְ⁠פָנַ֣⁠י דַּרְכֶּֽ⁠ךָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The author speaks of following Yahweh’s commands as if doing so were a path. He asks Yahweh to put a straightened path before him, because a straight path is easy to see or walk on. If it would be helpful in your language, you could state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [show me clearly how to live in the right way] or [make it easy for me to do what is right]

## Psalms 5:9

**Quote:** אֵ֪ין בְּ⁠פִ֡י⁠הוּ נְכוֹנָה֮
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
Here, **firmness** means trustworthiness or dependability. If your language does not use an abstract noun for this idea, you could express the same idea in another way. Alternate translation: [you cannot trust what they say] or [you cannot rely on them]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠פִ֡י⁠הוּ & גְּרוֹנָ֑⁠ם & לְ֝שׁוֹנָ֗⁠ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
The **his mouth**, **their throat**, and **their tongue** represent people’s speech. If it would be helpful in your language, you could use an equivalent expression or state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [in their speech … are their words … their talk] or [what they say … is everything they say … everything they say]

**Quote:** קִרְבָּ֪⁠ם הַ֫וּ֥וֹת
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
The **inward being** represents people’s thoughts and desires. If it would be helpful in your language, you could use an equivalent expression or state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [their thoughts and desires are for destruction]

**Quote:** קֶֽבֶר־פָּת֥וּחַ גְּרוֹנָ֑⁠ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Their **throat** is spoken of as if it were an open tomb. This could mean: (1) what they say is bad, like the smell of dead bodies. Alternate translation: [Their words are as bad as the smell from a tomb] or (2) it is ready for dead bodies to be put into it. Alternate translation: [They say that they will kill people] or [What they say kills people] If it would be helpful in your language, you could use an equivalent expression or state the meaning plainly.

**Quote:** לְ֝שׁוֹנָ֗⁠ם יַחֲלִֽיקוּ⁠ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here the author is comparing what the enemies say to something **smooth**, which means they flatter people. If it would be helpful in your language, you could use a comparable expression or state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [they lie to people to get what they want] or [they say nice things about people without really meaning it]
