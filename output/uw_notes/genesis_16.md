## Genesis 16:1

**Quote:** וְ⁠שָׂרַי֙ אֵ֣שֶׁת אַבְרָ֔ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-background
This verse begins a new topic by giving background information about Sarai. Some languages have a special conjunction for this. Other languages do not use a conjunction here.

**Quote:** לֹ֥א יָלְדָ֖ה ל֑⁠וֹ
Sarai’s barrenness is first mentioned in Genesis [11:30](../11/30.md). Alternate translation: [still had not birthed any children for him]

**Quote:** שִׁפְחָ֥ה מִצְרִ֖ית
See how you translated “female slaves” in Genesis [12:16](../12/16.md). Alternate translation: [an Egyptian slave woman] or [a female slave from the country of Egypt]

## Genesis 16:2

**Quote:** וַ⁠תֹּ֨אמֶר שָׂרַ֜י אֶל־אַבְרָ֗ם
Alternate translation: [Then she told Abram]

**Quote:** הִנֵּה־נָ֞א
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Sarai is using the term **Behold** to focus Abram’s attention on what she is about to say. Your language may have a comparable expression that you can use in your translation. Also, see how you translated this phrase in Genesis [12:11](../12/11.md). Alternate translation, as a complete sentence: [Hear me, please.] or [Please listen to me]

**Quote:** עֲצָרַ֤⁠נִי יְהוָה֙ מִ⁠לֶּ֔דֶת
Alternate translation: [Since Yahweh has not permitted me to bear any children] or [As you know, Yahweh has not permitted me to bear any children, so]

**Quote:** בֹּא־נָא֙ אֶל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-euphemism
See how you translated the idiom “went to” in Genesis [6:4](../06/04.md). Alternate translation: [Please sleep with] or [Please have sexual relations with]

**Quote:** שִׁפְחָתִ֔⁠י
See how you translated **maidservant** in [verse 1](../16/01.md). Alternate translation: [my slave woman Hagar]

**Quote:** אוּלַ֥י
Alternate translation: [Maybe] or [so that perhaps]

**Quote:** אִבָּנֶ֖ה מִמֶּ֑⁠נָּה
Alternate translation: [I can build a family through her children] or [she can have children for me so that I can build a family]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשְׁמַ֥ע אַבְרָ֖ם לְ⁠ק֥וֹל שָׂרָֽי
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
The author is using the term **voice** to mean her words. If it would be helpful in your language, you could state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [Abram heeded Sarai’s request] or [So Abram listened to the request made by Sarai]

## Genesis 16:3

**Quote:** וַ⁠תִּקַּ֞ח שָׂרַ֣י אֵֽשֶׁת־אַבְרָ֗ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicitinfo
In the Hebrew text, the phrase “Abram’s wife” is repeated (in [verses 1](../16/01.md) and [3](../16/03.md)) to emphasize Sarai’s relationship to Abram in contrast to the relationship of Hagar. For some languages it is redundant to repeat that phrase, and so they leave it implied here. However, if possible, it is best to keep this repetition in your translation. Alternate translation: [Then Sarai took]

**Quote:** אֶת־הָגָ֤ר הַ⁠מִּצְרִית֙ שִׁפְחָתָ֔⁠הּ
The word **Egyptian** is repeated (in [verses 1](../16/01.md) and [3](../16/03.md)) to emphasize the fact that Hagar was a foreigner. If possible, it is best to keep this repetition in your translation. Alternate translation: [her Egyptian servant woman Hagar] or [her servant Hagar]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תִּתֵּ֥ן אֹתָ֛⁠הּ לְ⁠אַבְרָ֥ם אִישָׁ֖⁠הּ ל֥⁠וֹ לְ⁠אִשָּֽׁה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For most languages it is clearer and more natural to change the order of the clauses in this verse and say, “So Abram’s wife Sarai gave her servant Hagar to her husband Abram to be his second wife. This happened after Abram and Sarai had lived in the land of Canaan for ten years.” Make sure your translation does not sound like [verse 3](../16/03.md) happened ten years after Abram agreed to sleep with Hagar. Alternate translation: [and she gave her servant to her husband Abram to be his second wife]

## Genesis 16:4

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּבֹ֥א אֶל־הָגָ֖ר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-euphemism
See how you translated the idiom “go to,” the past tense of **went to**, in [verse 2](../16/02.md). Also, consider whether it is better in your language to refer to Abram by his name or by a pronoun here at the beginning of this paragraph. Alternate translation: [So Abram slept with Hagar] or [So he had sexual relations with Hagar]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תַּ֑הַר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-words-phrases
If the connection between this statement and the previous one is not clear, you may want to use a different connecting word to show how this statement relates to what comes before it. Alternate translation: [and as a result she became pregnant] or [and so she conceived]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תֵּ֨רֶא֙ כִּ֣י הָרָ֔תָה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The word **saw** is used here as an idiom that means “realized” or “perceived.” Consider whether or not you have a similar idiom in your language that would work well here. Alternate translation: [She knew that she was pregnant]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תֵּקַ֥ל גְּבִרְתָּ֖⁠הּ בְּ⁠עֵינֶֽי⁠הָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
Consider what is the best way to translate this passive clause in your language. Alternate translation: [and she despised her owner Sarai] or [and she treated her owner Sarai with contempt] or [she started to look down on her mistress Sarai]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תֵּקַ֥ל גְּבִרְתָּ֖⁠הּ בְּ⁠עֵינֶֽי⁠הָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The phrase **despised in her eyes** is an idiom that refers to Hagar’s attitude and disrespectful actions toward Sarai. Consider whether or not your language has a similar idiom. Alternate translation: [and her mistress was treated with contempt]

## Genesis 16:5

**Quote:** חֲמָסִ֣⁠י עָלֶי⁠ךָ֒
Alternate translation: [I am being mistreated because of you!] or [It is your fault that I am being treated wrongly]

**Quote:** אָנֹכִ֗י נָתַ֤תִּי שִׁפְחָתִ⁠י֙ בְּ⁠חֵיקֶ֔⁠ךָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-euphemism
The idiom **put my maidservant into your arms** refers to Abram’s sexual relationship with Hagar. Make sure that your translation of this clause is not offensive to your readers. Alternate translation: [I gave my servant to you to be your wife] or [I allowed my servant to have marital relations with you]

**Quote:** וָ⁠אֵקַ֖ל בְּ⁠עֵינֶ֑י⁠הָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
Consider what is the best way to translate this passive clause in your language. Also see how you translated a similar clause in [verse 4](../16/04.md). Alternate translation: [she despises me!] or [she treats me with disdain]

**Quote:** יִשְׁפֹּ֥ט יְהוָ֖ה בֵּינִ֥⁠י וּ⁠בֵינֶֽיׄ⁠ךָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Sarai assumes that Abram will know that as the head of the household, he is responsible to rebuke Hagar and require her to respect Sarai. You could say that explicitly or put that information in a footnote if that would be helpful to your readers. Alternate translation: [I ask Yahweh to decide which one of us is responsible for this] or [May Yahweh decide who is wrong, you or me]

## Genesis 16:6

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֨אמֶר אַבְרָ֜ם אֶל־שָׂרַ֗י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
Alternate translation: [But Abram said to her]

**Quote:** הִנֵּ֤ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Abram is using the term **Behold** to focus Sarai’s attention on what he is about to say. Your language may have a comparable expression that you can use in your translation. Alternate translation: [Look]

**Quote:** שִׁפְחָתֵ⁠ךְ֙ בְּ⁠יָדֵ֔⁠ךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The phrase **in your hand** is an idiom that refers to having control or authority over someone. Consider whether or not your language has a similar idiom. Also see how you translated **maidservant** in [verses 1–3](../16/01.md), [5](../16/05.md). Alternate translation: [your slave woman is under your authority] or [you have authority over your slave]

**Quote:** עֲשִׂי־לָ֖⁠הּ
Alternate translation: [So you can do to her]

**Quote:** הַ⁠טּ֣וֹב בְּ⁠עֵינָ֑יִ⁠ךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The phrase **in your eyes** is an idiom that refers to Sarai’s opinion. See how you translated a similar idiom (“in her eyes”) in [verses 4](../16/04.md) and [5](../16/05.md). Alternate translation: [whatever you decide is good] or [whatever pleases you]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תְּעַנֶּ֣⁠הָ שָׂרַ֔י
See how you translated “afflict” in Genesis [15:13](../15/13.md). Alternate translation: [So Sarai oppressed her greatly] or [So Sarai treated her very badly]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תִּבְרַ֖ח מִ⁠פָּנֶֽי⁠הָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-pronouns
Make sure it is clear in your translation that Hagar is the one who fled, not Sarai. Alternate translation: [and Hagar ran away from Sarai]

## Genesis 16:7

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יִּמְצָאָ֞⁠הּ מַלְאַ֧ךְ יְהוָ֛ה
Make sure that the way you translate **found** does not sound like the angel encountered Hagar accidentally. Rather, he knew where she was and went to her intentionally. Also, this messenger from Yahweh was an angel, not a human being. Alternate translation: [But an messenger sent from Yahweh met Hagar]

**Quote:** עַל־עֵ֥ין הַ⁠מַּ֖יִם
Alternate translation: [beside a spring of water] or [beside a spring]

**Quote:** בַּ⁠מִּדְבָּ֑ר
See how you translated **wilderness** in Genesis [14:6](../14/06.md)

**Quote:** עַל־הָ⁠עַ֖יִן
Alternate translation: [She was at the spring]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠דֶ֥רֶךְ שֽׁוּר
Alternate translation: [that was by the road that goes to the city of Shur] or [that was by the road near the city of Shur]

## Genesis 16:8

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּאמַ֗ר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
The angel asked Hagar a question, so for some languages it is better to use a word like “asked” here. Consider what is the best way to translate this quote margin in your language. Alternate translation: [And the angel inquired of her] or [The angel asked her]

**Quote:** מִ⁠פְּנֵי֙ שָׂרַ֣י גְּבִרְתִּ֔⁠י אָנֹכִ֖י בֹּרַֽחַת
See how you translated “fled from her face” in [verse 6](../16/06.md) and how you translated **mistress** in [verse 4](../16/04.md). Alternate translation: [I am running away from my owner, Sarai]

## Genesis 16:9

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֤אמֶר לָ⁠הּ֙ מַלְאַ֣ךְ יְהוָ֔ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-words-phrases
Consider what is the best way to begin this verse in your translation after what Hagar said in [verse 8](../16/08.md). Alternate translation: [The angel told Hagar]

**Quote:** שׁ֖וּבִי אֶל־גְּבִרְתֵּ֑⁠ךְ
See how you translated “mistress” in [verses 4](../16/04.md) and [8](../16/08.md). Alternate translation: [Go back to your owner]

**Quote:** וְ⁠הִתְעַנִּ֖י תַּ֥חַת יָדֶֽי⁠הָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The angel is using **under her hands** as a common expression of this culture to mean “under her authority.” If this phrase does not have that meaning in your language, you could use a comparable expression from your language that does have that meaning, or you could state the meaning plainly. See how you translated a similar idiom (“in your hand”) in [verse 6](../16/06.md). Alternate translation: [and humbly submit yourself to her authority]

## Genesis 16:10

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֤אמֶר לָ⁠הּ֙ מַלְאַ֣ךְ יְהוָ֔ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
Some languages do not include the quote margins here and in [verse 11](../16/11.md), because the angel is still talking to Hagar. However, the repeated quote margins could be there to emphasize what the angel says next or to show that he paused or that he is changing the topic. Alternate translation: [Then the angel told her] or [Then he continued]

**Quote:** הַרְבָּ֥ה אַרְבֶּ֖ה אֶת־זַרְעֵ֑⁠ךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The pronoun **I** refers to Yahweh here. So either the angel was representing Yahweh and speaking Yahweh’s words to Hagar or it was Yahweh himself who had taken on the form of an angel. Alternate translation: [I, Yahweh, will greatly multiply the number of your offspring so that]

**Quote:** מֵ⁠רֹֽב
Alternate translation: [because of their large number]

## Genesis 16:11

**Quote:** הִנָּ֥⁠ךְ הָרָ֖ה וְ⁠יֹלַ֣דְתְּ בֵּ֑ן
Make sure it is clear in your translation that Hagar already knew that she was pregnant but did not know she would have a son or what she should name him. Alternate translation: [Look, as you know, you are expecting a child. You will bear a son] or [Look, you are pregnant with a son]

**Quote:** וְ⁠קָרָ֤את שְׁמ⁠וֹ֙
Alternate translation: [give him the name]

**Quote:** יִשְׁמָעֵ֔אל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Some translation teams make the meaning of Ishmael’s name explicit in their translation. Other teams put the meaning of his name in a footnote. See what you did for similar cases in
Genesis [3:20](../03/20.md), [4:1, 16, 25](../04/01.md), [6:29](../06/29.md), [10:25](../10/25.md), and [11:9](../11/09.md).

**Quote:** כִּֽי־שָׁמַ֥ע יְהוָ֖ה אֶל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
If you included the meaning of Ishmael’s name earlier in this verse, make sure it fits with how you translate this clause. Alternate translation: [because Yahweh heard] or [because Yahweh paid attention to]

**Quote:** עָנְיֵֽ⁠ךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The word that means **listened to** implies here that God also took action to help Hagar. Alternate translation: [your cries of misery and has helped you]

## Genesis 16:12

**Quote:** וְ⁠ה֤וּא
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-pronouns
The pronoun **he** is emphatic here to emphasize Ishmael. Make sure that your translation refers here to Ishmael, not Yahweh. Alternate translation: [As for Ishmael, when he grows up, he]

**Quote:** יִהְיֶה֙ פֶּ֣רֶא אָדָ֔ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The angel compares Ishmael to a wild donkey. Consider whether or not your language can use a similar image or a comparison here. Alternate translation: [will be like a untamed donkey that no one can control] or [will not be ruled by anyone else]

**Quote:** יָד֣⁠וֹ בַ⁠כֹּ֔ל וְ⁠יַ֥ד כֹּ֖ל בּ֑⁠וֹ
Alternate translation: [He will be enemies with everyone]

**Quote:** וְ⁠עַל־פְּנֵ֥י & יִשְׁכֹּֽן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-parallelism
The rest of this verse forms a parallelism with the first half, emphasizing what Ishmael (and his descendants) will be like. Try to keep both parts of the parallelism in your translation, since each part has a different focus and the second part adds important information. Alternate translation: [Yes he will even fight against]

**Quote:** כָל־אֶחָ֖י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
The word **brothers** is used here to refer to relatives in general. Consider what is the best way to translate this synecdoche in your language. Alternate translation: [all of his own relatives] or [all of his own family members]

## Genesis 16:13

**Quote:** וַ⁠תִּקְרָ֤א שֵׁם־יְהוָה֙ הַ⁠דֹּבֵ֣ר אֵלֶ֔י⁠הָ אַתָּ֖ה אֵ֣ל רֳאִ֑י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Some translations transliterate (copy or borrow) the name **El Roi** from the Hebrew, some translate its meaning, and some do both. Decide what is best in your language.

**Quote:** כִּ֣י אָֽמְרָ֗ה
Alternate translation: [She named him that, because she had said to herself]

**Quote:** הֲ⁠גַ֥ם הֲלֹ֛ם רָאִ֖יתִי אַחֲרֵ֥י רֹאִֽ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Hagar’s rhetorical question shows surprise and amazement. Consider what is the best way to communicate that in your translation. Alternate translation: [How can it be that I was able to look at the back of the God who looks after me?] or [I just now saw the back of him who sees me!]

## Genesis 16:14

**Quote:** עַל־כֵּן֙
See how you translated this phrase in Genesis [11:9](../11/09.md).

**Quote:** קָרָ֣א לַ⁠בְּאֵ֔ר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Frequently in the Hebrew text, this phrase has a general meaning and does not refer to someone specific. Also, this well is the same as the spring in [verse 7](../16/07.md). For some languages, it may be necessary to translate these words the same way so that it is clear that they refer to the same place. Alternate translation: [the well is named] or [people call that well] or [the name of the well is]

**Quote:** הִנֵּ֥ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The author is using the term **Behold** to focus his reader’s attention on what he is about to say. Your language may have a comparable expression that you can use in your translation. Alternate translation: [Look, that well is still there;]

**Quote:** בֵין־קָדֵ֖שׁ וּ⁠בֵ֥ין בָּֽרֶד
Be consistent here with how you spelled **Kadesh** in Genesis [14:7](../14/07.md)
