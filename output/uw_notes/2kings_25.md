## Chapter intro

# 2 Kings 25 Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

  1. King Zedekiah of Judah (24:18–25:7)
  2. Destruction of Jerusalem by the Babylonians (25:8–21)
  3. The remaining Judeans flee to Egypt (25:22–26)
  4. The Babylonian king shows favor to imprisoned King Jehoiachin (25:27–30)

In this chapter, by completing his account of the reign of Zedekiah, the author concludes the story of the kings who ruled the kingdom of Judah. He also concludes the entire story that is told throughout the books of 1 and 2 Samuel and 1 and 2 Kings. That is the story of how the Israelites of both kingdoms disobeyed Yahweh so seriously that Yahweh allowed enemies to conquer them and take them into exile away from their land. As the author describes the conquest and destruction of Judah and the exile of its people, he does not say specifically that this was the result of the people breaking their covenant with Yahweh, as he did in the case of the northern kingdom in 17:7–23. That is because he has already made that point by quoting Yahweh’s decree of judgment in 21:10–15 and saying in 23:26 that Yahweh did not rescind that decree even after the thorough reforms of Josiah. The overall message of this long historical account is nevertheless clear. Yahweh was not at fault when the Israelites were conquered and exiled from their land. Instead, this happened because the people disobeyed Yahweh and broke their covenant with him.
