## Genesis 21:1

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יהוָ֛ה פָּקַ֥ד אֶת־שָׂרָ֖ה כַּ⁠אֲשֶׁ֣ר אָמָ֑ר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The word **visited** is used here as an idiom that means God blessed Sarah and was kind to her (by enabling her to have a son). The same idiom is used in [1 Samuel 21:Samuel](../../1sa/21/Samuel.md) and [Luke 1:68](../../luk/01/68.md). Alternate translation: [Then Yahweh blessed Sarah as he had promised that he would] or [Then Yahweh was gracious to Sarah, just as he had promised he would be]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּ֧עַשׂ יְהוָ֛ה לְ⁠שָׂרָ֖ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-parallelism
The two halves of [verse 1](../21/01.md) form a parallelism to emphasize what God did for Sarah. In your translation it is best, if possible, to keep both parts of the parallelism, since each part has a different emphasis. Alternate translation: [Yes, Yahweh did for her]

**Quote:** כַּ⁠אֲשֶׁ֣ר אָמָ֑ר
Alternate translation: [just as he had said he would do]

## Genesis 21:2

**Quote:** וַ⁠תַּהַר֩ & שָׂרָ֧ה
Translate this clause in a way that is not offensive or awkward, especially when read aloud in public. Alternate translation: [So she became pregnant] or [As a result, Sarah conceived]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תֵּ֨לֶד & בֵּ֖ן
Alternate translation: [and gave birth to a son] or [and had a son]

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁר־דִּבֶּ֥ר אֹת֖⁠וֹ אֱלֹהִֽים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For some languages it may be clearer or more natural to change the order of the clauses in this sentence and say, “So Sarah became pregnant, and at the time God had set, she gave birth to a son for Abraham when he was old, just as God had promised him that she would.” Alternate translation: [when God had told him it would happen]
