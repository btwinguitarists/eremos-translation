## Genesis 7:1

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֤אמֶר יְהוָה֙ לְ⁠נֹ֔חַ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
For some languages it may be necessary to make it explicit that the boat was completed before Yahweh told Noah to enter it. Alternate translation: [Then after they had finished building the ark, Yahweh told Noah] or [After the ark was finished, Yahweh told Noah]

**Quote:** בֹּֽא־אַתָּ֥ה וְ⁠כָל־בֵּיתְ⁠ךָ֖ אֶל־הַ⁠תֵּבָ֑ה
See how you translated “ark” in Genesis [6:14–16](../06/14.md). Alternate translation: [Board the ark along with your entire family] or [You and your entire family must get into the ark]

**Quote:** כִּֽי & רָאִ֛יתִי
Alternate translation: [because I have found] or [because I see]

**Quote:** אֹתְ⁠ךָ֥ & צַדִּ֥יק
In the Hebrew text, the pronoun **you** is emphasized to show that Noah was the only one who was righteous. See how you translated **righteous** in Genesis [6:9](../06/09.md). Alternate translation: [that you alone are upright] or [that you are the only one who is living the right way]

**Quote:** לְ⁠פָנַ֖⁠י
Alternate translation: [in my eyes] or [according to my will]

**Quote:** בַּ⁠דּ֥וֹר הַ⁠זֶּֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For some languages it may be more natural to put this phrase earlier in this verse, after “because.” Alternate translation: [among the people living today]

## Genesis 7:4

**Quote:** כִּי֩
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-result
The word translated as **because** indicates that what follows is a reason for what came before. Use a connector in your language that makes it clear that what follows is a reason for what came before. Alternate translation: [The reason you must do so is that]

**Quote:** לְ⁠יָמִ֨ים ע֜וֹד שִׁבְעָ֗ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-numbers
The phrase **seven more days** could be translated as “another week” here and in [verse 11](../07/11.md). However, it is probably best to keep the number seven in the text in both places, especially since the number seven often signifies perfection or completion in the Bible, perhaps also here. Alternate translation: [seven days from now]

**Quote:** אָֽנֹכִי֙ מַמְטִ֣יר
Alternate translation: [I will send rain]

**Quote:** עַל־הָ⁠אָ֔רֶץ
Alternate translation: [all over the world]

**Quote:** אַרְבָּעִ֣ים י֔וֹם וְ⁠אַרְבָּעִ֖ים לָ֑יְלָה
This phrase refers to 40 complete 24-hour days.

**Quote:** וּ⁠מָחִ֗יתִי
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
See how you translated this common expression in [6:7](../06/07.md). Alternate translation: [in order to blot out] or [That will wash away]

**Quote:** אֶֽת־כָּל־הַ⁠יְקוּם֙ אֲשֶׁ֣ר עָשִׂ֔יתִי
See how you translated a similar phrase (“living creature”) in Genesis [6:19](../06/19.md). Alternate translation: [all the living things that I have made]

**Quote:** מֵ⁠עַ֖ל פְּנֵ֥י הָֽ⁠אֲדָמָֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
It may be clearer to put this phrase earlier in the sentence and say, “in order to completely destroy from the ground every living creature that I have made.” See how you translated this phrase in Genesis [6:7](../06/07.md). Alternate translation: [off the surface of the earth] or [off the land]

## Genesis 7:8

**Quote:** וְ⁠כֹ֥ל אֲשֶׁר־רֹמֵ֖שׂ עַל־הָֽ⁠אֲדָמָֽה
Here, **and everything that moves on the ground** could (1) include every kind of creature that moves on land. Alternate translation: [which includes every kind of animal that lives on land] According to this interpretation, the rest of [verse 8](../07/08.md) emphasizes that the clean and unclean animals and birds ([verses 2–3](../07/02.md), [8](../07/08.md)) were all creatures that lived on land (which are the creatures that the flood affected). (2) refer to every kind of creature that crawls along the ground. According to that interpretation, the rest of [verse 8](../07/08.md) refers to a fourth category of creatures besides the clean animals, unclean animals, and birds.

**Quote:** מִן־הַ⁠בְּהֵמָה֙ הַ⁠טְּהוֹרָ֔ה וּ⁠מִן־הַ֨⁠בְּהֵמָ֔ה אֲשֶׁ֥ר אֵינֶ֖נָּ⁠ה טְהֹרָ֑ה
See how you translated **clean** and “unclean” animals in verse [7:2](../07/02.md). Alternate translation: [Also, every kind of clean animal and every kind of unclean animal]

## Genesis 7:10

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יְהִ֖י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-newevent
This phrase focuses attention on an important event, the beginning of the flood. Use a word, phrase, or other method in your language that is natural for introducing a new event. Alternate translation: [Now it came about]

**Quote:** לְ⁠שִׁבְעַ֣ת הַ⁠יָּמִ֑ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
This phrase refers to the seven days which God talked about in [verse 4](../07/04.md). It does not mean seven days after Noah and the animals went into the ark ([verses 7–9](../07/07.md)); they went into the ark on the same day that the flood started ([verse 13](../07/13.md)). Translate the beginning of this sentence in a way that makes that clear. Alternate translation: [after seven days]

**Quote:** וּ⁠מֵ֣י הַ⁠מַּבּ֔וּל הָי֖וּ עַל
Alternate translation: [the floodwaters started to come on] or [water started to cover]

**Quote:** הָ⁠אָֽרֶץ
Alternate translation: [the whole earth]

**Quote:** הֵ֜מָּה וְ⁠כָל
Alternate translation: [They took with them every]

**Quote:** וְ⁠כָל־הַ⁠בְּהֵמָה֙ לְ⁠מִינָ֔⁠הּ
Alternate translation: [every kind of tame animal]

**Quote:** וְ⁠כָל־הָ⁠רֶ֛מֶשׂ הָ⁠רֹמֵ֥שׂ עַל־הָ⁠אָ֖רֶץ לְ⁠מִינֵ֑⁠הוּ

**Quote:** וְ⁠כָל־הָ⁠ע֣וֹף לְ⁠מִינֵ֔⁠הוּ כֹּ֖ל צִפּ֥וֹר כָּל־כָּנָֽף
This phrase probably refers not only to birds, but also to other creatures that have wings and fly, such as bats and insects. Alternate translation: [and every kind of bird and everything else that has wings] or [and every kind of bird and every other kind of creature that flies]
