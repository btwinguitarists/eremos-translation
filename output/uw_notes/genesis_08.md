## Genesis 8:1

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּזְכֹּ֤ר אֱלֹהִים֙
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The phrase **God remembered** is a common expression in the Bible that often means that God was paying special attention to certain people and was about to do something. Make sure that the way you translate this phrase does not imply that God had forgotten about Noah. Alternate translation: [God kept in mind] or [But God did not forget]

**Quote:** אֶת־נֹ֔חַ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Noah is the person on whom the author is focusing here, but Noah’s family and all the animals are also included. Consider whether or not you need to make that explicit in your translation.

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֵ֤ת כָּל־הַֽ⁠חַיָּה֙ וְ⁠אֶת
The phrase **all the living things** could mean: (1) all the creatures (2) all the wild animals. See how you translated “living thing” in Genesis [7:14](../07/14.md).

**Quote:** כָּל־הַ⁠בְּהֵמָ֔ה אֲשֶׁ֥ר אִתּ֖⁠וֹ בַּ⁠תֵּבָ֑ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
Some translations combine this phrase with the previous phrase and say, “and all the animals, both wild and tame, that were with him in the boat.” Alternate translation: [all the farm animals that were with him in the ark]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּעֲבֵ֨ר אֱלֹהִ֥ים ר֨וּחַ֙ עַל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-pronouns
Alternate translation: [and he sent a wind over] or [So God sent a wind to blow over]

**Quote:** הָ⁠אָ֔רֶץ
Alternate translation: [the water that was over the earth]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּשֹׁ֖כּוּ הַ⁠מָּֽיִם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Notice that the floodwaters did not totally recede until [verse 14](../08/14.md), so the way you translate this clause should allow for that. Alternate translation: [so that the water started to recede] or [so that the water level started to go down]

## Genesis 8:9

**Quote:** וְ⁠לֹֽא־מָצְאָה֩ הַ⁠יּוֹנָ֨ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-pronouns
Consider what is the best way to refer to the dove at this point in the paragraph in your language. Alternate translation: [But the dove could not find] or [But it was not able to find]

**Quote:** מָנ֜וֹחַ לְ⁠כַף־רַגְלָ֗⁠הּ
In Hebrew the word **dove** is grammatically a feminine noun (for both male and female doves), so some translations say “her feet … she returned …” here. For languages such as English that do not assign grammatical gender to nouns, it is more accurate to use **its** and **it** here, because it is not known whether this dove was male or female. Alternate translation: [anywhere to perch and rest]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תָּ֤שָׁב
Alternate translation: [So it returned] or [So the dove flew back]

**Quote:** אֵלָי⁠ו֙ אֶל־הַ⁠תֵּבָ֔ה
Alternate translation: [flew back to him at the ark]

**Quote:** וְ⁠לֹֽא־מָצְאָה֩ הַ⁠יּוֹנָ֨ה מָנ֜וֹחַ לְ⁠כַף־רַגְלָ֗⁠הּ וַ⁠תָּ֤שָׁב אֵלָי⁠ו֙ אֶל־הַ⁠תֵּבָ֔ה כִּי־מַ֖יִם עַל־פְּנֵ֣י כָל־הָ⁠אָ֑רֶץ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-result
If it would be more natural in your language, you could reverse the order of these phrases, since the second phrase gives the reason for the result that the first phrase describes. Alternate translation: [But since the waters were over the face of all of the earth, the dove did not find a resting place for the sole of its foot, so it returned to him in the ark]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשְׁלַ֤ח יָד⁠וֹ֙
Alternate translation: [So he reached out] or [and he reached out]

**Quote:** אֵלָ֖י⁠ו אֶל־הַ⁠תֵּבָֽה
Alternate translation: [back to himself in the ark] or [back into the ark]

**Quote:** וּ⁠בְ⁠כָל־הָ⁠רֶ֛מֶשׂ הָ⁠רֹמֵ֥שׂ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-poetry
The words **every creeper creeping** could mean: (1) “every other creature that moves,” which seems to fit this context best since it is general and includes all the land animals that were not birds or livestock. (2) “and every animal that crawls,” which is more specific and refers to animals like lizards, snakes, mice, and other animals that travel close to the ground.

**Quote:** וּ⁠בְ⁠כָל־הָ⁠רֶ֛מֶשׂ הָ⁠רֹמֵ֥שׂ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-poetry
For emphasis, the speaker is using a construction in which a verb and its object, **creeper creeping**, come from the same root. You may be able to use the same construction in your language to express the meaning here. Alternatively, your language may have another way of showing the emphasis. [and every creature that moves about]
