## Genesis 27:1

**Quote:** וַ⁠יְהִי֙ כִּֽי
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-newevent
The events in [chapter 27](../27/01.md) happened about 23 years after the end of [chapter 26](../26/01.md). Make sure your translation begins this verse in a way that fits with that fact. Alternate translation: [Now when]

**Quote:** זָקֵ֣ן יִצְחָ֔ק
Alternate translation: [Isaac was very old]

**Quote:** וַ⁠תִּכְהֶ֥יןָ עֵינָ֖י⁠ו מֵ⁠רְאֹ֑ת
Isaac was either totally blind or so blind that he could not recognize people anymore (See: [verses 18–27](../27/18.md), [32–33](../27/32.md)). Alternate translation: [and his eyes were so weak that he could not see] or [and he was almost blind]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקְרָ֞א
Isaac probably told a servant to go and tell Esau to come to him. Alternate translation: [he called for] or [he summoned]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֥אמֶר אֵלָ֖י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
Consider what is the best way in your language to translate this quote margin. Alternate translation: [Esau replied]

**Quote:** הִנֵּֽנִ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-politeness
See how you translated the phrase **Behold me** in Genesis [22:1](../22/01.md), [7](../22/07.md), [11](../22/11.md). Alternate translation: [Here I am] or [Yes, sir what is it?]

**Quote:** עַד־שׁ֨וּב אַף־אָחִ֜י⁠ךָ מִמְּ⁠ךָ֗
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
Notice that the Hebrew word for **anger** here is different from the stronger word (“fury”) in [verse 44](../27/44.md). Alternate translation: [until his anger against you is gone] or [After a while, when your brother is no longer angry with you]

**Quote:** וְ⁠שָׁכַח֙ אֵ֣ת אֲשֶׁר־עָשִׂ֣יתָ לּ֔⁠וֹ
Rebekah is not saying that Esau would totally forget what Jacob did, but that at some point he would not hold it against him anymore. Alternate translation: [and he no longer thinks about what you did]

**Quote:** וְ⁠שָׁלַחְתִּ֖י וּ⁠לְקַחְתִּ֣י⁠ךָ מִ⁠שָּׁ֑ם
Alternate translation: [I will send someone to tell you when it is safe to come back home]

**Quote:** לָ⁠מָ֥ה אֶשְׁכַּ֛ל גַּם־שְׁנֵי⁠כֶ֖ם י֥וֹם אֶחָֽד
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Rebekah knew that if Esau were to kill Jacob, he himself would have been killed for that crime. Consider whether it is best in your language to use a rhetorical question or an exclamation to emphasize Rebekah’s concern. Alternate translation: [I do not want both my sons to be killed!]

## Genesis 27:46

**Quote:** וַ⁠תֹּ֤אמֶר רִבְקָה֙ אֶל־יִצְחָ֔ק
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
Alternate translation: [Soon after that, Rebekah said to Isaac]

**Quote:** קַ֣צְתִּי בְ⁠חַיַּ֔⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-hyperbole
Rebekah is exaggerating how she feels to influence Isaac to do what she wants. Alternate translation: [I do not enjoy living] or [I am very upset]

**Quote:** מִ⁠פְּנֵ֖י בְּנ֣וֹת חֵ֑ת
To show that Rebekah is speaking with strong emotion, you could use an exclamation point at the end of this sentence and the following one. Alternate translation: [because of the Hittite women whom Esau married!]

**Quote:** אִם־לֹקֵ֣חַ יַ֠עֲקֹב אִשָּׁ֨ה מִ⁠בְּנֽוֹת־חֵ֤ת כָּ⁠אֵ֨לֶּה֙ מִ⁠בְּנ֣וֹת הָ⁠אָ֔רֶץ
Alternate translation: [If Jacob also marries a Hittite woman from around here]

**Quote:** לָ֥⁠מָּה לִּ֖⁠י חַיִּֽים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Rebekah uses a rhetorical question and an extreme statement to emphasize how upset she would be if Jacob married a Hittite woman. Alternate translation: [my life will not be worth living!]
