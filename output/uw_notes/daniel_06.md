# Daniel 6 General Notes

## Structure and Formatting

Some translations set the content of letters farther to the right on the page than the rest of the text. The ULT does this with the letter in [6:25–27](../06/25.md).

Some translations set each line of poetry farther to the right than the rest of the text to make it easier to read. The ULT does this with the poetry in the letter in [6:26–27](../06/26.md).

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### Daniel and the lions

Daniel was thrown into the lions’ den for praying to Yahweh, but Yahweh protected him and the lions did not hurt him at all.

## Daniel 6:25

**Quote:** עַֽמְמַיָּ֞⁠א אֻמַיָּ֧⁠א וְ⁠לִשָּׁנַיָּ֛⁠א
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
Here **nations** and **languages** represent people from different **nations** who speak different languages. See how you translated this in [Daniel 3:4](../03/04.md). Alternate translation: [the people from different nations and who spoke different languages]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠כָל־אַרְעָ֖⁠א
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-hyperbole
King Darius wrote his message to his entire kingdom which was huge. Here it says **all the earth** as a generalization to emphasis how large his kingdom was, though it did not include everyone on the earth. Alternate translation: [in his entire kingdom]

**Quote:** שְׁלָמְ⁠כ֥וֹן יִשְׂגֵּֽא
This is a form of greeting that is used to wish someone well in all areas of life.
