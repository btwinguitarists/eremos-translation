## Genesis 24:9

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּ֤שֶׂם הָ⁠עֶ֨בֶד֙ אֶת־יָד֔⁠וֹ תַּ֛חַת יֶ֥רֶךְ אַבְרָהָ֖ם אֲדֹנָ֑י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-symaction
The servant putting **his hand under the thigh of Abraham his master** was a symbolic action by which the servant showed that he swore to do everything possible to carry out the wishes of his master. This action was reserved for only the most serious oaths and occurred only twice in Scripture, both times in Genesis. If it would be helpful to your readers, you could explain the significance of this action in the text of your translation or in a footnote. Alternate translation: [Abraham’s servant indicated his total allegiance as he placed his hand under his master’s thigh]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשָּׁ֣בַֽע ל֔⁠וֹ
See how you translated **swore** in [verse 7](../24/07.md) and “swear” in [verse 3](../24/03.md). Alternate translation: [and made an vow to him] or [and made an unbreakable promise to him]

**Quote:** עַל־הַ⁠דָּבָ֖ר הַ⁠זֶּֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Some languages need to make explicit what **this matter** refers to. Alternate translation: [that he would do what he had told him to do] or [that he would go find a wife for Isaac]

## Genesis 24:14
