## Chapter intro

# Deuteronomy 12 Chapter Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

This chapter continues Moses’ second speech, which began in chapter five.

1. The statutes and the ordinances that Yahweh commands the Israelites to keep in the land that Yahweh had promised them (1–31)
2. Yahweh commands the Israelites not to add or take away from the commands he has given them (32)

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### Covenant with Moses

This chapter is a continuation of the covenant Yahweh made with Moses. The reasons for these food restrictions are not always known. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/covenant]] and [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit]])

### Israel and Yahweh

In the ancient Near East, when nations conquered other nations, they often added the gods of the conquered nation into the collection of gods they worshiped. It was rare to worship only one God. Israel was to be known for worshiping Yahweh and Yahweh alone. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/falsegod]])

## Translation Issues in This Chapter

### Words for the law

  - statutes
  - ordinances
  - judgments

Moses uses several words to refer to the law that Yahweh commanded the Israelites to obey. It is unclear what the differences are between these terms. If your language has different terms for the law, you could use them here to differentiate between the different Hebrew words. (See: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-unknown]])

### “you” and “your” singular and plural

The pronouns “you” and “your” are plural in this chapter unless otherwise noted. Although Moses is speaking to all the Israelites, from verses 13–32 he addresses them as “you” singular. Moses wants each individual to obey the law for themselves. If the singular forms of these pronouns would not be natural in your language, you could use the plural forms in your translation. (See [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-youcrowd]])

### “the place that Yahweh your God will choose”

Moses uses phrases such as this to refer to the future place where the temple will be built. These phrases emphasize that Yahweh will be the one who decides where the Israelites will build his temple as well as how they will worship him. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/temple]])

### “the place Yahweh your God will choose … to put his name,” “the place that Yahweh your God will choose to make his name stay,” “the place that Yahweh your God chooses to put his name”

In [12:5](../12/05.md), [12:11](../12/11.md) and [12:21](../12/21.md) the author uses God’s name to represent his reputation and presence. These phrases indicate that God would choose a place and make his presence and reputation stay there. That place would be the future temple. (See: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy]])
