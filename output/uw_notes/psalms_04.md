## Chapter intro

# Psalm 4 Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

  1. Distress (1)
  2. Rebuke (2–6)
  3. Blessing (7–8)

## About the Psalm

  1. Purpose: to intercede for the people and call them to repentance
  2. Content: People, trust in Yahweh alone! He has relieved our suffering in the past, and he will help us again when I call to him. Yahweh, shine on us!
  3. Message: Yahweh alone gives peace and security in times of distress.

## Translation Issues in This Psalm

  1. Features between verses 1, 6, and 8. See the note in verse 6.
  2. Hebrew roots from verse 5 repeat in verses 7–9. See the note in verse 5.
  3. There seems to be a morning and night pattern. The word translated **call** in verses 1 and 3 sound like the Hebrew word for morning, and then bed is mentioned in verse 4. Yahweh lifting up the light of his face is like the dawn in verse 6 and in verse 8 the author lies down to sleep.
  4. The last verse of the psalm seems to wind down peacefully, even repeating “s” sounds. See the note in verse 8.

## Psalms 4:1

**Quote:** אֱלֹ֘הֵ֤י צִדְקִ֗⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-possession
Here **God of my righteousness** could mean: (1) my righteous God or (2) God who vindicates me. Both options are grammatically possible, and both fit the context well. The following relative clause (verse 1b), which highlights God’s past faithfulness, may suggest that it is God’s righteousness that is in view (hence the UST rendering). You could include this information if that would be helpful to your readers.

**Quote:** אֱלֹ֘הֵ֤י צִדְקִ֗⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
If your language does not use an abstract noun for the idea of **righteousness**, you could express the same idea in another way. Alternate translation: [my God, the righteous one]

**Quote:** בַּ֭⁠צָּר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
If your language does not use an abstract noun for the idea of **distress**, you could express the same idea in another way. The original word is related to narrowness. Alternate translation: [in trouble] or [when I am oppressed]

## Psalms 4:2

**Quote:** בְּנֵ֥י אִ֡ישׁ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
**Son of man** could refer to: (1) the mortality and finitude of his enemies (as opposed to God’s eternal infinitude), (2) important or wealthy men, or (3) the fact that the author is now addressing people rather than God. You could include this information if that would be helpful to your readers. Alternate translation: [You men]

**Quote:** עַד־מֶ֬ה כְבוֹדִ֣⁠י לִ֭⁠כְלִמָּה תֶּאֱהָב֣וּ⁠ן רִ֑יק תְּבַקְשׁ֖וּ כָזָ֣ב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
The author is using the question form to rebuke his enemies. If you would not use the question form for this purpose in your language, you could translate these three questions as statements or exclamations. Alternate translation: [stop calling glory shame! Stop loving futility! Stop seeking falsehood!]

**Quote:** כְבוֹדִ֣⁠י לִ֭⁠כְלִמָּה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
If your language does not use abstract nouns for the ideas of **glory** and **shame**, you could express the same ideas in another way. Alternate translation: [you consider my glorious deeds as shameful deeds]

**Quote:** תֶּאֱהָב֣וּ⁠ן רִ֑יק תְּבַקְשׁ֖וּ כָזָ֣ב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
If your language does not use abstract nouns for the ideas of **futility** and **falsehood**, you could express the same ideas in another way. Alternate translation: [How long will you love vain things? How long will you pursue untrue things?]

## Psalms 4:4

**Quote:** רִגְז֗וּ וְֽ⁠אַל־תֶּ֫חֱטָ֥אוּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The implication could be: (1) that the hearers should **tremble** in fear of Yahweh’s punishment for the unfaithful or (2) that if the hearers are angry, they should control themselves and not sin. You could include this information if that would be helpful to your readers. Alternate translation: [Tremble in fear and do not sin] or [Even if you tremble in anger, do not sin]

**Quote:** אִמְר֣וּ בִ֭⁠לְבַבְ⁠כֶם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here the **heart** represents where a person’s thoughts and motives exist. You may have a comparable expression in your language that you can use in your translation, or you could state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [Consider your thoughts and motives]

**Quote:** אִמְר֣וּ בִ֭⁠לְבַבְ⁠כֶם עַֽל־מִשְׁכַּבְ⁠כֶ֗ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-poetry
The Hebrew root words for speak, **heart**, and **bed** in this verse appear in verses 6 (as “say”), 7, and 8 (as “lie down”) respectively (in the same order). Consider if a similar pattern would create a natural poetic effect in your language.

**Quote:** אִמְר֣וּ בִ֭⁠לְבַבְ⁠כֶם
Alternate translation: [Think to yourselves]

**Quote:** וְ⁠דֹ֣מּוּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Here, **be silent** means that the listeners should show self-control and not act on sinful impulses. You could include this information in your translation if it would be helpful to your readers. Alternate translation: [and do not act on evil thoughts]

## Psalms 4:6

**Quote:** מִֽי־יַרְאֵ֪⁠נ֫וּ ט֥וֹב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
The people asking this are using the question form to make a complaint and an implied request. If you would not use the question form for this purpose in your language, you could translate this as an exclamation or plain request. Alternate translation: [No one is showing us good!] or [Please, someone show us good!]

**Quote:** מִֽי־יַרְאֵ֪⁠נ֫וּ ט֥וֹב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here, **good** is spoken of as if it were something that someone could **show** to someone else. If it would be clearer in your language, you could state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [Who will do good things for us]

**Quote:** נְֽסָ⁠ה־עָ֭לֵי⁠נוּ א֨וֹר פָּנֶ֬י⁠ךָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The writer speaks of Yahweh acting favorably towards them as if Yahweh’s face shone a light on them. If it would be clearer in your language, you could state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [Act favorably towards us]

**Quote:** נְֽסָ⁠ה־עָ֭לֵי⁠נוּ א֨וֹר פָּנֶ֬י⁠ךָ יְהוָֽה
This is a reference to the high priest’s blessing in [Numbers 6:25](../../num/06/24.md).
