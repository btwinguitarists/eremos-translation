## Genesis 9:1

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֧אמֶר לָ⁠הֶ֛ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
The phrase “blessed … and said” is a Hebrew expression that refers to one event and emphasizes it. It does not refer to two separate events. The words of the blessing are in [verses 1–7](../09/01.md). See how you translated this phrase in Genesis [1:28](../01/28.md). Alternate translation: [He said]

**Quote:** פְּר֥וּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
Alternate translation: [Bear many children] or [You will bear many children]

**Quote:** וּ⁠רְב֖וּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-goal
See how you translated “be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth” in Genesis [1:28](../01/28.md). Alternate translation: [so that you and your descendants will increase greatly in number] or [so that you and your descendants will become very many]

**Quote:** וּ⁠מִלְא֥וּ אֶת־הָ⁠אָֽרֶץ
Alternate translation: [and fill the earth with people] or [and inhabit the whole earth] or [and live everywhere on the world]

## Genesis 9:2

**Quote:** וּ⁠מוֹרַאֲ⁠כֶ֤ם וְ⁠חִתְּ⁠כֶם֙ יִֽהְיֶ֔ה עַ֚ל כָּל־חַיַּ֣ת הָ⁠אָ֔רֶץ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-doublet
The phrase **the fear of you and the dread of you** repeats the same idea in two different ways to emphasize how afraid the creatures will be. For some languages it may be better to combine the phrase and say “very afraid.” Alternate translation: [Every living thing on the earth will be afraid and terrified of you] or [All the animals on earth will be very afraid of you]

**Quote:** וְ⁠עַ֖ל כָּל־ע֣וֹף
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicitinfo
It might seem that the phrase **bird of the sky** contains extra information that would not be natural to express in your language. If so, you can shorten it. Consider again how you translated “birds of the sky” earlier in the book of Genesis; see [1:26, 28,30](../01/26.md); [2:19–20](../02/19.md); [6:7](../06/07.md); [7:3](../07/03.md), and [7:23](../07/23.md). Alternate translation: [including every flying bird] or [including all the birds]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠כֹל֩ אֲשֶׁ֨ר תִּרְמֹ֧שׂ הָֽ⁠אֲדָמָ֛ה
Alternate translation: [along with every animal that lives on land] or [as well as all animals that live on land]

**Quote:** וּֽ⁠בְ⁠כָל־דְּגֵ֥י הַ⁠יָּ֖ם
Alternate translation: [and all the fish in the sea]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠יֶדְ⁠כֶ֥ם נִתָּֽנוּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The common expression **Into your hand** means God was again giving mankind the responsibility to manage and control all the rest of what God had created. Consider whether your language has a similar expression that would work well here. Alternate translation: [Into your control they are placed]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠יֶדְ⁠כֶ֥ם נִתָּֽנוּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
If your language does not use this passive form, you could express the idea in active form or in another way that is natural in your language. If you need to say who does the action, the context suggests that it is God. Alternate translation: [I have put you in charge of them all]

## Genesis 9:3

**Quote:** כָּל־רֶ֨מֶשׂ֙ אֲשֶׁ֣ר הוּא־חַ֔י
See how you translated “every moving creature” in Genesis [1:26](../01/26.md), and see the note about that there. It may be necessary to translate this phrase in different ways in different contexts. Alternate translation: [From now on every kind of living thing that moves] or [From now on every animal that is alive and moves]

**Quote:** לָ⁠כֶ֥ם יִהְיֶ֖ה לְ⁠אָכְלָ֑ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For some languages it is more natural to change the order of this sentence and say, “You may now have/eat any kind of living creature that moves, for food.” or “I now permit you to eat every kind of living creature that moves.” Alternate translation: [is available for you to eat as food]

**Quote:** כְּ⁠יֶ֣רֶק עֵ֔שֶׂב
Consider again how you translated **green plants** in Genesis [1:30](../01/30.md) and how you translated **plants** in Genesis [1:11–12](../01/11.md), [1:19](../01/19.md), [2:5](../02/05.md), [3:18](../03/18.md). and [9:3](../09/03.md). Alternate translation: [plants to eat]

**Quote:** נָתַ֥תִּי לָ⁠כֶ֖ם אֶת־כֹּֽל
Make sure that the way you translate this phrase means that God is now permitting people to eat both plants and animals. Alternate translation: [now I give you everything for food] or [so now I permit you to eat everything] or [so now I permit you to eat both plants and animals]

## Genesis 9:4

**Quote:** אַךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-exceptions
Make sure it is clear in your translation that God is not contradicting himself here, but rather he is giving an exception to what he said in [verse 3](../09/03.md). God is putting a limit on the way that people may eat meat. Alternate translation: [You may eat meat, but not when it still has the blood of life in it]

**Quote:** בָּשָׂ֕ר & לֹ֥א תֹאכֵֽלוּ
Alternate translation: [you must never eat animal flesh]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠נַפְשׁ֥⁠וֹ דָמ֖⁠וֹ
Since life is in the blood, God did not want people to eat meat that still had its blood in it. Rather he wanted them to drain it out first before they cooked it (also see Deuteronomy [12:16, 23](../12/16.md)). You could put that information in a footnote. Alternate translation: [that still has its life in it, which is in its blood] or [with its blood in it, which made it live] or [that still has its lifeblood in it]

## Genesis 9:6

**Quote:** שֹׁפֵךְ֙
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-parallelism
The first half of [verse 6](../09/06.md) repeats and summarizes [verse 5](../09/05.md). These two phrases mean basically the same thing. The second emphasizes the meaning of the first by repeating the same idea with different words. Hebrew poetry was based on this kind of repetition, and it would be good to show this to your readers by including both phrases in your translation rather than combining them. Alternate translation: [Yes, if anyone]

**Quote:** שֹׁפֵךְ֙ דַּ֣ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The phrase **shedding the blood of** is a common expression that means deliberately killing someone. Blood is an important concept in this passage and throughout the Bible, so if possible, it is best to keep this term in your translation. If that is not possible, you should put the literal text in a footnote. Alternate translation: [Yes, if anyone kills] or [Whoever takes the life of]

**Quote:** הָֽ⁠אָדָ֔ם
Alternate translation: [a person] or [another human being] or [someone]

**Quote:** בָּֽ⁠אָדָ֖ם דָּמ֣⁠וֹ יִשָּׁפֵ֑ךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For some languages it is more natural to put this clause first in this verse and say, “That’s right, you must put to death anyone who murders a human being, because I, God, made human beings in my own image.” Alternate translation: [Yes, by people the blood of this man must be shed]

**Quote:** בָּֽ⁠אָדָ֖ם דָּמ֣⁠וֹ יִשָּׁפֵ֑ךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
If your language does not use this passive form, you could express the idea in active form or in another way that is natural in your language. If you need to say who does the action, the context suggests that it is God. Alternate translation: [people must execute him] or [people must put him to death] or [mankind must execute him]

**Quote:** כִּ֚י בְּ⁠צֶ֣לֶם אֱלֹהִ֔ים עָשָׂ֖ה אֶת־הָ⁠אָדָֽם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-123person
Make sure it is clear in your translation that God is referring to himself in this clause. See how you translated a similar clause in Genesis [1:27](../01/27.md). Alternate translation: [because I, God made people in my own image] or [because I created human beings to be like myself]

## Genesis 9:28
