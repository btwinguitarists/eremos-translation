# Nehemiah 5 Chapter Introduction

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### Equality

The rich made money from the poor. The rich oppressed the poor by charging interest on loans. Because Nehemiah wanted to treat everyone fairly, he did not collect any taxes from them. This chapter also emphasizes that it was wrong to enslave a fellow Jew.

### Governor

Nehemiah was a governmental leader in Jerusalem, but he was not a king. Jerusalem had a great deal of independence, but it was under the authority of the Persian king. The term “governor” reflects this idea, but a different term may be used in translation.

## Nehemiah 5:2

**Quote:** וְ⁠יֵשׁ֙ אֲשֶׁ֣ר אֹמְרִ֔ים
Verses 2, 3, 4, and 5 do not seem to be different complaints that people brought to Nehemiah separately. Rather, the whole group seems to be complaining that because they need to get food for their families, wealthier and more powerful Jews are exploiting them. They are forcing them to take mortgages and loans and even sell their children into slavery. Nehemiah is probably depicting one speaker after another in the crowd adding details to this picture. You could suggest this to the readers of your translation by introducing these comments with a translation such as, “some of them began to say”

**Quote:** בָּנֵ֥י⁠נוּ וּ⁠בְנֹתֵ֖י⁠נוּ אֲנַ֣חְנוּ רַבִּ֑ים

**Quote:** וְ⁠נִקְחָ֥ה דָגָ֖ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
This does not mean only grain. The people complaining to Nehemiah are using one kind of food, grain, the staple of their diet, to refer to all the foods they would need to eat. Alternate translation: [we need food]

**Quote:** וְ⁠נִקְחָ֥ה דָגָ֖ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The implication is that because the families are large, a lot of food is needed. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this explicitly. Alternate translation: [we need to get a lot of food]

**Quote:** וְ⁠נֹאכְלָ֥ה וְ⁠נִחְיֶֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-doublet
These two short phrases mean similar things. The people use them together to emphasize the urgency and importance of having food. If it would be helpful in your language, you could combine these phrases. Alternate translation: [to get enough food to stay alive]

**Quote:** וְ⁠הִנֵּ֣ה
**Behold** is a term meant to focus the attention of the listener on what the speaker is about to say. If it would be helpful to your readers, you could use some emphatic term or expression in your language that would have this same effect.

**Quote:** אֲנַ֣חְנוּ כֹ֠בְשִׁים אֶת־בָּנֵ֨י⁠נוּ וְ⁠אֶת־בְּנֹתֵ֜י⁠נוּ לַ⁠עֲבָדִ֗ים וְ⁠יֵ֨שׁ מִ⁠בְּנֹתֵ֤י⁠נוּ נִכְבָּשׁוֹת֙

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֵ֣ין לְ⁠אֵ֣ל יָדֵ֔⁠נוּ וּ⁠שְׂדֹתֵ֥י⁠נוּ וּ⁠כְרָמֵ֖י⁠נוּ לַ⁠אֲחֵרִֽים

Alternate translation: [because our creditors took the fields and vineyards we pledged as security for loans, there is nothing else we can do in this situation.]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֵ֣ין לְ⁠אֵ֣ל יָדֵ֔⁠נוּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
**There is nothing to God in our hand** is an idiom that means that the speaker does not have the power to do what they are describing. Alternate translation: [there is nothing else we can do in this situation]

**Quote:** וּ⁠שְׂדֹתֵ֥י⁠נוּ וּ⁠כְרָמֵ֖י⁠נוּ לַ⁠אֲחֵרִֽים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The implication is that when the poor could not pay back their loans, their creditors took the fields and vineyards they had pledged as collateral. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this explicitly. Alternate translation: [because our creditors took the fields and vineyards we pledged as security for loans.]

## Nehemiah 5:7

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּמָּלֵ֨ךְ לִבִּ֜⁠י עָלַ֗⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-personification
Here Nehemiah is speaking about his heart as if it were a living thing that could act like a king and rule over him. However, he is not saying that his feelings commanded his actions. (He says in the previous verse that he became very angry, but in the next verse he describes acting in a careful and deliberate manner.) Instead, this means that his heart “took counsel” with him, the way a king would take counsel with advisors. In effect, Nehemiah is saying that he talked the matter over with himself. Alternate translation: [I thought hard about what to do]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּמָּלֵ֨ךְ לִבִּ֜⁠י עָלַ֗⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here, the **heart** represents the thoughts and the will. Alternate translation: [I thought hard about what to do]

**Quote:** וָ⁠אָרִ֨יבָ⁠ה֙
**Contended** is a technical term that means “to bring charges.” It means to initiate a lawsuit that would require the defendants to answer for themselves publicly, in the presence of their fellow citizens. Alternate translation: [Then I brought charges]

**Quote:** הַ⁠חֹרִ֣ים & הַ⁠סְּגָנִ֔ים
See how you translated these terms in [2:16](../02/16.md). Alternate translation: [the leading citizens and the city officials]

**Quote:** מַשָּׁ֥א & אַתֶּ֣ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom

The Law of Moses specifically forbade charging interest on a loan to a fellow Jew. So this was not just an exploitive business practice, it was a violation of God’s Law. In this context, “a man” means “each person.” It does not mean only an adult male.

**Quote:** אִישׁ־בְּ⁠אָחִ֖י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here, **brother** could conceivably include even biological brothers, but in context it likely refers to fellow Jews. Alternate translation: [to your fellow Jews]

**Quote:** וָ⁠אֶתֵּ֥ן עֲלֵי⁠הֶ֖ם קְהִלָּ֥ה גְדוֹלָֽה

**Quote:** וָ⁠אֶתֵּ֥ן עֲלֵי⁠הֶ֖ם קְהִלָּ֥ה גְדוֹלָֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-events
To present the events in chronological order, if that would be helpful to your readers, you could put this before the previous sentence, which describes the first charge that Nehemiah brought at this trial.

**Quote:** זָכְרָ⁠ה־לִּ֥⁠י & לְ⁠טוֹבָ֑ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
To remember someone **for good** is an idiom that means to reward someone with good things for the good that they have done.

**Quote:** זָכְרָ⁠ה־לִּ֥⁠י אֱלֹהַ֖⁠י לְ⁠טוֹבָ֑ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
In this context, **remember** means to think about someone and consider what action you can take on their behalf. Nehemiah is not suggesting that God has forgotten him. Alternate translation: [think of me]

**Quote:** כֹּ֥ל אֲשֶׁר־עָשִׂ֖יתִי עַל־הָ⁠עָ֥ם הַ⁠זֶּֽה
Nehemiah is once again leaving out some words that a sentence would ordinarily need. He is asking God to do good things for him because of all the good things he has done for the people of Judah as their governor. Alternate translation: [reward me because of all the good that I have done for the people of Judah]
