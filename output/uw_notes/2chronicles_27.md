## Chapter intro

# 2 Chronicles 27 General Notes

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### Trust in Yahweh

Jotham was able to conquer the people of Ammon because he obeyed the Lord. For Yahweh, trust in Yahweh is the key to victory in battle. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/trust]])

## 2 Chronicles 27:2

**Quote:** He did what was right in the eyes of Yahweh
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The eyes here represent seeing, and seeing represents judgment. Yahweh saw and approved of Jotham’s actions. See how you translated this in [2 Chronicles 14:2](../14/02.md). Alternate translation: [He did what Yahweh judged to be right] or [He did what Yahweh considered to be right]

## 2 Chronicles 27:5

**Quote:** He fought also with the king of the people of Ammon
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
You may need to translate so that the reader understands that Jothan led his army to fight. Alternate translation: [He also led his army to fight with the king of the people of Ammon]

**Quote:** with the king of the people of Ammon
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
You may need to translate so that the reader understands that the king of Ammon led his army to fight. Alternate translation: [with the king of Ammon and his army]

**Quote:** one hundred talents of silver
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-bweight
You may convert this to a modern measure. A “talent” is equivalent to 33 kilograms. Alternate translation: [100 talents of silver] or [3,300 kilograms of silver] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-numbers]])

**Quote:** ten thousand measures of wheat
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-bvolume
You may convert this to a modern measure. The word translated here as “measure” is “cor” and is equivalent to 220 liters. Alternate translation: [10,000 measures of wheat] or [2,200 kiloliters of wheat] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-numbers]])

**Quote:** ten thousand measures of barley
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-bvolume
You may convert this to a modern measure. The word translated here as “measure” is “cor” and is equivalent to 220 liters. Alternate translation: [10,000 measures of barley] or [2,200 kiloliters of barley] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-numbers]])

**Quote:** in the second and third years
Alternate translation: [in the second and third years after he defeated them]

## 2 Chronicles 27:6

**Quote:** he walked firmly before Yahweh his God
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Jotham’s actions and lifestyle are spoken of as if they were a path upon which he walked. That he walked firmly means that he was devoted to living in this way. Alternate translation: [he faithfully obeyed Yahweh his God]

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
If your language does not use the passive form in this way, you can state this in active form or in another way that is natural in your language. Alternate translation: [they are in the book] or [you can read of them in the book]
