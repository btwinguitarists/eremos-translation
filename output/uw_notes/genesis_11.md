## Genesis 11:1

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יְהִ֥י כָל־הָ⁠אָ֖רֶץ שָׂפָ֣ה אֶחָ֑ת
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-time-background
The events in [chapter 11:1–9](../11/01.md) happened before the people listed in [chapter 10](../10/01.md) had their own languages and before they spread out over the earth ([Genesis 10:5](../10/05.md), [10:19–20](../10/19.md), and [10:30–32](../10/30.md)). You could include some of that information in a footnote. Alternate translation: [Now at first, everyone on the earth shared the same language] or [Now for a while, all the people on the earth spoke one language]

**Quote:** וּ⁠דְבָרִ֖ים אֲחָדִֽים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-doublet
The phrases **one language** and **the same words** are very similar in meaning and emphasize the fact that everyone spoke the same language. Consider what is the best way to communicate that in your language. Alternate translation: [and shared the same vocabulary] or [and spoke words everyone knew]

## Genesis 11:2

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יְהִ֖י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-newevent
The author is using the word translated as **And it happened** to introduce a new development in the story. Use a word, phrase, or other method in your language that is natural for introducing a new development.

**Quote:** בְּ⁠נָסְעָ֣⁠ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-pronouns
Consider whether it is better in your language to use a pronoun (**they**) or a noun phrase (“the people”) here. Alternate translation: [they traveled] or [the people moved]

**Quote:** מִ⁠קֶּ֑דֶם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
At first after the flood, people lived in the regions around the mountains of Ararat (where the ark had landed). As they began to migrate and spread out to different places (as God had told them to do), they must have headed south at some point since they ended up at the plain of Shinar, which was south of where the Ararat Mountains were. The reference to **the east** probably reflects the viewpoint of the author (Moses), since the Ararat mountains and the plain of Shinar were both far to the east of where he was living. Alternate translation: [around in the eastern regions]

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יִּמְצְא֥וּ
Make sure the way you translate this phrase fits with the way you translated the first half of this verse. Alternate translation: [that they discovered] or [that they eventually came to]

**Quote:** בִקְעָ֛ה בְּ⁠אֶ֥רֶץ שִׁנְעָ֖ר
This plain was a wide, flat area between mountains. Also be consistent here with how you translated **land of Shinar** in Genesis [10:10](../10/10.md). Alternate translation: [a wide flat area in the land called Shinar]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֵּ֥שְׁבוּ שָֽׁם
Alternate translation: [and lived there] or [and began to live there]

Also, throughout this chapter, be consistent with how you have decided to handle numbers in the Bible. This is especially true in translations that use a large font size for verse numbers so that people doing group Bible study can easily see the numbers and be in the same verse together. ## Genesis 11:26

**Quote:** וַֽ⁠יְחִי־תֶ֖רַח שִׁבְעִ֣ים שָׁנָ֑ה וַ⁠יּ֨וֹלֶד֙ אֶת־אַבְרָ֔ם אֶת־נָח֖וֹר וְ⁠אֶת־הָרָֽן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-kinship
Your translation needs to allow for the fact that Terah’s sons were not triplets (just as Noah’s sons were not triplets; see [15:32](../15/32.md)), rather, he started having children after he turned seventy and his sons were born in different years after that. In fact, Abram was not born until Terah was at least 130 years old ([11:321](../11/321.md), [12:4–5](../12/04.md); Acts [7:4](../07/04.md)), and he was probably Terah’s youngest son. Abram is listed first (instead of Haran or Nahor) probably because of his importance as the one whom Yahweh made His covenant with. Some of this information could be put in a footnote. Alternate translation: [After Terah had lived 70 years, his wife birthed three sons.] or [After Terah turned 70 years old]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יּ֨וֹלֶד֙ אֶת־אַבְרָ֔ם אֶת־נָח֖וֹר וְ⁠אֶת־הָרָֽן
Alternate translation: [he had sons named Abram, Nahor, and Haran] or [he fathered sons whose names were Abram, Nahor, and Haran]

## Genesis 11:31

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקַּ֨ח תֶּ֜רַח
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-newevent
Consider what is the best way to begin this new paragraph in your language. Alternate translation: [One day, Terah called together]

**Quote:** אֶת־אַבְרָ֣ם בְּנ֗⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-pronouns
Make sure it is clear in your translation of this phrase that **his son** refers to Abram, not another person.

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־ל֤וֹט בֶּן־הָרָן֙ בֶּן־בְּנ֔⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-kinship
The phrases “Lot,” “Haran’s son,” and **his son’s son** all refer to Lot. Make sure that is clear in your translation and that it does not sound like three people.

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֵת֙ שָׂרַ֣י כַּלָּת֔⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-kinship
The phrases “Sarai,” “his daughter-in-law,” and **the wife of his son Abram** all refer to Sarai. Make sure that is clear in your translation.

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֵּצְא֨וּ אִתָּ֜⁠ם
Alternate translation: [and all of them set out together] or [Then they all departed]

**Quote:** מֵ⁠א֣וּר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
See the note at [10:19](../10/19.md) about how to translate the names of cities. Alternate translation: [from Ur City]

**Quote:** כַּשְׂדִּ֗ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
See how you translated this phrase in [verse 28](../11/28.md). Alternate translation: [of the Chaldeans] or [where the Kasdim people lived]

**Quote:** לָ⁠לֶ֨כֶת֙ אַ֣רְצָ⁠ה כְּנַ֔עַן
Alternate translation: [to go to the region of Canaan] or [to go to Canaan Land]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּבֹ֥אוּ עַד־חָרָ֖ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-contrast
The city of Haran was not in the land of Canaan; rather, it was around 450 kilometers (280 miles) northeast of there. The author uses the word **But** here to indicate a strong contrast between what was expected and what happened. In your translation, indicate this strong contrast in a way that is natural in your language. Alternate translation: [But on the way there, they came to the city of Haran, and] or [But along the way, when they stopped at the city of Haran]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֵּ֥שְׁבוּ שָֽׁם
Alternate translation: [they stayed there instead] or [they decided to settle there instead]
