## Genesis 2:2

**Quote:** בַּ⁠יּ֣וֹם הַ⁠שְּׁבִיעִ֔י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-ordinal
Make sure it is clear in your translation that God had completed his work by the seventh day and did not work on that day. Also consider again how you translated ordinal numbers in Genesis [1:5](../01/05.md), [8](../01/08.md), [13](../01/13.md), [19](../01/19.md), [23](../01/23.md), [31](../01/31.md); [2:2](../02/02.md). Alternate translation: [By day seven]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשְׁבֹּת֙ בַּ⁠יּ֣וֹם הַ⁠שְּׁבִיעִ֔י מִ⁠כָּל
God **rested** in the sense that he stopped his work and did not work on that day. It does not mean he was tired or had lost his strength. Rest is an important theme in the Bible, so you should try to be consistent in how you translate this term here and in other passages, such as [Exodus 23:12](../../exo/23/12.md), [31:17](../../exo/31/17.md) and [Hebrews 4:4](../../heb/04/04.md), [10](../../heb/04/10.md). Part of this theme includes the Hebrew word for Sabbath, which has the same root word as the verb for “rest” and is often translated as “resting day.” Alternate translation: [so he ceased on that day from all] or [so on the seventh day he no longer did]

**Quote:** מְלַאכְתּ֖⁠וֹ אֲשֶׁ֣ר עָשָׂ֑ה
Alternate translation: [the work that he had been doing]

## Genesis 2:4

**Quote:** אֵ֣לֶּה
This phrase refers forward to Genesis [2:5–24](../02/05.md) and begins a new section. [Chapter 1](../01/01.md) of Genesis gives an overview of what God did each day of creation week, including that he created human beings on day six. [Chapter 2](../02/01.md) gives more details about how God created Adam and Eve, who were his most important creations. Each chapter emphasizes different parts of the same history; they are not two different histories. Make sure that is clear in your translation. Alternate translation: [These are more details about] or [This is more about] or [What follows gives more details about]

**Quote:** תוֹלְד֧וֹת הַ⁠שָּׁמַ֛יִם וְ⁠הָ⁠אָ֖רֶץ בְּ⁠הִבָּֽרְאָ֑⁠ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
The Hebrew text uses a passive construction here to emphasize the heavens and the earth. Decide the best way to do that in your language. Alternate translation: [the history of the heavens and the earth when they were created by God] or [what happened when God created the heavens and the earth]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠י֗וֹם
Alternate translation: [during the days when] or [During the days when]

**Quote:** עֲשׂ֛וֹת & אֶ֥רֶץ וְ⁠שָׁמָֽיִם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-poetry
This clause forms a poetic device called a chiasm with “the heavens and the earth … created” in the first part of this verse. Try to keep the same phrase order in your translation. See how you translated **the heavens** and **the earth** in [1:1](../01/01.md) and [2:1](../02/01.md). Alternate translation: [created the earth and the heavens]

See how you translated **earth** in [1:1](../01/01.md) and [2:1](../02/01.md). Alternate translation: [had not yet caused it to rain on the earth] or [had not yet caused rain to water the earth]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אָדָ֣ם אַ֔יִן

**Quote:** לַֽ⁠עֲבֹ֖ד אֶת־הָֽ⁠אֲדָמָֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom

It may be necessary to translate this term in different ways, depending on the context. Alternate translation: [to farm the land]

## Genesis 2:7

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּיצֶר֩ יְהוָ֨ה אֱלֹהִ֜ים
The word **formed** describes what an expert craftsmen would do; it implies artistic skills and design. Alternate translation: [Then Yahweh who is God shaped]

**Quote:** אֶת־הָֽ⁠אָדָ֗ם
The definite article **the** is used here because this refers to a specific man, the first male human. For many languages it is more natural to use an indefinite article (“a”) to introduce him here or else to not use an article at all. Alternate translation: [the first man]

**Quote:** עָפָר֙
The word used here in the Hebrew text is usually translated as **dust** (as in [13:16](../13/16.md), [28:14](../28/14.md)), but it can also be translated as “dirt” (as in [26:15](../26/15.md)) or “mortar/mud/clay” (as in [Leviticus 14:42](../../lev/14/42.md)). The dirt would have been damp enough to be formed or shaped. Alternate translation: [out of mud]

**Quote:** מִן־הָ֣⁠אֲדָמָ֔ה
For some languages it may be better to change the order of the phrases in the first half of this verse and say, “Then Yahweh who is God took some dirt from the ground and formed a man out of it,” Also, see how you translated **ground** in [1:25](../01/25.md) and [2:5–6](../02/05.md). You may need to translate this term in different ways, depending on the context. Alternate translation: [from the earth]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּפַּ֥ח
Alternate translation: [and he blew] or [Then he exhaled]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּפַּ֥ח בְּ⁠אַפָּ֖י⁠ו נִשְׁמַ֣ת חַיִּ֑ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-pronouns
This refers to God breathing God’s breath into man’s nostrils. As you translate the pronouns **he** and **his** in this verse, make sure it is clear in your language to whom each pronoun refers.

## Genesis 2:8

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּטַּ֞ע יְהוָ֧ה אֱלֹהִ֛ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-background
The clause **Now Yahweh God had planted** could mean: (1) “Now Yahweh God had previously planted” or “Before that, Yahweh God had planted.” This interpretation fits the context of chapter one, which says that God had made all the trees and plants on day three ([1:11–12](../01/11.md)), three days before he created man ([1:26–27](../01/26.md); [2:7](../02/07.md)). Following this view, [verses 8–14](../02/08.md) give background information for what happens in [verses 15–24](../02/15.md). (2) “Then Yahweh God planted” According to this interpretation, God made the Garden of Eden on day six, after he created man.

**Quote:** גַּן
The man lived in this garden, and there were animals and many trees and other plants in it too, so it was fairly large. Make sure your translation of this term does not refer to something that is too small. Alternate translation: [a large park]

**Quote:** בְ⁠עֵ֖דֶן מִ⁠קֶּ֑דֶם
In this context, the phrase **in the east** could refer to: (1) the eastern part of the region of Eden; or (2) a region east of where Moses lived when he wrote this. It is best, if possible, to keep your translation of this phrase general here (like the Hebrew text). Alternate translation: [in the region called Eden, which was to the east] or [in the eastern part of the land of Eden]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּ֣שֶׂם שָׁ֔ם אֶת־הָֽ⁠אָדָ֖ם
Alternate translation: [and in that garden he put the man] or [That is where he put the man]

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁ֥ר יָצָֽר
See how you translated **formed** in [verse 7](../02/07.md). Alternate translation: [whom he had shaped] or [whom he had created]

**Quote:** וְ⁠נָהָרּ֙ יֹצֵ֣א
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-words-phrases

**Quote:** לְ⁠הַשְׁק֖וֹת

**Quote:** אֶת־הַ⁠גָּ֑ן
See how you translated this phrase in [verse 8](../02/08.md). Alternate translation: [the park]

**Quote:** וּ⁠מִ⁠שָּׁם֙

**Quote:** יִפָּרֵ֔ד וְ⁠הָיָ֖ה לְ⁠אַרְבָּעָ֥ה רָאשִֽׁים
This verse could refer to: (1) a river in Eden which was the source river that watered the garden and then divided downstream into the four smaller rivers. Alternate translation: [the river divided into four smaller rivers]; (2) the four rivers being source headwaters that flowed downstream and joined to form the larger river in Eden. Alternate translation: [there were four smaller source rivers that joined together and formed the larger river] If possible, leave your translation ambiguous here (as the Hebrew text does), so that either interpretation is possible.

## Genesis 2:11

**Quote:** שֵׁ֥ם הָֽ⁠אֶחָ֖ד
Most translations use past tense in [verse 10](../02/10.md), but then use present tense throughout [verses 11–14](../02/11.md). It may be best to use past tense throughout [verses 10–14](../02/10.md), especially since the rivers and places that are named in these verses no longer existed when Genesis was written, because the Flood completely changed the entire surface of the earth; after the Flood, the names of some of those rivers and places were given to new rivers and places. Alternate translation: [The first river is named] or [The first river is]

**Quote:** פִּישׁ֑וֹן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Consider whether or not it is natural in your language to use the article (the) with the name of a river.

**Quote:** ה֣וּא הַ⁠סֹּבֵ֗ב
The words **winds** or “meanders” mean that the river changed direction from time to time. Alternate translation: [It meanders through] or [which flows around]

**Quote:** אֵ֚ת כָּל־אֶ֣רֶץ הַֽ⁠חֲוִילָ֔ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁר־שָׁ֖ם הַ⁠זָּהָֽב
Alternate translation: [a place where there is much gold] or [which has a lot of gold]

## Genesis 2:12

**Quote:** וּֽ⁠זֲהַ֛ב הָ⁠אָ֥רֶץ הַ⁠הִ֖וא
This sentence emphasizes the previous statement about the gold and gives more information about it. Alternate translation: [In fact, the gold from that land] or [Indeed, the gold that is found in that land]

**Quote:** ט֑וֹב
Alternate translation: [is very pure] or [is very high quality]

**Quote:** הַ⁠בְּדֹ֖לַח
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-unknown
The meaning of this Hebrew word is not certain. In many translations it is transliterated, that is, spelled the way the Hebrew word is pronounced. It may refer to: (1) a fragrant tree resin that was used to make perfume; or (2) a type of stone or crystal, or possibly pearl. Alternate translation: [A kind of sweet-smelling resin called bdellium] or [A kind of fragrant perfume]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶ֥בֶן הַ⁠שֹּֽׁהַם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-unknown
It is not certain what kind of stone this was, but most Bible scholars think it was onyx, which is a kind of quartz that is often black but also occurs in many other colors and can have stripes or bands of white (or other colors) running through it. Alternate translation: [and valuable stones called onyx] or [and valuable gemstones]

**Quote:** שָׁ֥ם
Make sure the verb tense you choose here fits with the way you translated [verse 11](../02/11.md). Alternate translation: [are also found there] or [are also in that land]
