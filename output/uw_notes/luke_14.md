# Luke 14 Chapter Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

1. Jesus attends a banquet and tells a parable about a banquet ([14:1–24](../14/01.md))
2. Jesus teaches more about being his disciple ([14:25–35](../14/25.md))

## Translation Issues in This Chapter

### Parable

Jesus told the parable in [Luke 14:15–24](../14/15.md) to teach that the kingdom of God will be something that everyone can enjoy, but many people will refuse to be part of it. (See: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor]] and [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/kingdomofgod]])

## Translation Issues in This Chapter

### Paradox

A paradox is a statement that describes two things that seem as if they cannot both be true at the same time, but which actually are both true. Jesus speaks a paradox in this chapter: “For everyone who exalts himself will be humbled, and he who humbles himself will be exalted” ([14:11](../14/11.md)).
