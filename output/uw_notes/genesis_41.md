## Genesis 41:10

**Quote:** פַּרְעֹ֖ה קָצַ֣ף עַל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The events in [verses 10–13](../41/10.md) had happened two years earlier ([verse 1](../41/01.md)); [chapter 40](../40/01.md) gives the details about those events. See how you translated the idiom **broke out in anger against** in Genesis [40:2](../40/02.md). Alternate translation: [Some time ago, King Pharaoh, you were very angry at]

**Quote:** עֲבָדָ֑י⁠ו וַ⁠יִּתֵּ֨ן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-123person
In [verses 10](../41/10.md) and [13](../41/13.md), the cupbearer uses third person (Pharaoh, his, he) to show respect for Pharaoh. For some languages, it is confusing to do that, so second person (“you … your …you …”) must be used instead. Some languages have an honorific such as “your Majesty” that could be used occasionally in these verses instead of third person. Alternate translation: [your officers, and you put]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּתֵּ֨ן אֹתִ֜⁠י בְּ⁠מִשְׁמַ֗ר בֵּ֚ית שַׂ֣ר הַ⁠טַּבָּחִ֔ים אֹתִ֕⁠י וְ⁠אֵ֖ת שַׂ֥ר הָ⁠אֹפִֽים
See how you translated “put … in custody in the house of the chief of the guards” in Genesis [40:3](../40/03.md). Also consider again how you translated **chief of the bakers** and “baker” in the book of Genesis. See [40:1–2](../40/01.md), [4–5](../40/04.md), [16–17](../40/16.md), [20](../40/20.md), [22](../40/22.md); [41:10](../41/10.md), [13](../41/13.md).
