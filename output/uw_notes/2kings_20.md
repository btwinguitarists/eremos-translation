## Chapter intro

# 2 Kings 20 Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

In this chapter, the author concludes his detailed account of the reign of Hezekiah, one of the great kings of Judah.

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### Why did Isaiah say to put figs on Hezekiah’s sore?

In the ancient world, figs were a known remedy for sores and boils. Figs contain natural compounds and enzymes that have anti-inflammatory, soothing, and drawing effects. These can help bring a festering boil to a head and reduce swelling and infection. However, Hezekiah was so ill that he was near death, so it was improbable that the effects of the figs alone would have cured him. So agreeing to have his servants put the figs on his sore anyway was therefore an act of faith, trust, and obedience by Hezekiah. It showed that he was prepared to follow the instructions of Yahweh’s prophet, even if it did not seem that the measures he specified would be sufficient. Isaiah had the king’s servants use the best means available, so that they and he and the king were doing their part. But Hezekiah ultimately had to trust Yahweh for his healing.

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-unknown
The phrase **the steps of Ahaz** describes a structure that King Ahaz, the father of Hezekiah, built to track the passage of time. If your readers would not be familiar with such a structure, you could use the name of a similar thing that your readers would recognize, or you could use a more general term. **Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Hezekiah does not mean that the content of this message from Yahweh is objectively **good**. He means that he accepts and submits to what Yahweh has decided. You could indicate this explicitly in your translation if that would be helpful to your readers. The UST models one way to do that.

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֕אמֶר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
See how you translated the similar expression in [11:15](../11/15.md). Alternate translation: [For he thought]

**Quote:** הֲ⁠ל֛וֹא אִם־שָׁל֥וֹם וֶ⁠אֱמֶ֖ת יִהְיֶ֥ה בְ⁠יָמָֽ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-ellipsis
Hezekiah is leaving out some of the words that in many languages a sentence would need in order to be complete. You can supply these words from the context if that would be clearer in your language. Alternate translation: [Is it not good, if peace and stability will be in my days?]

**Quote:** הֲ⁠ל֛וֹא אִם־שָׁל֥וֹם וֶ⁠אֱמֶ֖ת יִהְיֶ֥ה בְ⁠יָמָֽ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Hezekiah is using the question form for emphasis. If a speaker of your language would not use the question form for that purpose, you could translate this as a statement or as an exclamation. Alternate translation: [It is good, since peace and stability will be in my days.]

**Quote:** אִם־שָׁל֥וֹם וֶ⁠אֱמֶ֖ת יִהְיֶ֥ה בְ⁠יָמָֽ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-abstractnouns
If your language does not use abstract nouns for the ideas of **peace** and **stability**, you could express the same idea in another way. Alternate translation: [if my kingdom will be peaceful and stabile in my days]

**Quote:** בְ⁠יָמָֽ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metonymy
See how you translated the similar expression in [10:36](../10/36.md). Alternate translation: [during my lifetime]
