# Daniel 8 General Notes

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### The vision of the ram and the male goat

Although specific interpretation of this vision is not given, most scholars believe Daniel saw Greece overthrowing Media-Persia before breaking up into four kingdoms. One of these kingdoms stopped the temple worship for a while and then it was restored. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/temple]] and [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/restore]])

## Daniel 8:1

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-symlanguage
# General Information:

Chapters 7 and 8 are not in chronological order. They happened while Belshazzar was still the king, before the rule of Darius and Cyrus that was discussed in chapter 6. In Daniel’s vision, he saw animals that were symbols of other things. Later in the vision someone explains the meaning of those symbols.

**Quote:** בִּ⁠שְׁנַ֣ת שָׁל֔וֹשׁ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-ordinal
“In year three”

**Quote:** בֵּלְאשַׁצַּ֣ר
**Belshazzar** is the son of Nebuchadnezzar who became king after his father. See how you translated this name in [Daniel 5:1](../05/01.md).

**Quote:** חָז֞וֹן נִרְאָ֤ה אֵלַ⁠י֙ & אַחֲרֵ֛י הַ⁠נִּרְאָ֥ה אֵלַ֖⁠י בַּ⁠תְּחִלָּֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-background
This is background information to remind the reader that this is Daniel’s second vision. Alternate translation: [had a second vision appear to me]

## Daniel 8:11

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-personification
# General Information:

The horn is given additional qualities of a person.

**Quote:** שַֽׂר־הַ⁠צָּבָ֖א
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
This refers to God himself, who is the leader of the angel army.

**Quote:** וּ⁠מִמֶּ֨⁠נּוּ֙ הרים הַ⁠תָּמִ֔יד

**Quote:** וְ⁠הֻשְׁלַ֖ךְ מְכ֥וֹן מִקְדָּשֽׁ⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive

## Daniel 8:20-26

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-symlanguage
# General Information:

In verses 20–26, the angel explains to Daniel the symbolic meaning of the things he saw in his vision. The animals and horns actually represent human rulers and kingdoms.
