## Genesis 10:1

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֵ֨לֶּה֙ תּוֹלְדֹ֣ת
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-newevent
This sentence begins a new section. Alternate translation: [Here is the list of the names of]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּוָּלְד֥וּ לָ⁠הֶ֛ם בָּנִ֖ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The following record includes the names of Noah’s sons and grandsons and their descendants (including some of the people groups who came from them). Here, **sons** means “descendants. The author is identifying these people as descendants of their ancestor Noah and his sons. If it would be helpful in your language, you could state the meaning plainly. Alternate translation: [and their sons and other descendants who were born] or [and the sons they had]

**Quote:** אַחַ֥ר הַ⁠מַּבּֽוּל
Alternate translation: [after the flood was gone]

## Genesis 10:4

**Quote:** וּ⁠בְנֵ֥י יָוָ֖ן
Compare how you translated a similar clause in [verses 2](../10/02.md) and [3](../10/03.md).

**Quote:** אֱלִישָׁ֣ה וְ⁠תַרְשִׁ֑ישׁ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
These are the names of Javan’s sons, but the next two names are the names of people groups that descended from him. Alternate translation, with no comma following: [named Elishah and Tarshish, and he was the ancestor of]

**Quote:** כִּתִּ֖ים וְ⁠דֹדָנִֽים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-transliterate
Most translation teams transliterate people-group names in the Bible, but handle the suffix endings in different ways. For example, you could: (1) transliterate the Hebrew plural suffix “-im”; or (2) use a suffix that your language has for names of people groups (such as “-ites” in English); or (3) translate “-im” as “people” or “people group.” Try to be consistent in how you handle people group names throughout this chapter and the rest of the book of Genesis. Alternate translation: [the Kitt people group and the Dodan people group]

## Genesis 10:5

**Quote:** מֵ֠⁠אֵלֶּה
This phrase probably refers to Japheth’s descendants ([verses 2–4](../10/02.md)), especially since the descendants of his brothers Ham ([verse 20](../10/20.md)) and Shem ([verse 31](../10/31.md)) are summarized in a similar way.

**Quote:** נִפְרְד֞וּ אִיֵּ֤י הַ⁠גּוֹיִם֙
Alternate translation: [came the people groups who lived in regions beside the sea. They moved to different places and settled]

**Quote:** בְּ⁠אַרְצֹתָ֔⁠ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
Alternate translation: [in their own regions]

**Quote:** אִ֖ישׁ לִ⁠לְשֹׁנ֑⁠וֹ
Alternate translation: [They all spoke their own languages]

**Quote:** לְ⁠מִשְׁפְּחֹתָ֖⁠ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-kinship
Here the term **clans** specifically refers to a group of families who are descended from a common ancestor. If your language has a specific word for clans, it would be appropriate to use it here. Alternate translation: [and each of them lived in their own extended families] or [They each had their own family groups]

**Quote:** מֵ֠⁠אֵלֶּה נִפְרְד֞וּ אִיֵּ֤י הַ⁠גּוֹיִם֙ בְּ⁠אַרְצֹתָ֔⁠ם אִ֖ישׁ לִ⁠לְשֹׁנ֑⁠וֹ לְ⁠מִשְׁפְּחֹתָ֖⁠ם בְּ⁠גוֹיֵ⁠הֶֽם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
for some languages, it may be clearer to change the order of the phrases in this verse. Alternate translation: [Japheth’s descendants spread out and settled near the Mediterranean Sea. They all formed their own family groups, which grew and became their own people groups that spoke their own languages and lived in their own territories]

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure

**Quote:** ה֣וּא הֵחֵ֔ל לִֽ⁠הְי֥וֹת
The Hebrew text is ambiguous here. It could mean: (1) “who was the first person to be” or (2) “who became”

## Genesis 10:16

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־הַ⁠יְבוּסִי֙ וְ⁠אֶת־הָ֣⁠אֱמֹרִ֔י וְ⁠אֵ֖ת הַ⁠גִּרְגָּשִֽׁי
In [verses 16–18](../10/16.md), the people group names are singular collective nouns in Hebrew (in contrast to the people group names in [verses 4](../10/04.md), [13–14](../10/13.md) that are plural nouns). Most English versions translate these names with a plural “-ites” suffix so that it is clear that these names refer to more than one person. Alternate translation: [and Canaan was also the ancestor of the Jebusites, Amorites, Girgashites]
