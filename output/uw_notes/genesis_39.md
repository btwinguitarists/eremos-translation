## Genesis 39:1

**Quote:** וְ⁠יוֹסֵ֖ף הוּרַ֣ד מִצְרָ֑יְמָ⁠ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-background
[Chapter 39](../39/01.md) picks up where [37:36](../37/36.md) left off and repeats some events (that happened in [chapter 37](../37/01.md)) to remind readers of what happened before [chapter 38](../38/01.md) interrupted the narrative. Make sure your translation does not sound like Joseph was taken to Egypt twice or sold to Potiphar twice. Alternate translation: [Now as you know, Ishmaelite merchants had brought Joseph down to the country of Egypt] or [Returning now to Joseph: Remember that he had been taken down to the country of Egypt by Ishmaelite traders]

**Quote:** פּוֹטִיפַר֩ סְרִ֨יס פַּרְעֹ֜ה שַׂ֤ר הַ⁠טַּבָּחִים֙ אִ֣ישׁ מִצְרִ֔י
See how you translated **an official of Pharaoh, the chief of the guards** in Genesis [37:36](../37/36.md).

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקְנֵ֡⁠הוּ & מִ⁠יַּד֙ הַ⁠יִּשְׁמְעֵאלִ֔ים אֲשֶׁ֥ר הוֹרִדֻ֖⁠הוּ שָֽׁמָּ⁠ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For some languages it is clearer and more natural to change the order of clauses in this verse and say, “Now as you know, some Ishmaelite traders had taken/brought Joseph down to the country of Egypt, and an Egyptian named Potiphar had bought him as a slave/servant from them. Potiphar was one of Pharaoh’s officials/officers, the captain over the king’s personal guards.” See how you translated **Ishmaelites** in Genesis [37:25](../37/25.md), [27–28](../37/27.md). Alternate translation: [had bought him as a slave from the Ishmaelites who had brought him there]

## Genesis 39:3

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּ֣רְא אֲדֹנָ֔י⁠ו כִּ֥י יְהוָ֖ה אִתּ֑⁠וֹ
In [verses 2](../39/02.md) through 20, Potiphar is referred to as Joseph’s master ([verses 2–3](../39/02.md), [7–8](../39/07.md), [16](../39/16.md), [19–20](../39/19.md)) and “the Egyptian” ([verse 5](../39/05.md)); he is no longer mentioned by his name in the Hebrew text. Some translations continue to use Potiphar’s name occasionally in [verses 2](../39/02.md) through 20 to prevent confusion and make it clear that all of these references refer to the same person, not two or three different people. See how you translated **master** or “owner” in Genesis [24:9–10](../24/09.md).

**Quote:** וְ⁠כֹל֙ אֲשֶׁר־ה֣וּא עֹשֶׂ֔ה יְהוָ֖ה מַצְלִ֥יחַ בְּ⁠יָדֽ⁠וֹ
See how you translated **successful** in [verse 2](../39/02.md). Alternate translation: [and was helping Joseph to prosper in everything that he did] or [and was helping Joseph to do everything very well]

Also see how you translated the idiom “gave … into the hand of” in Genesis [32:16](../32/16.md). Alternate translation: [He also put Joseph in charge of managing his household and taking care of everything else that he owned] or [He also appointed him as the overseer of his entire household and entrusted into his care everything that belonged to him]

## Genesis 39:6

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּעֲזֹ֣ב כָּל־אֲשֶׁר־ל⁠וֹ֮ בְּ⁠יַד־יוֹסֵף֒
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom

**Quote:** וְ⁠לֹא־יָדַ֤ע אִתּ⁠וֹ֙ מְא֔וּמָה כִּ֥י אִם־הַ⁠לֶּ֖חֶם אֲשֶׁר־ה֣וּא אוֹכֵ֑ל

**Quote:** וַ⁠יְהִ֣י יוֹסֵ֔ף יְפֵה־תֹ֖אַר וִ⁠יפֵ֥ה מַרְאֶֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-background
Some languages have a conjunction such as **Now** that introduces background information and a change of topic. For other languages a paragraph break is enough (without a conjunction). Alternate translation: [Joseph was muscular and good-looking] or [Now Joseph was a very handsome young man]

## Genesis 39:9

**Quote:** אֵינֶ֨⁠נּוּ גָד֜וֹל בַּ⁠בַּ֣יִת הַ⁠זֶּה֮ מִמֶּ⁠נִּי֒
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
By saying **He is not greater than me in this house**, Joseph means that Potiphar still had more authority than Joseph, but he had given Joseph authority to act in his place over everything in his household. You could indicate this explicitly in your translation if that would be helpful to your readers. Alternate translation: [As a result, even he does not exercise more authority in his household than I do]

**Quote:** וְ⁠לֹֽא־חָשַׂ֤ךְ מִמֶּ֨⁠נִּי֙ מְא֔וּמָה כִּ֥י אִם־אוֹתָ֖⁠ךְ בַּ⁠אֲשֶׁ֣ר אַתְּ־אִשְׁתּ֑⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-litotes
Joseph is using a figure of speech here that emphasizes a positive meaning by using a negative word, **not**, together with a mild expression that is the opposite of the intended meaning. If it would be helpful in your language, you could emphasize the positive meaning in a strong way. The phrase **has not withheld** emphasizes the freedom that Potiphar had given Joseph. Some languages must translate this in a way that does not use a negative word such as **not**. Alternate translation: [and he has given me freedom with everything in his household, except you, of course, because you are his wife]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֵ֨יךְ אֶֽעֱשֶׂ֜ה הָ⁠רָעָ֤ה הַ⁠גְּדֹלָה֙ הַ⁠זֹּ֔את וְ⁠חָטָ֖אתִי לֵֽ⁠אלֹהִֽים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Joseph uses this rhetorical question to emphasize why he will not do what Potiphar’s wife wants. Alternate translation: [So I refuse to do such an evil thing, which would be a terrible sin against God!]

## Genesis 39:10

**Quote:** וַ⁠יְהִ֕י כְּ⁠דַבְּרָ֥⁠הּ אֶל־יוֹסֵ֖ף י֣וֹם ׀ י֑וֹם

Many English translations have omitted this phrase, possibly for reasons of naturalness. Consider whether you have an expression like this that is natural in your language.

Alternate translation: [and he stayed away from her] or [and would not even go near her]
