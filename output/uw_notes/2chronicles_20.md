## Chapter intro

# 2 Chronicles 20 General Notes

## Structure and Formatting

The story of King Jehoshaphat is completed in this chapter.

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### Deliverance and trust

God rescues those who trust him. An enormous army came against Jehoshaphat but God promised that he would defeat them without Judah having to fight. Then the enemy fought among themselves and when the army of Judah arrived there were dead bodies. (See: [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/trust]] and [[rc://*/tw/dict/bible/kt/promise]])

This phrase is used here to mark an important event in the story. If your language has a way for doing this, you could consider using it here.

## 2 Chronicles 20:6

**Quote:** are you not God in heaven? Are you not the ruler over all the kingdoms of the nations?
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Jehoshaphat uses this rhetorical question to emphasize that God is in heaven and his authority over all things. This question can be written as statement. Alternate translation: [you are indeed God in heaven and the ruler over all the kings of the earth.]

**Quote:** Power and might are in your hand
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-doublet
The words “Power” and “might” mean basically the same thing and emphasize the greatness of Yahweh’s power. The word “hand” refers to possession. Alternate translation: [You possess great power] or [You have great power and might] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche]])

## 2 Chronicles 20:7

**Quote:** Our God, did you not drive out the inhabitants of this land … to the descendants of Abraham?

## 2 Chronicles 20:32

**Quote:** He walked in the ways of Asa, his father; he did not turn away from them
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
This means that he lived in a way that pleased Yahweh as his father had done. Here “walking” is an idiom for the way a person lives and behaves. Alternate translation: [He did things that were pleasing to Yahweh, like his father Asa had done, and he did not stop doing those things] (See also: [[rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom]])

**Quote:** he did what was right in the eyes of Yahweh
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
The eyes here represent seeing, and seeing represents judgment. Yahweh saw and approved of Jehoshaphat’s actions. See how you translated a similar phrase in [2 Chronicles 14:2](../14/02.md). Alternate translation: [he did what Yahweh judged to be right] or [he did what Yahweh considered to be right]
