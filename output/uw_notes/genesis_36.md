## Genesis 36:1

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֵ֛לֶּה תֹּלְד֥וֹת עֵשָׂ֖ו
See how you translated **the generations of** in [25:12](../25/12.md), [19](../25/19.md). Alternate translation: [Now this is the family-line of Esau] or [These are the descendants of Esau] or [Here is the history about the family of Esau]

**Quote:** ה֥וּא אֱדֽוֹם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The name Edom means “red” and is the nickname that Esau got for trading his birthright to Jacob for some reddish-colored stew (Genesis [25:29–34](../25/29.md)). The region of Edom and the Edomites were named after Esau, which is probably why this chapter repeatedly mentions that Esau is Edom. Alternate translation: [who was also called Edom] or [that is, Edom]

## Genesis 36:2

**Quote:** עֵשָׂ֛ו לָקַ֥ח אֶת־נָשָׁ֖י⁠ו מִ⁠בְּנ֣וֹת כְּנָ֑עַן
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Only the first two wives mentioned in this chapter were Canaanites; see the comment about Basemath at [verse 3](../36/03.md). The Canaanites were the descendants of Noah’s grandson Canaan (Ham’s son) and included the Hittites and Hivites ([verse 2](../36/02.md); Genesis [10:15–18](../10/15.md)) and several other people groups. You could put some of that information in a footnote. Alternate translation: [Esau had chosen two of his wives from among the Canaanite women:] or [Esau had married two women who were descendants of Canaan]

**Quote:** אֶת־עָדָ֗ה
Alternate translation: [whose names were Adah]

**Quote:** בַּת־אֵילוֹן֙ הַֽ⁠חִתִּ֔י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Make sure your translation does not sound like Elon was the only Hittite. See how you translated “Hittites” in Genesis [27:46](../27/46.md). Alternate translation: [whose father was Elon, who was a descendant of Heth] or [the daughter of a man named Elon, who was a member of the Heth people group]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־אָהֳלִֽיבָמָה֙
Notice that the names of Esau’s two Canaanite wives here are different from the names of his wives mentioned in Genesis [26:34](../26/34.md). It could be that they each had two names, or these may be different wives that he married later. Alternate translation: [and also Oholibamah] or [as well as Oholibamah]

**Quote:** בַּת־עֲנָ֔ה בַּת
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Notice that Elon, Anah, and Zibeon are the names of men here, not women.

**Quote:** צִבְע֖וֹן הַֽ⁠חִוִּֽי
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Make sure that your translation of this phrase does not sound like Zibeon was the only Hivite. Consider again how you translated “Hivites” in the book of Genesis. See [10:17](../10/17.md); [34:2](../34/02.md); [36:2](../36/02.md). Alternate translation: [Zibeon who was a descendant of Hivi] or [Zibeon who was a member of the Hivi people group]

## Genesis 36:3

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־בָּשְׂמַ֥ת
Since this Basemath was a daughter of Ishmael (who was a descendant of Noah’s son Shem), she was not a Canaanite, in contrast to Esau’s first two wives ([verse 2](../36/02.md)). She was also known as Mahalath (Genesis [28:9](../28/09.md)); you could include some of that information in a footnote. Be consistent here with how you spelled Basemath (a different woman with the same name) in Genesis [26:34](../26/34.md).

**Quote:** בַּת־יִשְׁמָעֵ֖אל אֲח֥וֹת נְבָיֽוֹת
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-kinship
Since Nebaioth was Ishmael’s oldest child (Genesis [25:13](../25/13.md)), Basemath (Mahalath) was his younger sister, probably from the same mother. Use a kinship term here in your language that fits with those facts. See how you translated **the sister of Nebaioth** in Genesis [28:9](../28/09.md). Alternate translation: [whose father was Ishmael and whose older brother was Nebaioth]

## Genesis 36:6

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקַּ֣ח עֵשָׂ֡ו אֶת־נָ֠שָׁי⁠ו וְ⁠אֶת־בָּנָ֣י⁠ו וְ⁠אֶת־בְּנֹתָי⁠ו֮

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־כָּל־נַפְשׁ֣וֹת בֵּית⁠וֹ֒
This phrase refers to other relatives (besides Esau’s wife and children), as well as servants or slaves who lived with him.

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־מִקְנֵ֣⁠הוּ וְ⁠אֶת־כָּל־בְּהֶמְתּ֗⁠וֹ וְ⁠אֵת֙ כָּל־קִנְיָנ֔⁠וֹ
Alternate translation: [as well as his livestock and all the rest of his animals and all the other possessions]

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁ֥ר רָכַ֖שׁ בְּ⁠אֶ֣רֶץ כְּנָ֑עַן
See how you translated **acquired** and **land of Canaan** in Genesis [12:5](../12/05.md) and [31:18](../31/18.md). Alternate translation: [that he had gotten while they lived in the land of Canaan]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֵּ֣לֶךְ אֶל־אֶ֔רֶץ מִ⁠פְּנֵ֖י יַעֲקֹ֥ב אָחִֽי⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For some languages it is more natural to put this clause at the beginning of this verse and say, “Then/Later Esau moved to a land that was away from his brother Jacob. He took with him his wives …” Alternate translation: [and he moved to an area that was away from his brother Jacob] or [and he traveled to a different region and settled there, away from where his brother Jacob was living]

## Genesis 36:7

**Quote:** כִּֽי
Alternate translation: [He did that because] or [The reason Esau moved away is that]

**Quote:** הָיָ֧ה רְכוּשָׁ֛⁠ם רָ֖ב מִ⁠שֶּׁ֣בֶת יַחְדָּ֑ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
The phrase **their possessions** is general, but here it refers primarily to livestock. Make sure your translation of this phrase refers to both Esau and Jacob’s livestock, not just Esau’s. Alternate translation: [he and Jacob both owned so many livestock that they could no longer live near each other] or [they both owned so many livestock that they could not live together in the same area]

**Quote:** אֶ֤רֶץ מְגֽוּרֵי⁠הֶם֙
See how you translated Genesis [13:6](../13/06.md), which is similar in content to this verse. Alternate translation: [The land where they were living]

**Quote:** וְ⁠לֹ֨א יָֽכְלָ֜ה & לָ⁠שֵׂ֣את אֹתָ֔⁠ם מִ⁠פְּנֵ֖י מִקְנֵי⁠הֶֽם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-versebridge
For some languages it is necessary to switch the order of [verses 6](../36/06.md) and [7](../36/07.md), and say, “After a while, Esau and Jacob had so many animals and other possessions that the land was not able to support both their families. So Esau took his wives …” If you do this in your translation, you should label these verses as “6–7.” Alternate translation: [was just not big enough to feed them all because they owned so many animals] or [did not have enough grazing areas or water to support all the livestock that both families owned]
