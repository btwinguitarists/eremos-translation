## Genesis 33:1

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשָּׂ֨א יַעֲקֹ֜ב עֵינָ֗י⁠ו וַ⁠יַּרְא֙
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
Make sure your translation of this idiom does not mean that Jacob looked straight up. Rather, he probably looked carefully to the south (the direction that his brother was coming from). Alternate translation: [Then Jacob looked up and saw] or [Then Jacob looked ahead of him and saw]

**Quote:** וְ⁠הִנֵּ֣ה עֵשָׂ֣ו בָּ֔א
Esau was still a fair distance away, which gave Jacob time to do everything that he does in [verses 1–3](../33/01.md). Alternate translation: [that Esau was in the distance coming his way]

**Quote:** וְ⁠עִמּ֕⁠וֹ אַרְבַּ֥ע מֵא֖וֹת אִ֑ישׁ
Alternate translation: [with 400 men!] or [and that he had 400 men with him!]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּ֣חַץ אֶת־הַ⁠יְלָדִ֗ים עַל־לֵאָה֙ וְ⁠עַל־רָחֵ֔ל וְ⁠עַ֖ל שְׁתֵּ֥י הַ⁠שְּׁפָחֽוֹת
Each of the women had her own children with her ([verse 2](../33/02.md)). See how you translated **divided** in Genesis [32:7](../32/07.md) and “his two maidservants” in Genesis [32:22](../32/22.md). Alternate translation: [So he quickly put his children into groups with their own mothers, Leah, Rachel and his two servant wives] or [So he had his children quickly gather with their own mothers, so that Leah, Rachel and his two concubines were each with her own children]

## Genesis 33:2

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּ֧שֶׂם אֶת־הַ⁠שְּׁפָח֛וֹת וְ⁠אֶת־יַלְדֵי⁠הֶ֖ן
See how you translated “his two maidservants” in Genesis [32:22](../32/22.md). Alternate translation: [He put his two servant wives with their children] or [He put the group that had his two servant wives and their children]

**Quote:** רִֽאשֹׁנָ֑ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
For some languages it is more natural to put phrases like **first** and **after them** at the beginning of their clauses and say, “First in line he put his two servant wives and/with their children. Behind them he put Leah and/with her children, and last of all he put Rachel and/with her son Joseph.” Alternate translation: [in front of all the others]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־לֵאָ֤ה וִֽ⁠ילָדֶ֨י⁠הָ֙
Alternate translation: [then he put Leah and her children]

**Quote:** אַחֲרֹנִ֔ים
Alternate translation: [next]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אֶת־רָחֵ֥ל וְ⁠אֶת־יוֹסֵ֖ף
Alternate translation: [and he put Rachel and her son Joseph]

**Quote:** אַחֲרֹנִ֔ים
Alternate translation: [behind them all] or [in the rear]

## Genesis 33:4

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּ֨רָץ עֵשָׂ֤ו לִ⁠קְרָאת⁠וֹ֙ וַֽ⁠יְחַבְּקֵ֔⁠הוּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-contrast
This sentence shows a contrast between what Jacob was expecting and what actually happened.

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּפֹּ֥ל עַל־צַוָּארָ֖⁠ו וַׄ⁠יִּׄשָּׁׄקֵ֑ׄ⁠הׄוּׄ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
See how you translated “hugged him … and kissed him on the cheek/cheeks” in Genesis [29:13](../29/13.md). Make sure that the way you translate **fell on his neck** does not imply that Esau accidentally fell. Rather, this is an idiom that means he embraced Jacob warmly, with much emotion. Alternate translation: [and he threw his arms around his shoulders and kissed him] or [He held Jacob tightly and kissed him on the cheek]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּבְכּֽוּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-youdual
Also, some languages have a dual pronoun for **they** that could be used here. Alternate translation: [and they both cried for joy] or [Both of them cried because they were so happy to see each other again]

## Genesis 33:5

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשָּׂ֣א אֶת־עֵינָ֗י⁠ו
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
See how you translated the idiom “lifted … eyes” in [verse 1](../33/01.md) and Genesis [31:10](../31/10.md). It may be necessary to translate this idiom in different ways, depending on the context. Alternate translation: [Then Esau looked up]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יַּ֤רְא אֶת־הַ⁠נָּשִׁים֙ וְ⁠אֶת־הַ⁠יְלָדִ֔ים
Alternate translation: [and saw the women and children who were there with Jacob]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֖אמֶר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
Alternate translation: [so he asked him]

**Quote:** מִי־אֵ֣לֶּה לָּ֑⁠ךְ
Esau wants to know if these people are Jacob’s family members or his servants or someone else; he is not asking what their names are. Alternate translation: [How are these people related to you?]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּאמַ֕ר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-pronouns
Make sure it is clear in your translation who is speaking to whom here. Alternate translation: [He answered him]

**Quote:** הַ⁠יְלָדִ֕ים
Alternate translation: [They are the children and the wives] or [They are my wives and my children]

**Quote:** אֲשֶׁר־חָנַ֥ן אֱלֹהִ֖ים אֶת־עַבְדֶּֽ⁠ךָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
Jacob uses the phrase **your servant** to be polite and show respect to Esau; he was not actually Esau’s servant. For some languages it is more natural to put the honorific title first in this sentence and say, “Sir, they are the wives and children whom God has graciously given to me.” Alternate translation: [whom God has kindly given to me, your servant] or [whom God has graciously given to me, sir]

## Genesis 33:10

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֣אמֶר יַעֲקֹ֗ב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
Alternate translation: [But Jacob responded to him]

**Quote:** אַל־נָא֙
In [verses 10](../33/10.md) and [11](../33/11.md), Jacob speaks urgently to Esau, because in that culture if Esau accepts Jacob’s gift, he is committing himself to treat Jacob as a friend. Alternate translation: [No, I beg you]

**Quote:** וְ⁠לָקַחְתָּ֥ מִנְחָתִ֖⁠י מִ⁠יָּדִ֑⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
Alternate translation: [please accept from me what I have given you]

**Quote:** כִּ֣י עַל־כֵּ֞ן
Alternate translation: [The fact is]

**Quote:** רָאִ֣יתִי פָנֶ֗י⁠ךָ כִּ⁠רְאֹ֛ת פְּנֵ֥י אֱלֹהִ֖ים וַ⁠תִּרְצֵֽ⁠נִי
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-simile
In Old Testament times it was terrifying for someone to see God in person, because normally people could not see God’s face and live ([Exodus 33:20](../../exo/33/20.md)). However, God had been gracious to Jacob and allowed him to live (Genesis [32:30](../32/30.md)). In the same way, it was a terrifying thing for Jacob to see Esau who had 400 men with him, but Esau was gracious to him ([33:4](../33/04.md)). See how you translated a different Hebrew word that means “receive” or “welcome” in Genesis [32:20](../32/20.md). Alternate translation: [I thought you were still angry at me, so I was afraid of seeing you again in person, just as I would be afraid of meeting God in person, but you forgave me and welcomed me!]

## Genesis 33:14

**Quote:** יַעֲבָר־נָ֥א אֲדֹנִ֖⁠י לִ⁠פְנֵ֣י עַבְדּ֑⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-politeness
See how you translated Jacob’s use of **my lord** (in [verse 8](../33/08.md)) and **servant** (in [verse 5](../33/05.md)) to show respect and deference to Esau. Here he uses both terms for emphasis.

**Quote:** וַ⁠אֲנִ֞י אֶֽתְנָהֲלָ֣ה לְ⁠אִטִּ֗⁠י לְ⁠רֶ֨גֶל הַ⁠מְּלָאכָ֤ה אֲשֶׁר־לְ⁠פָנַ⁠י֙ וּ⁠לְ⁠רֶ֣גֶל הַ⁠יְלָדִ֔ים
Alternate translation: [Then I will travel along slowly at a speed that is good for the livestock and children that are with me]

**Quote:** עַ֛ד אֲשֶׁר־אָבֹ֥א אֶל־אֲדֹנִ֖⁠י
Alternate translation: [until we reach you, sir] or [We will catch up to you later, sir]

**Quote:** שֵׂעִֽירָ⁠ה
