## Genesis 20:1

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּסַּ֨ע & אַבְרָהָם֙
Consider what is the best way to introduce this new episode in your language, where the focus shifts to Abraham. Alternate translation: [Then Abraham and his family left]

**Quote:** מִ⁠שָּׁ֤ם
Abraham and Sarah had been living by the trees of Mamre, near the city of Hebron (Genesis [13:18](../13/18.md); [18:1](../18/01.md)). Alternate translation: [from the place where he were living] or [from near the city of Hebron]

**Quote:** אַ֣רְצָ⁠ה הַ⁠נֶּ֔גֶב
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
See how you translated “the Negev Wilderness” in Genesis [12:9](../12/09.md) (note); [13:1](../13/01.md), [3](../13/03.md). Alternate translation: [and went south to the Negev Desert area]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֵּ֥שֶׁב בֵּין־קָדֵ֖שׁ וּ⁠בֵ֣ין שׁ֑וּר
Also, be consistent here with how you spelled **Kadesh** in Genesis [14:7](../14/07.md) and **Shur** in [16:7](../16/07.md). Alternate translation: [There he lived between the cities of Kadesh and Shur]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּ֖גָר בִּ⁠גְרָֽר
See how you translated “sojourn” in Genesis [12:10](../12/10.md). Also be consistent here with how you translated **Gerar** in Genesis [10:19](../10/19.md). Alternate translation: [and they stayed there for a while as foreigners, in the city of Gerar] or [and they stayed in the city of Gerar for a while]

## Genesis 20:2

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֧אמֶר אַבְרָהָ֛ם אֶל־שָׂרָ֥ה אִשְׁתּ֖⁠וֹ אֲחֹ֣תִ⁠י הִ֑וא
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-quotesinquotes
Sarah was Abraham’s younger sister (Genesis [19:19](../19/19.md)). Some languages use special words for **sister** and “brother” in Genesis [12:13](../12/13.md), [19](../12/19.md); [20:2](../20/02.md), [5](../20/05.md), [12–13](../20/12.md), [16](../20/16.md), to distinguish when someone is younger or older than his or her sibling. Alternate translation: [While they were there, he told people that his wife Sarah was his sister] or [While they were there, he told people that Sarah was his sister, not his wife]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשְׁלַ֗ח אֲבִימֶ֨לֶךְ֙ מֶ֣לֶךְ גְּרָ֔ר וַ⁠יִּקַּ֖ח אֶת־שָׂרָֽה
The city of Gerar was the Philistines’ capital city where King Abimelech ruled from (Genesis [21:2](../21/02.md), [32](../21/32.md); [26:1](../26/01.md)). Alternate translation: [So one day Abimelech, who was the king over the city of Gerar, had some of his servants bring Sarah to his home to be his wife] or [So one day King Abimelech, who ruled the Philistines from the city of Gerar, had Sarah brought to him to be his wife]

## Genesis 20:3

**Quote:** וַ⁠יָּבֹ֧א אֱלֹהִ֛ים אֶל־אֲבִימֶ֖לֶךְ בַּ⁠חֲל֣וֹם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-politeness
For some languages it is necessary to use an honorific title for a king each time (or most of the time) that he is referred to by name, in order to show him proper respect. Do what is best in your language throughout this chapter. Alternate translation: [But God appeared to King Abimelech in a dream]

**Quote:** הַ⁠לָּ֑יְלָה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
It may be more natural to put this time phrase earlier in this sentence and say, “But that night God appeared to King Abimelech in a dream …” Alternate translation: [during the night] or [by night]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֣אמֶר ל֗⁠וֹ
Alternate translation: [and told him]

**Quote:** הִנְּ⁠ךָ֥ מֵת֙
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-tense
God is using the present tense to describe something that is going to happen in the future in order to warn that the event could happen soon. If it would be clearer in your language, you could use the future tense. Make sure that the way you translate this clause does not make it sound like King Abimelech is already dead. Alternate translation: [Hear me warn you, you are a dead man]

**Quote:** עַל־הָ⁠אִשָּׁ֣ה אֲשֶׁר־לָקַ֔חְתָּ וְ⁠הִ֖וא
See how you translated “took” in [verse 2](../20/02.md).

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּאמַ֕ר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
The way you translate this quote margin will depend on how you translate the rhetorical question that follows it.

**Quote:** אֲדֹנָ֕⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-politeness
See how you translated this title of respect for God in Genesis [18:27](../18/27.md), [30–32](../18/30.md). Alternate translation: [My Master] or [Master]

**Quote:** הֲ⁠ג֥וֹי גַּם־צַדִּ֖יק תַּהֲרֹֽג

Make sure in your translation that he sounds humble and not angry or rude. Alternate translation: [please do not kill me and the people I rule, since we are righteous.]

**Quote:** צַדִּ֖יק
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Consider again how you translated the word **righteous** in the book of Genesis. See [6:9](../06/09.md), [7:1](../07/01.md), [18:23–26](../18/23.md), [28](../18/28.md); [20:4](../20/04.md). It may be necessary to translate this term in different ways, depending on the context. Alternate translation: [we are actually righteous]

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom

**Quote:** עָשִׂ֣יתָ זֹּ֔את
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure

**Quote:** וָ⁠אֶחְשֹׂ֧ךְ גַּם־אָנֹכִ֛י אֽוֹתְ⁠ךָ֖ מֵ⁠חֲטוֹ־לִ֑⁠י
In the Hebrew text, **I** is an emphatic pronoun. Try to communicate that emphasis in a natural way in your translation. Alternate translation: [and in fact, it is I who kept you from doing this sin against me] or [In fact, I am the one who prevented you from sinning against me]

**Quote:** לֹא־נְתַתִּ֖י⁠ךָ
Alternate translation: [I did not permit you to]

**Quote:** לִ⁠נְגֹּ֥עַ אֵלֶֽי⁠הָ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-infostructure
See how you translated the idiom “gone near to her” in [verse 4](../20/04.md), which has a meaning that is similar to the idiom here. Also, for some languages it may be better to change the order of these clauses and say, “I am the one who did not allow you to touch her so that you would not sin against me.” Alternate translation: [have sexual relations with her] or [do anything to her]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּאמֶר֩ אֵלָ֨י⁠ו הָֽ⁠אֱלֹהִ֜ים בַּ⁠חֲלֹ֗ם
Alternate translation: [God answered King Abimelech in the dream]

## Genesis 20:9

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּקְרָ֨א אֲבִימֶ֜לֶךְ לְ⁠אַבְרָהָ֗ם וַ⁠יֹּ֨אמֶר ל֜⁠וֹ מֶֽה־עָשִׂ֤יתָ לָּ֨⁠נוּ֙ וּ⁠מֶֽה־חָטָ֣אתִי לָ֔⁠ךְ כִּֽי־הֵבֵ֧אתָ עָלַ֛⁠י וְ⁠עַל־מַמְלַכְתִּ֖⁠י חֲטָאָ֣ה גְדֹלָ֑ה מַעֲשִׂים֙ אֲשֶׁ֣ר לֹא־יֵֽעָשׂ֔וּ עָשִׂ֖יתָ עִמָּדִֽ⁠י

And how have I sinned against you, Abraham, so that you brought a great sin on me, King Abimelech, and on my kingdom? Abraham, you have done deeds to me that should not be done]

**Quote:** וַ⁠יֹּ֨אמֶר ל֜⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-quotations
The way you translate this quote margin will depend on how you translate the rhetorical question that follows it. Alternate translation: [and asked him]

**Quote:** מֶֽה־עָשִׂ֤יתָ לָּ֨⁠נוּ֙
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Abimelech uses two rhetorical questions here to express his anger and to rebuke Abraham for what he had done. Consider whether or not a rhetorical question is the best way to communicate that in your language. Alternate translation: [You have treated us unjustly!] or [What you have done to us is not right!]

**Quote:** וּ⁠מֶֽה־חָטָ֣אתִי לָ֔⁠ךְ כִּֽי

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion

**Quote:** מַעֲשִׂים֙ אֲשֶׁ֣ר לֹא־יֵֽעָשׂ֔וּ עָשִׂ֖יתָ עִמָּדִֽ⁠י

Coins did not exist at that time. Rather, people paid each other with shekels of silver or gold shekels, which was a weight measurement. A thousand shekels of silver was about 25 pounds (11.5 kilos) of silver. Many translation teams like to keep the same numbers that are in the Hebrew text in their translation, and then in a footnote give the equivalent amount in their own currency. Other teams put the equivalent amount in the text and put the literal phrase in a footnote. Alternate translation: [I have given 1,000 pieces of silver to your brother] or [I have given your brother 1,000 pieces of silver]

**Quote:** הִנֵּ֤ה הוּא
Alternate translation: [I am doing this] or [The reason I am doing that is]

**Quote:** לָ⁠ךְ֙ כְּס֣וּת עֵינַ֔יִם לְ⁠כֹ֖ל אֲשֶׁ֣ר אִתָּ֑⁠ךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
The phrase **a covering of the eyes** is an idiom which means that King Abimelech’s payment to Abraham “covered” (that is, “protected”) Sarah’s good reputation and showed that she was innocent. Alternate translation: [to show everyone that you are innocent of any wrongdoing] or [to show to everyone that you are an honorable woman]

## Genesis 20:18

**Quote:** כִּֽי & יְהוָ֔ה
Alternate translation: [because previously Yahweh]

**Quote:** עָצֹ֤ר עָצַר֙ & בְּעַ֥ד כָּל־רֶ֖חֶם לְ⁠בֵ֣ית אֲבִימֶ֑לֶךְ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-politeness
Consider again how often you need to use the honorific title “King” with Abimelech’s name in this chapter in your language.

**Quote:** עַל־דְּבַ֥ר
Alternate translation: [on account of] or [to punish him for taking]

**Quote:** שָׂרָ֖ה אֵ֥שֶׁת אַבְרָהָֽם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-versebridge
For some languages, it may be more natural to change the order of the clauses in this sentence (which begins in [verse 17](../20/17.md)) and say, “Before this, Yahweh had completely closed up every womb in the household of King Abimelech because he had taken Abraham’s wife Sarah. Then Abraham prayed …” If the order is changed, it will be necessary to combine these two verses and to put the verse numbers “17–18” in front of the sentence. Some translation teams have decided that they will never combine verses like that. Other teams have decided that occasionally it is acceptable to do that if it makes the meaning clearer.
