## Chapter intro

# Nehemiah 6 Chapter Introduction

## Structure and Formatting

The building of the wall is completed in this chapter.

The ULT sets the lines in [6:6–7](../06/06.md) farther to the right on the page than the rest of the text because they are part of a long quotation.

## Religious and Cultural Concepts in This Chapter

### Miracle

Completing this city wall in only 52 days was considered proof that God had helped the Jews, especially given the opposition that they had experienced from the people in surrounding areas.

## Nehemiah 6:1

**Quote:** וַ⁠יְהִ֣י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/writing-newevent
Nehemiah uses this phrase to introduce the next event in his story. You do not need to represent it in your translation unless your language has a similar expression that it characteristically uses.

**Quote:** נִשְׁמַ֣ע
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
This means that these enemies learned somehow that the wall was finished. Perhaps someone came and told them, or perhaps they received a written report. Nehemiah uses hearing, one means of discovering things, to describe these men learning this. Alternate translation: [when our enemies learned]

**Quote:** כַ⁠אֲשֶׁ֣ר נִשְׁמַ֣ע & וּ⁠לְ⁠יֶ֣תֶר אֹֽיְבֵ֗י⁠נוּ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this with an active form, and you could say who did the action. Alternate translation: [when our enemies learned]

**Quote:** לְ⁠סַנְבַלַּ֣ט וְ֠⁠טוֹבִיָּה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
These are the names of men. See how you translated them in [2:10](../02/10.md).

**Quote:** וּ⁠לְ⁠גֶ֨שֶׁם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
This is the name of a man and his people group. See how you translated these names in [2:19](../02/19.md).

**Quote:** בָנִ֨יתִי֙ אֶת־הַ֣⁠חוֹמָ֔ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
As in [4:14](../04/14.md), Nehemiah is using himself to represent the entire community that he is a part of. Alternate translation: [that we had finished rebuilding the wall]

**Quote:** גַּ֚ם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-contrast
This term expresses a weak contrast between the sentence that comes before and the sentence that comes after. There was no break in the wall except for the spaces where the doors had not yet been hung in the gates. Alternate translation: [however]

**Quote:** עַד־הָ⁠עֵ֣ת הַ⁠הִ֔יא דְּלָת֖וֹת לֹא־הֶעֱמַ֥דְתִּי בַ⁠שְּׁעָרִֽים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
Once again Nehemiah is using himself to represent the entire community that he is a part of. Alternate translation: [we had not yet put the doors in the gates]

## Nehemiah 6:2

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשְׁלַ֨ח & אֵלַ֣⁠י
Alternate translation: [sent me a messenger to me]

**Quote:** לְכָ֞⁠ה וְ⁠נִֽוָּעֲדָ֥ה יַחְדָּ֛ו
These enemies use the word **come** to encourage Nehemiah to accept their invitation. If it would be helpful in your language, you could translate the word as something other than an imperative to make the message sound inviting. Alternate translation: [We would like to arrange to meet with you]

**Quote:** בַּ⁠כְּפִירִ֖ים בְּ⁠בִקְעַ֣ת אוֹנ֑וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Ono is the name of a place about twenty miles from Jerusalem. It is down from the mountains towards the Mediterranean coast, so it can be described as a “valley” or “plain.” Alternate translation: [in one of the villages in the plain of Ono]

**Quote:** וְ⁠הֵ֨מָּה֙ חֹֽשְׁבִ֔ים לַ⁠עֲשׂ֥וֹת לִ֖⁠י רָעָֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-idiom
In this context, **evil** means “harm.” Alternate translation: [they wanted to harm me]

**Quote:** וְ⁠הֵ֨מָּה֙ חֹֽשְׁבִ֔ים לַ⁠עֲשׂ֥וֹת לִ֖⁠י רָעָֽה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The implication is that Nehemiah figured this out even though in their invitation these enemies pretended to be sincere. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this explicitly. Alternate translation: [But I recognized that they were saying this because they wanted to harm me.]

## Nehemiah 6:3

**Quote:** מְלָאכָ֤ה גְדוֹלָה֙ אֲנִ֣י עֹשֶׂ֔ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-synecdoche
Nehemiah is once again using himself to represent the whole community and its work of rebuilding of the wall. Alternate translation: [We are doing a great work]

**Quote:** וְ⁠לֹ֥א אוּכַ֖ל לָ⁠רֶ֑דֶת
Nehemiah does not mean that he is physically unable to travel to Ono. He means that he cannot leave his supervision of the work for the several days it would take to travel there and back. Alternate translation: [I am not able to travel while it is going on]

**Quote:** לָ⁠רֶ֑דֶת
Nehemiah uses the word **down** because the plain of Ono is at a lower elevation than Jerusalem. Alternate translation: [meet with you]

**Quote:** לָ֣⁠מָּה תִשְׁבַּ֤ת הַ⁠מְּלָאכָה֙ כַּ⁠אֲשֶׁ֣ר אַרְפֶּ֔⁠הָ וְ⁠יָרַדְתִּ֖י אֲלֵי⁠כֶֽם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Nehemiah is making a statement, not asking a question. He does not expect Sanballat and Geshem to give him reasons why the work should stop as he travels to meet with them. Instead, Nehemiah is using the question form to emphasize how important it is for the work of rebuilding the walls to continue. If it would be helpful in your language, you could translate his question as a statement. Alternate translation: [I cannot let the work stop and come down to you]

**Quote:** וְ⁠יָרַדְתִּ֖י אֲלֵי⁠כֶֽם
The word **down** is used here because the plain of Ono where they were requesting Nehemiah to come is at a lower elevation than Jerusalem.

## Nehemiah 6:5

**Quote:** וַ⁠יִּשְׁלַח֩ אֵלַ֨⁠י סַנְבַלַּ֜ט כַּ⁠דָּבָ֥ר הַ⁠זֶּ֛ה פַּ֥עַם חֲמִישִׁ֖ית אֶֽת־נַעֲר֑⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-ordinal
Alternate translation: [Sanballat sent his servant to me with this same message for a fifth time] or [this was time number five that Sanballat sent his servant to me with this same message]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אִגֶּ֥רֶת פְּתוּחָ֖ה בְּ⁠יָדֽ⁠וֹ
This means that this fifth message was written in a letter, but the letter was unsealed. Alternate translation: [This time the message was written, but it was not sealed]

**Quote:** וְ⁠אִגֶּ֥רֶת פְּתוּחָ֖ה בְּ⁠יָדֽ⁠וֹ
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
As a diplomatic communication, the letter should have been sealed. Because it was not, others could read it and spread its contents among the people of the region. It appears that Sanballat deliberately left the letter unsealed to pressure Nehemiah to meet with him. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say that explicitly. Alternate translation: [Sanballat left the letter unsealed so that others would find out what it said, because he wanted to pressure me to meet with him]

## Nehemiah 6:7

**Quote:** וְ⁠גַם
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-words-phrases
This phrase indicates that what follows is more of the report that Sanballat claims he is hearing from the people in the surrounding countries. If it would be helpful in your language, you could put something in your translation to indicate this. Alternate translation: [These people are also saying that]

**Quote:** נְבִיאִ֡ים הֶעֱמַ֣דְתָּ לִ⁠קְרֹא֩ עָלֶ֨י⁠ךָ בִֽ⁠ירוּשָׁלִַ֜ם
Alternate translation: [you have appointed prophets to make this proclamation about you in Jerusalem]

**Quote:** לֵ⁠אמֹ֗ר מֶ֚לֶךְ בִּֽ⁠יהוּדָ֔ה
The proclamation can be presented as a direct quotation. “Saying” can be represented by the quotation marks and whatever punctuation your language uses to introduce a quotation. Alternate translation: [The Jews now have a king of their own]

**Quote:** וְ⁠עַתָּה֙
This is not a reference to time. Sanballat is using this expression to introduce a further inference. Alternate translation: [certainly]

**Quote:** יִשָּׁמַ֣ע לַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ כַּ⁠דְּבָרִ֣ים הָ⁠אֵ֑לֶּה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this with an active form. Alternate translation: [King Artaxerxes will certainly hear these reports]

**Quote:** יִשָּׁמַ֣ע לַ⁠מֶּ֔לֶךְ כַּ⁠דְּבָרִ֣ים הָ⁠אֵ֑לֶּה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The implication is that when Artaxerxes hears these reports, he will be very angry with Nehemiah. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say that explicitly. Alternate translation: [King Artaxerxes will certainly hear these reports, and when he does, he will be very angry with you]

**Quote:** וְ⁠עַתָּה֙
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-logic-result
This is not a reference to time. Sanballat is using this expression to introduce his conclusion. This term expresses that the sentences that came before provide the reason for the sentence that comes after. Alternate translation: [Therefore]

**Quote:** לְכָ֔⁠ה וְ⁠נִֽוָּעֲצָ֖ה יַחְדָּֽו
As in [6:2](../06/02.md), “come” is a word of encouragement rather than a command. Alternate translation: [So we really should meet together and talk about this]

## Nehemiah 6:9

**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
This likely means, “they were all trying to frighten us.” The implication is that Nehemiah recognized that this was the motive behind the letters. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say that explicitly. Alternate translation: [I knew that they were all just trying to frighten us]

**Quote:** לֵ⁠אמֹ֔ר
Alternate translation: [They were thinking]

**Quote:** יִרְפּ֧וּ יְדֵי⁠הֶ֛ם מִן־הַ⁠מְּלָאכָ֖ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here, **hand** represents strength, power, control, or action. Alternate translation: [The workers will stop doing the work on the wall]

**Quote:** יִרְפּ֧וּ יְדֵי⁠הֶ֛ם מִן־הַ⁠מְּלָאכָ֖ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
The implication is that the enemies expected the Jews to become so afraid of being accused of rebelling for rebuilding the wall that they would stop doing that. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this explicitly. Alternate translation: [The Jews will become so afraid that they will stop working on the wall]

**Quote:** מִן־הַ⁠מְּלָאכָ֖ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
This means specifically the work on the wall. If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this explicitly. Alternate translation: [working on the wall]

**Quote:** וְ⁠לֹ֣א תֵעָשֶׂ֑ה
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
If it would be helpful in your language, you could say this with an active form, and you could say who will do the action. Alternate translation: [they will never finish rebuilding it]

**Quote:** וְ⁠עַתָּ֖ה חַזֵּ֥ק אֶת־יָדָֽ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-aside
Nehemiah speaks directly to God once again here. This instance is like the one in [4:4](../04/04.md), where Nehemiah records what he prayed at the time of the events he is describing. If it would be helpful to your readers, you could indicate this by introducing the prayer with a phrase such as “so I prayed,” by presenting the prayer as a direct quotation, and by having Nehemiah address God directly at the beginning. Alternate translation: [So I prayed, ‘O God, give me courage’]

**Quote:** חַזֵּ֥ק אֶת־יָדָֽ⁠י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Here the strength of a person’s hands stands for the courage they are feeling inside. Alternate translation: [strengthen me]

## Nehemiah 6:10

**Quote:** וַ⁠אֲנִי
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/grammar-connect-time-simultaneous
Nehemiah uses this phrase to introduce something else that happened around this same time. Alternate translation: [Around this time]

**Quote:** בָ֗אתִי בֵּ֣ית
Alternate translation: [I went to visit]

**Quote:** שְֽׁמַֽעְיָ֧ה בֶן־דְּלָיָ֛ה בֶּן־מְהֵֽיטַבְאֵ֖ל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-names
Shemaiah is the name of a man, Delaiah is the name of his father, and Mehetabel is the name of his grandfather. Alternate translation: [Shemaiah, the son of Delaiah and grandson of Mehetabel]

**Quote:** וְ⁠ה֣וּא עָצ֑וּר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-activepassive
You can say this with an active form, and you can say who did the action. Alternate translation: [he was not leaving his house.]

**Quote:** וְ⁠ה֣וּא עָצ֑וּר
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/translate-symaction
Nehemiah does not say why Shemaiah was not leaving his house. He could have been sick or he could have been ceremonially unclean, so you might choose not to specify any reason. However, the context suggests that Shemaiah may have been trying to symbolize that it was not safe for Jewish leaders to go around in public. A man by the name of Shemaiah is listed in [10:8](../10/08.md) as one of the priests who signed the community agreement. This could be the same person since he has access to the temple and is therefore likely a priest. If it would be helpful in your language, you could suggest this reason explicitly. Alternate translation: [He was a priest, and he was trying to show that it was not safe for Jewish leaders to go out in public, so he was not leaving his house]

**Quote:** נִוָּעֵד֩ אֶל־בֵּ֨ית הָ⁠אֱלֹהִ֜ים אֶל־תּ֣וֹךְ הַֽ⁠הֵיכָ֗ל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
Shemaiah seems to be proposing that he and Nehemiah move their meeting to the temple, suggesting that they are not even safe in his house. Alternate translation: [We are not safe even here. We need to go into the temple, to the sacred place inside the temple]

**Quote:** אֶל־בֵּ֨ית הָ⁠אֱלֹהִ֜ים אֶל־תּ֣וֹךְ הַֽ⁠הֵיכָ֗ל
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-parallelism
These two phrases mean similar things. Shemaiah uses the repetition to emphasize the danger he is suggesting they are in. You do not need to repeat both phrases in your translation if that would be confusing for your readers. Alternate translation: [into the temple]

**Quote:** בֵּ֨ית הָ⁠אֱלֹהִ֜ים
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-metaphor
Shemaiah is referring to the temple as the “house of God,” as if it were God’s dwelling place. Alternate translation: [the temple]

**Quote:** וְ⁠נִסְגְּרָה֙ דַּלְת֣וֹת הַ⁠הֵיכָ֔ל
This means to not just close the doors but lock them. Alternate translation: [and lock the doors]

**Quote:** כִּ֚י בָּאִ֣ים לְ⁠הָרְגֶ֔⁠ךָ
Shemaiah does not say who these people are. It would be possible to say something based on what can reasonably be inferred. For example, “Your enemies have hired people to kill you.” However, the vagueness seems to be part of Shemaiah’s strategy to frighten Nehemiah. So it might be more effective to leave this indefinite. Alternate translation: [because people are trying to kill you]

**Quote:** וְ⁠לַ֖יְלָה בָּאִ֥ים לְ⁠הָרְגֶֽ⁠ךָ
Shemaiah’s confidence in the detail of “at night” seems designed to make Nehemiah even more afraid, because this means that the killers will come in the dark when he is asleep, so he will not be able to protect himself. It could be helpful to your readers for your translation to reflect this confidence. Alternate translation: [I know that one night they are going to come and kill you]

## Nehemiah 6:11

**Quote:** וָ⁠אֹמְרָ֗⁠ה
Alternate translation: [I responded]

**Quote:** הַ⁠אִ֤ישׁ כָּמ֨וֹ⁠נִי֙ יִבְרָ֔ח
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Nehemiah is making a statement, not asking a question. He does not expect Shemiah to tell him whether or not he should run away. Instead, Nehemiah is using the question form to emphasize that he is not going to do what Shemaiah has suggested. If it would be helpful in your language, you could translate this question as a statement. Alternate translation: [A man like me would not run away.]

**Quote:** וּ⁠מִ֥י כָמ֛וֹ⁠נִי אֲשֶׁר־יָב֥וֹא אֶל־הַ⁠הֵיכָ֖ל וָ⁠חָ֑י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-rquestion
Once again Nehemiah is making a statement, not asking a question. He does not expect Shemiah to tell him whether going into the temple would save his life. Alternate translation: [A man like me would not go into the temple just to hide to stay alive.]

**Quote:** וּ⁠מִ֥י כָמ֛וֹ⁠נִי אֲשֶׁר־יָב֥וֹא אֶל־הַ⁠הֵיכָ֖ל וָ⁠חָ֑י
**Support:** rc://*/ta/man/translate/figs-explicit
It is not entirely clear what Nehemiah means by this. He could be saying that he should not go into the temple because he is not a priest. However, the Bible records instances of other people who were not priests going into the temple to seek sanctuary. So Nehemiah may be saying instead that because he is the governor he is too well known to escape from assassins by hiding in the temple. You could say that explicitly. Alternate translation: [Besides, I am the governor, and everyone knows me, so I could not save my life by trying to hide in the temple]

**Quote:** לֹ֖א אָבֽוֹא
Alternate translation: [I refuse to do it!]
