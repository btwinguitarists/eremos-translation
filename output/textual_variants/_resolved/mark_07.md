## Per-candidate disposition

### Mark 7:4

Translator's notes (verbatim):

> TWO HAPAX in one verse: ξέστης (Latinism) and χαλκίον. TEXTUAL VARIANT 'καὶ κλινῶν': SBLGNT retains; NA28 brackets; the Byzantine majority and many late MSS include it. We follow SBLGNT. The variant doesn't change theological force — only the breadth of the inventory. The sequence of vessel-purity regulations corresponds to Mishnah tractate Kelim ('Vessels'), which devotes 30 chapters to such rules.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream English (BSB/ESV/NIV/CSB) and current Thai (THSV) follow the same shorter reading; reader-trust is preserved.

### Mark 7:17

Translator's notes (verbatim):

> TEXTUAL ISSUE for v.16: SBLGNT/NA28 omit Mark 7:16 entirely. Some MSS (A D Θ etc., later texts) include 'Εἴ τις ἔχει ὦτα ἀκούειν, ἀκουέτω' ('If anyone has ears to hear, let him hear') — almost certainly assimilation from Mark 4:9, 4:23. Earliest witnesses (א B L Δ) lack it. We follow SBLGNT and skip v.16, jumping verse-numbering 15 → 17 (preserving traditional verse-numbering even though no text occupies v.16). PRIVATE EXPLANATION pattern: as in Mark 4:10–20 (sower parable), Jesus teaches the crowd in parables and then explains in private to the disciples. The motif underlines that genuine understanding of the kingdom is given to the inner circle.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream English (BSB/ESV/NIV/CSB) and current Thai (THSV) follow the same shorter reading; reader-trust is preserved.
