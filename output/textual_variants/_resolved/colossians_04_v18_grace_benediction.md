# Colossians 4:18 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate(s) from `colossians_04.json`:

- Colossians 4:18 — closing benediction extension `τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ μετὰ τοῦ πνεύματος ὑμῶν, ἀμήν` (omitted by SBLGNT, included by some manuscripts as scribal-harmonization to standard Pauline letter-closings)

SBLGNT here gives the very-brief closing `ἡ χάρις μεθ’ ὑμῶν` ("Grace be with you"). Some later manuscripts (Byzantine majority) extend the formula to `ἡ χάρις τοῦ κυρίου ἡμῶν Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ μετὰ τοῦ πνεύματος ὑμῶν, ἀμήν` — the fuller closing pattern that matches Php 4:23, Phm 25, etc.

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

This is a textbook case of scribal-harmonization: scribes familiar with longer Pauline closing-formulas (especially Php 4:23, 1Th 5:28, 2Th 3:18, Phm 25) naturally elaborated the unusually-brief Colossians ending. The early-Alexandrian + Western witness coverage for the shorter reading is overwhelming.

Notably, the same kind of variant (closing `ἀμήν` add) was resolved for Philippians 4:23 — see `output/textual_variants/_resolved/philippians_04_final_amen.md`. Pauline-letter-closings are a systematic site of scribal-harmonization additions.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream critical-text consensus matches our practice; the brief-and-unembellished closing aligns with what readers familiar with the BSB / ESV / NIV / CSB Colossians ending will expect.
