# 1 Timothy 5:16 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate from `1timothy_05.json`:

- 1 Timothy 5:16 — variant `πιστή` ("believing-woman") vs `πιστὸς ἢ πιστή` ("believing-man-or-woman")

SBLGNT here gives the shorter `πιστή` ("believing-woman"). Some manuscripts (Byzantine majority) read the extended `πιστὸς ἢ πιστή` ("believing-man-or-woman"), which gender-broadens the responsibility to extended-family-of-widows.

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

### 1 Timothy 5:16 (variant feminine vs masculine-or-feminine)

Manuscript witnesses:
- **Shorter `πιστή`**: א A C F G P 048 33 81 1175 1739 etc. (early-Alexandrian + Western)
- **Extended `πιστὸς ἢ πιστή`**: D K L Ψ 𝔐 (Byzantine majority)

uW FRONT explicitly lists this variant: "'believing woman' ([5:16](../05/16.md)). Some ancient manuscripts have this: 'believing man or woman.'"

Mainstream evangelical English Bibles all follow SBLGNT shorter:
- **BSB**: "any believing woman" ✓
- **ESV**: "any believing woman" ✓
- **NIV**: "any woman who is a believer" ✓
- **CSB**: "any believing woman" ✓
- **NASB**: "any woman who is a believer" ✓
- **KJV/NKJV**: "any believing man or woman" (Byzantine TR)

The early-Alexandrian + Western witness coverage for the shorter feminine reading is decisive. The Byzantine extended reading was likely a scribal-amplification to broaden the responsibility (concerned that men might also have widow-relatives).

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream critical-text consensus matches our practice; reader-trust is preserved.
