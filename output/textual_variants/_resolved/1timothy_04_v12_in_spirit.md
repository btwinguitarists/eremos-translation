# 1 Timothy 4:12 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate from `1timothy_04.json`:

- 1 Timothy 4:12 — interpolation `ἐν πνεύματι` ("in spirit") in the virtue-list

SBLGNT here gives a 5-fold list: `ἐν λόγῳ, ἐν ἀναστροφῇ, ἐν ἀγάπῃ, ἐν πίστει, ἐν ἁγνείᾳ` ("in speech, in conduct, in love, in faith, in purity"). Some manuscripts (Byzantine majority) extend to a 6-fold list with `ἐν πνεύματι` ("in spirit") inserted before `ἐν πίστει`.

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

### 1 Timothy 4:12 (interpolation "in spirit")

Manuscript witnesses:
- **5-fold reading** (no `ἐν πνεύματι`): א* A C F G P 33 81 1739 etc. (early-Alexandrian + Western)
- **6-fold reading** (with `ἐν πνεύματι`): א² D K L Ψ 𝔐 (Byzantine majority + secondary correctors)

uW FRONT explicitly lists this variant: "'in love' ([4:12](../04/12.md)). Some ancient manuscripts have this: 'in love, in spirit.'"

Mainstream evangelical English Bibles all follow SBLGNT 5-fold:
- **BSB**: 5-fold (no "in spirit") ✓
- **ESV**: 5-fold ✓
- **NIV**: 5-fold ✓
- **CSB**: 5-fold ✓
- **NASB**: 5-fold ✓
- **KJV/NKJV**: 6-fold (Byzantine TR with "in spirit")

This is a textbook scribal-amplification: the 5-fold list seemed incomplete to a scribe accustomed to Pauline-virtue-lists that often include πνεῦμα. The early-Alexandrian + Western witness coverage for the 5-fold reading is decisive.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream critical-text consensus matches our practice; reader-trust is preserved.
