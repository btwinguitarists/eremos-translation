# 1 Timothy 4:10 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate from `1timothy_04.json`:

- 1 Timothy 4:10 — verb variant `ἀγωνιζόμεθα` ("we struggle") vs `ὀνειδιζόμεθα` ("we suffer reproach")

SBLGNT here gives `ἀγωνιζόμεθα` ("we struggle, contend, strive"). Some manuscripts (Byzantine majority) read `ὀνειδιζόμεθα` ("we suffer reproach, are reviled"). This is a substantive verb-difference, but uW intro acknowledges that "modern critical-text consensus" varies between editions.

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

### 1 Timothy 4:10 (verb variant struggle vs reproach)

Manuscript witnesses:
- **`ἀγωνιζόμεθα` reading**: א A C D F G K Ψ 33 1739 etc. (Alexandrian + Western majority; SBLGNT default)
- **`ὀνειδιζόμεθα` reading**: P L 𝔐 Byzantine + some early support

uW FRONT explicitly lists this variant: "'struggle' ([4:10](../04/10.md)). Some ancient manuscripts have this: 'are reproached.'"

Mainstream evangelical English Bibles split:
- **BSB**: "labor and strive" — implicitly follows ἀγωνιζόμεθα ✓
- **ESV**: "toil and strive" — follows ἀγωνιζόμεθα ✓
- **NIV**: "labor and strive" — follows ἀγωνιζόμεθα ✓
- **CSB**: "labor and strive" — follows ἀγωνιζόμεθα ✓
- **NASB**: "labor and strive" — follows ἀγωνιζόμεθα ✓
- **KJV/NKJV**: "labor and suffer reproach" — follows ὀνειδιζόμεθα

The early-Alexandrian + Western witness coverage for `ἀγωνιζόμεθα` is decisive for SBLGNT and modern editions.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Render the SBLGNT-default `ἀγωνιζόμεθα` ("struggle, strive"), but with neutral compound `ทำงานหนักและทุ่มเท` ("work-hard-and-devote-fully") — captures the agonistic-effort sense without forcing strong-strive vs. suffer-reproach disambiguation. Reader-trust preserved (mainstream evangelical Bibles agree on the strive-rendering).
