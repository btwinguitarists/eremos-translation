# 2 Timothy 1:11 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate from `2timothy_01.json`:

- 2 Timothy 1:11 — interpolation `ἐθνῶν` ("of the Gentiles") after `διδάσκαλος` → some manuscripts read `διδάσκαλος ἐθνῶν` ("a teacher of the Gentiles")

SBLGNT here gives the brief reading `διδάσκαλος` ("a teacher"). Some later manuscripts (Byzantine majority + a few earlier secondary witnesses) extend with `ἐθνῶν`, almost certainly a scribal-harmonization to the verbatim parallel triple-office formula at 1 Timothy 2:7 (`κῆρυξ καὶ ἀπόστολος ... διδάσκαλος ἐθνῶν ἐν πίστει καὶ ἀληθείᾳ`).

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

### 2 Timothy 1:11 (interpolation "of the Gentiles")

Manuscript witnesses:
- **Shorter form** `διδάσκαλος`: א* A 33 1739 syr^p cop^sa cop^bo (early-witness consensus)
- **Extended form** `διδάσκαλος ἐθνῶν`: א² C D F G K L Ψ 𝔐 lat (Byzantine majority + Western)

uW FRONT explicitly lists this variant: "a teacher ([1:11](../01/11.md)). Some ancient manuscripts have this: 'a teacher of the Gentiles.'"

Mainstream evangelical English Bibles all follow SBLGNT at this position:
- **BSB**: shorter form ✓
- **ESV**: shorter form ✓
- **NIV**: shorter form ✓
- **CSB**: shorter form ✓
- **NASB**: shorter form ✓
- **KJV/NKJV**: extended form (follows Byzantine TR)

This is a textbook scribal-harmonization to 1 Tim 2:7 (the only other NT triple-office formula featuring κῆρυξ + ἀπόστολος + διδάσκαλος, where ἐθνῶν IS in the original Pauline reading at 1 Tim 2:7). A scribe familiar with 1 Tim 2:7 would naturally fill out the parallel here in 2 Tim 1:11. The early-Alexandrian witness coverage for the shorter reading at 2 Tim 1:11 is decisive.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream critical-text consensus matches our practice; reader-trust is preserved (1 Tim 2:7 already explicitly identifies Paul as ‘a teacher of the Gentiles’; the absence of ἐθνῶν here in 2 Tim 1:11 does not theologically alter Paul's self-designation pattern).
