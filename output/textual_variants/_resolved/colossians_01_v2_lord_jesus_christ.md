# Colossians 1:2 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate(s) from `colossians_01.json`:

- Colossians 1:2 — closing `καὶ κυρίου Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ` (omitted by SBLGNT, included by some manuscripts as scribal-harmonization to standard Pauline greeting-formula)

The Pauline opening grace-formula normally takes the form `χάρις ὑμῖν καὶ εἰρήνη ἀπὸ θεοῦ πατρὸς ἡμῶν καὶ κυρίου Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ` (cf. Rom 1:7, 1Co 1:3, 2Co 1:2, Gal 1:3, Eph 1:2, Php 1:2, 2Th 1:2, Phm 3, etc.). At Col 1:2, SBLGNT — following the earliest manuscript witnesses — gives the shorter form `χάρις ὑμῖν καὶ εἰρήνη ἀπὸ θεοῦ πατρὸς ἡμῶν` only. Some later manuscripts (Byzantine majority) supply the missing clause through scribal-harmonization to the standard Pauline pattern.

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."
