# 1 Peter 1:22 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate from `1peter_01.json`:

- 1 Peter 1:22 — interpolation `διὰ πνεύματος` ("through the Spirit") modifying the obedience-of-the-truth clause

SBLGNT here gives `Τὰς ψυχὰς ὑμῶν ἡγνικότες ἐν τῇ ὑπακοῇ τῆς ἀληθείας` ("having purified your souls in obedience to the truth") without `διὰ πνεύματος`. A subset of mainly Byzantine + later witnesses inserts `διὰ πνεύματος` after `ἀληθείας` — almost certainly a scribal-amplification harmonizing-to-the-pneumatological-emphasis of the surrounding passage.

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

Manuscript witnesses:

Mainstream evangelical English Bibles uniformly omit:
- **BSB**: omits ✓ (no "through the Spirit")
- **ESV**: omits ✓
- **NIV**: omits ✓
- **CSB**: omits ✓
- **NASB**: omits ✓
- **KJV/NKJV**: include "through the Spirit" (Byzantine TR)

Mainstream Thai Bibles:
- **THSV (สมาคมพระคริสตธรรมไทย / Thai Standard Version)**: omits — follows critical text
- **THKJV (Thai King-James-Version family)**: includes (follows TR)

This is a clear modern-critical-text-consensus case: all major modern English + Thai critical-text-following translations omit. Reader-trust is preserved by the silent-omission. The phrase is not narratively-load-bearing — its presence or absence does not change which clause governs `Τὰς ψυχὰς ὑμῶν ἡγνικότες`; it merely amplifies the agency.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream critical-text consensus (and BSB) matches our practice; no Tier 1/2/3 disposition warranted; reader-trust is preserved.
