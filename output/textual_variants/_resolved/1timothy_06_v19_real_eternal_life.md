# 1 Timothy 6:19 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate from `1timothy_06.json`:

- 1 Timothy 6:19 — variant `ὄντως ζωῆς` ("the real life") vs `αἰωνίου ζωῆς` ("eternal life")

SBLGNT here gives `ὄντως ζωῆς` ("the real / true life"). Some manuscripts (Byzantine majority) read `αἰωνίου ζωῆς` ("eternal life"). The Byzantine reading is likely a scribal-harmonization to Pauline-frequent eternal-life-formula.

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

### 1 Timothy 6:19 (variant real-life vs eternal-life)

Manuscript witnesses:
- **`ὄντως ζωῆς`**: א A D F G I 33 1739 etc. (early-Alexandrian + Western)
- **`αἰωνίου ζωῆς`**: K L Ψ 𝔐 (Byzantine majority)

uW FRONT explicitly lists this variant: "'the real life' ([6:19](../06/19.md)). Some ancient manuscripts have this: 'eternal life.'"

Mainstream evangelical English Bibles all follow SBLGNT real-life:
- **BSB**: "that which is truly life" ✓
- **ESV**: "that which is truly life" ✓
- **NIV**: "the life that is truly life" ✓
- **CSB**: "what is truly life" ✓
- **NASB**: "that which is truly life" ✓
- **KJV/NKJV**: "eternal life" (Byzantine TR)

The early-Alexandrian + Western witness coverage for `ὄντως ζωῆς` is decisive. The Byzantine reading is the easier/expected reading (eternal-life is Pauline-frequent), making it a textbook-case of harmonization. The harder reading `ὄντως ζωῆς` (the-real-life — using ὄντως as 1Tim 5:3, 5, 16's-genuine-widow distinction) is original.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream critical-text consensus matches our practice; reader-trust is preserved. Render preserves the connection to 1Tim 5's ὄντως-genuine theme (genuine-widow + genuine-life).
