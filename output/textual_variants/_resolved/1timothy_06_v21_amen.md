# 1 Timothy 6:21 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate from `1timothy_06.json`:

- 1 Timothy 6:21 — interpolation `ἀμήν` ("Amen") at the close of the letter

SBLGNT here gives `Ἡ χάρις μεθ’ ὑμῶν` ("Grace be with you") without final ἀμήν. Some manuscripts (Byzantine majority) extend with `ἀμήν` ("Amen"), almost certainly a scribal-amplification to liturgically-conform the closing.

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

### 1 Timothy 6:21 (interpolation "Amen")

Manuscript witnesses:
- **Without ἀμήν**: F G 33 81 1739 1881 etc.
- **With ἀμήν**: א A D K L Ψ 𝔐 (mixed Alexandrian + Byzantine)

uW FRONT explicitly lists this variant: "'you' ([6:21](../06/21.md)). Some ancient manuscripts have this: 'you. Amen.'"

Mainstream evangelical English Bibles split — some include "Amen" in italics/footnotes, others omit:
- **BSB**: omits ✓ (no Amen)
- **ESV**: omits ✓
- **NIV**: omits ✓
- **CSB**: omits ✓
- **NASB**: omits ✓
- **KJV/NKJV**: includes "Amen" (Byzantine TR)

Note: Both readings are well-supported here, making this a closer-call than the v.19 variant. Modern critical editions slightly favor the shorter reading because liturgical-amplification is the more common direction of textual-drift.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream critical-text consensus (and BSB) matches our practice; reader-trust is preserved.
