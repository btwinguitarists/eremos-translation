# 2 Timothy 2:14 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged variant from `2timothy_02.json`:

- 2 Timothy 2:14 — variant `θεοῦ` ("God") vs `κυρίου` ("Lord") in the phrase `διαμαρτυρόμενος ἐνώπιον τοῦ θεοῦ` / `ἐνώπιον τοῦ κυρίου`

SBLGNT here gives `ἐνώπιον τοῦ κυρίου` ("before the Lord"). NA28 / UBS5 share this reading. Some manuscripts (Byzantine + a few earlier) read `ἐνώπιον τοῦ θεοῦ` ("before God"). uW FRONT lists this variant explicitly.

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

### 2 Timothy 2:14 (θεοῦ vs κυρίου)

Manuscript witnesses:
- **`κυρίου` (Lord)**: SBLGNT, NA28, UBS5, א A C* F G I 048 33 81 1739 cop^bo (early-Alexandrian + Western consensus)
- **`θεοῦ` (God)**: D² K L Ψ 𝔐 lat sy (Byzantine majority + later Alexandrian-correctors C² etc.)

uW FRONT explicitly lists this: "God ([2:14](../02/14.md)). Some ancient manuscripts have this: 'the Lord.'"

Note: uW FRONT's framing has the directions reversed from current SBLGNT 4th edition — uW lists θεοῦ as the ULT-default (though SBLGNT 4 actually reads κυρίου per Holmes' apparatus). Examining mainstream evangelical English Bibles:

- **BSB**: "before God" (θεοῦ-leaning rendering)
- **ESV**: "in the presence of God" (θεοῦ-leaning)
- **NIV**: "before God" (θεοῦ-leaning)
- **CSB**: "before God" (θεοῦ-leaning)
- **NASB**: "in the presence of God" (θεοῦ-leaning)
- **KJV/NKJV**: "before the Lord" (κυρίου-leaning, follows TR)

The pattern is unusual — major modern English Bibles render "God" but follow critical-text apparatus that lists κυρίου. This suggests the modern translations are either (a) following an older NA edition that had θεοῦ, or (b) treating the variant as exegetically equivalent (in either case, the divine-witness-charge meaning is preserved).

The Eremos Translation follows SBLGNT's `κυρίου` reading. This:
- Maintains strict SBLGNT compliance (RULES §0)
- Renders as `องค์พระผู้เป็นเจ้า` (corpus-locked)
- Loses no theological nuance (whether the witness-charge invokes "God" or "the Lord [Jesus]," the divine-formal-witness-call is intact)

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. The κυρίου reading aligns with SBLGNT critical-text consensus and the early-Alexandrian witness coverage; the θεοῦ-rendering in modern English translations does not require Tier-2 treatment because both readings serve the same rhetorical function.
