# Philippians 4:23 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate(s) from `philippians_04.json`:

- Philippians 4:23 — post-verse closing `ἀμήν` (omitted by SBLGNT, included by some manuscripts)

The closing benediction `ἡ χάρις τοῦ κυρίου Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ μετὰ τοῦ πνεύματος ὑμῶν` is sometimes followed by `ἀμήν` in manuscript witnesses (notably Byzantine majority text) but SBLGNT — following the earliest manuscript witnesses (𝔓⁴⁶ B etc.) — omits the closing `ἀμήν`. The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

### Philippians 4:23 (final ἀμήν)

Manuscript witnesses:
- **Omits ἀμήν**: 𝔓⁴⁶ א* A B F G 048 33 1739 etc. (early Alexandrian + Western)
- **Includes ἀμήν**: א² D Ψ 075 1175 1881 𝔐 (Byzantine majority + some others)

Mainstream evangelical English Bibles all follow SBLGNT at this position:
- **BSB**: omits closing Amen ✓
- **ESV**: omits closing Amen ✓
- **NIV**: omits closing Amen ✓
- **CSB**: omits closing Amen ✓
- **NASB**: omits closing Amen ✓

uW notes acknowledge the variant: "Some versions have 'Amen' at the end of the final verse in the letter (4:23). The ULT, UST, and some modern versions include it, but many other versions do not."

The included `ἀμήν` is a common scribal-liturgical addition at the close of NT epistles — a pattern of pious-liturgical-completion that scribes added to many letters' endings (cf. similar variants at Rom 16:24, 1Co 16:24, 2Co 13:14, Gal 6:18, Eph 6:24, etc.). Modern critical-text scholarship treats these as scribal additions, not original.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream critical-text consensus matches our practice; reader-trust is preserved (Thai readers familiar with THSV may notice — THSV varies; THKJV would include based on Byzantine base — but the omission is well within the evangelical critical-text-standard practice).
