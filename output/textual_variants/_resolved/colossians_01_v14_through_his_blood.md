# Colossians 1:14 — silent-omission per RULES §5

**Status:** silent-omission (no Tier 1/2/3 treatment).

This doc resolves the following audit-flagged candidate(s) from `colossians_01.json`:

- Colossians 1:14 — `διὰ τοῦ αἵματος αὐτοῦ` ("through his blood") — omitted by SBLGNT, included by some manuscripts as scribal-harmonization to Eph 1:7

SBLGNT here follows the earliest manuscript witnesses with the shorter reading: `ἐν ᾧ ἔχομεν τὴν ἀπολύτρωσιν, τὴν ἄφεσιν τῶν ἁμαρτιῶν` ("in whom we have redemption, the forgiveness of sins"). Some later manuscripts add `διὰ τοῦ αἵματος αὐτοῦ` ("through his blood") between `ἀπολύτρωσιν` and the apposition, almost certainly via scribal-harmonization to the very-similar Ephesians 1:7 (`ἐν ᾧ ἔχομεν τὴν ἀπολύτρωσιν διὰ τοῦ αἵματος αὐτοῦ, τὴν ἄφεσιν τῶν παραπτωμάτων`).

The RULES §5 category 4 applies: "Variants where modern critical-text consensus matches our practice — silent omission with no surprise to mainstream readers; no special treatment needed."

## Per-candidate disposition

### Colossians 1:14 (διὰ τοῦ αἵματος αὐτοῦ)

Manuscript witnesses:
- **Omits "διὰ τοῦ αἵματος αὐτοῦ"**: 𝔓⁴⁶ א A B C D F G Ψ 048 075 33 81 1739 etc. (overwhelming early-witness consensus)
- **Includes the phrase**: 614 630 2464 + a few late minuscules (very weak attestation)

Mainstream evangelical English Bibles all follow SBLGNT at this position:
- **BSB**: omits "through his blood" ✓
- **ESV**: omits ✓
- **NIV**: omits ✓
- **CSB**: omits ✓
- **NASB**: omits ✓

uW notes acknowledge: "Some later manuscripts add 'through his blood' after **redemption**. Most likely, 'through his blood' was accidentally added because of how similar this verse is to Ephesians 1:7, which does include 'through his blood.' Most likely, you should not include 'through his blood' in your translation."

The early-witness coverage for omission is overwhelming — this is one of the clearest scribal-harmonization additions in the NT manuscript tradition. The blood-of-the-cross reference appears explicitly at Col 1:20 (the cosmic-reconciliation clause), so omitting from v.14 does not deprive the reader of Pauline-blood-atonement teaching elsewhere in the chapter.

**Disposition:** silent-omission per RULES §5. Mainstream critical-text consensus matches our practice; reader-trust is preserved (v.20's `αἷμα τοῦ σταυροῦ αὐτοῦ` provides the cross-blood reference within the same chapter).
